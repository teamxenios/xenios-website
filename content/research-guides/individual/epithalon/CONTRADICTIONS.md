# Epithalon Contradictions and Divergences

Where the sources disagree, and what the Guide should say when they do. Source ids refer to
SOURCE_REGISTRY.md. Claim ids refer to CLAIM_TABLE.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

There are genuine contradictions here, and they matter. The first two concern claims a member is
very likely to encounter before reading the Guide. The third is unusual and important: for this
compound, the story told as the benefit and the story told as the risk are the same story.

## C-01. Whether meaningful human evidence exists at all

**Position A.** Commercial and secondary sources present a substantial human record, typically
pointing to the retinitis pigmentosa series and to multi-year elderly mortality reductions.

**Position B.** Direct registry checks found no ClinicalTrials.gov registration under either
spelling (S08, S09). The retinitis pigmentosa work is a non-randomized single-institution report
by the developing group (S05). The mortality studies used the Epithalamin extract and Thymalin,
not the synthetic tetrapeptide (S06).

**Assessment.** Position B is what the retrieved primary records support. The apparent human
evidence base is thinner than the citation count suggests, and part of it is about a different
substance. No randomized, blinded, placebo-controlled human trial of the AEDG tetrapeptide was
retrieved.

**What the Guide says.** No controlled human trial of the tetrapeptide has been located. The two
human items are described individually, with their designs and their substances named. See
EPI-H-01 through EPI-H-04 and the prohibitions at EPI-X-01, EPI-X-02, and EPI-X-07.

## C-02. Independent replication outside the originating Russian programme

**Position A.** Critics state that nearly every study comes from one Russian laboratory and that
independent replication is absent.

**Position B.** At least one 2025 in vitro study from Brunel University London (S02) and one 2025
Italian in vitro study (S12) exist without the originating investigator as an author, and a 2022
mouse-oocyte study (S11) appears to be from a separate group.

**Assessment.** Both are partly right and the distinction matters. Independent work does exist,
but everything retrieved that is plausibly independent is cell-culture or animal work, and one of
those papers carries an unexplained published correction (S03). Some non-Russian publications are
collaborations including the originating investigator (S04) and are therefore not independent.
**No independent human replication was found.**

This is a case where the honest answer is more nuanced than either the promotional framing or the
dismissive one, and where taking the dismissive side uncritically would itself be inaccurate.

**What the Guide says.** Independent preclinical work exists and is named. Independent human
replication does not. See EPI-H-05, EPI-Q-02, EPI-Q-05, and the prohibition at EPI-X-08.

## C-03. Telomerase activation as a benefit versus as a risk

**Position A.** Telomerase and hTERT upregulation, and telomere lengthening, are presented as the
mechanism of geroprotective benefit.

**Position B.** The same mechanism is a recognised oncological concern, and the strongest
retrieved in vitro paper reports telomere lengthening in human cancer cell lines via alternative
lengthening of telomeres (S02).

**Assessment.** The mechanism claimed as the benefit is the same mechanism generating the safety
question. A Guide must not present the mechanistic story as favourable without carrying that
tension explicitly. No human carcinogenicity data were retrieved either way, and a 2025
peer-reviewed review states that carcinogenicity and genotoxicity assessment are needed and were
not available (S01).

Note the further tension inside the preclinical record itself. A title-level 2002 report describes
suppression of breast adenocarcinoma development in transgenic HER-2/neu mice (S13), which reads
in the opposite direction from the cancer cell line finding. That report is title-level only, its
effect sizes and controls are unverified, and mouse tumour models do not settle a question raised
in human cell lines. The Guide states both and states that nothing retrieved resolves them.

**What the Guide says.** Mechanism and safety are written as one passage, not two. See EPI-M-01,
EPI-M-02, EPI-S-02, EPI-U-05, and the prohibitions at EPI-X-05 and EPI-X-06.

## C-04. In vitro telomerase activity versus in vivo human effect

**Position A.** Cell-culture telomerase and telomere findings are cited as showing the compound
works.

**Position B.** Activity in cultured cells does not establish activity in adult human tissue in
vivo, and no retrieved human study measured telomere length before and after in a controlled
design.

**Assessment.** The in vitro to in vivo gap is unbridged in the retrieved record. **This is the
largest inferential leap in the popular presentation of this compound.** It is not a disagreement
between researchers so much as a gap between what the laboratory literature reports and what
commercial writing asserts on its authority.

**What the Guide says.** Every cell-culture finding carries its model in the same sentence, and
the gap is named explicitly as an unknown rather than left for the reader to notice. See EPI-M-05,
EPI-U-01, and the prohibition at EPI-X-03.

## C-05. Two spellings, one compound, and a third name

This is not a disagreement between sources. It is a retrieval hazard that produces
disagreement-shaped results.

Epithalon, Epitalon, and Epithalone all refer to the same AEDG tetrapeptide, and the same authors
use the spellings interchangeably (S01). A search on one spelling alone under-retrieves, so two
people searching in good faith can reach different pictures of the evidence base.

Both spellings were therefore checked deliberately and separately in this session, including both
ClinicalTrials.gov queries (S08, S09). Both spellings must remain searchable in any published
Guide. Selecting a single canonical scientific label is a reviewer decision and is deliberately
not made in this record.

**What the Guide says.** See EPI-I-02. The terminology section names all three forms.

## C-06. Name-versus-substance confusion with Epithalamin

This is the most consequential error in the popular literature about this compound, and it is a
factual error rather than a simplification.

The synthetic tetrapeptide was designed based on the amino acid composition of the bovine pineal
gland extract Epithalamin, also written Epithalamine (S01). They are not the same substance. The
long-term elderly mortality claims come from a study of the extract and of Thymalin (S06), and the
retrieved record does not establish that the synthetic tetrapeptide was administered in that work.

Any Guide citing those mortality numbers as Epithalon or Epitalon human evidence is making a
substance-identity error. Because that study is also the source of the compound's most attractive
commercial claim, this error is both very common and very load-bearing.

**What the Guide says.** The distinction is stated in the identity section, before any evidence is
discussed, and the mortality study is introduced by its substance rather than by its result. See
EPI-I-03, EPI-H-04, EPI-U-03, and the prohibitions at EPI-X-01 and EPI-X-02.

## C-07. Publication volume versus evidence weight

A PubMed query for both spellings returned 142 records at retrieval (S07). That number is used in
commercial writing as though it were a measure of how well established the compound is.

It is not. The total is dominated by mechanistic, cell-culture, and animal reports plus reviews
from the originating network, and it includes work on related peptides. Volume here reflects the
productivity of one research programme rather than accumulated independent confirmation.

**What the Guide says.** The count appears only as a description of the literature map, with that
qualification attached. See EPI-Q-03 and the prohibition at EPI-X-07.

## C-08. Jurisdictional differences

No genuine conflict between jurisdictions was found, because **no jurisdiction produced a
retrievable primary statement this session.**

- Every fda.gov retrieval returned HTTP 404, including the meeting calendar page, the 503A bulk
  drug substances page, and both briefing PDFs (S14). No FDA wording was read.
- wada-ama.org returned no usable page content (S15). The only sources asserting an anti-doping
  classification were vendor, supplement-marketing, and SEO pages, which are never acceptable
  evidence.
- ClinicalTrials.gov is the one registry that did return a usable primary result, and what it
  returned was two empty result sets (S08, S09).

The absence of a conflict here reflects the absence of retrievable regulatory information, not
agreement among regulators. No regulator outside the United States was consulted.

**What the Guide says.** See REGULATORY_STATUS.md. Every FDA and WADA line carries its
unverified status in the same sentence as the statement.

## C-09. A note on the integrity of the record

Two structural weaknesses run through everything above, and they are recorded here so they are
not lost in the gaps list.

**The regulatory chain is broken at the source.** Every regulatory statement available to this
record rests on search-result summaries, because the regulator's own pages could not be read. That
is not a contradiction between sources. It is a missing link that a reviewer must close by opening
those pages directly before publication.

**The scientific chain is concentrated at the source.** Developer, primary investigator, and
reviewer are substantially the same network (S01, EPI-Q-01). This does not make the findings
wrong, and it should not be written as though it did. It does mean that the usual reassurance
provided by independent confirmation is not available here, and that a reader should be told so
plainly rather than left to infer it from author lists.

**A retrieval-integrity control was run.** A deliberately fabricated identifier, PMID 99999999,
returned HTTP 404 (S10), and the ClinicalTrials.gov queries returned genuinely empty sets rather
than invented records. That confirms the retrieval path is real, which is what allows the two
empty registry results to be reported as findings rather than as failures.
