# Epithalon Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Grades apply to individual claims, never to the compound as a whole.
Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## The headline finding

**No randomized, blinded, placebo-controlled human trial of the AEDG tetrapeptide was retrieved,
under either spelling.** Direct ClinicalTrials.gov queries for "epitalon" (S08) and for
"epithalon" (S09) both returned empty result sets. Both spellings were checked deliberately,
because the same authors use them interchangeably and a single-spelling search under-retrieves.

Two human records exist in the retrieved set, and neither carries the weight commercial writing
places on it:

1. A 2002 non-randomized retinitis pigmentosa report from the institute that developed the
   compound (S05), framed as a responder percentage rather than a controlled between-group
   estimate.
2. A 6 to 8 year observational report of mortality reductions in 266 elderly people (S06), which
   studied the **Epithalamin extract and Thymalin, not the synthetic tetrapeptide.**

That second item is the most commonly miscited source in popular writing about this compound.
Presenting it as Epithalon or Epitalon human evidence is a substance-identity error, not a
simplification.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical (animal or cultured cells) |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

No claim in this table is graded A or B. One claim reaches grade C, and it does so only as a
description of what a single non-randomized report asserts, not as evidence that the asserted
effect is real.

## Reviewer sign-off checklist

1. Every preclinical sentence names its species or model in the same sentence as the finding.
2. Every human sentence names which substance was actually administered, the peptide or the extract.
3. Both spellings, Epithalon and Epitalon, remain searchable wherever the compound is named.
4. No sentence contains a dose, route, schedule, cycle, or preparation detail as instruction.
5. No sentence states or implies human benefit, human safety, or a regulatory outcome.
6. Every regulatory sentence carries a jurisdiction, a date, and a source URL, and every FDA and
   WADA sentence carries its unverified status.
7. Every claim graded G or marked [UNVERIFIED] has either been closed by a human check or removed.
8. The oncological tension at EPI-S-02 travels with every mechanism passage.
9. No citation appears that is not present in SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.
