# Epithalon: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing this
compound could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of Epithalon,
and it applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state** for this compound specifically. Xenios has not
confirmed any of these items. **Every checkbox below is unchecked, every item is NOT CONFIRMED**,
and none is expected to be checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Three facts frame everything below.

1. **This compound is not an approved drug in any jurisdiction that was checked** (checked
   2026-07-21). There is therefore no approved manufacturer, no approved specification, and no
   regulatory authority inspecting a production line against a filed standard. Note also that the
   regulatory checks behind that statement are themselves incomplete: every fda.gov retrieval
   returned HTTP 404 this session, so the FDA position is unverified and flagged for human check.

2. **No analytical purity, identity, or content-verification data for marketed material was
   retrieved.** No study testing material sold under any of this compound's names was located.
   Nobody appears to have systematically checked what is in these products. **That is an absence of
   information, not a favourable result.**

3. **The naming situation is itself a quality risk.** Three names, Epithalon, Epitalon, and
   Epithalone, refer to the same AEDG tetrapeptide. A fourth similar name, Epithalamin (also
   Epithalamine), refers to a **different substance**, a bovine pineal gland extract from which the
   synthetic peptide was designed. A single name on a label does not establish which material is
   inside, and the two substances are conflated constantly in commercial writing.

Retailer and forum pages surfaced heavily in every search for this compound and are recorded as
market claims only, at grade E or G. They are never evidence of quality, identity, or content.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any
further conversation. **Status for this compound as of 2026-07-21 is NOT CONFIRMED for every
single item.**

### A. Identity

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of the exact substance.** Not a trade name. The specific
      synthetic AEDG tetrapeptide, stated as such. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Explicit exclusion of Epithalamin and Epithalamine.** Documented evidence that the
      material is the synthetic four-amino-acid peptide and not the bovine pineal gland extract
      from which it was designed. This confusion is pervasive in commercial material and is the
      single most consequential identity risk for this compound. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A3. Verified registry identifiers**, confirmed against a primary chemical registry rather
      than a supplier claim. No such identifiers were retrieved for this record.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED. [UNVERIFIED - requires human source check]
- [ ] **A4. Confirmed amino acid sequence for the specific batch**, by an orthogonal analytical
      method (for example mass spectrometry combined with sequencing), not by supplier assertion.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Name mapping stated on the documentation.** Which of Epithalon, Epitalon, or
      Epithalone the supplier uses, with an explicit statement that all three denote the same
      substance. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A7. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set
      before testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **B1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, with the method and conditions
      disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of it
      there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Peptide-related impurities characterised**, including truncated, deletion, and modified
      sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Aggregation assessed.** Peptide clumping is a recognised contributor to immune
      reactions against a peptide, and immunogenicity for this compound is entirely unassessed.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **C1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any route
      requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process, documented.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Stability

- [ ] **D1. Stability data under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not just a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **E1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a
      distributor, not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis through to the finished unit**, including
      every intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Batch and lot traceability**, so that any single unit can be traced back to its
      manufacturing record. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **F1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis** matching the exact lot in hand, not a
      representative or example certificate for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. The certificate issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation
      stated and independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Test methods named on the certificate**, with acceptance criteria and measured results,
      not "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no
      commercial interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. Certificate authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** Such
      documents are forged. One that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **G1. Label content matching the tested content**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material** that exceed the
      evidence, which for this compound means no human efficacy or safety claims at all.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. No lifespan, anti-aging, or telomere claim on the label or accompanying material.**
      Those are the specific overstatements attached to this compound, and the mortality evidence
      usually cited for them describes a different substance. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **H1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for this compound.** No supplier has been
assessed against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent
testing has been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed.
The purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly
what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide
exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the compound does anything. Perfect
documentation here would establish that a vial contains the AEDG tetrapeptide and not the bovine
pineal extract or something else. It would not create the controlled human evidence that, as set
out in the Guide, does not exist.
