## Standing rules for this gate

1. **One person, one role. No single person may sign more than one of the five reviewer roles.**
   The five gates exist because each looks for a different class of failure, and a person who has
   already approved their own work cannot function as an independent check on it. One
   self-approving actor holding two or more roles defeats the five-gate review entirely, and it
   does so silently, because the sign-off table still looks complete. If five distinct qualified
   people are not available, publication waits. It does not proceed with a doubled signature, and
   it does not proceed with a role marked "covered by" another reviewer.
2. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   single research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human
   sign-off is complete.
3. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a
   reviewer or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed and
   logged as a gap. Omitting is always better than guessing.
5. **Concluding that controlled human evidence is absent is a successful outcome**, not a failure of
   the research. An honest account of a thin and structurally non-independent evidence base is the
   correct result for this compound and must not be padded.
6. **A substance-identity error is treated as a factual error, not a simplification.** Any text
   presenting Epithalamin results as Epithalon or Epitalon results fails this gate outright,
   regardless of hedging.
7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification. The FDA advisory committee review makes this compound
   specifically time-sensitive, and both the FDA and WADA entries are currently unverified.
8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, identity, or
   regulatory statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed.
