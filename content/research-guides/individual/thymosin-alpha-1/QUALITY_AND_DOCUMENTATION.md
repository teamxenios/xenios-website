# Thymosin Alpha-1: Quality and Documentation Requirements

## Purpose and current position

This document states what quality documentation would have to exist before Xenios could say
anything at all about a physical product containing this compound.

**Current state, stated plainly: Xenios has not confirmed any of it.** Every item on the
checklist below is currently unmet. Not partially met, not pending review of a supplied
document. Unmet, because no supplier documentation for this compound has been received,
assessed, or verified.

This is a checklist a supplier must satisfy. It is not a description of a product Xenios
offers, and its existence should not be read as an intention to offer one.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.
Nothing in this document describes preparation, handling, or use.

---

## Why this compound needs the checklist more than most

Two features of thymosin alpha-1 raise the identity risk above the category baseline.

First, it genuinely is an approved medicine in some places, sold as Zadaxin. That gives
unregulated sellers real credibility to borrow. A person encountering "thymosin alpha-1" from
an unregulated source has no assurance it is the same substance as the studied and approved
product, and every trial safety observation in the Guide applies to pharmaceutical-grade
material used under medical supervision.

Second, regulators have described specific product-quality concerns for compounded injectable
peptides as a class. Per the retrieved ProPublica investigation (VERIFIED 2026-07-19,
https://www.propublica.org/article/peptide-safety-fda-compounding-pharmacies), the United
States Food and Drug Administration's stated rationale for the 2023 Category 2 placement of
19 peptides including thymosin-alpha included immunogenicity risk, manufacturing impurities
such as bacteria or heavy metals, and sensitivity to temperature and vigorous handling in ways
that can alter chemical composition.

Note the boundary of what is known here. Peer-reviewed analytical work exists on falsified and
impurity-laden polypeptide drugs in gray markets, and search metadata indicated thymosin
**beta-4** appears among frequently encountered falsified polypeptides in that literature.
Those full texts were not retrieved, and **no analytical data specific to thymosin alpha-1 was
found**. Beta-4 findings are not alpha-1 findings. Purity failure rates circulating online
(for example a claimed 43 percent label-purity failure rate) all traced to commercial testing
or vendor marketing pages and are not treated as evidence here.

So the accurate position is not "gray-market thymosin alpha-1 has been shown to be
adulterated." It is: **no one has publicly checked, and nothing is known.**

## The checklist

Status key: **UNMET** means Xenios has not received, assessed, or verified documentation for
this item for this compound.

### 1. Identity

| # | Requirement | Status |
|---|-------------|--------|
| 1.1 | Documented confirmation that the material is thymosin alpha-1 (28 amino acids, N-terminally acetylated) and not thymosin beta-4, thymosin fraction 5, or a related peptide | UNMET |
| 1.2 | Amino acid sequence confirmation by an appropriate analytical method, reported on the specific lot | UNMET |
| 1.3 | Mass confirmation by mass spectrometry on the specific lot | UNMET |
| 1.4 | Declared salt or counter-ion form (for example acetate versus free base), consistently stated across label, certificate of analysis, and specification | UNMET |
| 1.5 | Documented distinction between the material supplied and pharmaceutical thymalfasin, with no claim that they are equivalent unless independently substantiated | UNMET |

### 2. Purity and impurities

| # | Requirement | Status |
|---|-------------|--------|

| 2.3 | Residual solvent testing against a recognised standard | UNMET |
| 2.4 | Heavy metals and elemental impurity testing, given that regulators specifically named heavy metals as a compounded-peptide concern | UNMET |
| 2.5 | Water or moisture content and residual counter-ion content | UNMET |

## Summary of current status

**Items on the checklist: 33. Items met: 0. Items unmet: 33.**

Xenios has not confirmed identity, purity, sterility, endotoxin status, stability, chain of
custody, certificate of analysis integrity, or third-party verification for any product
containing thymosin alpha-1. No supplier documentation for this compound has been received or
assessed.

This document should be updated only when a specific, dated, lot-specific document has been
received and independently verified. A supplier assertion is not a met item. A general company
quality statement is not a met item. A certificate without measured values is not a met item.
