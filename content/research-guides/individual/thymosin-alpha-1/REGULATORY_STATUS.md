# Regulatory Status: Thymosin Alpha-1 (Thymalfasin)

**Status checked: 2026-07-19. This status must be re-verified against primary regulatory
sources immediately before publication, and again at each review cycle.**

**Verification warning specific to this record.** Every fda.gov URL attempted in the
research session returned HTTP 404 through the available fetch tool. No statement about
United States regulatory action below rests on an FDA document. Each such statement rests on
independent journalism or on a secondary drug reference, and is marked UNVERIFIED. A human
reviewer with direct access to FDA sources must confirm this entire section before anything
here reaches a member.

**Principle that governs this whole document.** Approval or registration in one jurisdiction
is not approval in another. A regulator's decision reflects that regulator's evidence
standard, at that time, for that indication, for that manufactured product. It does not
transfer across borders. Separately, orphan drug designation is a development incentive
status and is not marketing approval. Both confusions are common in marketing for this
compound and both are corrected wherever this compound is discussed.

## 4. What this means for a member, stated plainly

- Thymosin alpha-1 is not approved for marketing in the United States.
- It is reported to be an approved prescription medicine in some other countries, mainly for
  chronic hepatitis B. That approval belongs to those jurisdictions and does not extend here.
- Orphan drug designation, where it exists, is a development incentive and is not approval.
- Its United States compounding status has been contested since 2023 and, on the most recent
  independent reporting available to this Guide, was unsettled. Claims that availability has
  been restored come from commercially interested sources and could not be verified.
- No anti-doping status is stated here. Athletes should check with their anti-doping authority.
- Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.
