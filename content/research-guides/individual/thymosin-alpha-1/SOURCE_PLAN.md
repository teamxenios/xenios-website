# Source Plan: Thymosin Alpha-1 (Thymalfasin)

This document records how the evidence base for this Guide was assembled, what was
deliberately excluded, and what a human reviewer still needs to search before publication.

## 1. Compound scope and disambiguation

The subject of this Guide is thymosin alpha-1, whose synthetic pharmaceutical form is
called thymalfasin and is marketed under the brand name Zadaxin.

Three near-neighbours were held outside scope and must not be conflated with it:

1. Thymosin beta-4 and its analogue TB-500. A structurally and functionally unrelated
   peptide. Findings about beta-4 may never be presented as findings about alpha-1.
2. Thymosin fraction 5, the crude thymic extract from which alpha-1 was originally
   purified. Not the same material as the single purified peptide.
3. Research-grade or gray-market powder sold as "thymosin alpha-1", which is not the
   same product as the regulated pharmaceutical and carries no assurance of identity.

This compound is unusual in the peptide category because it genuinely is an approved
medicine in some jurisdictions. That makes jurisdictional precision the single highest
risk area of the Guide, and it shaped the search plan accordingly.

## 3. Query lines used

Search was organised by question rather than by keyword volume. The lines pursued were:

- Thymosin alpha-1 and thymalfasin randomised controlled trial evidence, by indication.
- Sepsis mortality trials, including the confirmatory phase 3 trial and its predecessor.
- COVID-19 randomised evidence, and the meta-analyses pooling COVID-19 data.
- Chronic hepatitis B efficacy evidence, including any Cochrane review.
- Registry status and posted results for the trials identified.
- United States regulatory status: marketing approval, 503A bulk substance categorisation,
  Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee proceedings, enforcement letters.
- Non-United States approval status for Zadaxin.
- Orphan drug designation status in the United States and the European Union.
- Anti-doping status on the current WADA Prohibited List.
- Analytical chemistry literature on falsified or impurity-laden polypeptide products.

## 4. Inclusion criteria

A source was eligible to support a member-facing claim only if all of the following held:

1. It was actually retrieved in this session at a recorded URL. Nothing was cited from memory.
2. It is a primary study, a trial registry record, a systematic review, a regulator or
   anti-doping authority document, a source reflecting approved product labeling, or
   independent journalism used only for regulatory reporting and labelled as such.
3. The population, design and outcome could be read directly from the retrieved text.
4. Where the finding is a biomarker rather than a clinical outcome, the retrieved text
   made that distinction clear.

## 5. Exclusion and disqualification criteria

The following source types are disqualified as evidence for this Guide, without exception:

- Peptide vendor, reseller, and retailer pages.
- Peptide directory and aggregator sites.
- Clinic, wellness practice, and telehealth marketing pages.
- Testing laboratory marketing pages, including purity failure rate statistics used as
  promotional material.
- Search engine result snippets and AI-generated summaries where the underlying document
  could not be opened. Snippet metadata is recorded as a lead for a human reviewer, never
  as a source.
- Any page whose commercial interest lies in the compound being available for purchase.
  A source with a financial stake in availability is the weakest possible source for a
  claim about legal availability.

Two specific disqualifications applied here and are worth naming:

- Every search result asserting that thymosin alpha-1 is absent from the WADA Prohibited
  List came from vendor or directory sites. All were discarded and no anti-doping status
  claim appears anywhere in this Guide.
- Every source asserting a completed February 2026 reclassification of thymosin alpha-1
  out of Category 2 was a vendor or clinic page. All were discarded and the claim is
  recorded only as an unverified marketing assertion to be corrected.

## 6. Handling of dosing and administration

Per the research brief, no dosing, amount, concentration, frequency, timing, cycle
structure, route, or administration detail was recorded at any point, including where a
retrieved source stated it.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## 7. What remains to be searched by a human reviewer

These are the open items, ordered by how much they matter to publication safety.

1. **All United States regulatory statements, against FDA primary sources.** Every
   fda.gov URL returned HTTP 404 through the available tooling, so no FDA statement in
   this Guide rests on an FDA document. A reviewer must confirm marketing approval status
   via Drugs@FDA, the 503A bulk drug substances categorisation pages, and the FDA briefing
   document on thymosin alpha-1 related bulk drug substances.
2. **Current compounding status as of the publication date.** This is genuinely in flux
   and a wrong statement could influence a purchasing decision. Re-verify immediately
   before publication, not earlier.
3. **The December 4, 2024 Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting record**,
   for the formal outcome and vote, and for whether it addressed thymosin alpha-1 acetate,
   the free base, or both.
4. **The FDA warning letter reportedly issued to Paradigm RE LLC** concerning a thymosin
   alpha 1 product with COVID-19 claims. The URL returned 404. If retrievable, it is a
   useful enforcement citation.
5. **The current WADA Prohibited List**, directly from the authority. Until then the
   Guide directs athletes to check with their anti-doping authority and makes no claim.
6. **Non-United States approval records**, from national regulators or company regulatory
   filings, to confirm which countries have approved thymalfasin. The widely repeated
   country count traces to marketing material and is not published here.
7. **FDA and EMA orphan drug designation records**, for designation numbers and dates.
   Designation is a development incentive and is not approval, and the Guide says so
   regardless of whether the records are retrieved.
8. **Full text of the 2023 COVID-19 meta-analysis**, to establish how many of the eight
   pooled studies were randomised. This materially changes how much weight the positive
   pooled estimate deserves.
9. **Full text of the 2008 hepatitis B meta-analysis**, for the risk of bias assessment
   of its four included trials.
10. **Whether the Cochrane review on thymosin alpha-1 for chronic hepatitis B has since
    been completed.** The record retrieved is a protocol only and contains no pooled
    findings. Search engine summaries misrepresented it as a completed review during this
    session, which is itself a caution. A completed version would become the best
    available evidence for the hepatitis B indication.
11. **Primary mechanistic literature.** The receptor level mechanism described in
    circulation was not established from a retrieved primary paper in this session.
12. **Preclinical literature.** No primary preclinical study was retrieved. The
    preclinical evidence table is empty because nothing was retrieved, not because
    nothing exists.
13. **Analytical identity and purity data specific to thymosin alpha-1.** The falsified
    polypeptide literature encountered concerns other molecules and may not be borrowed.
