# SLU-PP-332: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any material containing this
compound could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of SLU-PP-332
and applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state** for SLU-PP-332 specifically. Xenios has not
confirmed a single item below. Every checkbox is unchecked, and none is expected to be checked in
the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because no evaluation is under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below.

1. **SLU-PP-332 is not an approved drug in any jurisdiction that was checked** (checked
   2026-07-21). There is therefore no approved manufacturer, no approved specification, and no
   regulatory authority inspecting a production line against a filed standard. Only the United
   States was checked at all, and the United States finding is the absence of a retrieved
   document rather than a regulator's position. See REGULATORY_STATUS.md.

2. **No independent third-party analytical survey of marketed product was located.** No study
   testing identity, purity, or contamination of material sold under this name was found. Nobody
   appears to have systematically checked what is in these products. That is an absence of
   information, not a favourable result, and it does not support a claim in either direction
   about any specific product.

3. **The substance is routinely mislabelled in the market.** SLU-PP-332 is a synthetic small
   molecule, not a peptide, yet it is sold widely through peptide-branded channels. A buyer who
   believes they hold a peptide has a wrong model both of the substance and of what analytical
   test would verify it. Identity confusion is therefore a live quality risk here, not a
   theoretical one.

4. **The oral bioavailability contradiction is a quality problem, not only a science problem.**
   The peer-reviewed literature states that the parent compound lacks oral bioavailability in
   mice, and gives that as the reason a chemically distinct successor, SLU-PP-915, was developed
   (PMID 41421047). Oral products are nonetheless sold under the SLU-PP-332 name. Either such a
   product does not behave as claimed by that route, or it is not what its label says. This
   document records the tension and states no conclusion about any named seller.

A fifth point belongs here for honesty about this packet's own record. The chemical identity
values carried forward from the prior packet (molecular formula, molar mass, CAS registry number,
IUPAC name) were sourced from a tertiary reference and were **not re-retrieved on 2026-07-21**.
They are flagged [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check] throughout. An
identity specification cannot be checked against values that have not themselves been confirmed,
which is why item A2 below is a prerequisite rather than a formality.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any
further conversation. Status for SLU-PP-332 as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single
item.

### A. Identity

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of the exact substance.** Not the name "SLU-PP-332". The
      specific chemical entity, salt form, and any isomeric designation.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Verified registry identifiers.** Molecular formula, molar mass, CAS registry number,
      and IUPAC name confirmed against a primary chemical registry. The values circulating in this
      packet came from a tertiary reference and were not re-retrieved this session.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
      [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]
- [ ] **A3. Confirmed structural identity for the specific batch** by an orthogonal analytical
      method appropriate to a small molecule, not by supplier assertion.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A4. Explicit exclusion of the confusable compound.** Documented evidence that the material
      is SLU-PP-332 and **not** SLU-PP-915, which is a chemically distinct successor from the same
      research program with different pharmacokinetic properties. This conflation is routine in
      commercial copy and is a real identity risk.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Correct substance-class labelling.** Documentation stating plainly that the material is
      a synthetic small molecule and not a peptide, so that handling, storage, and verification
      assumptions are not imported from the wrong class.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A7. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set
      before testing rather than after. No pharmacopoeial monograph for this compound was located,
      so any specification would be the supplier's own and must be disclosed in full.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **B1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, with the method and conditions
      disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of it
      there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Synthesis-related impurities characterised**, including intermediates, side products,
      and unreacted starting materials arising from the route used.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Formulation excipients disclosed and qualified**, for any product presented in a
      finished form. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **C1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is presented in any form
      requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process, documented.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Stability

- [ ] **D1. Stability data under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. Solubility and physical-form stability characterised.** The published chemistry
      literature reports poor solubility for the parent compound in vitro (PMID 41850449 /
      PMC13112601), which makes physical form a documentation requirement rather than a detail.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not just a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **E1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a
      distributor, not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis through to the finished unit**, including
      every intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Batch and lot traceability**, so that any single unit can be traced back to its
      manufacturing record. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **F1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis (COA)** matching the exact lot in hand, not a
      representative or example COA for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. The COA issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated and
      independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Test methods named on the COA**, with acceptance criteria and measured results, not
      "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no
      commercial interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. COA authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** COAs are forged. A
      document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **G1. Label content matching the tested content**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. Substance class stated correctly on the label**, as a small molecule, with no peptide
      framing. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material** that exceed the
      evidence, which for this compound means no human efficacy or safety claims at all.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. No presentation implying that a route of administration is supported** where the
      published literature reports it is not. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. No use of "research use only" or "not for human consumption" framing as an implicit
      signal of human benefit.** That framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G6. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date**, including the fact
      that no approval exists and that anti-doping status is unresolved.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **H1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for SLU-PP-332.** No supplier has been
assessed against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent
testing has been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed.
The purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly
what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide
exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the compound does anything. Perfect
documentation for SLU-PP-332 would establish that a container holds what it claims. It would not
create the human evidence that, as set out in the Guide, does not exist at all.
