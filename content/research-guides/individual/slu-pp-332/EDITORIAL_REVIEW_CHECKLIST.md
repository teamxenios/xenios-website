## Standing rules for this gate

1. **No single person may sign more than one reviewer role.** The five roles exist to bring five
   independent perspectives to bear, and each is meant to catch what the others would let through.
   One person holding two signatures is one perspective wearing two labels. One person holding all
   five is a self-approving actor, which defeats a five-gate review entirely and makes the gate
   worse than no gate, because it produces the appearance of scrutiny without the substance. If
   five people are not available, publication waits. It does not proceed with doubled signatures.

2. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human sign-off
   is complete.

3. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.

4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a
   reviewer or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed
   and logged as a gap. Omitting is always better than guessing.

5. **Concluding that human evidence is absent is a successful outcome**, not a failure of the
   research. An honest empty evidence table is the correct result for this compound and must not be
   padded, softened, or supplemented with rodent findings dressed in human-sounding sentences.

6. **A preclinical finding without its species in the same sentence is a defect, not a style
   issue.** It is the specific mechanism by which this compound's real rodent science becomes a
   false human claim, and section 4 exists solely to catch it.

7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification.

8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, or
   regulatory statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed by the
   reviewer who owns it.
