# Source Registry: SLU-PP-332

Every source relied on for this Guide, with its retrieval status as of 2026-07-21. Sources marked
UNVERIFIED were attempted and failed, or were reached only through an intermediary summary. Nothing in
this registry was reconstructed from memory. No source here is a vendor or retailer page, because vendor
and retailer pages are disqualified as evidence.

**Changes made on 2026-07-21.** Three sources that the prior session could reach only through search
summaries (S8, S9, S10) were retrieved directly and are upgraded from provisional. Two indexed
publications that the prior registry did not enumerate were retrieved and added (S14, S15), so the
registry now accounts for all 10 publications in the PubMed listing. The Wikipedia identity entry
(S11) was **not** re-retrieved this session and its specific values are downgraded accordingly. The
WADA retrieval failed again, for the second consecutive session (S13, S16). Retrieval integrity
controls were run and are recorded (S17 to S19).

## Notes on use

1. S1 and S2 are the backbone of the central finding. The claim that no human evidence exists rests on a
   registry query that returned zero results and on a complete listing of indexed publications, none of
   which administered the compound to a living person.
2. S4 is the source most likely to be misread downstream. It enrolled human participants and is indexed
   in a way that invites a human evidence reading, but the compound was applied only to cultured cells.
   Any reviewer or writer citing S4 must carry the ex vivo qualifier in the same sentence.
3. **S8, S9 and S10 were upgraded on 2026-07-21.** All three were retrieved directly, so the
   "provisional, obtained via search summary" caveat that the prior packet attached to them no longer
   applies and should be removed wherever it survives in sibling files. S10's full text is still not
   retrieved; its abstract is.
4. **S11 was downgraded on 2026-07-21.** It was not re-fetched, so the specific chemical identity values
   carried from it are flagged as requiring a human source check. The substance-class correction (small
   molecule, not peptide) is not affected.
5. **S12 is a negative search finding, not a regulator position.** No FDA document naming the compound
   was located. That is all it establishes.
6. **S13 and S16 failed, on two consecutive sessions.** The absence of a retrieved WADA document is a
   persistent retrieval failure, recorded as a gap. It is not evidence that the compound is absent from
   the List, and it is not evidence that it is present.
7. **S14 and S15 are new to this registry.** The prior packet's source list did not enumerate them
   despite the PubMed listing containing them, which meant the registry accounted for 8 of 10 indexed
   publications. It now accounts for all 10. Neither changes the central finding, and S15 independently
   confirms it.
8. **S17, S18 and S19 are controls, not evidence about the compound.** They exist so that the central
   negative finding can be defended as a true negative rather than a failed search.
9. No source in this registry supports any claim about effects in a living human being, because no such
   source exists.
