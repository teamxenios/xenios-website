## Notes on use

1. S1 and S2 are the backbone of the central finding. The claim that no human evidence exists rests on a
   registry query that returned zero results and on a complete listing of indexed publications, none of
   which administered the compound to a living person.
2. S4 is the source most likely to be misread downstream. It enrolled human participants and is indexed
   in a way that invites a human evidence reading, but the compound was applied only to cultured cells.
   Any reviewer or writer citing S4 must carry the ex vivo qualifier in the same sentence.
3. S8, S9 and S10 are marked UNVERIFIED by direct fetch. Their PMIDs and titles come from the verified
   PubMed listing (S2), not from memory, but their content reached this registry through summaries.
   Claims resting on them are graded accordingly in CLAIM_TABLE.md.
4. S13 failed. The absence of a retrieved WADA document is recorded as a gap. It is not evidence that the
   compound is absent from the list, and it is not evidence that it is present.
5. No source in this registry supports any claim about effects in humans, because no such source exists.
