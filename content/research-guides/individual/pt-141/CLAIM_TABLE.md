# Claim Table: PT-141 (bremelanotide)

This is the controlling artifact for the PT-141 Guide. Nothing may appear on a
member-facing surface unless it is represented here and marked allowed.

Grades: A established, B supported human evidence, C early human evidence, D preclinical,
E manufacturer or supplier reported, F traditional or historical, G unverified,
PROHIBITED may not appear on any member-facing surface.

Source IDs refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## H. PROHIBITED claims

These may never appear on any member-facing surface for this compound, in any wording,
including softened, hedged, implied, or testimonial forms.

| ID | Prohibited claim | Why |
|---|---|---|
| PR-01 | Any statement that PT-141 is FDA approved, without immediately naming the specific product, the specific indication, and the specific population. | The unqualified form is technically anchored to a real approval and functionally false for almost every reader who encounters it. |
| PR-02 | Any statement or implication that PT-141 is approved, indicated, or supported for use in men. | The approved label's limitations of use exclude men explicitly. |
| PR-03 | Any statement or implication that it is approved, indicated, or supported to enhance sexual performance, in anyone. | Excluded by the label's own limitations of use. |
| PR-04 | Any efficacy figure, response rate, or satisfaction finding drawn from the 2008 male erectile dysfunction trial. | That trial carries an Expression of Concern and studied a formulation that was never approved and was discontinued. |
| PR-05 | Any statement or implication of approval or indication in postmenopausal women. | Excluded by the label's limitations of use, and no trial in that population was retrieved. |
| PR-06 | Any bare mechanism sentence asserting that the compound acts on brain desire pathways as established fact. | The approved label states the therapeutic mechanism is unknown. Asserting a pathway contradicts the regulator's own position. |
| PR-07 | Any quantitative claim about the purity, identity, sterility, or mislabeling rate of vendor-sold material, including any circulating label-failure percentage. | No acceptable source was found. Every figure encountered came from vendor, affiliate, or laboratory marketing pages. |
| PR-08 | Any specific claim about FDA enforcement action, warning letters, or named pharmacies. | Nothing was verified against an FDA document. |
| PR-09 | Any anti-doping status claim, in either direction. | The WADA list could not be retrieved. Both a permitted claim and a prohibited claim would be unsupported. |
| PR-10 | Any statement that the compound is safe, well tolerated, side-effect free, or gentle. | Contradicted by the label's own data, including nausea in 40 percent and 8 percent discontinuation, and prohibited as a general safety claim regardless. |
| PR-11 | Any guaranteed or deterministic outcome language, including will, proven to, restores, cures, eliminates, reverses, or fixes. | House standard, and factually wrong for an intervention with a small average effect and a large expectancy component. |
| PR-12 | Any dose, amount, concentration, volume, frequency, timing, cycle, titration, loading approach, stacking combination, reconstitution instruction, or administration technique. | Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides. |
| PR-13 | Any guidance on where to obtain the compound, how to select a supplier, or how to evaluate a vendor. | Sourcing and acquisition guidance is out of scope. |
| PR-14 | Any framing of the compound as research use only in a way that implies human benefit. | That framing is prohibited outright. |
| PR-15 | Any claim that non-US jurisdictions permit, approve, or register the compound, or that US approval applies elsewhere. | No non-US regulator was checked in this session. |
| PR-16 | Any statement of a specific FDA approval date or application number. | Neither could be verified from an FDA-hosted document. |
| PR-17 | Any presentation of extension-phase improvement figures as placebo-adjusted effect. | The extension was open label with no control arm. |
| PR-18 | Any suggestion, direct or implied, that a member should try, consider trying, or discuss trying this compound. | No medical advice, no self-treatment framing. |
