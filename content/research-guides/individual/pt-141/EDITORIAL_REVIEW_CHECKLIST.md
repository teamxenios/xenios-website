# PT-141 Guide: editorial review and sign-off gate

**AI drafts never publish automatically.** Every file in this folder was drafted by an AI system from
a research record and has received no human review. Nothing here may reach a member-facing surface
until every box below is checked by a named human, and until the founder sign-off at the end is
complete.

Every box below starts UNCHECKED. An unchecked box blocks publication. Do not check a box because
something looks right. Check it only after doing the verification the box describes.

Files in scope for this review:

- `GUIDE_DRAFT.md`
- `FAQ_DRAFT.md`
- `QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md`
- `EDITORIAL_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md` (this file)

**Compound-specific warning for reviewers.** PT-141 is different from the other compounds in this
series in a way that changes the risk profile of the review. There is a genuine FDA approval here.
That makes it far easier for a draft to slide from a true statement about the approved product into
an implied statement about an unapproved product, an unapproved population, or an unapproved use.
Read every claim asking: which product, which population, which use. That is where this Guide will
fail if it fails.

## Section 1. Content safety, hard gates

Any single failure here blocks publication outright.

- [ ] **No dosing anywhere.** No amounts, mg, mcg, IU, mL, units, concentrations, frequency, timing,
      cycle length, titration, loading, stacking, reconstitution, or injection technique, in any
      file, including tables, footnotes, and captions.
- [ ] Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact sentence "Dosing and administration information
      is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides." is present.
- [ ] Any route or formulation mentioned (the approved injectable product, the discontinued
      nasal-spray formulation) appears only as a factual description of the product a cited study or
      regulator was describing, never as instruction. Checked line by line.
- [ ] Numbers quoted from studies are outcome measures, incidence rates, or participant counts only,
      and no quoted number is or could be read as an amount to use.
- [ ] **No treatment directions, protocols, or regimens.** Nothing in any file could be followed as
      instructions.
- [ ] **No sourcing or acquisition guidance.** No supplier named as an option, no purchasing route,
      no advice on obtaining the compound, including for the approved prescription product.
- [ ] **No guaranteed outcomes.** The words "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures", "eliminates",
      "reverses" do not appear as claims about effect. Searched explicitly.
- [ ] Effect language uses "has been studied for", "reported in", "investigated as", "associated
      with in [population]" throughout.
- [ ] **No unsupported safety claims.** "Safe", "well tolerated", or "no side effects" never appear
      as a general statement of fact. Where a trial author used such wording, it is attributed to
      that author and immediately qualified.
- [ ] The trial authors' reported conclusion of "no new safety signals" in the open-label extension
      is presented as their reported conclusion, with the design limitation attached, and never as a
      Xenios safety statement.
- [ ] **No medical advice, diagnosis, or self-treatment framing** in any file.
- [ ] **"Research use only" framing is not used to imply human benefit.** Any mention of such
      labelling is accompanied by an explicit statement that it implies nothing about human benefit
      or safety.
- [ ] No em dashes appear in any file. Searched explicitly.
- [ ] No exclamation marks, hype, sales language, or flattery.

This is the highest-risk failure mode for this specific compound. Check line by line, not by
impression.

- [ ] **Every efficacy claim states its population.** No claim about benefit appears without
      identifying that it applies to premenopausal women with a formal diagnosis of acquired,
      generalized hypoactive sexual desire disorder.
- [ ] The distinction between VYLEESI (the approved product) and vendor-sold "PT-141" is made
      explicitly, and is not left to inference anywhere.
- [ ] The label's Limitations of Use (not indicated in postmenopausal women, not indicated in men,
      not indicated to enhance sexual performance) appear in the one-minute summary, not only deep in

- [ ] No sentence anywhere could be read as suggesting the 2019 approval supports use in men, use in

      because its abstract was never retrieved.

## Section 6. Claim grades

Grades apply to individual claims, never to the compound as a whole.

- [ ] Every substantive claim in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` and `FAQ_DRAFT.md` carries a grade, or sits under a
      graded block.
- [ ] Grades use only the approved scale: A Established, B Supported human evidence, C Early human
      evidence, D Preclinical, E Manufacturer or supplier reported, F Traditional or historical,
      G Unverified, PROHIBITED.
- [ ] Grade A is used only for regulatory facts read directly off the FDA label (approval status,
      indication, Limitations of Use, contraindication, and the label's statement that the mechanism
      is unknown). Confirmed that no efficacy claim is graded A.
- [ ] Grade B is applied only where a specific retrieved, adequately powered human study supports it,
      and the study's limitations appear in the same place as the grade.
- [ ] The open-label extension findings are graded no higher than C, given the absence of a control
      group and the 39.8 percent completion rate.
- [ ] The exit-study patient experience findings are graded no higher than C and are labelled as
      self-report rather than efficacy.
- [ ] All claims of benefit in men are graded G, and no grade was upgraded on the basis of the 2008
      trial.
- [ ] The proposed central mechanism is graded G.
- [ ] Any quantitative claim about vendor product quality is absent. Confirmed that no purity,
      mislabelling, or counterfeiting figure appears in any file.
- [ ] The default of D or G has been applied wherever a better grade could not be specifically
      justified.
- [ ] **PROHIBITED claims are absent.** Specifically confirmed absent: that PT-141 works in men, that
      it enhances sexual performance, that it is appropriate for postmenopausal women, that it is
      safe, that it is well tolerated, that vendor-sold material is equivalent to the approved
      medicine, that any product is verified or pharmaceutical grade, that laboratory-use labelling
      implies human benefit, that it is legal to buy from an unregulated seller, that it is permitted
      or prohibited in sport (neither, since the status is unverified), and any comparison presenting
      it as equivalent or superior to an approved therapy for a use it is not approved for.
