# PT-141 (bremelanotide): quality and documentation

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## What this file is, and what it is not

This file sets out the quality documentation that would need to exist for any product containing
bremelanotide before Xenios would treat statements about that product as established.

**Current status, stated plainly: Xenios has not confirmed any of it for this compound. Every item
below is UNMET.**

Two clarifications, because this file is easy to misread.

**This is not a supplier onboarding path, and it is not a signal that Xenios intends to sell this
compound.** Xenios does not sell PT-141 or bremelanotide and does not supply it. This checklist
exists so that a member reading vendor claims has a concrete standard to measure them against, and
so that any future evaluation starts from a written bar rather than an improvised one.

**Meeting every item below would establish material identity and manufacturing control. It would not
establish efficacy, safety, or legality.** A product can be exactly what it says it is, made to a
high standard, and still be ineffective, unsafe for a given person, or unlawful to supply. Those are
separate questions, addressed in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md`.

## The situation specific to this compound

Bremelanotide is unusual in this Guide series because a regulated version genuinely exists.

The FDA-approved product VYLEESI has been reviewed for identity, purity, and manufacturing quality
as part of its approval, per the approved label retrieved on 2026-07-19
(https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/fda/fdaDrugXsl.cfm?setid=8c9607a2-5b57-4a59-b159-cf196deebdd9).

Material sold as "PT-141" by peptide and research-chemical vendors is not that product and carries
none of that review. That is the entire reason this checklist exists.

**A search that found nothing, and what we did with the nothing.** During research on 2026-07-19 we
looked specifically for independent analytical data on the purity, identity, and mislabelling rate of
peptides sold by research-chemical vendors. Every result returned was a peptide vendor blog, an
affiliate "peptide guide" page, or a testing laboratory's own marketing. None of those meets the
evidence standard for this Guide series. **No purity figure, mislabelling rate, or counterfeiting
statistic appears anywhere in this folder.** Specific numbers circulating in that ecosystem were seen
only on such pages and are deliberately not repeated here, because repeating an unverified figure
gives it credibility it has not earned. This is a real gap and it should be closed by a human before
any Xenios surface says anything quantitative about product quality for this compound.

## The checklist

Status key: **UNMET** means Xenios holds no verified evidence for this item. No item may be moved off
UNMET on the basis of a supplier's assurance. Moving an item requires documented evidence,
independently verified, attached to the record, with the verifier named and dated.

### 1. Identity

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Confirmation that the material is bremelanotide and not a related melanocortin compound | **UNMET** | Mass spectrometry confirming the expected molecular mass, from a laboratory independent of the supplier |
| 1.2 | Confirmation of amino acid sequence | **UNMET** | Sequencing data, or peptide mapping with a documented method, traceable to the specific batch |
| 1.3 | Confirmation of correct structure, including the cyclic ring | **UNMET** | Structural characterisation data, since a compound of the right mass can still have the wrong structure |
| 1.4 | Distinction from melanotan II and afamelanotide documented | **UNMET** | Analytical evidence specifically excluding the compounds bremelanotide is most often confused with |
| 1.5 | Counter-ion and salt form identified | **UNMET** | Documented salt form, since the same peptide can be supplied in different salt forms |

### 2. Purity

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | Purity by a validated separation method, with the method stated | **UNMET** | Chromatographic purity data with the method, column, and detection conditions disclosed. A bare percentage with no method is not evidence |
| 2.2 | Identification of the major impurities, not just a total | **UNMET** | A named impurity profile. "Purity above 99 percent" says nothing about what the remainder is |
| 2.3 | Residual solvent testing | **UNMET** | Results against a recognised residual solvent standard |
| 2.4 | Heavy metals and elemental impurity testing | **UNMET** | Elemental impurity results against a recognised standard |
| 2.5 | Residual synthesis reagents quantified | **UNMET** | Results for the reagents used in the specific synthesis route |

### 3. Sterility

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Sterility testing of the finished container | **UNMET** | Sterility test results for the specific batch, by a recognised method |
| 3.2 | Documented aseptic manufacturing or a validated sterilisation step | **UNMET** | Process documentation showing how sterility is achieved, not merely tested for |
| 3.3 | Container closure integrity | **UNMET** | Evidence that the container maintains its seal through shipping and storage |
| 3.4 | Bioburden control before any sterilisation step | **UNMET** | In-process bioburden data |

### 4. Endotoxin

Endotoxins are fragments of bacterial cell walls. They can provoke a strong reaction even when no
living bacteria remain, so sterility testing alone does not cover this.

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | Bacterial endotoxin testing on the finished batch | **UNMET** | Endotoxin results by a recognised method, against a stated limit |
| 4.2 | The acceptance limit stated and justified | **UNMET** | A written limit with the basis for it, not a bare pass or fail |
| 4.3 | Water and excipient inputs controlled for endotoxin | **UNMET** | Input specifications and testing records |

### 5. Stability

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5.1 | Stability data supporting the stated shelf life | **UNMET** | Real-time stability data under defined storage conditions, not extrapolation alone |
| 5.2 | Degradation products identified and limited | **UNMET** | A stability-indicating method that separates degradation products, with limits |
| 5.3 | Storage and transport conditions defined and monitored | **UNMET** | Documented conditions plus evidence they were maintained in transit |
| 5.4 | Behaviour on excursion from stated conditions characterised | **UNMET** | Data covering what happens when temperature limits are exceeded, which is a real-world shipping reality |

### 6. Chain of custody

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6.1 | Manufacturing site identified and inspectable | **UNMET** | A named site, not a broker or a reseller of unnamed origin |
| 6.2 | Batch traceability from raw materials to finished container | **UNMET** | A batch record that traces each input |
| 6.3 | Every intermediary between manufacturer and end product documented | **UNMET** | A complete custody chain. Each undisclosed intermediary is an unverified link |
| 6.4 | Quality agreement in force with the manufacturer | **UNMET** | A signed agreement defining specifications, testing, and change control |
| 6.5 | Change control, so the material cannot change without notice | **UNMET** | Documented change control obligations and a notification process |
| 6.6 | Retained samples held for each batch | **UNMET** | Retention policy plus evidence samples are actually held |

### 7. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7.1 | A certificate of analysis for the specific batch, not a representative or historical one | **UNMET** | A batch-specific certificate naming the batch, the date, and the tests performed |
| 7.2 | Every test method disclosed on the certificate | **UNMET** | Methods named for each result, so a reviewer can judge whether the test measures what it claims |
| 7.3 | Independent third-party confirmation of the supplier's certificate | **UNMET** | Testing by a laboratory with no commercial relationship to the supplier, on material drawn independently |
| 7.4 | Certificate authenticity verifiable with the issuing laboratory | **UNMET** | Direct confirmation from the laboratory. Certificates are straightforward to fabricate |
| 7.5 | The tested batch is the batch supplied | **UNMET** | Documentation linking the tested batch to the supplied units, which is the link most often broken |
| 7.6 | Testing laboratory accreditation confirmed | **UNMET** | Current accreditation confirmed with the accrediting body |

### 8. Regulatory and labelling honesty

| # | Requirement | Status | What would satisfy it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8.1 | The product's actual regulatory status stated accurately | **UNMET** | Documentation that does not present an approval belonging to a different product as its own |
| 8.2 | No laboratory-use labelling used to imply human benefit | **UNMET** | Labelling that does not use "research use only" framing as a marketing device. That framing implies nothing about human benefit or safety, and Xenios will not carry it |
| 8.3 | No claim of equivalence to the FDA-approved product without evidence | **UNMET** | Comparative analytical evidence, if such a claim were made at all |
| 8.4 | Adverse event reporting route in place | **UNMET** | A documented process for receiving, recording, and acting on reports |
