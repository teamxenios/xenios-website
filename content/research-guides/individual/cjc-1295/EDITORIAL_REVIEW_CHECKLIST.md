## Section 1. Content safety, hard gates

Any single failure here blocks publication outright.

- [ ] **No dosing anywhere.** No amounts, mg, mcg, IU, mL, units, concentrations, frequency, timing,
      cycle length, titration, loading, stacking, reconstitution, injection technique, or route of
      administration presented as guidance, in any file, including tables, footnotes and captions.
- [ ] Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact sentence "Dosing and administration information is
      intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides." is present.
- [ ] Half-life, fold-change and duration figures are confirmed to be pharmacology, not dosing, and are
      not written in a way that could be read as a schedule or regimen.
- [ ] The phrase "after administration" and similar appear only as neutral description of what a study
      did, never as instruction, and no route of administration is stated.
- [ ] **No treatment directions, protocols or regimens.** Nothing in any file could be followed as
      instructions.
- [ ] **No sourcing or acquisition guidance.** No supplier names presented as options, no purchasing
      routes, no advice on obtaining the compound.
- [ ] **No guaranteed outcomes.** The words "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures", "eliminates",
      "reverses" do not appear as claims about effect. Searched explicitly.
- [ ] Effect language uses "has been studied for", "reported in", "investigated as", "associated with in
      [population]" throughout.
- [ ] **No unsupported safety claims.** "Safe", "well tolerated" or "no side effects" never appear as a
      general statement of fact.
- [ ] The Teichman 2006 authors' conclusion that the compound was safe and relatively well tolerated is
      attributed to those authors, is bounded to that study and to the DAC form, and is immediately
      qualified wherever it appears. Checked in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` and `FAQ_DRAFT.md`.
- [ ] **No medical advice, diagnosis or self-treatment framing** in any file.
- [ ] The "Questions for a qualified professional" section contains questions only, and no question is
      phrased so as to smuggle in a recommendation.
- [ ] **"Research use only" framing is not used to imply human benefit.** Confirmed absent, or, if such
      labelling is mentioned, accompanied by an explicit statement that it implies nothing about human
      benefit or safety.
- [ ] No em dashes appear in any file. Searched explicitly.
- [ ] No exclamation marks, hype, sales language or flattery.
- [ ] No competitor, retailer or manufacturer text has been copied or lightly paraphrased anywhere.
      Originality of wording confirmed.

## Section 2. The two-molecule problem, and preclinical to human separation

This compound has two distinct high-risk failure modes. Check both line by line, not by impression.

### 2a. DAC versus no-DAC evidence transfer

- [ ] **Every human finding in every file is attributed to the DAC form**, in the same place as the
      finding, and never left ambiguous.
- [ ] The half-life figure of 5.8 to 8.1 days appears only ever attached to the DAC form.
- [ ] No pharmacokinetic figure of any kind for modified GRF(1-29) appears anywhere, since none was
      confirmed by a peer-reviewed or regulatory source.
- [ ] Every claim about modified GRF(1-29) in humans is graded G, with the absence of controlled human
      studies stated plainly.
- [ ] The identity problem appears in the one-minute summary, not only deep in the Guide.
- [ ] The Guide states that a purchaser cannot determine which molecule is present from the common product
      name alone.
- [ ] Evidence-transfer between the two forms is named as a safety issue, not merely a labelling one.
- [ ] The amino acid substitution positions for modified GRF(1-29) remain excluded, unless a human has
      confirmed them against a peer-reviewed or regulatory source.

### 2b. Preclinical to human separation

- [ ] **Every preclinical finding carries its species or model in the same sentence as the finding.**
      Checked individually for the rat pituitary receptor work, the GHRH knockout mouse work, and the
      equine detection methodology work, in both `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` and `FAQ_DRAFT.md`.
- [ ] The GHRH knockout mouse is described as a model of a specific genetic deficiency and explicitly not
      as a model of a healthy adult human, wherever that finding appears.
- [ ] The equine papers are described as detection methodology only, and are never presented as efficacy
      or safety evidence.
- [ ] No bare mechanism sentence exists anywhere that a reader could take as a statement about humans.
- [ ] The Proposed mechanism section is labelled as proposed, is hedged throughout, and names the species
      or model for each pathway claim.
- [ ] Section headings themselves distinguish human evidence from preclinical evidence.
- [ ] The gap between a hormone-level change and a clinical outcome is stated wherever the hormone finding
      appears, and not only once.
- [ ] The terminated trial NCT00267527 is presented as producing no evidence, and is never cited as
      support for or against any claim.

## Section 5. Claim grades

Grades apply to individual claims, never to the compound as a whole.

- [ ] Every substantive claim in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` and `FAQ_DRAFT.md` carries a grade, or sits under a
      graded block.
- [ ] Grades use only the approved scale: A Established, B Supported human evidence, C Early human
      evidence, D Preclinical, E Manufacturer or supplier reported, F Traditional or historical,
      G Unverified, PROHIBITED.
- [ ] No claim is graded A or B. Confirmed by direct check, given that the three human studies are small,
      old, restricted to hormone-level endpoints, and come from a single investigator group connected to
      the developer.
- [ ] The grade C applied to "the DAC form raises growth hormone and IGF-1 in healthy adults" is justified
      by the specific retrieved studies, and those studies' design limitations appear in the same place as
      the grade.
- [ ] Every rodent, mouse and equine finding is graded D.
- [ ] Every claim about modified GRF(1-29) in humans is graded G.
- [ ] Every claim about a clinical benefit in humans is graded G.
- [ ] Vendor and retailer pharmacokinetic figures are graded E where mentioned, and are never presented as
      evidence.
- [ ] The default of D or G has been applied wherever a better grade could not be specifically justified.
- [ ] No grade was upgraded on the basis of the mechanism being plausible.
- [ ] **PROHIBITED claims are absent.** Specifically confirmed absent: that CJC-1295 improves body
      composition, strength, recovery, sleep, injury healing or longevity in humans; that it is safe; that
      it is well tolerated as a general statement; that it is a natural or safer alternative to growth
      hormone; that it is legal to buy or use; that any product is verified, pharmaceutical grade or
      research grade in a way implying human benefit; that the no-DAC form shares the DAC form's
      pharmacokinetics or safety record; and any comparison presenting it as equivalent or superior to an
      approved therapy, including tesamorelin.
