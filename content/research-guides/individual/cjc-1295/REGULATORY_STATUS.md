## Part 1. United States

### US-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction.** United States, Food and Drug Administration.

**Statement.** No FDA-approved drug product containing CJC-1295 in any form was identified
during this review. The only registered clinical trial found on ClinicalTrials.gov
(NCT00267527, sponsored by ConjuChem, Phase 2) is listed as Terminated with no results
posted. A 2026 peer-reviewed narrative review states that neither the DAC nor the no-DAC
variant holds FDA approval for performance or body-composition indications.

**Date checked.** 2026-07-19.

- https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies/NCT00267527
- https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/endocrinology/articles/10.3389/fendo.2026.1822475/full

**Verification.** VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

### US-02. Section 503A bulk drug substances list, December 2024 advisory committee meeting

**Jurisdiction.** United States, Food and Drug Administration, Pharmacy Compounding Advisory
Committee.

**Statement.** A Federal Register notice published October 25 2024 (docket FDA-2024-N-4777)
announced a Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting to be held December 4 2024, at
which five CJC-1295 forms were listed as being considered for inclusion on the section 503A
bulk drug substances list: CJC-1295 free base, CJC-1295 acetate, CJC-1295 DAC free base,
CJC-1295 DAC acetate, and CJC-1295 DAC trifluoroacetate. The notice states only that these
forms were being considered. It does not state FDA's recommendation, and it does not report
an outcome.

**Date checked.** 2026-07-19.

**Source.** https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2024-10-25/html/2024-24828.htm

**Verification.** VERIFIED 2026-07-19. The notice itself was retrieved and read.

**What remains unverified.** The outcome of that meeting. FDA's actual stated proposal on
each of the five forms, the committee's discussion, and the committee's vote were not
retrievable this session. The meeting page, the briefing document and the transcript all
returned HTTP 404 on fda.gov.

**Reviewer note.** Preserve the word "considered" exactly. Any paraphrase that converts
consideration into a listing, a rejection or an approval misstates a primary document.

### US-03. The Category 1, 2 and 3 framework

**Jurisdiction.** United States, Food and Drug Administration.

**Statement.** A Federal Register notice published January 7 2025 (docket FDA-2015-D-3517)
announced an FDA final guidance on compounding using bulk drug substances under section
503A. That guidance ends the categorization of bulk drug substances into Categories 1, 2 or
3 for substances nominated on or after the guidance's publication date. The notice does not
mention CJC-1295 or peptides by name, so it does not by itself establish this compound's
current status.

**Date checked.** 2026-07-19.

**Source.** https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2025-01-07/html/2024-31546.htm

**Verification.** VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

**Reviewer note.** Always publish the second sentence with the first. Without it, the
statement becomes an inference about CJC-1295 that the document does not support. The
practical consequence for a reader is that Category 2 language encountered elsewhere may
describe a framework that has since changed, so its currency cannot be assumed.

## Part 2. Sport, international governing body

### SPORT-01. World Anti-Doping Agency

**Jurisdiction.** World Anti-Doping Agency, applying to athletes bound by the World
Anti-Doping Code. This is a sport eligibility rule, not a national law.

**Statement.** Growth hormone-releasing hormone analogues, including CJC-1295 and CJC-1293,
are prohibited in sport at all times, meaning both in-competition and out-of-competition,
not in-competition only.

**Date checked.** 2026-07-19.

**Source.** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4830873/

**Verification.** VERIFIED 2026-07-19 as to the prohibition itself. This was confirmed from
a peer-reviewed anti-doping analytical paper that references the WADA Prohibited List; the
paper's reference is to the 2015 Prohibited List.

**What remains unverified.** The current year Prohibited List section number and exact
wording. The WADA Prohibited List page (wada-ama.org) is JavaScript rendered and returned no
parseable content to automated retrieval this session.

**Reviewer note.** State the prohibition, do not cite a section number, and confirm against
the current year Prohibited List PDF before publication. Do not imply that WADA prohibition
carries any legal meaning outside sport, and do not imply that it constitutes a safety
finding.

## Part 4. Adjacent facts that are not regulatory status

Recorded here because they are frequently presented as if they were regulatory findings.
They are not.

- **The Phase 2 trial termination.** The registered Phase 2 study (NCT00267527, 120
  participants, HIV-associated visceral obesity) is listed as Terminated with no results
  posted and no reason given. This is a registry fact, not a regulatory action, and it
  supports no conclusion about efficacy or safety in either direction.
  Source: https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies/NCT00267527. VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

- **Presence in the unregulated market.** CJC-1295 has been identified by mass spectrometry
  in an unlabeled illicit preparation of unknown origin submitted by Norwegian police and
  customs authorities, published 2010. This documents law enforcement seizure, not a
  regulatory classification.
  Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21204297/. VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

- **Reported discontinuation of development following a trial subject's death.** Appears
  only in a tertiary encyclopedia source, unconfirmed by any primary source retrieved this
  session. It is not a regulatory finding, it is not established, and it may not be stated
  as fact or as disproven. Flagged for human verification.
  Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CJC-1295. UNVERIFIED.
