# CJC-1295: quality and documentation

## Purpose and current position

This document sets out the quality documentation that would need to exist before any product containing
CJC-1295 could be discussed as a characterised material, and records the honest current state.

**Current state, stated plainly: Xenios has NOT CONFIRMED any of the items below for this compound.
Every item on every checklist in this document is currently UNMET. No supplier has been assessed. No
batch has been reviewed. No certificate of analysis has been seen or verified. No manufacturing site has
been identified. Nothing here is a supplier onboarding path, and nothing here should be read as Xenios
preparing to supply this compound.**

Two structural facts sit above every individual checklist item and make this harder than it would be for
most ingredients.

1. **The product name does not identify the molecule.** A product labelled "CJC-1295" may be the
   albumin-binding DAC form or the short-acting modified GRF(1-29), which are pharmacologically very
   different. A purchaser cannot determine which molecule is present from the common product name alone.
   Every vendor page encountered during source gathering used the two names interchangeably or as
   parenthetical synonyms. This is an identity defect at the level of the name itself, before any
   analytical question arises. Source: Frontiers in Endocrinology 2026 review,
   https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/endocrinology/articles/10.3389/fendo.2026.1822475/full
   (verified 2026-07-19)

2. **The supply chain is unregulated and documented as variable.** The same 2026 review states that
   real-world use is complicated by quality-control uncertainty and compositional variability
   characteristic of unregulated peptide supply chains, and notes this is especially acute for agents
   lacking any human validation, which includes the no-DAC variant. Separately, CJC-1295 has been
   identified by mass spectrometry in an unlabelled illicit preparation of unknown origin submitted by
   Norwegian police and customs authorities, whose contents were unknown until analysed (PMID 21204297,
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21204297/, verified 2026-07-19), and the peer-reviewed anti-doping
   literature describes illicit synthesis of analogue peptide hormones and a growing GHRH black market
   (PMID 26879649, https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4830873/, verified 2026-07-19).

A third point belongs here because it is easy to misread. No applicable pharmacopoeial monograph (the
published specification defining what a substance must contain and how it must be tested) was identified
for CJC-1295 in the retrieved record. The pharmacopoeias were not searched directly during source
gathering, so this is recorded as unestablished rather than as a confirmed absence. Either way, the
requirement in row 1.6 below stands.

Meeting every item in this document would establish that a material is characterised. It would not
establish that the compound works, that it is safe, or that supplying it is lawful. Those questions are
separate, and on the evidence set out in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` they remain unanswered.

## Status key

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| UNMET | Not satisfied, not documented, or not verified by Xenios |
| PARTIAL | Some documentation exists but is incomplete or unverified |
| MET | Documented and independently verified by Xenios |

**Every row in this document is currently UNMET.** No row may be changed to PARTIAL or MET without
documented, independently verified evidence attached and a named reviewer recorded.

## 1. Identity

What a supplier would have to establish: that the material is the specific substance intended, named
unambiguously, and not a different form, salt or analog.

| # | Requirement | Status | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Explicit declaration of which molecule is supplied, DAC-modified CJC-1295 or modified GRF(1-29) without DAC, stated as the primary identity and not as a parenthetical alias | UNMET | This is the first and most consequential row. The two are pharmacologically very different and share one commercial name |
| 1.2 | Full chemical name and complete amino acid sequence, with every substitution position stated | UNMET | Substitution positions for modified GRF(1-29) appeared only in vendor material during source gathering and are excluded from the Xenios record as unverified |
| 1.3 | Where DAC is present, explicit identification of the conjugating group and its attachment | UNMET | The DAC group is described in the record as a maleimidopropionyl-lysine group that binds albumin covalently. No supplier material has been checked against this |
| 1.4 | CAS number, molecular formula and molecular weight stated and matched to the material supplied | UNMET | No supplier material reviewed |
| 1.5 | Confirmatory identity testing on the actual batch, including mass spectrometry and amino acid sequence confirmation | UNMET | No batch data reviewed. Mass spectrometry is the technique that identified this compound in a seized unlabelled preparation, so the capability exists and has simply not been applied here |
| 1.6 | A named applicable monograph or, in its absence, a written in-house specification with a documented scientific basis | UNMET | No applicable monograph identified in the retrieved record. Absent one, any specification offered is the supplier's own |
| 1.7 | Salt or counterion form identified and quantified | UNMET | Five distinct forms were named in the December 2024 Federal Register notice (free base, acetate, DAC free base, DAC acetate, DAC trifluoroacetate), which demonstrates that form distinctions are real and formally recognised |
| 1.8 | Declaration that the product contains only the named substance, with any co-formulated peptide disclosed | UNMET | No supplier declaration reviewed. Combination products are common in this market |
| 1.9 | Labelling that does not cite human evidence generated on a different molecule | UNMET | Vendor pages encountered during source gathering presented pharmacokinetic figures for the no-DAC variant while citing DAC-form human trials as the evidence base. Any such labelling would fail this row |

## 7. What would still be unresolved even if every box above were met

This section exists so that the checklist is never mistaken for a green light.

Full documentation would establish material identity, purity, sterility, stability and provenance. It
would leave every one of the following untouched.

- Whether the compound produces any clinical benefit in humans. No retrieved human study measured a
  clinical outcome.
- Whether it is safe over any meaningful duration. The longest human exposure identified was 49 days, and
  the 2026 review states no long-term safety data are available for either version.
- Whether supplying or compounding it is lawful. That is a regulatory question, unresolved in the
  retrieved record, and it is addressed in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` and `REGULATORY_STATUS.md`, not here.
- Whether the theoretical oncologic concern arising from growth hormone and IGF-1 signalling is real in
  people. The 2026 review states no established clinical carcinogenic signal exists, which is not the
  same as an answer.

A perfectly characterised material of unknown effect and unknown long-term safety is still a material of
unknown effect and unknown long-term safety.
