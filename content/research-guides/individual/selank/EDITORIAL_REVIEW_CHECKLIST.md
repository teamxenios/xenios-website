# Selank Research Guide: editorial review and sign-off gate

**This Guide is an AI draft. AI drafts never publish automatically.** Nothing in this compound's
Guide set reaches a member-facing surface until every box below is checked by a named human and the
sign-off table at the end is complete. An unchecked box blocks publication. There is no partial
release, no soft launch, and no internal preview that a member can reach.

Every box below starts **UNCHECKED**. As of 2026-07-21 the checklist is empty in full.

## 1. Content safety: dosing and administration

- [ ] 1.1 No dose, amount, mg, mcg, IU, mL, unit, or concentration appears anywhere in any file.
- [ ] 1.2 No frequency, timing, schedule, cycle length, loading, titration, or tapering appears.
- [ ] 1.3 No route of administration, injection technique, reconstitution, or preparation
      instruction appears as guidance. Naming a route only to state that evidence is absent for it
      is permitted and has been checked line by line.
- [ ] 1.4 No stacking, combination, or sequencing guidance appears. The benzodiazepine
      co-administration material is framed as a research context and an open safety question, never
      as a combination that a reader could follow.
- [ ] 1.5 Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact sentence "Dosing and administration
      information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides." is present.
- [ ] 1.6 No source-quoted dose has been reproduced, including inside a quotation or a table.
- [ ] 1.7 No sourcing, acquisition, vendor, or supplier guidance appears in any file.

## 2. Content safety: outcomes and claims language

- [ ] 2.1 No guaranteed-outcome verbs appear: "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures",
      "eliminates", "reverses", "guarantees".
- [ ] 2.2 All effect statements use permitted framing: "was reported in", "was studied in", "was
      compared with", "investigated as".
- [ ] 2.3 No treatment direction, protocol, or regimen appears that a reader could follow as
      instructions.
- [ ] 2.4 No diagnosis, self-assessment tool, or self-treatment framing appears.
- [ ] 2.5 The phrase "research use only" does not appear as a device implying human benefit.
- [ ] 2.6 No comparison to an approved medicine that implies equivalence. This is the highest-risk
      language failure for this compound, because two of the three trials are benzodiazepine
      comparisons and the vendor sector routinely converts them into equivalence claims.
- [ ] 2.7 No hype, sales language, flattery, or exclamation marks. Tone is calm and precise.
- [ ] 2.8 Zero em dashes across all files, confirmed by search.

## 3. Content safety: safety claims

- [ ] 3.1 The words "safe", "well tolerated", and "no side effects" never appear as a general
      statement of fact about Selank.
- [ ] 3.2 The Guide states explicitly that no human safety dataset was retrieved and that this is an
      absence of retrieved evidence, not evidence of safety.
- [ ] 3.3 Every safety section is framed as a category of concern, never as reassurance.
- [ ] 3.4 The vendor claim of zero dependence and zero withdrawal across roughly 192 subjects is
      addressed and rejected in both the Guide and the FAQ, and appears nowhere as fact.
- [ ] 3.5 The absence of adverse event tables, dropout counts, laboratory monitoring, and long-term
      follow-up is stated plainly and early, not buried.
- [ ] 3.6 The unstudied-populations list (pregnancy, breastfeeding, children, older adults, and
      people with other medical or psychiatric conditions) is present and complete.
- [ ] 3.7 The interactions section lists categories requiring professional review and does not imply
      that unstudied means absent.
- [ ] 3.8 The benzodiazepine co-administration context is presented as an open interaction question,
      not as a characterised profile and not as a suggestion.

## 4. Preclinical labelling: the standing highest-risk failure

- [ ] 4.1 Every preclinical finding carries its species or model **in the same sentence as the
      finding**, checked line by line, not paragraph by paragraph.
- [ ] 4.2 No bare mechanism sentence reads as human fact anywhere in any file, including the summary
      and the FAQ.
- [ ] 4.3 Rat findings (PMID 28280289, 36322304, 31243679) are labelled as rats in every instance.
- [ ] 4.4 The mouse finding (PMID 27878720) is labelled as DBA/2 mice in every instance.
- [ ] 4.5 The cell-line finding (PMID 28293190) is labelled as IMR-32 human neuroblastoma cells in a
      dish in every instance, including where a reader might treat human-derived cells as human
      evidence.
- [ ] 4.6 The rat liver study (PMID 31243679) carries its no-direction caveat wherever it is cited.
      No directional statement, protective or harmful, appears anywhere.
- [ ] 4.7 The rat diazepam study (PMID 28280289) is described as an enhancement of another drug's
      effect, never as a standalone anxiolytic result.
- [ ] 4.8 The entire mechanism section is explicitly labelled as proposed, with hedged language
      throughout, and states that mechanism is not efficacy.
- [ ] 4.9 The serotonergic mechanism is presented as a proposal without retrieved support, never as
      a finding.

## 5. Human evidence integrity: the section that differs from most Guides

- [ ] 5.1 The human evidence section states that human evidence exists rather than padding an empty
      table or talking real trials down.
- [ ] 5.2 The non-independence finding appears **before** any individual trial result, in both the
      Guide and the FAQ.
- [ ] 5.3 The patient totals are stated accurately: 62, 60, and 70, approximately 192, plus one
      study of unstated size.
- [ ] 5.4 The 2015 study (PMID 26356395) carries its add-on framing wherever it is cited, and is
      never presented as evidence of standalone efficacy.
- [ ] 5.5 The absence of any placebo-controlled trial is stated, with the reason it matters
      specifically in anxiety.
- [ ] 5.6 The ClinicalTrials.gov zero-result queries have been re-run by a human and confirmed, with
      the date recorded, and the consequence (no pre-specified endpoints, selective outcome reporting
      cannot be excluded) is stated alongside the count.
- [ ] 5.7 The abstract-only limitation is stated for all four human records, with the specific list
      of what could not be assessed.
- [ ] 5.8 The vendor claim of more than 800 patients is addressed and corrected in both the Guide and
      the FAQ.
- [ ] 5.9 The PubMed false-positive problem is explained, so that no raw count is quoted anywhere.
- [ ] 5.10 The absence of any systematic review or meta-analysis is stated.
- [ ] 5.11 No sentence anywhere implies that the human evidence is settled, independent, or
      controlled.

## Named reviewer sign-off

No publication occurs until all five reviewers below have signed. A signature attests that the
reviewer personally checked the boxes in their area, not that they read a summary of them.

| Review | Scope | Reviewer (named) | Date | Signed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scientific review | Sections 4, 5, 6, and 8. Evidence accuracy, human evidence integrity, preclinical labelling, citation verification, claim grades. | *(unassigned)* | | [ ] |
| Claims review | Sections 2, 3, 8, and 9. Claims language, prohibited claims, outcome language, safety framing, benzodiazepine equivalence. | *(unassigned)* | | [ ] |
| Quality review | Section 6.9 and the whole of `QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md`. Checklist completeness and honest unmet status. | *(unassigned)* | | [ ] |
| Legal and regulatory review | Sections 1, 7, 9, and 10, and the whole of `REGULATORY_STATUS.md`. Regulatory accuracy and dating, the deregistration finding, jurisdictional statements, disclosure. | *(unassigned)* | | [ ] |
| Founder review | Whole document set. Final publication decision. | Samuel Boadu | | [ ] |

**One person, one role. No individual may sign more than one of the five roles above.** A single
person holding two signatures is not a five-gate review, it is a smaller review wearing a larger
name. The whole point of five separate gates is that five separate minds look at the material from
five different angles, and a self-approving actor defeats that entirely, including when the person
is competent, senior, in a hurry, or the only one available. If a role cannot be staffed by a
distinct person, publication waits. It does not proceed with a doubled signature, and it does not
proceed with a role left blank.

**Publication decision**

- [ ] All 11 sections complete, every box checked.
- [ ] All five reviewers named, dated, and signed, by five distinct people.
- [ ] Regulatory section re-verified within 7 days of publication and re-dated accordingly.
- [ ] Final approval to publish recorded, with date, by the founder review.

## Standing rules for this gate

1. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human sign-off
   is complete.
2. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
3. **No single person may sign more than one reviewer role.** See the sign-off table above. This is a
   standing rule of the gate, not a preference, and it cannot be waived by the person who would
   benefit from waiving it.
4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a
   reviewer or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed and
   logged as a gap. Omitting is always better than guessing.
5. **Reporting real but weak evidence honestly is a successful outcome.** Selank is not a
   zero-evidence compound and must not be written as one. It is a compound with small,
   non-independent, unregistered, uncontrolled human evidence, and describing it precisely is the
   correct result. Neither padding it nor flattening it is acceptable.
6. **The regulatory finding is the highest-risk item in this folder.** The claim that Selank is
   currently approved in Russia appears on nearly every commercial page and appears to be false as of
   January 2026. Publishing that stale claim would be a serious factual failure. Publishing the
   correction without a primary registry check would be a different kind of failure. The gate is not
   passed until a human has resolved which.
7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification.
8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, or regulatory
   statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed by its distinct
   named reviewer.
