# Selank Contradictions and Divergences

Where the sources disagree, and what the Guide should say when they do. Source ids refer to
SOURCE_REGISTRY.md. Claim ids refer to CLAIM_TABLE.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

Selank has more genuine contradictions than most compounds in this series, and the first one is
the most consequential single finding in this folder.

## C-01. Whether Selank is currently an approved medicine in Russia

**Position A.** Essentially every vendor, clinic, and blog page retrieved states that Selank is
currently approved in Russia and available on prescription, commonly citing a 2009 Ministry of
Health approval and the brand name Selanc. Examples encountered include peptidepedia.org,
trimrx.com, drlewis.com, and peptivigor.com.

**Position B.** Two independent Russian pharmaceutical trade outlets report that on 20 to 21
January 2026 the Russian Ministry of Health cancelled the marketing registration of 71 medicinal
products and excluded 14 pharmaceutical substances from the State Register of Medicines. The
nootropic products Semax and Selank from the Russian company Peptogen are named among the
cancelled registrations, and selank is named among the substances excluded from the register. The
reported reason is not a safety withdrawal. All entries were removed at the request of the
registration certificate holders and authorised legal entities (S15, S16).

**Assessment.** Position B should be preferred and Position A treated as stale. The reasoning is
about the shape of the two claims, not about which sounds better. The regulatory reporting is
specific, falsifiable, and corroborated: two independent outlets name the same company, the same
two products, and the same stated reason, published one day apart. The vendor claim is generic,
uncited, and repeated near-verbatim across pages, which is the signature of copied copy rather
than independent verification.

The accurate framing is that Selank was previously registered in Russia, and that the registration
was cancelled in January 2026 at the holder's request, which reads as a commercial or
administrative withdrawal rather than a reported safety action. Nothing in the retrieved record
supports describing it as a safety withdrawal, and nothing supports describing it as currently
approved.

**Residual uncertainty, stated honestly.** This session could not read the Russian state registry
itself. grls.minzdrav.gov.ru was not queried successfully and a mirror record returned no readable
content. A human must confirm directly against grls.minzdrav.gov.ru before publication.

**What the Guide says.** Selank is not described as currently approved anywhere. See SEL-X-01,
SEL-U-02, SEL-U-03, and REGULATORY_STATUS.md.

## C-02. Whether a prior Russian registration existed, and when

This is a sub-question of C-01 and it resolves differently, so it is separated out.

**Position A.** Vendor and blog sources state that Selank was approved by the Russian Ministry of
Health in 2009 and marketed on prescription as Selanc.

**Position B.** No registration certificate number and no registration date could be obtained.
The state registry was not read. The manufacturer's own product page lists indications for Selank
(anxiety, stress, depressive symptoms, heightened nervous fatigue, neurasthenia, and generalised
anxiety disorder) but publishes no registration certificate number and no registration date (S17).

**Assessment.** The prior existence of a Russian registration is strongly implied by the January
2026 cancellation, because a registration must exist in order to be cancelled. That inference is
sound. The date, the certificate number, and the brand name are not verified and rest only on
vendor sources.

**What the Guide says.** That a registration existed and was cancelled, and that its date and
certificate number are not established here. The 2009 date and the brand name Selanc are not
published as fact. See SEL-X-15.

## C-03. The size of the human evidence base

**Position A.** Vendor pages claim published trials enrolling more than 800 patients, and describe
Selank as one of the most clinically documented nootropic peptides available.

**Position B.** Systematic retrieval identified three genuine Selank clinical trial records, with
62, 60, and 70 patients, totalling approximately 192 patients, plus one immunological patient
study whose sample size was not stated (S03 through S07). Notably, a vendor page elsewhere in the
same sector cites roughly 192 trial subjects, matching the retrieved total and contradicting the
800-patient claim from within the sector itself.

**Assessment.** The 800-patient figure is unsupported by anything retrieved and is internally
inconsistent with other commercial claims. Use the retrieved figure. Approximately 192 patients
across three small trials is a small evidence base, not a large one.

There is a second inflation mechanism worth naming, because it is subtler and will recur. PubMed
term mapping produces false positives. A filtered search restricted to trial and review
publication types returned six records, of which three (strength training periodisation, lithium
augmentation in depression, and anaesthesia and circulating blood volume) have nothing to do with
Selank (S02). Anyone quoting a raw PubMed count is counting papers that are not about this
compound.

**What the Guide says.** Approximately 192 patients across three small trials. See SEL-H-00,
SEL-H-10, and SEL-X-02.

## C-04. Whether efficacy is equivalent to benzodiazepines

**Position A.** Vendor pages state that Selank has a pronounced anxiolytic effect comparable to
classical benzodiazepines, producing benzodiazepine-equivalent calm without sedation, cognitive
blunting, dependence, or withdrawal, and cite head-to-head trials in support.

**Position B.** The retrieved primary abstracts support a much narrower claim. The 2008 study
reports that the anxiolytic effects of selank and medazepam were similar in 62 patients (S04). The
2015 study tested selank as an add-on to phenazepam and reported reduced phenazepam side effects
in 70 patients (S06), which is a benzodiazepine-sparing result rather than standalone equivalence.
No placebo-controlled trial was retrieved.

**Assessment.** Prefer the primary abstracts. "Similar to medazepam in one 62-patient study by
non-independent investigators" is a defensible description. "Benzodiazepine-equivalent without
dependence or withdrawal" is not, because it performs two separate conversions at once: it turns a
single small non-independent comparison into a general equivalence, and it turns an absence of
reported adverse events into a positive safety claim. No adverse event data was retrieved from any
of the three trials, so there is nothing to support the second half of the sentence in either
direction.

Without a placebo arm, the trials cannot separate drug effect from expectancy or from the natural
resolution of anxiety symptoms. That limitation is particularly severe in anxiety, where symptoms
fluctuate and placebo response is characteristically large.

**What the Guide says.** See SEL-H-01, SEL-H-03, SEL-H-06, SEL-X-03, and SEL-X-10.

## C-05. WADA anti-doping status

**Position A.** Peptide vendor and supplement blogs assert that Selank is in category S0
(Non-Approved Substances) on the WADA Prohibited List, reasoning that it has Russian approval but
no FDA or EMA approval.

**Position B.** No WADA primary source was successfully read. Both the 2026 list PDF and the
prohibited-list page returned empty content (S19). Selank was not confirmed to appear by name on
any WADA list.

**Assessment.** Unresolved, and it must not be asserted in either direction. The vendor reasoning
is an inference rather than a retrieved finding, and it is additionally undermined because its
stated premise, current Russian approval, appears to be false as of January 2026 (C-01).

One further point a reviewer needs. The non-approved substances category by its nature does not
list substances individually, so the absence of the word Selank from the list would not settle the
question either. A human must read the list directly, and even then the honest output may remain a
referral rather than an answer.

**What the Guide says.** That the status could not be verified, with athletes referred to their
own anti-doping authority. See SEL-U-04 and SEL-X-05.

## C-06. Where independence, not sample size, is the real weakness

This is not a disagreement between sources. It is a structural feature of the evidence base that
commercial material never mentions, and it changes how every human result should be read.

All four human records come from an overlapping Russian institutional author network: Zozulya,
Neznamov, Siuniakov, Medvedev, Tereshchenko, and Kost. Myasoedov, credited with originating the
compound, is a co-author on the immunological patient study and on much of the surrounding
mechanistic literature. There is no non-Russian human trial, no independent replication group, and
no industry-independent verification.

Three of the four human records were published in a single Russian journal, all four are in
Russian, and only abstracts were retrieved, so the full texts have not been read here and have not
been subject to broad international peer scrutiny.

Zero trial registrations exist on ClinicalTrials.gov (S13, S14), so no pre-specified endpoints
exist for any Selank trial and selective outcome reporting cannot be excluded. No systematic
review or meta-analysis exists (S01), so there is no synthesis layer that would normally catch
exactly this pattern.

**What the Guide says.** That the human evidence is real but small and not independent, stated
before any individual trial result. See SEL-H-00.

## C-07. Where mechanism plausibility outruns the evidence

Also not a disagreement between sources, but a gap between how the mechanism is described
commercially and what the record supports.

- Gene expression relevant to GABAergic neurotransmission was affected in IMR-32 human
  neuroblastoma cells in vitro (S12). That is cells in a dish, not neurotransmission in a person.
- Selank enhanced diazepam's anxiety-reducing effect in rats under chronic mild stress (S08). That
  is a rat result, and it is an enhancement of another drug rather than a standalone action.
- One human study measured serum enkephalin activity alongside psychometric outcomes (S04).
  Measuring a marker is not demonstrating a mechanism, and no result values were retrievable.
- A naloxone-blockade study in the retrieved set is cited as implicating opioid involvement, but
  it was not individually retrieved and its species or model was not recorded, so no member-facing
  claim rests on it. [UNVERIFIED - requires human source check]

The commonly repeated framing that Selank modulates GABAergic and serotonergic pathways overstates
the certainty of a mechanism that remains largely inferred from preclinical models.

**What the Guide says.** Mechanism is presented as proposed, preclinical, and unconfirmed in
people, with the model named in the same sentence as every finding. See SEL-M-01 through SEL-M-05.

## C-08. Jurisdictional differences

No genuine conflict between jurisdictions was found, because only Russia produced a retrievable
regulatory statement.

Russia: registration cancelled and the substance excluded from the State Register of Medicines in
January 2026 (S15, S16).

United States: no FDA approval for any human indication. Search-result summaries indicate that FDA
warning letters to two compounding operations named Selank among substances used in compounded
products, and that Selank and Semax were not nominated for inclusion on the 503A bulks list. Every
direct fetch to www.fda.gov returned HTTP 404 to this retrieval path, so the verbatim FDA text was
not read (S18) and these statements are marked verified=false.

Anti-doping: unresolved (C-05).

Everywhere else: not checked. The absence of a conflict reflects the absence of retrievable
information from other jurisdictions, not agreement among them.

## C-09. A note on the integrity of the regulatory record

The most important regulatory statement in this folder rests on two trade outlets rather than on
the state registry, because the registry could not be read. The United States statements rest on
search-result summaries rather than on FDA text, because every FDA fetch failed. The anti-doping
question rests on nothing at all, because both WADA fetches returned empty content.

None of these gaps was filled from memory. Where a fetch failed, the resulting statement is marked
verified=false and flagged for human verification, and a retrieval control querying a fabricated
PubMed identifier returned an error rather than a fabricated record (S21), which confirms the
retrieval path is real.

That is not a contradiction between sources, but it is a weakness in the chain that a reviewer
must close before publication. It is recorded here so it is not lost in the gaps list.
