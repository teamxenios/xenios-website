# Selank Regulatory Status

**Status checked on 2026-07-21. This page must be re-verified before publication and re-checked on
any subsequent update.** Regulatory positions change, and the central item on this page changed
recently, materially, and in a direction that contradicts almost every commercial page about this
compound.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Standing principles for this page

1. Approval, authorisation, or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another, and a
   cancelled registration in one jurisdiction says nothing about the position in another.
2. Every statement below carries a jurisdiction, a date checked, and a source URL. A statement
   without all three does not belong on this page.
3. Where a status could not be confirmed by reading the primary document, that is stated explicitly
   rather than filled in by inference or from memory.
4. A registration cancelled at the request of its holder is a commercial or administrative event.
   It is not a safety withdrawal, and it must not be described as one.

## Russian Federation

### RU-01. Deregistration, January 2026. The central finding on this page.

**Jurisdiction:** Russian Federation, Ministry of Health.
**Statement:** On 20 to 21 January 2026 the Russian Ministry of Health cancelled the marketing
registration of 71 medicinal products and excluded 14 pharmaceutical substances from the State
Register of Medicines (GRLS). The nootropic products Semax and Selank from the Russian company
Peptogen are named among the cancelled registrations, and selank is named among the substances
excluded from the register. The reported reason is **not** a safety withdrawal. All entries were
removed at the request of the registration certificate holders and authorised legal entities.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://vademec.ru/news/2026/01/21/minzdrav-otmenil-registratsiyu-71-preparata-i-isklyuchil-iz-grls-14-substantsiy/
**Verification:** Retrieved and read. Russian pharmaceutical trade press, secondary. Not confirmed
against the state registry.
**Why this is the most important line on the page:** it directly contradicts the claim, repeated
across essentially every vendor page retrieved, that Selank is currently an approved prescription
medicine in Russia. Nothing on a member-facing surface may describe Selank as currently approved
anywhere while this stands.

### RU-02. Independent corroboration

**Jurisdiction:** Russian Federation, Ministry of Health.
**Statement:** A second, independent Russian healthcare trade outlet, publishing 22 January 2026,
reports the same regulatory action, names Semax and Selank from Peptogen among the cancelled
registrations, and gives the same holder-request reason.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.vshouz.ru/news/minzdrav/wcs-20789/
**Verification:** Retrieved and read. Independent secondary source.
**Why it matters:** two independent outlets carrying the same named detail, the same company, the
same two products, and the same stated reason, published one day apart, is what raises confidence
in RU-01 above the level of a single trade report.

### RU-03. Prior registration. NOT VERIFIED.

**Jurisdiction:** Russian Federation, Ministry of Health.
**Statement:** The widely repeated claim that Selank was approved by the Russian Ministry of Health
in 2009 and marketed on prescription under the brand name Selanc appeared this session only in
vendor and blog sources. Direct attempts to confirm a registration certificate number and date
failed. The state registry at grls.minzdrav.gov.ru was not queried successfully, a pharm-portal
mirror record returned no readable content, and the manufacturer's own product page lists
indications for Selank (anxiety, stress, depressive symptoms, heightened nervous fatigue,
neurasthenia, and generalised anxiety disorder) but publishes no registration certificate number and
no registration date.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://peptogen.ru/products/
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED. The manufacturer page was retrieved and read and is a grade E source.
The registration date and certificate number were not obtained from any source.
**Assessment:** the prior existence of a Russian registration is strongly implied by RU-01, because
a registration must exist in order to be cancelled. Its date, its certificate number, and the brand
name are not verified here and are not published.
**Handling rule:** state that a registration existed and was cancelled. Do not state when it was
granted or under what brand name.
[UNVERIFIED - requires human source check against grls.minzdrav.gov.ru]

### RU-04. Reason for the cancellation, and what is not known about it

**Jurisdiction:** Russian Federation, Ministry of Health.
**Statement:** The reported reason for all removals in the January 2026 action is a request from the
registration certificate holders and authorised legal entities. No safety rationale was reported by
either outlet.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Sources:** https://vademec.ru/news/2026/01/21/minzdrav-otmenil-registratsiyu-71-preparata-i-isklyuchil-iz-grls-14-substantsiy/
and https://www.vshouz.ru/news/minzdrav/wcs-20789/
**Verification:** Retrieved and read.
**Handling rule:** report the stated reason exactly and mark the rest unknown. Whether the decision
was purely commercial, purely administrative, or had any undisclosed safety or quality driver is not
established. Do not speculate in either direction, and specifically do not describe this as a safety
withdrawal.

## United States

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** Selank has no FDA approval for any human indication. No approval was found in any
retrieved source.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Verification:** Negative finding across the research session. No FDA primary text was read, for

### US-03. Substance registration

**Jurisdiction:** United States, FDA Global Substance Registration System.
**Statement:** Selank appears in the FDA Global Substance Registration System as both SELANK and
SELANK DIACETATE, which confirms it is a recognised discrete substance rather than a vendor coinage,
and which means the single name does not establish which material is in a given product.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED at the primary level. The substance records were located but did not
render usable content when fetched. A listing in a substance registry is a catalogue entry and is
**not** an approval of any kind. It must never be presented as one.
[UNVERIFIED - records did not render, requires human source check]

## Clinical trial registration

### CT-01. ClinicalTrials.gov

**Jurisdiction:** United States, ClinicalTrials.gov.
**Statement:** A query on selank as an intervention returned a total count of 2, and a query on
selank as a general term returned a total count of 10. Every returned record was an unrelated false
positive from fuzzy matching, covering transcranial magnetic stimulation, dyslexia stimulation,
brivaracetam in epilepsy, high-altitude hypoxia, and imagery rescripting. There are zero registered
Selank trials.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Sources:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.intr=selank&format=json&countTotal=true
and https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.term=selank&format=json&countTotal=true
**Verification:** Primary registry query, retrieved and read.
**Consequence to state alongside it:** the Russian trials were not pre-registered in this
international registry, so pre-specified endpoints cannot be checked and selective outcome reporting
cannot be excluded. The registry was not the only possible one, and the Russian national registry
was not searched.

## Re-verification checklist for the reviewer

1. Query grls.minzdrav.gov.ru directly and confirm RU-01, RU-02, and RU-04, including the precise
   date the cancellation took legal effect.
2. Establish whether a prior registration existed, with its certificate number and date, and close
   RU-03 either way.
3. Determine whether any safety or quality driver lay behind the January 2026 action, or confirm
   that the holder request is the whole of it.
4. Open both FDA warning letters and read the verbatim text, then close US-02 or remove it.
5. Read the FDA 503A bulk drug substances lists directly and confirm the not-nominated statement.
6. Retrieve the FDA Global Substance Registration System records for SELANK and SELANK DIACETATE and
   close US-03, including the amino acid sequence question.
7. Read the current WADA Prohibited List directly and close AD-01 as far as it can be closed,
   remembering that the non-approved category is not name-based.
8. Search at least one regulator outside Russia and the United States, so that the other
   jurisdictions section becomes a finding rather than a gap.
9. Search the Russian national trial registry, in case the Russian trials were registered there.
10. Update the dated header and the last_reviewed field together. A stale date on this page is itself
    a defect, and it is particularly serious here, because the whole point of this page is that the
    commercial sector is publishing a status that is out of date.
