# NAD+ Research Guide: editorial review and sign-off gate

Every box starts unchecked. AI drafts never publish automatically. A box is checked by a
named human who personally verified the item, not by whoever assembled the packet.

## 0. The failure mode specific to this compound

**Three different things are routinely conflated in consumer marketing, and they have
different evidence and different regulatory status:**

1. **Oral precursors** (NR, NMN). Nearly all randomized human trial evidence on "NAD" is
   here. Regulated as dietary supplements.
2. **Intravenous intact NAD+.** A compounded drug product. Human evidence extremely sparse.
3. **Injectable or intranasal NAD+.** No human trial evidence retrieved for these routes.

- [ ] **Precursor data is never presented as evidence for IV or injectable NAD+.** The
      oral NR and NMN trial literature says nothing about what an infusion of intact
      NAD+ does. Every evidence statement names which of the three it applies to.
- [ ] No sentence lets a reader carry a precursor finding across to an injection.
- [ ] The Guide does not use the bare term "NAD" where the route matters.

## 1. Content safety: dosing and administration

- [ ] No amount, mg, mcg, IU, mL, or concentration anywhere.
- [ ] No frequency, timing, cycle length, titration, loading, stacking, or infusion rate.
- [ ] No reconstitution, injection technique, or route-of-administration instruction.
- [ ] Naming a route to say evidence is ABSENT for it is permitted. Naming a route as
      part of a how-to is not. Each occurrence checked against that distinction.

## 2. Content safety: outcomes and claims language

- [ ] No "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures", "eliminates", "reverses".
- [ ] No anti-aging, longevity, or lifespan claim in any wording.
- [ ] No addiction, detox, or withdrawal treatment claim. "NAD therapy" for addiction is
      a clinic marketing category, not an approved indication, and the Guide says so.
- [ ] No energy, mitochondrial, or cognitive performance guarantee.

## 3. Content safety: safety claims

- [ ] The Guide never states NAD+ is safe, well tolerated, or free of side effects.
- [ ] The documented Class I endotoxin recall is stated plainly, not softened.
- [ ] Absence of reported harm is never presented as evidence of safety.

## 4. Preclinical labelling

- [ ] Every animal or in vitro finding names its species or model in the same sentence.
- [ ] No bare mechanism sentence reads as established human fact.

## 5. Human evidence integrity

- [ ] Every human study named states its population, design, and limitation.
- [ ] Precursor trials are labelled as precursor trials in the same breath.
- [ ] Where evidence is absent for a route, the Guide says absent rather than limited.

## 6. Citation verification: every item checked by a named human

- [ ] Every PMID, DOI, NCT number, and URL independently opened and confirmed to say
      what the Guide claims.
- [ ] The GeroScience label-accuracy study (PMID 38935229) confirmed, including the
      "-100 percent to +11.2 percent" range and the three non-detectable products.
- [ ] Anything marked `[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge]` either verified or removed.
- [ ] No citation appears in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` that is absent from `SOURCE_REGISTRY.md`.

## 7. Regulatory status: re-verified and re-dated

**This compound has the largest verification debt in the packet. Several regulatory
statements were retrieved from search snippets because direct FDA fetches returned 404.**

- [ ] **FDA 503A Category 2 listing** for NAD+ confirmed against the FDA page itself,
      with FDA's exact wording. Currently marked `verified: false`.
- [ ] **503B ineligibility** confirmed against the FDA bulks list directly. Currently
      `verified: false`.
- [ ] **GenoGenix warning letter** (dated 20 January 2026) read in full, not via snippet.
- [ ] **ProRx warning letter** (dated 7 April 2026) read in full.
- [ ] **Class I recall** record confirmed, including dates.
- [ ] **NMN dietary supplement reversal** (FDA letters, 29 September 2025) confirmed,
      including that NMN remains a New Dietary Ingredient requiring notification.
- [ ] **WADA 2026 status confirmed against the official List.** The full-text search
      could not be completed in session, and the only sources asserting NAD+ and NMN are
      absent from the List were a supplement vendor blog and a testing-lab blog, which
      are not acceptable. **No athlete-facing statement may publish until this is done.**
      Note the S0 catch-all could capture an unapproved injectable form whether or not
      the substance is named.
- [ ] Every regulatory line carries a date, a jurisdiction, and a source URL.

## 8. Claim grades

- [ ] Each claim graded individually, never the compound as a whole.
- [ ] No claim graded A, B, or C without a qualifying human source **for that route**.
- [ ] Vendor and manufacturer sources support no better than E.

## 9. Prohibited claims

- [ ] The prohibited list in `CLAIM_TABLE.md` is present and none of its entries appears
      anywhere as an assertion.
- [ ] No claim that any NAD+ product is FDA approved. It is not, for any indication.
- [ ] No implication that a compounded product carries an approval.

## 10. Disclosure and framing

- [ ] The Guide is educational, not a purchase recommendation.
- [ ] It states that Xenios does not sell this and does not advise how to obtain it.
- [ ] The unregulated-supply-chain reality is stated, with the recall as the example.

## 11. House style

- [ ] Zero em dashes.
- [ ] Every technical term glossed on first use.
- [ ] Calm and plain. No hype, no sales language.

## Standing rules for this gate

1. A reviewer signs only for what they personally verified.
2. No single person may sign more than one role. Five roles means five people.
3. An unchecked box blocks publication. There is no partial publish.
4. If a regulatory statement cannot be verified against a primary source, it is removed
   rather than published with a caveat.
