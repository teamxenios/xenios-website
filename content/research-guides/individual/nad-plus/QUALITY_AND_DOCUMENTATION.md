# NAD+: quality and documentation requirements

## Purpose of this document

What a supplier must be able to prove before a product containing this compound could
be offered, and what Xenios Research can currently prove. It is a checklist with every
item unmet, not a description of a system that exists.

## The starting position

Xenios has **NOT CONFIRMED** any of the items below for any NAD+ product. No supplier
document, certificate of analysis, stability study, or sterility record is on file.

This document is unusual among the Guide packets because NAD+ has **documented,
peer-reviewed quality failures in the real market**, not merely theoretical risk. That
evidence is set out first, because it changes what a buyer should assume.

## What the published evidence already shows about this market

**Oral NMN label accuracy is poor, and this is a peer-reviewed finding rather than a
vendor claim.** A laboratory study assayed 18 NMN supplements (15 non-liposomal, 3
liposomal) bought online, bought in pharmacies, or obtained from manufacturers.
Deviation from adjusted label claim ranged from **-100 percent to +11.2 percent**.
**Three products contained non-detectable NMN.** Only one exceeded its label claim; the
other 14 contained less than claimed. The authors concluded that the amount of active
ingredient on the label corresponds poorly to the detected amount.
*GeroScience, 2024. PMID 38935229, DOI 10.1007/s11357-024-01257-2. VERIFIED 2026-07-19.*

**Injectable NAD+ has a documented contamination event.** An FDA Class I recall was
issued for elevated endotoxin in a compounded NAD+ injection lot (GenoGenix LLC, recall
initiated 30 July 2025, classified Class I on 21 October 2025). A Class I classification
means a reasonable probability of serious harm or death. This is a real recall, not a
hypothetical.

**The identity problem may sit upstream of the pharmacy.** FDA has flagged that some
compounders sourced **food-grade** NAD+ from repackagers to make intravenous products.
Food-grade material is not qualified for sterile injectable manufacture and carries high
microbial and endotoxin risk. [UNVERIFIED - the FDA page was not directly retrievable in
session. A human must confirm FDA's exact wording before this line publishes.]

A note on what is deliberately excluded: several supplement vendor and industry blogs
assert far more dramatic failures, including a claim that half of tested NMN products
contained zero NMN. Those appeared on retailer and manufacturer pages, some selling
competing products. They are recorded as market claims at grade E or G and are **not**
used as evidence here. The GeroScience figure above is the citable number.

## The checklist

### A. Identity

- [ ] Which of the three distinct things is being supplied: an oral precursor (NR, NMN),
      intravenous intact NAD+, or an injectable or intranasal form. These are not
      interchangeable and have different evidence and different regulatory status.
- [ ] Analytical confirmation of identity by an orthogonal method, not a label claim.
- [ ] Assay of actual content against label claim, given the published deviation range.
- [ ] Grade of the raw material and its qualification for the intended route.
      **Food-grade material must be excluded explicitly.**

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] Purity by a validated, stability-indicating method.
- [ ] Named and quantified impurities and degradants, with limits.
- [ ] Residual solvents and elemental impurities.
- [ ] The repackager or upstream supplier in the chain, identified.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] Sterility testing for any injectable or infusible presentation.
- [ ] **Endotoxin testing with a stated limit.** This is not a generic item for NAD+: it
      is the specific failure that triggered a Class I recall.
- [ ] Container closure integrity.
- [ ] The manufacturing facility, its registration status, and its inspection history.

### D. Stability

- [ ] Stability data supporting the assigned shelf life, in the actual container.
- [ ] In-use and post-reconstitution stability where applicable.
- [ ] Storage and transport conditions, with excursion limits.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] The actual manufacturer, not only the distributor.
- [ ] Whether the product is compounded, and under which section.
- [ ] Whether the bulk substance is eligible for that compounding pathway at all.
- [ ] Transport and storage handling between manufacture and receipt.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] A lot-specific COA. A COA for a different lot proves nothing about this one.
- [ ] Independent third-party verification, not supplier self-report.
- [ ] Whether the COA covers identity and content, or purity alone.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] Labelling that meets the requirements for the product's actual classification.
- [ ] No therapeutic claim the classification does not support.
- [ ] Accurate statement of what is in the container, at what content.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] A named responsible person at the supplier.
- [ ] An adverse event reporting route and a recall contact.
- [ ] Insurance and indemnity position.
- [ ] Notification obligations if a lot is recalled.
