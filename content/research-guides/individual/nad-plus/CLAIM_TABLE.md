# Claim Table: NAD+ and its precursors (NR, NMN)

The evidence spine of this Guide. One row per discrete claim. Every claim is graded on its own
merit, never by the popularity of the compound and never by the volume of marketing around it.
Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
|---|---|
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

Default grade is D or G unless a specific retrieved human study supports better.

## The rule that governs this whole table

"NAD+" is not one thing. Three different products are sold under the same word, and their
evidence does not transfer between them.

1. Oral precursors (nicotinamide riboside, nicotinamide mononucleotide, nicotinamide, NADH).
   Nearly all randomized human evidence sits here.
2. Intravenous NAD+, the intact molecule given by infusion at wellness clinics. Two human
   studies were retrieved, neither of them an efficacy trial.
3. Injectable (subcutaneous, intramuscular) and intranasal NAD+. No human evidence was
   retrieved at all.

Every claim below is bound to one of these lanes. A claim from lane 1 may never be used to
support a statement about lane 2 or lane 3. That transfer is the single most common error in
consumer content on this compound, and it runs in the dangerous direction: the better-studied
route is the safer-appearing one, so borrowing its data flatters the less-studied route.

Routes are named in this Guide only to classify evidence. They are never guidance, and this
Guide contains no administration information of any kind.

## PROHIBITED claims

These may not appear on any member-facing surface for this compound, in any wording, in any
channel, including social copy, email, and product-adjacent text. This section is the most
important part of the table.

| id | Prohibited claim | Why it is prohibited |
|---|---|---|
| PROHIBITED-001 | That intravenous or injectable NAD+ improves energy, focus, cognition, mood, addiction recovery, longevity, or "cellular repair" | No randomized controlled evidence of clinical benefit for intravenous NAD+ for any indication was retrieved. The two human studies retrieved measured metabolites and adverse events. This is the largest gap between marketing and evidence in the entire record |
| PROHIBITED-002 | Using oral NR or NMN trial data to support any statement about intravenous, injectable, or intranasal NAD+ | Different exposures, different evidence. The transfer runs from the better-studied, safer-appearing route to the less-studied one, which makes it actively misleading rather than merely sloppy |
| PROHIBITED-003 | Describing NAD+ or any precursor as "safe", "well tolerated", or free of side effects as a general statement | A tolerability conclusion belongs to the specific trials, populations, durations, and route it came from. In the one retrieved intravenous tolerability study, every recipient reported moderate to severe symptoms. A general safety statement would contradict the record |
| PROHIBITED-004 | That NAD+ or its precursors reverse aging, restore youthful NAD+ levels, repair DNA, or activate sirtuins in people | The mechanism is a hypothesis. No human demonstration was retrieved, and no primary preclinical study was retrieved either, so the Guide cannot even reach for model-organism framing |
| PROHIBITED-005 | Stating the blood NAD+ increase without the null clinical outcomes in the same passage | The biomarker moves and the outcomes people buy this for do not. Presenting half of that is the defining failure mode of consumer content on this compound |
| PROHIBITED-006 | Presenting the FDA determination on NMN as approval, endorsement, validation, or a safety or efficacy finding, or extending it to intravenous or injectable NAD+ | It is a determination about whether an oral ingredient may lawfully be marketed as a dietary supplement, and a notification requirement still applies. Conflating it with approval of NAD+ therapy is the single most likely error in consumer content here |
| PROHIBITED-007 | Any statement that NAD+, NMN, or NR is permitted, cleared, or "not banned" for athletes in tested sport | Unresolved. The official List could not be searched this session, and the S0 catch-all could plausibly capture an unapproved injectable form regardless of whether the substance is named |
| PROHIBITED-008 | Any dosing, amount, concentration, frequency, timing, cycle, titration, loading, stacking, reconstitution, administration technique, or route guidance | Fixed Xenios rule. Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides. Routes are named in this Guide only to classify evidence |
| PROHIBITED-009 | "Research use only" framing, or any construction that uses a research or laboratory label to imply human benefit or to signal availability | Prohibited framing across all Xenios Research Guides |
| PROHIBITED-010 | Describing "NAD therapy" for addiction or detox as a treatment, a therapy, or an indication | This is a clinic marketing category. No approved indication and no retrieved efficacy evidence supports it |
| PROHIBITED-011 | Any guidance on where to obtain, how to select, how to source, or how to evaluate a supplier of NAD+ or its precursors | No sourcing or acquisition guidance appears in Xenios Research Guides. The quality findings in Section 6 are context about the category, not shopping advice |
| PROHIBITED-012 | Any statement about safety in people with a history of cancer, in pregnancy, or alongside any medication | The one retrieved intravenous study explicitly excluded people with a personal or family cancer history and did not explain why. No interaction, contraindication, or pregnancy search was run. Silence in this Guide is not evidence of absence of risk, and must never be presented as reassurance |
| PROHIBITED-013 | Any claim resting on an FDA or WADA page that a human has not opened directly | Every fda.gov fetch and both WADA fetches failed this session. Rows NAD-024, NAD-025, NAD-026, NAD-029, and NAD-031 are held for exactly this reason |
| PROHIBITED-014 | Any preclinical finding stated without its species or model in the same sentence | Standing rule, and unusually live here because the popular mechanism story for this compound originates in model organisms.
