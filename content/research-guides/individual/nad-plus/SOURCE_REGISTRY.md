# Source Registry: NAD+ and its precursors (NR, NMN)

Every source consulted for this Guide, with its retrieval status as of 2026-07-19. Source ids
(S1, S2, and so on) are referenced by CLAIM_TABLE.md, CONTRADICTIONS.md, and
REGULATORY_STATUS.md.

Retrieval status is recorded honestly. "VERIFIED 2026-07-19" means the page was actually
fetched and read this session. "UNVERIFIED" means the content is known only from a search
snippet or a secondary account, and a human must open the URL before anything resting on it
is published.

## Verified peer-reviewed human sources

| id | Title | Kind | URL | Status | What it supports |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | Effects of Nicotinamide Mononucleotide on Glucose and Lipid Metabolism in Adults: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis of Randomised Controlled Trials (Current Diabetes Reports, 2024; PMID 39531138; DOI 10.1007/s11892-024-01557-z) | Systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs, oral route | https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11557618/ | VERIFIED 2026-07-19 (full text read) | Oral NMN raises blood NAD+ in most included trials, and shows no significant benefit on fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, HOMA-IR, or lipid profile. | S2 | The Effect of Nicotinamide Mononucleotide and Riboside on Skeletal Muscle Mass and Function: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis (Journal of Cachexia, Sarcopenia and Muscle, 2025; PMID 40275690; DOI 10.1002/jcsm.13799) | Systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs, oral route | https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12022230/ | VERIFIED 2026-07-19 (full text read) | Oral NMN shows no significant effect on muscle index, grip strength, gait speed, or chair-stand performance in adults aged 60 and over. ## Sources deliberately not used

Supplement vendor pages, retailer pages, manufacturer pages, IV clinic marketing pages, and
testing-laboratory blogs were encountered during searching and were excluded as evidence. Two
such pages asserted that NAD+ and NMN are absent from the WADA Prohibited List. Both were
rejected, because a regulatory claim requires a regulatory source. One vendor-adjacent claim
about far more dramatic supplement quality failures was also rejected, and appears in
CLAIM_TABLE.md only as a market claim at grade G.
