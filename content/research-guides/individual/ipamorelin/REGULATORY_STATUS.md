# Ipamorelin: Regulatory Status

**Status checked: 2026-07-19. This status must be re-verified before publication and before
any subsequent republication. Regulatory positions on peptides are actively changing, and a
statement that is accurate on the date checked can be wrong within weeks.**

**Material limitation on everything below. fda.gov was unreachable throughout the research
session on 2026-07-19. Every attempted FDA URL failed, including the bulk drug substances
pages, the advisory committee calendar entry, the briefing document, and the meeting minutes.
Every United States regulatory statement in this document is therefore secondhand, taken from
investigative journalism, a trade association report, and a legal practice analysis. None of
it has been confirmed against the regulator. A human reviewer must verify each line directly
at fda.gov before any of it reaches a member.**

Approval or registration in one jurisdiction is never approval in another. A compound may be
approved in one country, investigational in a second, and prohibited in a third at the same
time. Read each entry only for the jurisdiction it names.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## 4. Sport and anti-doping

| Body | Statement | Date checked | Source URL | Source grade |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| World Anti-Doping Agency | Ipamorelin is prohibited in sport. A peer-reviewed anti-doping analytical paper studying ipamorelin states that growth hormone releasing peptides stimulate secretion of endogenous growth hormone and are listed on the World Anti-Doping Agency Prohibited List, with ipamorelin among the compounds studied. The same paper establishes that ipamorelin metabolites are detectable in human urine by doping control methods, so detection is not hypothetical. Athletes subject to testing should treat ipamorelin as prohibited. | 2026-07-19 | https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25869809/ | Primary peer-reviewed paper, used as a substitute because the Prohibited List document itself could not be retrieved |

Constraint on this entry: the WADA Prohibited List PDF, a WADA athlete guide, a third-party
mirror, and two other secondary references all failed to retrieve on 2026-07-19. The class
designation numbering and the verbatim list entry are therefore unconfirmed. No specific
Prohibited List subsection number may be published for this compound until a human confirms
it in the current WADA document. The prohibited status may be stated. The citation detail
may not be reconstructed.

## 5. What a reviewer must verify before publication

1. fda.gov directly, for the current 503A bulks list position, the 2023 Category 2
   designation, and the composition of the April 15, 2026 Category 2 removals.
2. The FDA advisory committee briefing document and meeting minutes for October 29, 2024,
   including the numeric vote tally, which this record does not contain.
3. The current WADA Prohibited List document.
4. At least one non-US regulator, so the Guide is not silently United States only.
5. Any FDA warning letters or import alerts naming ipamorelin sellers, which could not be
   searched because fda.gov was unreachable.
6. The date stamp on this file, which must be updated to the date of that verification.

If any of these cannot be verified, the corresponding statement is removed from the
member-facing Guide rather than published with a hedge. A regulatory claim that cannot be
checked at its source does not belong in front of a member.
