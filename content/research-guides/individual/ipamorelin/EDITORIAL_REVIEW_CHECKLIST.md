# Editorial review checklist: ipamorelin

This is the sign-off gate. The ipamorelin Guide, FAQ, and quality documents do not publish until
every box below is checked by a named human, and every named reviewer has signed.

**AI drafts never publish automatically.** No automated process, scheduled job, agent, or pipeline
may move any file in this folder from `workflow_state: draft` to any published state. Publication is
a deliberate human action taken after this checklist is complete. An unchecked box is a hard stop,
not a note.

**All boxes below start UNCHECKED. Nothing in this folder has been reviewed.**

Files in scope:

- GUIDE_DRAFT.md
- FAQ_DRAFT.md
- QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md
- EDITORIAL_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md (this file)

---

## A. Content safety

- [ ] No dosing appears anywhere. No amounts, mg, mcg, IU, mL, units, concentrations, frequency,
      timing, cycle length, titration, loading, stacking, reconstitution, injection technique, or
      route of administration, in any file, including tables, captions, and quoted material.
- [ ] Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact sentence "Dosing and administration information
      is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides." is present.
- [ ] No treatment directions, protocols, or regimens. Nothing a reader could follow as instructions.
- [ ] No guaranteed outcomes. The words "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures", "eliminates", and
      "reverses" do not appear as outcome claims.
- [ ] No unsupported safety claims. "Safe", "well tolerated", and "no side effects" do not appear as
      general statements of fact. Where the published trial's own characterisation is reported, it is
      attributed to that trial, in that setting, with its caveats attached.
- [ ] No sourcing, acquisition, vendor, or supplier guidance anywhere.
- [ ] No medical advice, no diagnosis, no self-treatment framing.
- [ ] Laboratory-chemical labelling is never used as a device to imply human benefit or suitability.
- [ ] The Safety categories section reads as categories of concern and cannot be read as a
      reassurance.

## B. Preclinical to human integrity

This is the highest-risk failure mode for this compound and is reviewed line by line.

- [ ] Every preclinical finding names its species or model in the SAME sentence as the finding.
- [ ] No bare mechanism sentence exists that a reader could take as established human physiology.
- [ ] The 1998 selectivity finding is described as rat, pig, and cultured-cell work every time it
      appears, including in the FAQ and in any summary.
- [ ] Title-level-only preclinical records (rats, ferrets, cichlid fish, rodent ileus model) are
      flagged as title level only, with detailed findings explicitly not relied upon.
- [ ] The rodent bone growth finding carries its caveat about closed growth plates in adults.
- [ ] The distinction between a biomarker change (growth hormone rising) and a clinical benefit is
      stated clearly and is not undermined anywhere else in the text.

## C. Citation integrity

- [ ] Every PMID in every file has been opened by a human reviewer and confirmed to exist, and to be
      the paper described.
- [ ] Every NCT number has been opened by a human reviewer and confirmed.
- [ ] Every URL has been loaded by a human reviewer and confirmed to resolve to the cited content.
- [ ] No citation was reconstructed from memory, inferred, or guessed. Any citation a reviewer
      cannot confirm is deleted and logged as a gap rather than softened.
- [ ] Every journal name, year, sample size, and reported number has been checked against the source.
- [ ] The reported primary endpoint result of PMID 25331030 (approximately 25 hours versus
      approximately 33 hours, p = 0.15, not statistically significant) has been checked against the
      publication.
- [ ] The unreported status of NCT01280344 has been re-checked on ClinicalTrials.gov on the day of
      publication, since results could be posted at any time.
- [ ] Every line marked [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check] has been
      either sourced by a human, or deleted. No such line publishes as-is.
- [ ] The gaps disclosed in the Limitations section match the gaps that actually remain at
      publication time.

## D. Regulatory verification

- [ ] Every regulatory statement carries a date, a jurisdiction, and a source URL.
- [ ] fda.gov has been reached by a human and the following confirmed directly: approval status,
      Category 2 status, the outcome of the 2024-10-29 advisory committee vote, and whether
      ipamorelin was among the 2026-04-15 Category 2 removals.
- [ ] The secondary-source disclosure is either removed because primary sources were obtained, or
      retained and accurate.
- [ ] The FDA advisory committee briefing document on ipamorelin has been sought again, and either
      obtained and incorporated, or its absence re-disclosed.
- [ ] The WADA Prohibited List has been checked directly by a human. No section number is published
      unless it was read in the actual WADA document.
- [ ] The date stamp on every regulatory statement has been updated to the actual publication date,
      not left at the drafting date.
- [ ] The distinction that Category 2 removal does not authorise compounding is stated explicitly and
      cannot be misread.
- [ ] Non-United States readers are not left to assume the United States position describes their
      jurisdiction.

## E. Claim grades

- [ ] Every claim is graded individually. No grade is applied to the compound as a whole.
- [ ] Grades are conservative. Anything without a specific retrieved human study supporting better is
      graded D or G.
- [ ] The claim grade summary table matches the grades used in the body text, with no conflicts.
- [ ] Every PROHIBITED claim is confirmed absent from all member-facing surfaces, specifically:
      - [ ] no claim of benefit for body composition, lean mass, fat loss, strength, recovery, sleep,
            injury healing, skin, or aging
      - [ ] no general statement that the compound is safe, well tolerated, or side-effect free
      - [ ] no statement or implication that it is FDA-approved, FDA-cleared, or lawful to compound
      - [ ] no use of "clinically studied" or "completed Phase 2 trials" as reassurance without the
            outcome of those trials stated in the same passage
      - [ ] no presentation of the animal selectivity finding as a human property

## F. House style and originality

- [ ] Zero em dashes in every file. Checked by search, not by reading.
- [ ] Plain, direct English. Every technical term glossed on first use.
- [ ] Calm and premium tone. No hype, no sales language, no flattery, no exclamation marks.
- [ ] All wording is original. No passage copies or lightly paraphrases competitor, retailer,
      manufacturer, or journalistic source text. Checked for the ProPublica, trade association, and
      legal-practice sources in particular, since they are the basis of the regulatory section.
- [ ] Quotation, where any exists, is minimal, marked, and attributed.

## G. Disclosure and framing

- [ ] The informational, not medical advice, not a recommendation disclosure is present in every
      member-facing file.
- [ ] The statement that Xenios does not sell, supply, source, or facilitate access to this compound
      is present and unambiguous.
- [ ] The statement that Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation for this compound is
      present and unambiguous.
- [ ] The AI-assisted draft disclosure is either removed at publication because human review is
      complete and recorded, or retained accurately.
- [ ] The Guide's honest verdict (empty human evidence base for the marketed uses) is stated in the
      one-minute summary and is not softened anywhere later in the document.

## H. Correction history and versioning

- [ ] The correction history table in GUIDE_DRAFT.md carries a row for this review, with date,
      version, change, author, and reviewer.
- [ ] The frontmatter `last_reviewed` date on every file matches the actual review completion date.
- [ ] The frontmatter `status` and `workflow_state` values are updated deliberately by a human, as
      the final step, and only after every box above is checked.
- [ ] A re-review date is set. Given the active 2026 regulatory picture, the regulatory section
      requires re-verification on a defined schedule regardless of whether anything else changes.
