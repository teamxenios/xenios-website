# Quality and documentation: ipamorelin

## Purpose of this document

This is the documentation standard a supplier would have to satisfy before Xenios would treat a
product containing ipamorelin as characterised. It is written as a checklist so that the gap between
what should exist and what does exist is visible at a glance.

Read the status line first, because it governs everything below.

**Current status, as of 2026-07-19: Xenios has NOT CONFIRMED any item on this checklist for
ipamorelin. Every box is unmet. No supplier has been assessed, no certificate of analysis has been
reviewed, no independent verification has been obtained, and no product has been qualified.**

This is a standard, not a claim that the standard has been met. Nothing in this document should be
read as suggesting that a compliant supply exists, that Xenios has identified one, or that Xenios
supplies, sources, or facilitates access to this compound. Xenios does not.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Why this matters more for this compound than for most

Three facts from the research record make the documentation gap consequential rather than academic.

1. **There is no lawful regulated supply route in the United States at present.** Ipamorelin is not
   an approved drug in any jurisdiction identified in our review, and it has not been added to the
   FDA 503A bulks list, so compounding pharmacy supply is not an authorised channel either
   (see the Regulatory status section of GUIDE_DRAFT.md, dated 2026-07-19). Without a regulated
   channel, no independent body is checking sterility, endotoxin content, identity, or purity.
2. **The synthetic peptide supply chain has documented failures.** In an analytical quality-control
   study of commercially obtained synthetic peptides (PMID 18342612), one of five products was a
   completely different peptide from the label, and two thirds of the rest fell below acceptable
   quality thresholds. Counterfeit and illegal injectable peptide preparations are enough of an
   enforcement problem in Europe that controlling agencies developed a dedicated screening method
   covering 25 peptides already found in seized products (PMID 26003685).
3. **Identity confusion is built into how this compound is sold.** Ipamorelin is very commonly
   marketed blended with CJC-1295, a different compound acting on a different receptor. A blend is
   two active substances, and our review located no trial of the combination. A documentation
   package that does not resolve exactly what is in the container resolves nothing.

There is a fourth point specific to this compound: **no published analytical survey testing the
identity and purity of consumer products labelled as ipamorelin was located in our review.** So the
honest position is not that these products test badly. It is that nobody has published a test.

## The checklist

Legend: `[ ]` unmet. Every box below is currently unmet for ipamorelin.

### 1. Identity

- [ ] Full chemical name, amino acid sequence, and salt or counterion form stated on the
      certificate of analysis and matching the label.
- [ ] Registry identifier stated and independently confirmed. (Xenios does not publish a CAS number
      for this compound in member-facing material until a human has verified it against a primary
      chemical registry. [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check])
- [ ] Confirmatory analytical identity data, for example mass spectrometry showing the expected
      molecular mass, and where applicable sequence confirmation.
- [ ] Explicit statement of whether the product is single-compound ipamorelin or a blend. If a
      blend, every active component identified separately, each with its own identity data.
- [ ] Batch or lot number on the container matching the certificate of analysis exactly.

### 2. Purity and impurities

- [ ] Chromatographic purity assay (for example HPLC) with the method, the acceptance threshold, and
      the measured result stated.
- [ ] Individual impurity profile reported, not only a single total purity percentage. The obestatin
      study (PMID 18342612) treats individual impurities above 1 percent as a quality failure even
      where total purity looks acceptable.
- [ ] Residual solvents reported against a recognised limit standard.
- [ ] Peptide content (net peptide versus total mass, including water and counterion) stated
      separately from chromatographic purity, because the two are routinely conflated.
- [ ] Heavy metals and elemental impurities tested and reported.

### 3. Sterility

- [ ] Sterility testing performed on the finished product by a recognised method, with the method,
      the standard, and the result stated.
- [ ] Documentation of the aseptic manufacturing environment, including the facility, its
      classification, and its regulatory registration status.
- [ ] Container closure integrity data.
- [ ] Explicit statement of whether the product was manufactured under any recognised pharmaceutical
      quality system, and if so which, with the certificate or registration number.

### 4. Endotoxin

- [ ] Bacterial endotoxin testing performed and the numerical result reported, with the method and
      the applied limit stated.
- [ ] The limit justified for the intended route, not simply asserted.

### 5. Stability and storage

- [ ] Stability data supporting the stated shelf life, generated on the actual formulation rather
      than inferred from the raw peptide.
- [ ] Storage conditions stated and justified by data.
- [ ] In-use stability after any preparation step, supported by data rather than by convention.
- [ ] Expiry or retest date on the container, matching the certificate of analysis.

### 6. Chain of custody and provenance

- [ ] Named manufacturer, named facility, and country of manufacture, not a distributor name only.
- [ ] Complete custody record from manufacture to receipt, with each transfer documented.
- [ ] Temperature excursion monitoring across transport and storage, with records.
- [ ] Named responsible person at the supplier, reachable, and accountable for the documentation
      package.
- [ ] Recall and complaint procedure documented, with evidence it has been exercised.

### 7. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] A certificate of analysis for the specific lot in hand, not a representative or example
      certificate for a different lot.
- [ ] The certificate covers identity, purity, individual impurities, sterility, endotoxin, and
      peptide content, with methods and acceptance criteria for each.
- [ ] Independent third-party laboratory verification of the supplier's certificate, commissioned by
      a party with no commercial interest in the result.
- [ ] The third-party laboratory named, its accreditation stated, and its accreditation
      independently checkable.
- [ ] The certificate is traceable to the physical container the member would hold, by lot number.
- [ ] The full documentation package is available for inspection rather than summarised in marketing
      material.

### 8. Regulatory and legal position of the supply

- [ ] Documented lawful basis for the supply in the relevant jurisdiction, dated, with a source.
- [ ] Confirmation that the supply route is not premised on a misreading of FDA Category 2 status.
      Removal from Category 2 does not authorise compounding, and substances remain unlawful for
      compounding until formally added to the Bulks List (see GUIDE_DRAFT.md, Regulatory status,
      dated 2026-07-19).
- [ ] Confirmation that the product is not represented, directly or by implication, as approved,
      cleared, or endorsed by any regulator.

Xenios does not accept laboratory-chemical labelling as a substitute for any item above, and does
not treat such labelling as implying anything about suitability for people. That framing is
prohibited in Xenios material.

## What documentation cannot fix

Completing every box above would establish that a container holds what its label claims, at a stated
purity, sterile, and within endotoxin limits. It would establish nothing about whether ipamorelin
does anything useful for a person, and nothing about whether it is safe to use.

Those are separate questions, and on the current record they are unanswered. Our review located no
human trial of ipamorelin for any outcome members care about, no long-term human safety data, and a
regulatory finding that the available safety data were insufficient to resolve known concerns. A
perfect certificate of analysis for an unstudied compound is a well-documented unknown.

Both gaps would need to close, independently, before any different conclusion could be reached.
