# Ipamorelin: Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Each claim is graded on its own evidence, never on the
reputation of the compound as a whole. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical (animal or in vitro) |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

Default is D or G. A grade of B or better requires a specific retrieved human study.

## Summary before the detail

The human evidence base for the reasons people actually take ipamorelin is empty. Not thin,
not mixed, empty. No human trial of body composition, lean mass, fat loss, strength,
recovery, sleep, injury healing, skin, or aging was located. The compound reliably raises
a hormone in a small human pharmacology study, its one published efficacy trial (in an
unrelated surgical indication) did not meet its primary endpoint, its largest trial has
never reported results, and its development program was discontinued. That is the whole
honest picture, and an empty efficacy table is the correct output here.

## 8. PROHIBITED claims

These may never appear on any member-facing Xenios surface for this compound, in any
wording, in any hedged form, in any headline, subheading, image caption, metadata field,
or search snippet. This section governs over every other section.

| ID | Prohibited claim | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| P-01 | Any statement that ipamorelin builds muscle, increases lean mass, reduces body fat, improves body composition, or produces a physique change | No human trial of any of these endpoints was located. The entire consumer use case is unstudied |
| P-02 | Any statement that it improves sleep, sleep quality, or sleep architecture | No located human study measured sleep |
| P-03 | Any statement that it improves recovery, reduces soreness, speeds return to training, or heals injuries, tendons, joints, or connective tissue | No located human study measured any of these |

## 9. Reviewer sign-off requirements

No member-facing surface may be published from this table until all of the following are true.

1. Every Grade G regulatory row (C-64, C-70 through C-75) has been verified against fda.gov,
   or removed.
2. C-52 has been approved as an exact sentence, with its causation caveat and population
   context intact.
3. C-05, C-55, C-62, and C-65 have each been sourced or deleted. None may ship as written.
4. C-40, C-41, and C-43 have had their abstracts retrieved, or remain out of member-facing use.
5. The remaining PubMed pages have been swept, so that C-27 (the absence of human outcome
   studies) is stated on a complete search rather than a partial one.
6. A reader who sees only the headings and the first sentence of each section comes away
   understanding that the human evidence for the marketed uses is absent. If a skim of the
   Guide reads as promising, the Guide is wrong even if every individual sentence is defensible.
