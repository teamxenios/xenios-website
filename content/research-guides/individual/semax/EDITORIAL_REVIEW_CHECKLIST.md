## Named reviewer sign-off

No publication occurs until all five reviewers below have signed. A signature attests that the
reviewer personally checked the boxes in their area, not that they read a summary of them.

**One person, one role. No exceptions.**

No individual may sign more than one of the five roles above, and no individual may sign a role
whose boxes they did not personally check. This rule is not administrative tidiness. A five-gate
review exists to put five independent judgements between a draft and a member, and a single actor
holding two or more signatures collapses those gates into one self-approving check. An actor who
approves their own work provides no assurance at all, however carefully they work, because the
error the gate is designed to catch is precisely the one that actor cannot see. If five
independent people are not available, publication waits. It does not proceed with doubled
signatures.

**Publication decision**

- [ ] All 12 sections complete, every box checked.
- [ ] All five reviewers named, dated, and signed, each by a different person.
- [ ] Regulatory section re-verified within 7 days of publication and re-dated accordingly.
- [ ] Final approval to publish recorded, with date, by the founder review.

## Standing rules for this gate

1. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human
   sign-off is complete.
2. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
3. **No single person may sign more than one reviewer role.** See the sign-off table above. One
   self-approving actor defeats a five-gate review entirely.
4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a
   reviewer or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed
   and logged as a gap. Omitting is always better than guessing.
5. **Concluding that the evidence cannot support the claims made for it is a successful outcome**,
   not a failure of the research. For Semax the honest finding is that human studies exist but are
   non-randomized, unregistered, unreplicated, and largely from groups affiliated with the
   compound's origin, and that the promoted cognitive use has no supporting study at all. That
   finding must not be softened into "promising early evidence".
6. **A jurisdictional status never travels.** Any edit that lets a Russian registration imply
   standing elsewhere returns the whole document set to this gate.
7. **Where a status could not be established, the folder says nothing rather than inferring.** The
   anti-doping section is the live example. Any edit that introduces an inferred classification
   returns the document set to this gate.
8. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification. The pending advisory committee meeting of July 23 to
   24, 2026 makes this compound specifically time-sensitive.
9. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, or
   regulatory statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed.
