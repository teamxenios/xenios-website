# Semax: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing this
compound could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of Semax and
applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state** for Semax specifically. Xenios has not confirmed
any of these items for this compound. Every checkbox below is unchecked, and none is expected to
be checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below.

1. **Semax is not an approved drug in any jurisdiction that was checked outside the Russian
   Federation** (checked 2026-07-21). It is not FDA approved in the United States, and no EMA
   marketing authorisation was found, although the EMA search endpoint returned HTTP 401 and could
   not be read, so that absence is a weak negative resting on a tertiary source.

2. **Semax is reported to be a registered prescription medicine in the Russian Federation**
   (checked 2026-07-21, reported not primary-verified). That registration confers no legal or
   scientific standing in any other jurisdiction, and it does not mean that material bought online
   in another country came from any regulated production line. A registration somewhere else is
   not a quality assurance for a vial in your hand.

3. **No independent testing of material sold as Semax could be located.** No product-testing
   dataset for Semax was retrieved during research. Nobody appears to have systematically checked
   what is in these products. That is an absence of information, not a favourable result.

4. **The consumer supply route sits outside any pharmacy or regulatory chain.** A tertiary source
   states that Semax is widely sold by online vendors and used as a purported nootropic. Identity,
   purity, sterility, and content in that channel are unverified by anyone.

**A note on the identity problem specific to this compound.** United States regulatory filings
treat "Semax (free base)" and "Semax acetate" as separate bulk substances (Federal Register,
Docket FDA-2025-N-6895, published 2026-04-16, retrieved 2026-07-21). A product labelled simply
"Semax" does not by itself establish which material it contains, which makes item A1 below a real
requirement rather than a formality.

**A note on numbers encountered elsewhere.** Research for this Guide surfaced peptide-vendor and
peptide-encyclopedia pages asserting specific purity and quality positions, and several publishing
dosing protocols. None is scientific evidence, none is cited here, and no dosing information from
any source has been reproduced anywhere in this folder.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any
further conversation. Status for Semax as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single
item.

### A. Identity

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of the exact substance.** Not the name "Semax". The
      specific form, since United States regulatory filings treat Semax free base and Semax acetate
      as separate bulk substances. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Verified registry identifiers.** A chemical registry number and molecular weight
      confirmed against a primary chemical registry. No such figures are reproduced anywhere in
      this folder because none was confirmed during research. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
      [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]
- [ ] **A3. Confirmed amino acid sequence for the specific batch**, by an orthogonal analytical
      method, not by supplier assertion. The sequence reported in the literature
      (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) itself rests on a tertiary source in this folder and should be
      confirmed against a primary registry. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A4. Explicit exclusion of confusable compounds.** Documented evidence that the material is
      Semax and not a related ACTH fragment, not Selank (with which Semax was co-studied in one
      retrieved imaging study), and not a different peptide entirely. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set
      before testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **B1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, with the method and conditions
      disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of
      it there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Peptide-related impurities characterised**, including truncated, deletion, and
      modified sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit
      standard. This item carries additional weight for Semax, since copper binding appears as a
      theme in the retrieved preclinical literature and metal content is therefore not a neutral
      variable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Aggregation assessed.** Peptide clumping is a recognised contributor to immune
      reactions against a peptide. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **C1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any
      route requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process,
      documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Stability

- [ ] **D1. Stability data under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not just a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **E1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a
      distributor, not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      A claim that a compound is registered in one country does not establish that a given
      producer is inspected by anyone. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis through to the finished unit**, including
      every intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Batch and lot traceability**, so that any single unit can be traced back to its
      manufacturing record. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **F1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis (COA)** matching the exact lot in hand, not a
      representative or example COA for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. The COA issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated and
      independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Test methods named on the COA**, with acceptance criteria and measured results, not
      "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no
      commercial interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. COA authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** COAs are forged. A
      document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **G1. Label content matching the tested content**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material that exceed the
      evidence.** For Semax that means no human cognitive or performance claims at all, and no
      human safety claims at all. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date**, and specifically no
      statement or implication that registration in the Russian Federation constitutes approval,
      clearance, or validation in any other jurisdiction. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. No anti-doping status asserted** unless sourced directly to the current WADA Prohibited
      List. Vendor inferences by analogy are not a source. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **H1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party. This
      matters more than usual here, since no human pharmacovigilance dataset for Semax was
      retrieved at all. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for Semax.** No supplier has been
assessed against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent
testing has been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed.
The purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly
what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide
exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the compound does anything. Perfect
documentation for Semax would establish that a container holds what it claims. It would not create
the adequately controlled, independently replicated human evidence that, as set out in the Guide,
does not exist.
