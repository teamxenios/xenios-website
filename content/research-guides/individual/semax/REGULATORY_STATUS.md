# Semax Regulatory Status

**Status checked on 2026-07-21. This page must be re-verified before publication and re-checked
on any subsequent update.** Regulatory positions change, and one item on this page concerns a
meeting that had not taken place at the time of checking.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Standing principles for this page

1. **Approval, authorisation, or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another.**
   For Semax this is the governing principle of the whole page. A registration recorded here for
   the Russian Federation says nothing about any other country, and no inference across borders
   may be drawn from it, in any phrasing, abbreviation, or paraphrase.
2. Every statement below carries a jurisdiction, a date checked, and a source URL. A statement
   without all three does not belong on this page.
3. Where a status could not be confirmed by reading the primary document, that is stated
   explicitly rather than filled in by inference.
4. A failed retrieval is not a confirmed negative. An HTTP 404 or HTTP 401 means the page was not
   read, not that the thing does not exist.
5. A nomination is not a review. An advisory committee recommendation is advisory and is not a
   final agency determination.
6. Where a status could not be established at all, this page states nothing rather than reasoning
   by analogy. That rule is what keeps the anti-doping section below empty of a classification.

### US-02. Section 503A bulk drug substances nomination, 2026

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** As of the Federal Register notice published 2026-04-16 (Docket FDA-2025-N-6895),
"Semax (free base)" and "Semax acetate" appear on the list of bulk drug substances **nominated**
for possible inclusion on the section 503A Bulk Drug Substances List. The nominated uses listed
are "Cerebral ischemia, migraine, and trigeminal neuralgia". The Pharmacy Compounding Advisory
Committee meeting to discuss these nominations was scheduled for July 23 to 24, 2026.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/full_text/text/2026/04/16/2026-07361.txt
**Source (metadata):** https://www.federalregister.gov/api/v1/documents/2026-07361.json
**Verification:** VERIFIED. Primary regulatory document, retrieved and read in full.

**Critical framing, and it is the most important paragraph on this page.** A 503A bulks-list
nomination is:

- **not** an approval;
- **not** an efficacy finding;
- **not** an endorsement;
- **not** FDA review of a drug application.

The 503A list is a compounding-eligibility mechanism, meaning it governs which raw substances
pharmacies may use when preparing customised medicines. It is not a drug approval pathway. The
nomination was made by an outside party. Nothing about a nomination indicates that a regulator
has assessed whether the substance works or is safe.

**Jurisdiction:** Sport, World Anti-Doping Agency.
**Statement:** **No WADA classification for Semax is stated in this folder.**
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source (not successfully retrieved):** https://www.wada-ama.org/en/prohibited-list
**Verification:** NOT VERIFIED. The WADA Prohibited List page could not be read this session; the
fetch returned no usable content.

**Why nothing is stated, rather than an inference.** Every Semax anti-doping status claim that
surfaced during research came from peptide-vendor and supplement-marketing sites. Several reasoned
by analogy to melanocortin peptides, to an S2 peptide category, or asserted an S0 catch-all
position. Those are vendor inferences, not WADA statements, and they are inadmissible as regulatory
evidence. Publishing an inference as a status would be worse than publishing nothing, because an
athlete could act on it.

**What the Guide says instead:** any athlete subject to testing should check with their own
anti-doping authority.

**Reviewer action:** read the current WADA Prohibited List directly and determine whether Semax is
named. Only then may any statement be made.
