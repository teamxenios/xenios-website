# Semax Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Grades apply to individual claims, never to the compound as a
whole. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## The headline finding

Semax is not a compound with no human evidence. It is a compound with human evidence that
cannot carry the weight placed on it.

Four human studies were retrieved. Two are Russian-language clinical studies in stroke and
cerebrovascular insufficiency, neither described as randomized, placebo-controlled, or blinded,
sharing a senior author (S03, S04). Two are small neuroimaging studies in healthy volunteers
reporting brain-connectivity differences rather than any clinical, cognitive, or symptomatic
outcome, sharing nearly identical author lists that include a figure named in search summaries
as a leader of the group that originally developed Semax (S01, S02).

Three structural facts follow, and they are the most important lines in this folder.

1. **Independence is absent.** Essentially the whole human literature traces to two affiliated
   Russian research groups, one of which appears to include the compound's originators. No
   replication by an unaffiliated group in another country was found.
2. **No trial of Semax is registered on ClinicalTrials.gov** (S07), so selective outcome
   reporting cannot be excluded. This matters acutely for S02, whose findings are explicitly
   described as post hoc.
3. **No systematic review or meta-analysis of Semax exists** in PubMed's review-filtered results
   (S06).

And the use the consumer market actually promotes, cognitive enhancement in healthy people, has
no supporting study at all. The two healthy-volunteer studies measured brain connectivity, not
cognition.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical (animal or cultured cells) |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

No claim in this table is graded A or B. A small number reach C, meaning only that a retrieved
human study reports the finding. Every C-graded claim here rests on a study that is
non-randomized or unregistered or both, and that comes from a group affiliated with the
compound's origin. The grade records what kind of source exists, not that the finding is
reliable.

## Reviewer sign-off checklist

1. Every preclinical sentence names its species or model in the same sentence as the finding, and
   no finding from PMID 16635254, PMID 16362768, or PMID 28255762 appears at all.
2. No sentence contains a dose, route as instruction, schedule, or preparation detail.
3. No sentence states or implies human benefit, human safety, or a regulatory outcome.
4. No sentence lets Russian registration imply standing in any other jurisdiction.
5. Every regulatory sentence carries a jurisdiction, a date, and a source URL.
6. Every claim graded G or marked [UNVERIFIED] has either been closed by a human check or removed.
7. No citation appears that is not present in SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.
