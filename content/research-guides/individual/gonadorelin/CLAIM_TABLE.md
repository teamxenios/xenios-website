# Gonadorelin Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Grades apply to individual claims, never to the compound as a whole. Source IDs refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides. No row in this table may be expanded with amounts, frequency, timing, duration, titration, or route.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

A note on how grade A is used here. Several claims below are graded A because they are statements about the contents of a primary regulatory record or the result of a database query, verified directly at source. Grade A on a regulatory or negative-search claim means the record says what we say it says. It does not mean an effect in humans is established. No efficacy claim in this table is graded above C.

| C-12 | A published case report describes a severe systemic hypersensitivity reaction, including loss of consciousness and tonic seizures, in an 8 year old girl undergoing a clinical stimulation test with gonadorelin acetate in a hospital setting. She was treated and her vital signs recovered within 30 minutes. The authors describe such life threatening reactions as extremely rare and conclude that administration should occur where systemic reactions can be managed. | C | S-07 | Single pediatric case, clinical diagnostic setting | Yes | A single case report is the weakest design for causation, and this must be stated in the same passage.
