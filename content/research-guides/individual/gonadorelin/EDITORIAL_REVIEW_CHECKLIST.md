## Standing rules for this gate

1. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human sign-off is
   complete.
2. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
3. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a reviewer
   or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed and logged as a
   gap. Omitting is always better than guessing. The deliberately omitted ClinicalTrials.gov
   identifiers are the working example of this rule for this compound.
4. **Concluding that human evidence is absent is a successful outcome**, not a failure of the research.
   For the popular use of this compound the correct finding is zero evidence, and it must not be padded
   with adjacent literature about GnRH analogues, cattle, or a rare congenital disease.
5. **A genuine historical approval is not current validation.** This compound's two discontinued FDA
   approvals are a fact about 1982 and 1989. Any drift towards using them as endorsement of a modern
   use returns the document to this gate.
6. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification. The contested 503A position makes this compound
   specifically sensitive to change.
7. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, or regulatory
   statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed.
