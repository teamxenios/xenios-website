# Gonadorelin: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing this
compound could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of gonadorelin and
applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state** for gonadorelin specifically. Xenios has not confirmed
any of these items for this compound. Every box below is unchecked, and none is expected to be checked
in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below.

1. **There is no FDA-approved human gonadorelin product on the United States market** (checked
   2026-07-19). FACTREL (NDA018123, approved 09/30/1982) and LUTREPULSE KIT (NDA019687, approved
   10/10/1989) both show marketing status Discontinued in Drugs@FDA. There is therefore no approved
   manufacturer, no approved specification, and no regulatory authority currently inspecting a human
   production line against a filed standard.

2. **No gonadorelin-specific product-quality study could be located.** A PubMed search combining
   gonadorelin with purity, counterfeit, content, or quality returned 25 records, and none of the
   top-ranked results on inspection was a product-quality analysis of gonadorelin (verified
   2026-07-19). Nobody appears to have systematically tested what is in material sold under this name.
   That is an absence of information, not a favourable result.

3. **A gonadorelin product is actively marketed for cattle under a brand name a discontinued human
   product also carried.** FACTREL from Zoetis Inc. (NADA 139-237) is labelled for use in cattle only
   (checked 2026-07-19). Veterinary material is a known gray-market supply route for compounds whose
   human product has been withdrawn, which makes species-of-origin a genuine identity question for any
   material presented under this name.

4. **The regulatory picture is being misreported.** Gonadorelin acetate appears in Category 1 of FDA's
   503B outsourcing facility bulk substances list (document updated March 21, 2025). It does not
   appear anywhere on the 503A pharmacy compounding list (document updated May 14, 2026), despite
   secondary sources claiming that it does. A supplier's own description of its regulatory position
   therefore cannot be taken at face value and must be checked against the primary documents.

Two things are deliberately **not** in this document. No dose, concentration, preparation, or handling
instruction of any kind appears, because those are excluded from Xenios Research Guides. And no vendor
or retailer material has been used as a source, because vendor claims are not evidence.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any
further conversation. Status for gonadorelin as of 2026-07-19 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single
item.

### A. Identity

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of the exact substance.** Not the word "gonadorelin". The
      specific form, since the compound has appeared as a hydrochloride and as an acetate and these are
      distinct materials. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Verified registry identifiers.** A CAS number and molecular weight confirmed against a
      primary chemical registry. No such identifiers were confirmed during research and none is
      reproduced anywhere in this document set. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A3. Confirmed amino acid sequence for the specific batch**, by an orthogonal analytical method
      (for example mass spectrometry combined with sequencing), not by supplier assertion.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A4. Explicit exclusion of confusable compounds.** Documented evidence that the material is
      gonadorelin and not a GnRH agonist analogue (leuprorelin, goserelin, triptorelin, buserelin,
      nafarelin, histrelin, deslorelin) and not a GnRH antagonist (cetrorelix, ganirelix, degarelix,
      elagolix). These are different molecules with different behaviour and different evidence bases.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Documented provenance excluding veterinary-market material.** Given that the only actively
      marketed gonadorelin product in the United States is a cattle drug, the origin of any material
      must be established rather than assumed. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A7. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set before
      testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **B1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, with the method and conditions
      disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of it
      there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Peptide-related impurities characterised**, including truncated, deletion, and modified
      sequences arising from synthesis. For a ten amino acid chain, a truncated or altered sequence is
      a different molecule with unknown behaviour. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Aggregation assessed.** Peptide clumping is a recognised contributor to immune reactions
      against a peptide, which is directly relevant given the documented hypersensitivity signal for
      this compound. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **C1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any route
      requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process, documented.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Stability

- [ ] **D1. Stability data under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not just a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **E1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a
      distributor, not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis through to the finished unit**, including every
      intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Batch and lot traceability**, so that any single unit can be traced back to its
      manufacturing record. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E7. Import provenance where material crossed a border**, including the regulatory basis on
      which it was imported. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **F1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis (COA)** matching the exact lot in hand, not a
      representative or example COA for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. The COA issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated and
      independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Test methods named on the COA**, with acceptance criteria and measured results, not
      "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no commercial
      interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. COA authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** Certificates are
      forged. A document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **G1. Label content matching the tested content**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material that exceed the
      evidence.** For gonadorelin that means no claim of any kind about testosterone therapy support,
      testicular function, or fertility preservation, because no human evidence for those uses exists.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date**, including that no
      FDA-approved human product exists and that the 503A position is contested rather than settled.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. No implication that the historical FDA approvals validate any current use.** Both human
      applications are discontinued and were for different purposes. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G6. No use of the brand name "Factrel" without stating which product is meant**, given that the
      only current product under that name is a cattle drug. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **H1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party. This matters
      particularly here, because FDA's adverse event database returned no gonadorelin records, meaning
      there is currently no surveillance system to fall back on. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. A documented plan for managing a severe hypersensitivity reaction**, given the published
      anaphylaxis case report and the study authors' conclusion that administration should occur in a
      setting equipped to manage systemic reactions. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H5. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for gonadorelin.** No supplier has been
assessed against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent testing
has been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed. The
purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly what has
and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the compound does anything. Perfect
documentation for gonadorelin would establish that a vial contains what it claims. It would not create
the human evidence for the popular use that, as set out in the Guide, does not exist at all.
