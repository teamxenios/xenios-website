# Gonadorelin Contradictions and Divergences

Where sources disagree, where evidence types diverge, and where circulating claims conflict with primary records. Source IDs refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md. Claim IDs refer to CLAIM_TABLE.md.

There are genuine contradictions for this compound, and one of them is unresolved. This is not a case where the record is clean.

## 1. Whether gonadorelin acetate is on the FDA 503A Category 1 list

**Position A.** Multiple secondary industry and vendor adjacent sources, including a compounding industry academy blog and a telehealth blog surfaced in search, state that gonadorelin acetate has been added to the FDA 503A Category 1 list, describing it as a peptide previously approved as Lutrepulse. (X-01, disqualified as evidence.)

**Position B.** The primary FDA document, bulk drug substances nominated for use in compounding under section 503A, updated May 14, 2026, does not contain gonadorelin in Category 1, Category 2, or Category 3. The full text of all seven pages was extracted and searched. (S-02.)

**Assessment.** The primary FDA document governs. Gonadorelin is not on the 503A nominations list as of May 14, 2026. It is on the 503B Category 1 list updated March 21, 2025, flagged with the double asterisk that designates a component of an FDA approved drug (S-03). Section 503B governs outsourcing facilities and is a different statutory pathway from section 503A, which governs pharmacy compounding. The secondary sources appear to be conflating the two lists.

**What must not be said.** A separate and plausible explanation for the absence from 503A nominations is that section 503A permits compounding from bulk substances that are components of approved drug products without requiring nomination to a bulks list, which would make nomination unnecessary. That reading could not be verified from any retrieved source and must not appear in the Guide.

**Status: UNRESOLVED. Flagged for human regulatory review.** The Guide must not assert 503A compounding status in either direction. See P-08.

## 2. Whether GnRH or gonadorelin is on the FDA Category 2 significant safety risk list

**Position A.** One search result summary asserted that gonadorelin remains permissible for compounding despite GnRH being Category 2. (X-02, disqualified as evidence.)

**Position B.** The FDA 503A Category 2 list, updated May 14, 2026, contains exactly six entries: cesium chloride, domperidone, germanium sesquioxide, ibutamoren mesylate, kisspeptin-10, and quinacrine hydrochloride for intrauterine administration. Neither GnRH nor gonadorelin appears. (S-02.)

**Assessment.** The circulating claim is false against the primary document. A likely source of the confusion is kisspeptin-10, a related upstream reproductive axis peptide that is on Category 2. This is worth knowing when members ask about adjacent compounds, and it is a useful illustration of how easily a claim about one reproductive axis peptide migrates to another.

**Status: RESOLVED against the secondary source.** See C-20.

## 3. What the brand name Factrel refers to

**Position A.** A historical human product: gonadorelin hydrochloride injection, NDA018123, originally approved 1982, all listed strengths now discontinued. (S-01.)

**Position B.** A currently marketed veterinary product: gonadorelin hydrochloride injection, Zoetis Inc., NADA 139-237, labelled for use in cattle only. (S-04.)

**Assessment.** Both are true and refer to different products that share a brand name. The human one is gone. The cattle one is current, and it is what search results for the brand name overwhelmingly return today. Any section of the Guide that mentions Factrel must make this explicit or it will mislead a member into reading a livestock label as evidence of a current human product.

**Status: RESOLVED, but requires explicit handling in the Guide.** See C-07, C-17, P-13, P-15.

## 4. Where animal findings and human findings diverge

There is no direct contradiction between the animal and human literature here, because they address unrelated questions. The divergence is one of relevance rather than of result.

The only large controlled gonadorelin trial retrievable is a field trial in 3,938 Holstein dairy cows comparing gonadorelin salts in a timed artificial insemination programme (S-10, C-18). It concerns livestock reproduction and supports nothing about humans. The only human therapeutic evidence retrieved is a 45 patient uncontrolled retrospective series in men with a rare congenital deficiency (S-06).

The contradiction worth naming is between the apparent volume of gonadorelin literature and its actual human relevance. A member or a clinician scanning PubMed will find a substantial body of gonadorelin work and may reasonably assume the compound is well studied. It is well studied in cattle. The human trial literature on gonadorelin as such is effectively absent. The Guide should make that asymmetry explicit rather than leaving a reader to infer volume equals evidence.

## 5. Where mechanism plausibility outruns evidence

This is the most consequential divergence for members, and it is not a disagreement between sources. It is a gap between what is widely assumed and what any retrieved source supports.

The mechanistic story behind the popular use is straightforward and, on its face, coherent. That coherence is doing all of the persuasive work, because the evidence layer underneath it is empty.

- Human evidence for gonadorelin alongside testosterone therapy, for preservation of testicular function, or for fertility preservation: none retrieved. A targeted PubMed search returned zero records (S-12, C-14).
- Registered trials with gonadorelin as an intervention: none returned (S-13, C-15).
- The human therapeutic evidence that does exist comes from pulsatile delivery as replacement therapy in patients whose own GnRH pulse generator has failed (S-06, C-25).
- Whether administration that is not pulsatile produces sustained stimulation, no effect, or downregulation is not established by anything retrieved here. This is labelled [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check] in C-26.

The honest position is that the popular use rests on mechanism reasoning and on a replacement therapy analogy, not on evidence in the population using it. The Guide should say this plainly. See P-01 through P-05 and P-12.

## 6. Where jurisdictions differ

The United States position and the anti doping position diverge in kind rather than conflicting.

- United States: no approved human product, but the substance sits in Category 1 of the 503B outsourcing facility bulks list, and a veterinary product is actively marketed (S-01, S-03, S-04).
- World Anti-Doping Agency: named explicitly as prohibited under S2.2.1, non Specified, with the prohibition as written applying in males (S-05).

These are not in conflict. A substance may be unapproved as a human drug in a country and separately prohibited in sport. Both statements must appear, and neither may be used to soften the other.

No non US national regulator was searched this session. The Guide therefore makes no claim about the European Union, the United Kingdom, Australia, Canada, Japan, or any other jurisdiction. Absence of a statement is not a statement of permissibility.

## 7. Where vendor and commercial claims conflict with the literature

Vendor, retailer, compounding pharmacy, and telehealth pages appeared throughout search results and were deliberately excluded as evidence (X-01, X-02, X-03). They conflict with the retrieved record in two identifiable ways.

1. On regulatory status, by asserting a 503A listing that the primary FDA document does not contain (section 1 above).
2. On efficacy, by presenting the testosterone therapy adjunct use as established when the retrieved human evidence for it is zero (S-12, S-13).

No vendor claim in this package is graded above E, and none is admitted to a member-facing surface. Commercial assertion is not evidence, and its volume online is not a signal of support.

## 8. Internal date discrepancy in this package

The workflow stamps this package to 2026-07-19. Every source in the underlying research record carries a retrieval date of 2026-07-20. Both are recorded as they stand rather than silently harmonised. A reviewer should reconcile the stamps before publication and should treat 2026-07-20 as the date the retrievals occurred.
