# LL-37 Contradictions and Divergences

Where the sources retrieved on 2026-07-19 disagree with each other, where laboratory findings diverge
from human findings, where mechanism runs ahead of evidence, and where market claims conflict with
the published literature.

LL-37 has genuine contradictions, not merely gaps. That is unusual in this category and it is the
defining feature of the compound's evidence record. Source IDs refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

## 1. Whether the largest human trial was a positive result

This is the single most important correction in this Guide.

Position A, as circulated in market materials. Vendor and vendor-adjacent pages present the Phase IIb
venous leg ulcer trial as supportive, quoting a comparison of 28.1 percent against 8.1 percent as the
headline result.

Position B, the published trial (S1). The trial did not meet its prespecified primary endpoint.
Confirmed complete wound closure occurred in 26.5 percent and 24.7 percent of the two active groups
against 25.3 percent of placebo, a difference that was not statistically significant. The 28.1
percent against 8.1 percent figure comes from a post-hoc analysis restricted to participants with
larger wounds, carried out after the primary endpoint had already failed.

Assessment. The primary source decisively contradicts the market framing. The quoted numbers are
real, but they are presented stripped of the facts that they are post-hoc, subgroup-restricted, and
generated after the trial's main result was null. This is not a disagreement between two credible
readings. It is a selective presentation. The Guide must state plainly that the largest human trial
of LL-37 did not meet its primary endpoint, and the subgroup figure may only appear alongside that
statement (claim rows C-011 and C-012, prohibition P-05).

## 2. Whether the early efficacy signal replicated

Position A. A 34-participant first-in-man randomized placebo-controlled trial (S2) reported improved
healing in the lower-strength groups compared with placebo, with a substantial reported difference.

Position B. The 148-patient Phase IIb trial (S1), in the same indication and with overlapping
authors, did not confirm this on its primary endpoint.

Assessment. This is not a contradiction between rival research groups. It is the same research
programme, at two sample sizes, producing two different answers, with the larger and better-powered
answer being null. That pattern has a standard interpretation, which is that a promising small
early-phase signal failed to hold at adequate sample size. Two further details support that reading.
The early trial's response was not consistent as strength increased, which is a weaker pattern than a
clean dose response would be, and the early trial was explicitly dose-finding rather than
confirmatory.

The correct handling is to treat the 2014 result as superseded rather than as independent supporting
evidence. Presenting the two trials additively as two positive trials would be misleading (C-014,
P-06).

## 3. Whether LL-37 is protective or harmful

Position A. LL-37 is a host-defence peptide, described in the reviewed literature as antimicrobial,
wound-healing, and immunomodulatory (S6).

Position B. LL-37 is described as a key autoantigen in psoriasis, is implicated in the
pathophysiology of systemic lupus erythematosus through complexes with the body's own DNA that drive
a type I interferon response (S7), is reported at elevated levels in several inflammatory diseases
(S6), and damages human red blood cells, lymphocytes, and fibroblasts in cultured cells at
concentrations similar to those needed for antimicrobial activity (S6).

Assessment. Both positions are supported in the retrieved peer-reviewed literature. Neither side is
wrong. This is a genuinely dual-natured molecule whose behaviour depends on context and
concentration, and the source literature itself frames it in those terms rather than resolving it.

The practical consequence is that the same membrane-disrupting property proposed to kill microbes is
also the proposed basis for damage to human cells, which means the separation between effect and host
toxicity is narrow by mechanism rather than by accident. Any account of LL-37 that presents only the
host-defence framing is materially incomplete, and that incompleteness is not a matter of tone. It
omits the compound's principal theoretical safety concern (C-030, C-031).

## 4. Mechanism plausibility against measured human outcome

Position A. LL-37 is characterized mechanistically as a broad-spectrum antimicrobial peptide, with
reported activity in laboratory systems against bacteria, some fungi, and some viruses (S6).

Position B. In the diabetic foot ulcer randomized trial (S3), topical LL-37 did not significantly
reduce bacterial colonization compared with placebo, and did not significantly reduce the measured
inflammatory markers.

Assessment. This is the clearest available example in this Guide of mechanism outrunning evidence. In
the one retrieved human trial that directly measured bacterial colonization, a peptide defined by its
antimicrobial mechanism did not measurably outperform placebo on that endpoint. The mechanistic
description is not thereby falsified, since laboratory conditions and an infected chronic wound are
very different environments, but the human measurement is the more relevant fact for a member and it
did not go the way the mechanism predicts (C-017, C-022).

A second, independent reason mechanism should not be extrapolated to human outcomes here is that
native LL-37 is documented as proteolytically unstable and of low stability in biological fluids
(S6). That is the field's own stated reason for pursuing engineered analogs.

## 5. Where preclinical and human findings sit in relation to each other

The preclinical and human records here do not conflict directly. They address different questions,
and the divergence is one of scope rather than direction.

The human record is narrow. Three published randomized trials, all applying LL-37 to a wound, plus a
four-person study injecting it into a skin tumour. The results range from null on the primary
endpoint to mixed on surrogate measures.

The preclinical record is broad and is where nearly all of the compound's reputation originates. It
supplies the antimicrobial mechanism, the immunomodulatory description, the wound-healing rationale,
the cytotoxicity signal, and the autoimmunity signal.

The divergence worth naming is that the promotional account of LL-37 draws its benefits from the
preclinical record and its safety reassurance from the human record, taking the favourable half of
each. The preclinical record's safety findings (host cell membrane damage at concentrations near the
antimicrobial range) and the human record's efficacy findings (a failed primary endpoint, no
reduction in bacterial colonization) are both routinely omitted. Stating this asymmetry plainly is
one of the more useful things this Guide can do.

## 6. An unresolved and potentially counter-directional preclinical claim

A PubMed Central article title asserts that LL-37 promotes the growth of a pulmonary fungal pathogen
(S8). If accurate, that would run directly against the broad antimicrobial framing.

It was surfaced in a search listing only. It was not retrieved and was not read. It is recorded here
rather than omitted precisely because it is directionally inconvenient, and dropping inconvenient
findings silently is how an evidence record becomes promotional. It must not be stated in the Guide
in either direction until a human reviewer has retrieved and read the full record (P-12).

## 7. Where jurisdictions differ, and where they are simply unknown

There is no retrieved evidence of jurisdictions taking different positions on LL-37, because only one
jurisdiction was searched and even that search did not resolve.

- United States. No FDA-approved LL-37 product was found. Secondary legal and trade sources report a
  future Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee review of cathelicidin LL-37, but every FDA and
  Federal Register page attempted returned an error or a block (S12, S13). The claim is unconfirmed.
- Anti-doping. Not resolved. The WADA Prohibited List could not be retrieved (S14). The only sources
  asserting a status were vendor pages, which this Guide does not accept.
- European Union, United Kingdom, Australia, Canada. Not searched. Nothing is known.

The honest characterization is an unresolved regulatory picture rather than a divergent one. Approval
or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another, and an absence of retrieved
information about a jurisdiction is not evidence of permissive treatment in that jurisdiction.

## 8. Contradictions that do not exist

Two apparent tensions are worth explicitly clearing, so a reviewer does not spend time on them.

Endogenous production against administered peptide. It can look contradictory that LL-37 is described
both as something the body makes constantly and as something with almost no human interventional
evidence. There is no contradiction. Nearly all literature under this term measures the body's own
LL-37 as a biomarker. A registry sweep of 50 records returned only two studies that administered
LL-37 as an intervention (S5). The two bodies of work answer different questions.

Tolerability against cytotoxicity. It can look contradictory that trials reported mild to moderate
local reactions while the laboratory literature reports damage to human cells. There is no
contradiction. The trials applied LL-37 topically to wounds over weeks in people with chronic ulcers,
and the cytotoxicity finding is from cultured cells at concentrations near the antimicrobial range.
Neither result speaks to the other, and neither speaks to systemic administration, long-term use, or
healthy adults (C-034, C-035).
