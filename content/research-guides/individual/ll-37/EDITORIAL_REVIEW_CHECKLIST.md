# LL-37 Research Guide, editorial sign-off gate

**AI drafts never publish automatically.** The LL-37 Guide, FAQ and quality document were drafted
by an AI research process on 2026-07-19. Nothing in this compound's folder may reach a
member-facing surface until every box below is checked by a named human, and until the founder
sign-off at the end is recorded with a date.

An unchecked box blocks publication. There is no partial publication path.

Every box below starts **UNCHECKED**.

---

## Section 1. Content safety

- [ ] No dosing information appears in any file. No amounts, no units of any kind, no
  concentrations, no frequency, no timing, no cycle length, no titration, no loading, no stacking,
  no reconstitution, no injection technique.
- [ ] Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact standard line appears: "Dosing and
  administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides."
- [ ] Route of administration appears only as a factual description of what a study did, never as
  instruction to a reader. Verified line by line in the human evidence section, where route is
  central to the evidence verdict.
- [ ] No treatment directions, protocols or regimens appear. Nothing in any file can be followed as
  instructions.
- [ ] No sourcing or acquisition guidance appears. No supplier names, no purchasing routes, no
  advice on obtaining the compound.
- [ ] No medical advice, no diagnosis, and no self-treatment framing appears.
- [ ] The phrase "research use only" does not appear anywhere as a device implying human benefit.

## Section 2. Claims and language

- [ ] No guaranteed-outcome language appears. Specifically checked for: "will", "proven to",
  "restores", "cures", "eliminates", "reverses", and equivalents.
- [ ] Effect language is confined to approved forms: "has been studied for", "reported in",
  "investigated as", "associated with in [population]".
- [ ] No unsupported safety claims appear. "Safe", "well tolerated" and "no side effects" do not
  appear as general statements of fact anywhere. Where trial investigators reported such findings,
  the statement is attributed to those investigators, bounded to that trial, and bounded to that
  route and duration.
- [ ] The safety section reads as categories of concern and never as reassurance.
- [ ] Prohibited claims are absent. Specifically verified absent: any anti-cancer claim derived
  from the four-person melanoma trial; any general immune-support claim; any claim that LL-37
  reduces infection in humans; any claim of benefit in healthy adults.
- [ ] House style holds throughout: zero em dashes in any file, plain English, no hype, no sales
  language, no flattery, no exclamation marks.
- [ ] All wording is original. No competitor, retailer or manufacturer text is copied or lightly
  paraphrased. Checked including the vendor pages referenced in the contradictions section.

## Section 3. Preclinical and human separation

This is the highest-risk failure category for this compound, because LL-37 is an endogenous human
molecule and mechanism sentences read as human fact by default.

- [ ] Every preclinical finding carries its species or model in the **same sentence** as the
  finding. Verified individually, not sampled.
- [ ] The cytotoxicity finding (red blood cells, immune cells, fibroblasts) is labelled as cultured
  human cells everywhere it appears, including in the summary and the safety section.
- [ ] The autoimmune signalling finding is labelled as cultured human immune cells, and the human
  blood-level observations are labelled as observational, everywhere they appear.
- [ ] The stability findings are labelled as biochemical and in vitro characterisation.
- [ ] The distinction between the body's own LL-37 measured as a biomarker and LL-37 given as an
  intervention is stated clearly and is maintained consistently across all files.
- [ ] The distinction between the natural peptide and engineered analogues is stated and
  maintained.
- [ ] No bare mechanism sentence exists anywhere that a reader could reasonably take as
  established human fact.

## Section 4. Citation integrity

- [ ] Every citation has been independently opened and verified by a named human reviewer. This
  means the source was retrieved, the identifier resolves, and the claim attributed to it is
  actually in it.
- [ ] No PMID, PMCID, NCT number, DOI, author name, year, journal name or URL was accepted without
  that check.
- [ ] The Phase IIb primary endpoint figures (26.5, 24.7, 25.3 percent) and the post-hoc subgroup
  figures (28.1 versus 8.1 percent, P = 0.0458) have been read directly from the published paper.
- [ ] The 2014 trial figures (approximately sixfold, p = 0.003, 68 percent and 50 percent mean
  ulcer area decrease) have been read directly from the source.
- [ ] The diabetic foot ulcer trial statements have been checked against the abstract, and the
  reviewer has decided whether to retrieve the full text before publication. Enrolment number,
  randomisation detail and the adverse event section are currently unverified.
- [ ] The melanoma registry record has been checked, including whether the posted efficacy results
  tables change the account given.
- [ ] The unconfirmed link between the diabetic foot ulcer publication and NCT04098562 is either
  confirmed, or remains explicitly labelled unconfirmed in the published text.
- [ ] The preclinical article surfaced by title only (PMCID PMC6013660) has been retrieved and read
  by a human, and a decision recorded on whether it belongs in the Guide. Its content is currently
  not stated anywhere and must not be added without retrieval.
- [ ] Any line marked "[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]" has been
  resolved to a verified source or removed.
- [ ] No claim survives whose source could not be retrieved.
- [ ] Vendor, affiliate and testing-service pages are used only as the object of the contradictions
  section, never as evidence for any factual claim.
- [ ] Purity, adulteration and counterfeiting figures circulating on vendor pages remain absent.
  If any purity claim has been added, it is sourced from a regulator warning letter, a regulator
  import alert, or peer-reviewed analytical chemistry literature.

## Section 5. Regulatory verification

- [ ] Every regulatory statement carries a date, a jurisdiction and a source. Verified
  individually.
- [ ] The regulatory section has been **re-verified on the day of publication** and the dates
  updated. Statements dated 2026-07-19 may not publish with a stale date.
- [ ] **FDA approval status:** re-checked directly. The current text records a negative search
  result rather than an affirmative FDA statement, and this framing has been confirmed as accurate
  or corrected.
- [ ] **FDA Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee status:** the reported February 2027 review of
  cathelicidin (LL-37) has been checked against the FDA advisory committee calendar or the Federal
  Register directly. This claim currently rests only on secondary legal and trade sources and is
  flagged unconfirmed. It either publishes with a direct source, or it does not publish.
- [ ] **WADA status:** the current Prohibited List has been checked directly. No WADA status may
  appear without that check, including the category covering substances with no current approval
  for human therapeutic use. If unverified, the Guide must continue to state that it is unverified
  rather than stating any status.
- [ ] Non-United States jurisdictions were either searched and reported, or the Guide continues to
  state explicitly that they were not searched.
- [ ] Legal review has confirmed that no statement in any file constitutes a medical claim, a
  marketing claim, or an implied offer to supply.

## Section 6. Claim grades

- [ ] Every substantive claim carries an individual grade on the A through G scale. Compounds are
  never graded as a whole.
- [ ] Grades default to D or G unless a specific retrieved human study supports better, and each
  upgrade above D has been justified against a named study by the reviewer.
- [ ] The grade for "improves health outcomes in healthy adults" is G, and this is confirmed as
  correct given that no retrieved human trial enrolled healthy people.
- [ ] The grade for topical use in hard-to-heal venous leg ulcers is confirmed, together with the
  accompanying statement that the largest trial testing it was negative on its primary endpoint.
- [ ] The large-wound subgroup benefit is graded G and is described as post-hoc and unreplicated
  wherever it appears.
- [ ] Any anti-cancer claim is graded PROHIBITED or is absent.
- [ ] Mechanism claims are graded D and are not presented at a higher grade anywhere.

## Section 7. Structure, disclosure and member experience

- [ ] Disclosure is present and unmistakable on every member-facing surface, including that the
  Guide was AI-drafted and human-reviewed, with the reviewers named.
- [ ] The statement that Xenios does not sell, supply, or advise on obtaining LL-37 appears
  clearly.
- [ ] The one-minute summary states the honest evidence verdict, including that the largest human
  trial missed its primary endpoint.
- [ ] Every technical term is glossed at first use in body text as well as in the terminology
  section.
- [ ] The unknowns section is present and is not softened.
- [ ] The limitations section discloses the research process's own failures, including the
  unretrieved sources.
- [ ] The correction history is present, seeded, and dated.
- [ ] The quality checklist accurately reports that Xenios has confirmed none of the 41
  documentation items.
- [ ] The questions for a qualified professional are usable as written and do not imply that
  professional approval is expected.
- [ ] A member who reads only the summary would not come away with a more favourable impression
  than a member who reads the whole Guide. Verified deliberately, since this is the most common
  way an honest document becomes misleading.
