# LL-37, quality and documentation

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document states what quality documentation **should** exist for any product containing LL-37,
and records the current state of that documentation at Xenios.

The current state is simple and should be read before anything else: **Xenios has not confirmed a
single item on this checklist for LL-37.** Every box below is unmet. That is not a placeholder
awaiting paperwork. It is the accurate status as of 2026-07-19.

This is written as a supplier-facing checklist so that it can be used directly, and so that the
absence of any item is visible rather than assumed.

---

## Why this matters more for LL-37 than for a typical compound

Two properties documented in the peer-reviewed literature make identity and quality verification
unusually important here, and both are reasoned from published findings rather than from any survey
of marketed products.

1. **The natural peptide is unstable.** Published characterisation reports that LL-37 is
   susceptible to being cut apart by enzymes, is unstable at high salt concentrations, and shows
   low stability in biological fluids (PMCID PMC12386566, VERIFIED 2026-07-19). A product that
   genuinely contained intact LL-37 at manufacture is not guaranteed to still contain intact LL-37
   after storage and shipping. Stability data is therefore not optional paperwork for this
   compound. It is central to whether the product is what the label says.
2. **The research field largely uses modified versions.** Much of the laboratory literature
   concerns chemically altered, shortened or ring-closed derivatives, developed specifically
   because the natural peptide is unstable. A product labelled "LL-37" could be a fragment or an
   engineered analogue whose evidence base is not the evidence base described in the Guide. Only
   full sequence confirmation distinguishes these.

**Important limit on what this document claims.** A search for documented purity, counterfeiting
and mislabelling data in the unregulated peptide market returned only vendor, affiliate and
testing-service marketing pages. Those do not meet the evidence standard for these Guides. Specific
figures circulating on such pages, including claimed rates of incorrect sequences, claimed
proportions falling below a stated purity, claimed discrepancy rates in certificates of analysis,
contamination claims, and a reported enforcement action against a vendor, are **not reproduced here
and are not treated as fact.** Any purity or adulteration claim in a published version of this
Guide must be sourced from a regulator warning letter, a regulator import alert, or peer-reviewed
analytical chemistry literature.

## The checklist

Legend: **[ ] UNMET** means Xenios has not received, reviewed, or confirmed this item.

### A. Identity

- [ ] **UNMET.** Full amino acid sequence confirmation, demonstrating the material is the
  37-residue human cathelicidin peptide and not a fragment, a truncated form, or an engineered
  analogue.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Mass spectrometry data confirming molecular weight consistent with the stated
  sequence, with the raw spectrum available and not only a summary figure.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Statement of the exact salt form or counterion, and of any modification to the
  peptide's ends, since these change the substance without changing the label name.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Confirmation that the substance corresponds to the material studied in the
  published human trials, or an explicit statement that it does not.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Manufacturer name, manufacturing site address, and country of manufacture.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Batch or lot number traceable to all documents below.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **UNMET.** Purity determination by a validated separation method, with the chromatogram
  supplied rather than a stated percentage alone.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Identification and quantification of individual impurities, including
  peptide-related impurities such as deletion sequences and truncated forms.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Residual solvent testing against a recognised limit standard.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Heavy metals and elemental impurity testing against a recognised limit standard.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Residual reagent testing appropriate to the synthesis route used.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Water content and counterion content, both of which affect how much peptide is
  actually present relative to total mass.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Aggregation assessment. Peptide clumping is relevant to immune response and is
  not visible in a purity percentage.

### C. Sterility and microbiological quality

- [ ] **UNMET.** Sterility testing by a recognised method, where the product form requires it.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Bioburden testing on the bulk material.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Container and closure integrity data.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Description of the manufacturing environment and its classification.

### D. Endotoxin

- [ ] **UNMET.** Bacterial endotoxin testing, with the method stated and the result expressed
  against a defined limit rather than reported as "passes".
- [ ] **UNMET.** Justification of the endotoxin limit applied.

### E. Stability

This section is the one most likely to be missing, and the one that matters most for this compound.

- [ ] **UNMET.** Real-time stability data for the specific formulation and container, over the
  claimed shelf life, showing that intact peptide content is maintained.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Accelerated stability data, with the conditions stated.
- [ ] **UNMET.** In-use stability data for the product after the container is first opened, where
  applicable.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Defined storage conditions, and evidence that they were maintained through
  transport, including any temperature excursion record.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Degradation product identification, since a peptide that has broken down does not
  simply become less potent, it becomes a mixture of other substances.
- [ ] **UNMET.** An expiry date supported by the data above rather than assigned by convention.

### F. Chain of custody

- [ ] **UNMET.** Documented custody from manufacturing site to final holder, with every
  intermediate party named.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Import documentation, where the material crossed a border.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Storage and transport temperature records across the whole chain, not only the
  final leg.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Evidence that no repackaging, re-labelling or reconstitution occurred outside a
  documented, controlled process.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Confirmation that the batch tested is the batch supplied. A certificate for a
  different batch is not evidence about the product in hand.

### G. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **UNMET.** A certificate of analysis issued by the manufacturer, naming the batch, the test
  methods, the specifications, and the results.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Independent third-party testing by a laboratory with no commercial relationship
  to the seller, covering identity and purity at minimum.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Laboratory accreditation status for that third party, verifiable independently.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Raw data behind the certificate, not only the summary sheet. A certificate is a
  claim; the underlying chromatogram and spectrum are the evidence.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Confirmation that the third-party sample was drawn from supplied stock rather than
  submitted by the seller.

### H. Regulatory and legal documentation

- [ ] **UNMET.** Statement of the regulatory status of the material in the jurisdiction of
  manufacture and the jurisdiction of supply, with dates and sources. See the Guide's Regulatory
  status section; several relevant items could not be confirmed as of 2026-07-19.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Confirmation of what the material is authorised to be used for, and by whom.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Any relevant regulator correspondence, warning letters, or import alerts affecting
  the manufacturer or supplier.

### I. Adverse event and traceability process

- [ ] **UNMET.** A documented process for reporting and investigating adverse events, with a named
  responsible person.
- [ ] **UNMET.** A recall process, with a demonstrated ability to trace a batch to its holders.
- [ ] **UNMET.** Retention samples held from each batch for independent re-testing.

## How a member can use this

If you are considering an unregulated product, this checklist is a set of things a supplier can
either produce or cannot. The most informative requests are usually the simplest ones:

- the full sequence confirmation and mass spectrometry raw data for **the batch you would receive**
- the real-time stability data for that formulation and container
- an independent third-party test, from a laboratory the seller does not pay, on stock drawn from
  supply rather than submitted by the seller

A supplier who cannot produce these is telling you something useful. So is a supplier who supplies
a certificate for a different batch, or a percentage without the underlying chromatogram.
