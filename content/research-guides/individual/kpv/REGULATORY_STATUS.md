# KPV Regulatory Status

**Status checked on 2026-07-19. This page must be re-verified before publication and re-checked
on any subsequent update.** Regulatory positions change, and one item on this page concerns a
review that had not yet taken place at the time of checking.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Standing principles for this page

1. Approval, authorisation, or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another. A
   position recorded here for the United States says nothing about any other country, and no
   inference across borders should be drawn from it.
2. Every statement below carries a jurisdiction, a date checked, and a source URL. A statement
   without all three does not belong on this page.
3. Where a status could not be confirmed by reading the primary document, that is stated
   explicitly rather than filled in by inference.
4. An advisory committee recommendation is advisory. It is not a final agency determination.

## Integrity note, read before using this page

Every United States statement below rests on secondary reporting from regulatory trade press,
not on the regulator's own pages. Direct fetches of fda.gov returned HTTP 404 and the
cacmap.fda.gov mirror returned HTTP 403, responses consistent with automated-access blocking
rather than with the pages not existing. The Federal Register notice could not be read in full
because the site redirected to an anti-bot interstitial and the public-inspection PDF was
unparseable.

A human reviewer must open the relevant regulator pages in a browser and confirm each statement
before publication.

## United States

### US-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** KPV is not an approved drug for any indication. No approval was found in any
retrieved source, and the agency's own process treats KPV as an unapproved bulk substance under
evaluation for pharmacy compounding, which is only coherent if it is unapproved.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.biospace.com/fda/fda-mulls-compounding-for-peptides-previously-flagged-over-safety-risks
**Verification:** Secondary trade reporting, retrieved and read. Not confirmed against a
regulator page.

### US-02. Category 2 placement under the 503A bulk drug substances framework, 2023

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** KPV was placed in Category 2 of the 503A bulk drug substances framework, the
category for substances raising significant safety risks pending further evaluation. As reported
by the trade press citing an agency statement of September 2023, a stated reason was an absence
of human exposure data for KPV.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.biospace.com/fda/fda-mulls-compounding-for-peptides-previously-flagged-over-safety-risks
**Verification:** Secondary trade reporting, retrieved and read. The underlying agency statement
was not retrieved directly.
**Why this is the most important line on the page:** the agency characterised the human data as
absent, not as thin or mixed. Nothing on a member-facing surface may describe KPV as having
human tolerability data while this stands.

### US-03. Reconsideration under way

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** The classification is being reconsidered as part of a broader review of peptides.
Reporting from the Regulatory Affairs Professionals Society, published 2026-04-16, states that
KPV-related bulk drug substances (free base and acetate) for wound healing and inflammatory
conditions were scheduled for review at the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of
July 23 to 24, 2026. Free base and acetate are treated as separate bulk substances in these
filings.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.raps.org/resource/fda-considers-adding-a-dozen-peptides-to-its-bulk-drug-compounding-list.html
**Verification:** Secondary regulatory reporting from a professional society, retrieved and read.

### US-04. The advisory committee review is PENDING, with no outcome

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** As of the research date, the advisory committee meeting concerning KPV was
scheduled for July 23 to 24, 2026, and had not occurred. No outcome is stated here. Multiple
search-engine summaries encountered during research asserted in the past tense that the
committee had met and that a position had been proposed. None of that could be verified, and the
two contemporaneous trade sources, both dated 2026-04-16, describe the meeting in the future
tense.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Sources:**
https://www.biospace.com/fda/fda-mulls-compounding-for-peptides-previously-flagged-over-safety-risks
https://www.raps.org/resource/fda-considers-adding-a-dozen-peptides-to-its-bulk-drug-compounding-list.html
**Verification:** Secondary reporting, retrieved and read. The briefing document that would
establish any pre-meeting agency position could not be retrieved, so no concern is attributed to
the agency anywhere in this folder.
**Handling rule:** describe the review as pending. State no result. When the record becomes
available, read it directly rather than a summary of it, and remember that a committee
recommendation is advisory rather than final.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

### US-06. Third-party characterisation of the evidence base

**Jurisdiction:** United States, public comment to the advisory committee.
**Statement:** The Partnership for Safe Medicines, in comments dated 2026-07-01 urging the
committee to reject compounding of these peptides, lists the substances under consideration as
BPC-157, KPV, TB-500, MOTS-C, Emideltide (DSIP), Semax, and Epitalon, and states that they are
being evaluated despite an absence of adequate human clinical trials establishing safety or
efficacy.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.safemedicines.org/2026/07/psm-pcac-comments.html
**Verification:** Retrieved and read. This is an advocacy submission, not a regulator finding,
and must be attributed as such. It independently corroborates the absence of human trials.

### US-07. Clinical trial registration

**Jurisdiction:** United States, ClinicalTrials.gov.
**Statement:** A direct query of the registry API for the term KPV returned zero studies. There
is no registered clinical trial of KPV, ongoing, completed, or terminated. A secondary query on
"lysine-proline-valine" returned only unrelated amino-acid studies matched on keyword.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.term=KPV&pageSize=50&fields=NCTId,BriefTitle,OverallStatus,InterventionName,Phase
**Verification:** Primary registry query, retrieved and read.

## Anti-doping

### AD-01. World Anti-Doping Agency

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency, international.
**Statement:** The status of KPV under the Prohibited List could not be confirmed. The current
Prohibited List PDF could not be parsed on two separate attempts, both returning empty content,
so neither the presence nor the absence of KPV on the List was established by direct reading.
The section covering non-approved substances prohibits, at all times, pharmacological substances
not addressed elsewhere on the List and with no current approval by any governmental regulatory
health authority for human therapeutic use, including substances in preclinical or clinical
development. Whether KPV falls within that section is an inference from the definition combined
with its unapproved status, and it is NOT a retrieved statement from the agency about KPV.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.wada-ama.org/en/prohibited-list
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED. The section wording came from a domain-restricted search summary,
not from reading the List itself.
**Handling rule:** the Guide states no anti-doping status for KPV. Athletes subject to testing
are directed to their own anti-doping authority. Do not publish the inference as a finding.

## Other jurisdictions

No regulatory statement was retrieved for the European Union, the United Kingdom, Canada,
Australia, Japan, or any other jurisdiction. No national regulator outside the United States was
consulted in this research session, and no trial registry outside ClinicalTrials.gov was
searched.

The absence of an entry here means the question was not investigated. It does not mean the
compound is unregulated, permitted, or approved anywhere. Approval or registration in one
jurisdiction is not approval in another, and the reverse also holds: an unconfirmed status
elsewhere may not be inferred from the United States position recorded above.

## Re-verification checklist for the reviewer

1. Open the regulator's bulk drug substances category pages directly and confirm US-01, US-02,
   and US-03.
2. Retrieve the advisory committee briefing document once published and check whether any
   concern circulating in secondary summaries is in fact the agency's stated position.
3. Confirm what actually happened at the July 23 to 24, 2026 meeting by reading the record, and
   remember that a committee recommendation is not a final agency determination.
4. Read Federal Register document 2026-07361 in full and confirm the verbatim substance list.
5. Read the current WADA Prohibited List directly and determine whether KPV is named.
6. Search at least one regulator outside the United States, so that the "other jurisdictions"
   section is a finding rather than a gap.
7. Update the dated header and the last_reviewed field together. A stale date on this page is
   itself a defect.
