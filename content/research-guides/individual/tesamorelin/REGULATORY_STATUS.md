# Regulatory Status: Tesamorelin

> **Status checked on 2026-07-19. This status must be re-verified before publication and
> before any member-facing surface using it goes live.**
>
> Regulatory status changes. Labels are revised, formulations are approved and withdrawn,
> indications are supplemented, and anti-doping lists are reissued annually. Every statement
> below is a snapshot of what was retrieved on the date shown, at the URL shown. A statement
> that was accurate on that date may not be accurate now.

Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## The principle that governs every statement below

**Approval or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another.** A product
approved by one national regulator has no status in a country whose regulator has not
approved it. Nothing in this document may be read across borders.

Two further limits apply specifically to this compound, and both matter more here than for
any other compound in this series, because tesamorelin genuinely holds an approval:

1. **An approval attaches to a specific licensed product, not to a molecule name.** Material
   sold under the name tesamorelin by a seller outside the licensed supply chain is not the
   approved product and carries none of its regulatory status.
2. **An approval attaches to a specific indication in a specific population.** It does not
   extend to any other use, however mechanistically related that use may seem.

## Part 1. United States

### US-01. Approval status and approved indication

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.

**Statement:** Tesamorelin is FDA-approved. The current label states an initial United States
approval of 2010. The approved indication is the reduction of excess abdominal fat in
HIV-infected adult patients with lipodystrophy. The population is therefore narrow and
specific: HIV-infected adult patients with lipodystrophy. It is not approved for adults
without HIV, not approved for general obesity, and not approved for body recomposition,
athletic, or anti-aging use. FDA labeling classifies it as a growth hormone-releasing factor
analog.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

**Source:** S1. https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=3d783378-b02d-4f19-99dd-0fc91a042224

**Status:** VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

**Note:** The approval **year** is verified from current labeling. A specific day and month
within 2010 appeared only in secondary sources and is not verified. The original approval
package PDFs on the FDA accessdata site returned HTTP 404 on all three attempts (S15). Do not
publish a specific approval date.

### US-02. Limitations of Use printed on the approved label

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.

**Statement:** The approved label carries three explicit Limitations of Use. First, long-term
cardiovascular safety has not been established. Second, the product is not indicated for
weight loss management. Third, there is no evidence that it improves compliance with
antiretroviral therapy.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

**Source:** S2. https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=839334d3-8c1d-4c26-9036-2ab524a6ea75

**Status:** VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

**Note:** The second limitation is the single most consequential regulatory line in this
Guide. It is the regulator, on the approved label, stating that this product is not a weight
loss product. It should appear on the member-facing surface, and it should not be paraphrased
into something softer.

### US-03. Contraindications carried on the approved label

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.

**Statement:** The label lists absolute contraindications including disruption of the
hypothalamic-pituitary axis arising from specified causes, active malignancy, known
hypersensitivity to tesamorelin or to a listed excipient, and pregnancy. The pregnancy
contraindication is stated on the basis that there is no benefit in pregnancy and there is
potential for fetal harm, with supporting animal findings cited in the labeling.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

**Source:** S2. https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=839334d3-8c1d-4c26-9036-2ab524a6ea75

**Status:** VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

**Note:** The animal findings supporting the pregnancy contraindication must be described as
animal findings in the same sentence wherever they appear. No exposure figures are reproduced
in this Guide, per the dosing exclusion.

### US-04. Current formulation approval, March 2025

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.

**Statement:** On 25 March 2025, FDA approved a supplemental Biologics License Application
for a new formulation of tesamorelin, commercialized as EGRIFTA WR. The indication was not
expanded. It remains the reduction of excess abdominal fat in HIV-infected adult patients
with lipodystrophy. This is a formulation change, not new therapeutic territory.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

**Sources:** S4 (manufacturer press release, corroborating context only).
https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/03/25/3049233/0/en/Theratechnologies-Receives-FDA-Approval-for-EGRIFTA-WR-Tesamorelin-F8-to-Treat-Excess-Visceral-Abdominal-Fat-in-Adults-with-HIV-and-Lipodystrophy.html
and S2 (approved labeling for the current formulation).
https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=839334d3-8c1d-4c26-9036-2ab524a6ea75

**Status:** VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

**Note:** A recent approval date can be read by a member as a recent expansion of what the
product is permitted to treat. It is not. Across fifteen years and multiple supplements the
indication has not widened.

### US-05. Licensed products currently listed

**Jurisdiction:** United States, product registry hosted by the National Library of Medicine.

**Statement:** Two tesamorelin products are currently listed, EGRIFTA SV and EGRIFTA WR, both
from a single manufacturer, Theratechnologies Inc. Any product presented as tesamorelin that
is not traceable to that licensed supply chain sits outside the approved product entirely,
and none of the human evidence summarised in this Guide was generated with such material.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

**Source:** S3. https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/search.cfm?labeltype=all&query=tesamorelin

**Status:** VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

**Note:** This is the most useful checkable fact in the Guide for a member trying to
distinguish the approved product from grey-market material. The registry stays current and
this document does not, so the member should be given the check itself, not only the answer.

## Part 2. Sport and anti-doping

This is a jurisdictional layer that applies to athletes under anti-doping rules across
countries, independently of any national drug approval.

### AD-01. World Anti-Doping Agency Prohibited List

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency, applying to athletes under anti-doping
jurisdiction.

**Statement:** Secondary and vendor-adjacent sources retrieved this session state that growth
hormone-releasing hormone and its analogs, with tesamorelin named among them, fall under
section S2 of the Prohibited List, covering peptide hormones, growth factors, related
substances and mimetics, and are prohibited at all times, both in and out of competition.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19, primary retrieval attempted and failed.

**Source:** S11. https://www.wada-ama.org/en/prohibited-list

**Status:** **UNVERIFIED.** The primary Prohibited List document was not successfully
retrieved. One mirror returned HTTP 403.

**Note:** The direction of this claim is very unlikely to be wrong, and that is precisely why
it must not be published unverified. A statement that looks obviously correct is a statement a
reviewer will wave through, and an athlete may act on it. The section reference and the
current-year wording are both unconfirmed. A human must confirm against the current-year
Prohibited List document directly before any anti-doping statement appears on a member-facing
surface. See CLAIM_TABLE.md REG-08 and prohibited claim P-14.

## Part 3. Other jurisdictions

**No status was retrieved for any jurisdiction other than the United States.**

No European, United Kingdom, Canadian, Australian, or other national regulator's position on
tesamorelin was retrieved this session. This Guide therefore states nothing, in either
direction, about the status of tesamorelin in any country other than the United States.

The absence of a retrieved status is not evidence that a product is unapproved somewhere, and
it is equally not evidence that it is approved. Both readings are unsupported. A member
outside the United States should be told plainly that this Guide's regulatory section covers
one jurisdiction only, and that their own country's regulator is the authority for them.

**To be added by a human reviewer before any non-US member-facing publication:** the position
of the relevant national regulator for each market the Guide is published into.
