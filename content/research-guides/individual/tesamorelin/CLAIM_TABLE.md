# Claim Table: Tesamorelin

The evidence spine of this Guide. One row per discrete claim. Every claim is graded on its
own merit, never by the reputation of the compound as a whole, and never by the fact that the
compound holds an approval. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
|---|---|
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

Default grade is D or G unless a specific retrieved human study supports better.

## The population rule that governs this whole table

Tesamorelin is the unusual compound in this series. It has a real FDA approval and a real,
replicated randomized human evidence base. The honest reading is still strongly cautionary,
for one reason: **the evidence is good, and it is about a population that almost certainly
does not describe the member reading this Guide.**

Every randomized trial retrieved this session that measured body composition was conducted in
HIV-infected adults on antiretroviral therapy with lipodystrophy or abdominal fat
accumulation. The single retrieved randomized trial in a population without HIV did not
measure body composition at all.

So the rule for this table is: **a grade earned in HIV-associated lipodystrophy does not
transfer to anyone else.** Every row below states the population it applies to. Any
member-facing surface that drops the population label converts a grade B claim into a grade G
claim without changing a single word of the sentence. That silent conversion is the primary
failure mode this Guide exists to prevent.

The second rule follows from the approval itself. **A grade earned by the licensed product
does not transfer to material sold under the same name by an unregulated seller.** None of
the evidence below was generated with such material.

## 9. Summary of grade distribution

| Grade | Count | Where |
|---|---|---|
| A | 22 | Identity, regulatory and label facts, and label safety warnings |
| B | 17 | Effect claims within HIV-associated lipodystrophy, human pharmacology, and sourced negative findings |
| C | 1 | Short-term glycemic neutrality in type 2 diabetes |
| D | 0 | No preclinical evidence retrieved |
| E | 0 | No claim rests on manufacturer-reported effect data |
| F | 0 | Not applicable |
| G | 10 | Anti-doping status, compounding status, enforcement claims, category quality risk, and every question about populations outside the approved indication |
| PROHIBITED | 21 | Section 8, plus NON-04 and SAFE-12 |
| Not graded | 1 | MECH-06, preclinical, because nothing was retrieved to grade |

The distribution is the finding. This compound has a large number of grade A regulatory facts
and a solid block of grade B effect claims confined to one narrow population, and almost
nothing in between that population and the member reading the Guide. A Guide that presented
only the A and B rows, without the population labels and without section 8, would be
accurate line by line and profoundly misleading as a whole.
