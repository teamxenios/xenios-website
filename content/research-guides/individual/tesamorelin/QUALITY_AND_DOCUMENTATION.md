# Tesamorelin: quality and documentation

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Current position, stated plainly

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation for any product containing this compound.**

Every item on the checklist below is currently unmet. Not partially met, not pending review, not
verbally assured. Unmet. No supplier has satisfied any of these requirements to Xenios, and no
document has been received and verified.

This document exists so a member can see the full standard and the full gap, rather than take a
general reassurance from us. If any item moves to met, it moves with a date, a document reference,
and the name of the person who verified it, recorded in the change log at the end of this file.

## Why this matters more than usual for this specific compound

Tesamorelin is genuinely FDA-approved in the United States, and only two licensed products are listed
on DailyMed, both from a single manufacturer (checked 2026-07-19,
https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/search.cfm?labeltype=all&query=tesamorelin).

That creates a specific documentation hazard. The phrase "FDA-approved" can travel with material that
is not the approved product. An approval is a statement about a licensed product made under a
licensed process, not a statement about a molecule name printed on a vial. None of the human evidence
described in the Guide was generated with material outside that licensed supply chain, so none of it
can be assumed to describe such material.

[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check] The general category of unregulated
bulk peptides carries documented risks of sterility failures, contamination, incorrect or variable
active content, and counterfeiting. A tesamorelin-specific enforcement claim referenced in a
secondary source could not be verified against the primary FDA document in this research session, so
no such claim is made here.

## How Xenios reads a supplier certificate

A certificate of analysis is a claim, not proof. Xenios treats a certificate as unverified until:

- the lot number on the document matches the lot number on the container received,
- the testing laboratory is named and independently identifiable,
- the methods are named specifically rather than described generically,
- the results are numerical values against stated specifications rather than pass or fail statements,
- a named person with a stated role signed it, and
- an independent laboratory with no commercial relationship to the supplier reproduced the identity,
  purity, and content results on the same lot.

A certificate failing any of these is recorded as received and unverified. It does not move any item
above to met.
