# Contradictions: Tesamorelin

Where sources disagree, where mechanism plausibility outruns the evidence, and where
commercial claims conflict with the literature. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

There are genuine contradictions in this record. Four are documented below. The second is the
most consequential, and a member-facing Guide that does not handle it explicitly will
mislead by omission.

---

## 1. Whether tesamorelin impairs glucose control

**Position A.** The FDA-approved label carries "Glucose Intolerance or Diabetes Mellitus" as
an explicit Warnings and Precautions heading. This is consistent with the known tendency of
growth hormone axis stimulation to affect glucose handling. Source: S2, retrieved 2026-07-19.

**Position B.** The 2026 meta-analysis of five randomized controlled trials reported that the
effect was achieved without perturbation of glucose (S8). The dedicated randomized trial in
adults with type 2 diabetes found no significant between-group difference in fasting glucose,
HbA1c or overall diabetes control at twelve weeks (S9).

**Assessment.** Both are probably correct, at different timescales and in different
populations, and the disagreement is more apparent than real.

The trial-level null results are short. The type 2 diabetes trial ran twelve weeks. The
pooled randomized trials largely ran six to twelve months. All of them are group-mean
comparisons, and a group mean can conceal individual susceptibility entirely. The label
warning is the conservative position, it is FDA's own position on the approved product, and
it is the position this Guide adopts.

**How the Guide must handle it.** State both, in this order. Short-term randomized data has
not shown a group-level glycemic penalty, FDA nonetheless requires the warning, and long-term
data does not exist. The null findings are not clearance and must never be written as
clearance. See CLAIM_TABLE.md SAFE-05, NON-02 and prohibited claim P-09.

## 2. Whether the approval and the trial evidence support general body composition use

This is the central contradiction in the record and the reason this Guide exists in its
current shape.

**Position A, as circulated commercially.** Retailer, telehealth and peptide vendor pages
surfaced during searching frame tesamorelin as a clinically proven peptide for visceral fat,
with an implication of general applicability. Several assert that smaller studies in
metabolic syndrome and abdominal obesity show similar visceral fat reduction in people
without HIV.

**Position B, as the retrieved literature and labeling actually stand.**

- Every randomized trial retrieved that measured body composition was conducted exclusively
  in HIV-infected adults with lipodystrophy or abdominal fat accumulation, on antiretroviral
  therapy (S5, S6, S7).
- The 2026 meta-analysis scopes the entire pooled evidence base to HIV-associated
  lipodystrophy in its own title (S8).
- The single randomized trial retrieved in a population without HIV did not report body
  composition outcomes at all (S9).
- The FDA label prints that the product is not indicated for weight loss management (S2).
- The meta-analysis found no significant change in body mass index and no significant change
  in subcutaneous fat (S8).

**Assessment.** Position B is correct, and Position A is not supported by anything retrieved
this session. A specific search was run for non-HIV human body composition evidence. It
returned no primary trial from PubMed or from the trial registry (S10). Every result was a
retailer, telehealth or peptide-vendor page, all of which are excluded source types under
this brief. The assertion about smaller studies in metabolic syndrome and abdominal obesity
could not be traced to a single retrievable trial.

This is a grade G marketing claim wearing the syntax of evidence, which is what makes it
effective. "Smaller studies show" is a sentence shape that a reader parses as a citation even
when no citation follows.

**How the Guide must handle it.** Explicitly, by name, not by omission. A member who has
already read the vendor version needs the specific counter, not a general caution. The
counter is: the trials exist, they are good, and they are all in one narrow population, and
the one trial outside that population measured nothing relevant. See CLAIM_TABLE.md NON-01
through NON-05 and prohibited claims P-04 and P-05.

## 3. Where mechanism plausibility outruns the evidence

Not a disagreement between sources, but a gap between what the mechanism suggests and what
has been demonstrated. It is recorded here because it produces the same failure as a
contradiction: a reader fills the gap with an assumption.

**What is demonstrated in humans.** Target engagement is real rather than hypothetical. A
pivotal randomized trial measured a substantial rise in IGF-I relative to placebo (S5), and
visceral fat reduction was replicated across trials and in pooled analysis in the studied
population (S5, S6, S7, S8).

**What is not demonstrated.** That any of it changes a clinical outcome. Every retrieved
endpoint is a surrogate: imaging-measured fat compartments, blood lipids, IGF-1, and hepatic
fat fraction. No cardiovascular outcome trial was found. The FDA label prints that long-term
cardiovascular safety has not been established (S2). The investigators on the liver fat trial
describe their own findings as preliminary and state that further study is needed to
determine clinical importance (S7).

**Assessment.** Visceral fat is a cardiometabolic risk marker, and reducing a risk marker is
a reasonable thing to hope will matter. It has not been shown to matter here. The label
carries the printed limitation precisely because that step has not been taken. Anyone using
this compound for cardiometabolic reasons is acting on a surrogate, and should know that they
are.

**How the Guide must handle it.** Keep the word "proposed" on the mechanism, and never let a
surrogate endpoint be described in outcome language. See CLAIM_TABLE.md MECH-03, MECH-04,
EFF-07, SAFE-08 and prohibited claims P-06 and P-08.

## 4. Where the approval itself is in tension with what it appears to authorise

**The tension.** Tesamorelin genuinely is FDA-approved. That is a fact, and this Guide states
it plainly. The approval nonetheless attaches to a much narrower object than the phrase
"FDA-approved peptide" implies:

- To specific licensed products from a single manufacturer, of which exactly two are
  currently listed on the product registry (S3).
- For one indication, the reduction of excess abdominal fat in HIV-infected adult patients
  with lipodystrophy (S1, S2).
- With three printed Limitations of Use, one of which states the product is not indicated for
  weight loss management (S2).

Across fifteen years and multiple supplements, including the March 2025 approval of a new
formulation, the indication has not widened (S1, S2, S4).

**Assessment.** This is not a contradiction between two sources. It is a contradiction
between a true statement and its ordinary implicature. "FDA-approved" carries an implicit
promise that something has been evaluated and permitted, and that promise does not survive
transfer to a different product, a different population, or a different purpose. Because the
underlying statement is true, the usual defence, checking whether the claim is accurate, does
not catch it.

That makes tesamorelin the most exploitable compound in this series. For a never-approved
peptide, the correction is simple. Here, the correction requires walking a member through
what an approval is and is not.

**How the Guide must handle it.** The approval and the indication must appear together, in
the same visual block, never with the indication further down the page. Give the member the
product registry check directly so they can verify a product's status themselves rather than
taking a conclusion on trust. See CLAIM_TABLE.md REG-01, REG-02, QUAL-01, QUAL-05 and
prohibited claim P-02.

## 5. Preclinical against human findings

No contradiction can be assessed, because no preclinical primary studies were retrieved this
session. The preclinical evidence table is deliberately empty rather than filled from memory.

Given the volume of randomized human evidence available for the approved indication,
preclinical data is low value for this compound, and its absence does not weaken the Guide.
Nothing here may be written from background knowledge. If preclinical material is added in a
later revision, the species or model must appear in the same sentence as every finding.

The one animal-derived statement anywhere in this Guide is the fetal harm basis for the
pregnancy contraindication, which is described on the label as an animal finding and is
reported in CLAIM_TABLE.md SAFE-09 with that framing intact and with no figures reproduced.

## 6. Jurisdictional differences

No genuine jurisdictional conflict was established, because only one jurisdiction was
successfully verified.

United States regulatory status is verified from current labeling (S1, S2, S3). No other
national regulator's status for tesamorelin was retrieved this session. The absence of a
retrieved status is not evidence of absence of approval, and the Guide states nothing either
way about any other country. See REGULATORY_STATUS.md.

The anti-doping position is a separate jurisdictional layer that applies across countries to
athletes under anti-doping rules. It could not be verified. Secondary sources indicate a
prohibited classification, and the primary list document was not retrieved (S11). This is
recorded as an unresolved verification gap, not as a contradiction, and no anti-doping
statement may be published until a human confirms the current-year list. See CLAIM_TABLE.md
REG-08 and prohibited claim P-14.

## 7. The three facts that must be presented together

Not a contradiction, but a set of interlocking findings that are each individually survivable
and jointly decisive. Presenting them in separate sections would let a reader miss the
connection, which is how the record would most plausibly be misread in good faith.

1. **The reported benefit was lost after discontinuation.** In the trial with a safety extension, the
   improvement seen over six months was rapidly lost in patients switched to placebo (S6).
2. **Therefore any use is effectively open-ended**, which means open-ended elevation of
   IGF-1, and the label states directly that the effects of prolonged IGF-1 elevation are
   unknown (S2).
3. **The label separately warns on neoplasms** and makes active malignancy an absolute
   contraindication (S2). This is a growth-signal compound, so the warning is mechanistically
   coherent rather than boilerplate.

Each of the three is reported in the record. The connection between them is not stated by any
single source, and it is the most important thing a member could take from this Guide. It
should appear in one place, with all three sources attached.
