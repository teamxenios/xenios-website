# Tesamorelin: editorial review and sign-off gate

**AI drafts never publish automatically.** The Guide, FAQ, and quality documentation for this compound
were drafted by an AI system from a research record assembled on 2026-07-19. They are drafts. Nothing
in this folder may reach a member-facing surface until every box below is checked by a named human
and the founder sign-off is recorded.

Every box starts unchecked. A box may only be checked by the named reviewer for that section, with a
date. An unchecked box blocks publication regardless of schedule pressure.

Files in scope: `GUIDE_DRAFT.md`, `FAQ_DRAFT.md`, `QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md`.

## 1. Content safety

- [ ] No dosing anywhere in any file. No amounts, mg, mcg, IU, mL, units, concentrations, frequency,
      timing, cycle length, titration, loading, stacking, reconstitution, injection technique, or
      route of administration.
- [ ] Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact required sentence appears: "Dosing and
      administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides."
- [ ] No treatment directions, protocols, or regimens. Nothing a reader could follow as instructions.
- [ ] No guaranteed outcomes. The words "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures", "eliminates", and
      "reverses" do not appear as claims about effect.
- [ ] Effect language is restricted to "has been studied for", "reported in", "investigated as", and
      "associated with in [named population]".
- [ ] No unsupported safety claims. "Safe", "well tolerated", and "no side effects" do not appear as
      general statements of fact.
- [ ] The safety section is framed as categories of concern and never as reassurance, and states that
      absence from the list is not evidence of absence.
- [ ] No medical advice, no diagnosis, no self-treatment framing.
- [ ] No sourcing or acquisition guidance for the compound anywhere in any file.
- [ ] "Research use only" framing is not used anywhere to imply human benefit.

## 2. Preclinical and species labelling

- [ ] Every preclinical finding carries its species or model in the same sentence as the finding.
- [ ] No bare mechanism sentence reads as established human fact.
- [ ] The empty preclinical evidence table is confirmed accurate. No preclinical primary studies were
      retrieved, and none were added from memory.
- [ ] The one animal statement carried in the Guide (label-reported hydrocephalus finding supporting
      the pregnancy contraindication) is confirmed to be labelled as an animal finding, reported as a
      label statement, and carries no exposure amounts.
- [ ] The proposed mechanism section is explicitly labelled as proposed, separates what is
      demonstrated in humans from what is hypothesized, and notes the species of the supporting data.

## 3. Citation integrity

- [ ] Every citation in every file was independently re-verified by a human against the live URL.
- [ ] Every PMID, DOI, author list, year, and journal name was checked character by character against
      the source record. No identifier was accepted on the AI draft's word.
- [ ] No trial registration number (NCT identifier) appears anywhere. The registration records could
      not be parsed in the research session, so none were recorded. Confirm none were reintroduced.
- [ ] No FDA warning letter or enforcement claim specific to tesamorelin appears as fact. All
      attempted retrievals failed and nothing enforcement-related is asserted.
- [ ] Every source is marked VERIFIED 2026-07-19 with the exact retrieved URL, or is listed in the
      attempted-and-not-retrieved section and supports no claim.
- [ ] Every line relying on background knowledge rather than a retrieved source carries the exact
      marker: [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check].
- [ ] The three flagged verification gaps are resolved or the associated statements are removed: the
      WADA classification, the sermorelin marketing status, and the compounding status under section
      503A.
- [ ] Effect sizes quoted in the Guide match the PubMed abstract records they are attributed to.
- [ ] The Guide states clearly that full texts were not retrieved and that evidence entries are drawn
      from abstract-level records.

## 4. Claim grades

- [ ] Every substantive claim carries a grade, and grades are applied per claim, never to the
      compound as a whole.
- [ ] Grade A claims are limited to statements printed on the FDA-approved label.
- [ ] Grade B claims are limited to the randomized human trial and meta-analysis findings, and each
      is explicitly restricted to the HIV-associated lipodystrophy population in which it was
      generated.
- [ ] Every general-population body-composition claim is graded G, unverified.
- [ ] No claim is graded higher than the retrieved evidence supports. Conservative grading was applied
      where there was doubt.
- [ ] The vendor claim that comparable visceral fat results exist in metabolic syndrome or general
      abdominal obesity is graded G and is presented as unverified marketing, not evidence.

## 5. Prohibited claims

- [ ] No claim presents the FDA approval as extending to general body composition, longevity,
      athletic, or anti-aging use.
- [ ] No claim presents the FDA approval as extending to material outside the licensed supply chain.
- [ ] No claim presents tesamorelin as a weight-loss product. The label's own limitation is stated.
- [ ] No claim presents visceral fat reduction as reduced cardiovascular risk or reduced clinical
      events.
- [ ] No claim transfers evidence from tesamorelin to other GHRH analogs or growth hormone
      secretagogues, or to recombinant human growth hormone.
- [ ] No claim implies Xenios supplies, endorses, or has quality-verified this compound.
- [ ] Prohibited-grade claims are absent from every member-facing surface in this folder.

## 9. Honest-verdict integrity

- [ ] The one-minute summary states the evidence verdict honestly, including that the evidence is
      strong for a population the reader is unlikely to belong to.
- [ ] The absence of randomized body-composition evidence outside HIV-associated lipodystrophy is
      stated as a finding, not softened or padded.
- [ ] The reversal of effect on discontinuation is presented in the summary, the evidence section, and

- [ ] Contradictions are presented with both positions and an assessment, not resolved silently.
