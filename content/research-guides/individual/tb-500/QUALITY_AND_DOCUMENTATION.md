# TB-500: quality and documentation

This document sets out the quality documentation that **should** exist for any product
containing this compound, and records the honest current state.

**Current state, stated plainly: Xenios has not confirmed any item on this checklist for
TB-500. Every item below is unmet.** No supplier has been assessed, no batch has been tested,
no certificate has been independently verified, and no chain of custody has been established.

This document is a standard, not a claim of compliance. Nothing here should be read as evidence
that a compliant product exists.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

---

## Why this document exists separately from the evidence

Two questions are commonly collapsed into one, and they should not be.

The first question is whether the compound does anything. For TB-500 the answer is that human
evidence is absent (see `GUIDE_DRAFT.md`).

The second question is whether the material in a given vial is what the label says, at the purity
claimed, free of contamination, and traceable to a manufacturer who can be held to account. That
question is independent of the first. A compound could in principle be effective and still be
sold in vials that contain something else, and an unregulated supply chain provides no mechanism
for a buyer to tell the difference.

Two documented facts make this concrete for TB-500 specifically:

- A 2012 analytical study reported that a non-natural synthesis impurity was detectable in blood
  after administration of a TB-500-containing product, confirming that marketed material carries
  manufacturing-derived contaminants (PMID 23084823,
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23084823/, VERIFIED 2026-07-19).
- The same paper notes that products sold online claim to contain either a synthetic acetylated
  fragment of thymosin beta-4 or the full-length protein itself. Because vendors use the two
  names interchangeably, a product name does not establish which molecule is claimed to be
  present, let alone which is actually present.

A 2015 analysis of illegal peptide preparations covered 25 peptides seized in Europe or
identified in underground forums, and stated that counterfeit and illegal peptide substances
could imply severe health threats (PMID 26003685,
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26003685/, VERIFIED 2026-07-19). **Limit:** the record available
to us does not state whether TB-500 was among those 25 peptides, and reports no per-sample
mislabelling rate. No mislabelling percentage is attached to TB-500 anywhere in this packet.

## The checklist

Every box is unchecked. A supplier would have to satisfy all of them, with documentation Xenios
can independently verify, before any product containing this compound could be considered.

### 1. Identity

- [ ] **Molecule stated unambiguously.** The document names either the 7-amino-acid acetylated
      fragment (Ac-LKKTETQ, thymosin beta-4 residues 17-23) or the full-length 43-amino-acid
      thymosin beta-4 protein. The names "TB-500" and "thymosin beta-4" are not treated as
      interchangeable anywhere in the documentation.
- [ ] **Sequence confirmed analytically**, not merely asserted, by a method capable of confirming
      the amino acid sequence.
- [ ] **Mass confirmed** by mass spectrometry, with the observed mass matching the theoretical
      mass for the stated molecule including the acetyl group.
- [ ] **Acetylation confirmed** at the stated position.
- [ ] **Counter-ion and salt form stated**, since the salt form affects the actual peptide
      content of the material.
- [ ] **Registry identifiers provided** where they exist, so the substance can be looked up
      independently rather than trusted on the label.
- [ ] **Raw analytical data available**, not only a summary table. A chromatogram and spectrum
      that a reviewer can examine.

*Current state: unmet. No identity documentation has been obtained or verified by Xenios for any
TB-500 material. Note also that a specific FDA substance-registry record for TB-500 could not be
retrieved on 2026-07-19, so even the public identity record was not captured.*

### 2. Purity and impurity profile

- [ ] **Purity determined by a validated, separation-based method**, with the method and its
      validation stated.
- [ ] **Named impurities listed individually**, not summarised as a single purity percentage.
      Peptide synthesis produces characteristic related substances, and the identity of each
      matters more than the total.
- [ ] **Synthesis-derived impurities specifically addressed**, given that a non-natural synthesis
      impurity has been documented in TB-500-containing material.
- [ ] **Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
- [ ] **Residual reagents from synthesis quantified**, including any coupling and deprotection
      chemistry residues.
- [ ] **Heavy metals and elemental impurities quantified.**
- [ ] **Water content determined**, since it affects the true peptide content of the material.
- [ ] **Peptide content stated** as distinct from purity. These are different numbers and are
      routinely conflated.

*Current state: unmet. No purity or impurity data has been obtained or verified by Xenios.*

### 3. Sterility and microbial control

- [ ] **Sterility testing performed** by a recognised compendial method, on the finished
      container, for any preparation intended to bypass the digestive tract.
- [ ] **Bioburden controlled and documented** at each relevant manufacturing stage.
- [ ] **Sterilisation or aseptic process validated and documented**, with the method stated.
- [ ] **Container closure integrity demonstrated.**
- [ ] **Manufacturing environment qualified**, with the facility classification stated and
      current.

*Current state: unmet. No sterility documentation has been obtained or verified by Xenios.*

### 4. Endotoxin

- [ ] **Bacterial endotoxin testing performed** by a recognised method, on the finished product
      rather than on the raw peptide only.
- [ ] **A specified limit defined and justified**, appropriate to the intended use.
- [ ] **Result reported per container** rather than as a generic pass statement.
- [ ] **Test interference (inhibition and enhancement) assessed**, since peptides can interfere
      with endotoxin assays.

*Current state: unmet. No endotoxin data has been obtained or verified by Xenios.*

### 5. Stability

- [ ] **Stability studies conducted** on the actual formulation and container, not on an
      analogous product.
- [ ] **Storage conditions established from data**, with the stability-indicating method stated.
- [ ] **Degradation products identified and quantified** over the study period.
- [ ] **Shelf life justified by data**, not assigned by convention.
- [ ] **In-use stability established** for any product presented as multi-use.
- [ ] **Shipping and excursion stability documented**, including exposure to temperature
      excursions in transit.

*Current state: unmet. No stability data has been obtained or verified by Xenios.*

### 6. Chain of custody and traceability

- [ ] **Actual manufacturing site named**, not a distributor or a brand.
- [ ] **Manufacturing site inspected or independently audited**, with the report available.
- [ ] **Raw material sources documented**, including the origin of the starting materials.
- [ ] **Batch and lot numbering traceable** from finished container back to synthesis batch.
- [ ] **Custody documented at every transfer**, including storage and transport conditions at
      each step.
- [ ] **Recall procedure defined and demonstrated**, with a named accountable person.
- [ ] **Complaint and adverse event intake process defined**, with a named accountable person.

*Current state: unmet. No manufacturer has been identified, audited, or verified by Xenios for
this compound.*

### 7. Certificate of Analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **A COA exists for the specific batch supplied**, matching the lot number on the container
      in the member's hand, not a representative or example certificate.
- [ ] **The COA states the test methods used**, not only results.
- [ ] **The COA is issued by a named laboratory**, with the laboratory's accreditation status
      stated and verifiable.
- [ ] **The testing laboratory is independent** of the manufacturer and the seller, with no
      shared ownership.
- [ ] **Xenios has independently commissioned testing** of a sample drawn from supplied stock,
      at a laboratory of Xenios' choosing.
- [ ] **Independent results match the supplier COA** on identity, purity, sterility, and
      endotoxin.
- [ ] **Raw data underlying the certificate is available for review**, not just the summary.
- [ ] **A retention sample is held** from every batch, by both the supplier and Xenios.

*Current state: unmet. No COA has been received, no independent testing has been commissioned,
and no retention samples are held.*

### 8. Regulatory and labelling

- [ ] **Regulatory status confirmed for every jurisdiction** in which a product would be offered,
      dated, with a source document.
- [ ] **US regulatory position verified directly against fda.gov by a human.** This is currently
      impossible from our research tooling. Every fda.gov page requested on 2026-07-19 returned
      an error, so no FDA claim of any kind is verified in this packet.
- [ ] **Anti-doping status disclosed on any member-facing surface.** TB-500 is named on the WADA
      Prohibited List as a prohibited growth factor, prohibited at all times
      (https://www.usada.org/athlete-advisory/2018-prohibited-list-summary-of-major-changes/,
      verified 2026-07-19). Any member who competes must be told this before anything else.
- [ ] **Labelling states the exact molecule**, resolving the fragment versus full-length protein
      ambiguity on the container itself.
- [ ] **No efficacy claim appears on any label, page, or supporting material**, consistent with
      the absence of human evidence.

*Current state: unmet, and one item (direct FDA verification) is currently blocked pending a
human check.*

## What a member can do with this document

If you are evaluating a supplier's claims, this checklist is the standard to hold them to. Three
questions separate a substantive answer from a marketing one.

1. Can you show me the certificate for **this lot number**, not an example certificate?
2. Which independent laboratory performed the testing, and can I verify their accreditation?
3. Can I see the raw chromatogram and spectrum, rather than a summary table?

A supplier who cannot answer all three is not offering you verified material, whatever the
website says.
