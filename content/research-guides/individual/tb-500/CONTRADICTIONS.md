# TB-500 Contradictions

Where the sources disagree, where the preclinical picture and the human picture diverge,
where mechanism plausibility outruns evidence, and where commercial claims conflict with the
literature. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

There are genuine contradictions here, and one of them is the most important thing in the
whole Guide.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## 1. Whether thymosin beta-4 trial evidence applies to TB-500

**This is the central contradiction. It is not a nuance, it is a correction the Guide must
make explicitly.**

**Position A, held by commercial and secondary sources.** Human trials of thymosin beta-4
(dry eye, neurotrophic keratopathy, venous and pressure ulcers, acute myocardial infarction)
establish that TB-500 is effective and tolerated.

**Position B, supported by the retrieved primary records.** Every one of those trials
administered full-length thymosin beta-4, a 43-amino-acid protein, or a formulated
investigational product built from it such as RGN-259 or NL005 (S04, S05, S06, S08). TB-500
is a seven-amino-acid acetylated fragment, Ac-LKKTETQ (S01, S09). The 2026 sports medicine
review states plainly that human orthopaedic data are lacking (S03). The only registered
human study of the fragment has posted no results (S09).

**Assessment.** Position B is correct. Position A is a category error, and it is made
routinely. It compounds three separate substitutions at once: a different molecule, a
different route (an eye or wound surface rather than systemic exposure), and a different
indication (an ocular or dermal condition rather than musculoskeletal recovery). Any one of
those would break the inference. Together they make it untenable.

The Guide must make this distinction early, name it as the reason so much published material
about "TB-500" is misleading, and never soften it.

## 2. Whether TB-500 itself is the active molecule

**Position A.** TB-500 corresponds to the active site of thymosin beta-4 and therefore drives
actin binding, cell migration, angiogenesis and tissue repair. This is the standard framing,
consistent with the description of the LKKTETQ segment as the parent protein's actin-binding
active site (S01) and with the 2026 review's summary that both the parent and the derivative
promoted angiogenesis and tissue repair in preclinical models (S03).

**Position B.** In a 2024 study, the parent TB-500 compound showed no wound-healing activity
in cultured fibroblasts while its metabolite Ac-LKKTE did. The authors concluded that
previously reported wound-healing activity may belong to the metabolite rather than to the
parent form (S02).

**Assessment.** Unresolved, and important. This is a single in-vitro finding inside an
analytical-chemistry paper rather than a dedicated pharmacology study, so it should not be
treated as settled or presented as a refutation. But it directly undercuts the simplest
version of the TB-500 story, and it should be surfaced rather than smoothed over. It also
means preclinical results obtained with TB-500 may reflect metabolite activity, which
complicates any read-across to humans even before the species gap is considered.

Practical consequence for the Guide: the phrase "the active fragment" should not be used
without this caveat attached.

## 3. Where mechanism plausibility outruns evidence

There is no source-versus-source conflict here. The conflict is between how confidently the
mechanism is described in circulation and what has actually been shown.

- The mechanistic account (actin sequestration, cell migration, angiogenesis, matrix
  remodelling) is described for the parent protein, in analytical and review literature
  (S01, S03).
- The retrieved model systems are rat in-vivo metabolism, human serum and in-vitro enzyme
  systems, cultured human fibroblasts, and equine doping-control matrices (S01, S02).
- No human mechanistic confirmation was retrieved. No human pharmacokinetic data was
  retrieved.

**Assessment.** The mechanism is a proposal with preclinical support for a related molecule.
It is not a demonstrated human effect. This gap is where most inaccurate writing about this
compound originates, because a plausible mechanism reads like a result. Every mechanism
sentence in the Guide must carry its species or model, and the Guide should say directly that
plausibility is not evidence.

## 4. Which anti-doping section prohibits TB-500

**Position A.** Section S2, specifically S2.3 growth factors. USADA's summary of the 2018
Prohibited List changes states that thymosin-B4 and its derivatives, for example TB-500, were
added as examples of prohibited growth factors under S2.3 (S10).

**Position B.** Several secondary and commercial sources encountered in search assert S0
(non-approved substances) instead, or assert S2 without a subsection, or give a 2011 listing
date rather than 2018.

**Assessment.** Position A is the only one traceable to an anti-doping authority document
actually retrieved, and should be preferred. WADA's own pages returned empty content this
session (F06), so the current 2026 subsection numbering is unconfirmed (UV01).

The practical point for a member is unaffected by the disagreement and is safe to state:
TB-500 is explicitly named and prohibited at all times. The precise section citation should
be checked by a human before publication, and until then the Guide should state the
prohibition without printing a section number.

## 5. FDA compounding status

**Position A.** Thymosin beta-4 and TB-500 sit in Category 2 of the FDA interim 503A bulks
list and are barred from compounding on that basis.

**Position B.** The substance was removed from Category 2 in April 2026 after nominators
withdrew, and is scheduled for Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee review in July 2026.

**Assessment.** Unresolved and unverifiable in this session. Both positions came only from
commercial or vendor-adjacent sources. Every fda.gov URL attempted returned HTTP 404 (F01 to
F05). The one Federal Register record retrieved, the April 2026 Pharmacy Compounding Advisory
Committee meeting notice, contains a generic abstract that names no substance (S11).

Neither position may be stated in the Guide, in either direction, until a human verifies
against fda.gov directly. See CLAIM_TABLE.md P07. This is a case where saying nothing is the
correct output.

## 6. Vendor claims that conflict with the literature

These are recorded as claims encountered, not as findings. None carries evidentiary weight.

| Vendor or secondary claim | What the retrieved literature supports |
| --- | --- |
| "TB-500" and "thymosin beta-4" used interchangeably as one product. | They are chemically different molecules with different evidence bases (S01, S09). The interchangeable usage is not supported by primary sources, and S01 notes that marketed products claim to contain either one. |
| Human trials demonstrate TB-500 works. | No completed human trial of the fragment exists. One registered study is recruiting with no results (S08, S09), and a 2026 review states human data are lacking (S03). |
| A 2007 European study showed thymosin beta-4 accelerated venous ulcer healing. | The retrieved record describes methodology for a study in progress with 21 of a planned 72 patients enrolled, and reports no outcome data (S06). |
| Roughly 30 percent of grey-market peptide samples are mislabelled, misdosed or contaminated. | No primary analysis supporting any such figure was retrieved (UV03). The one retrieved market-quality study does not report per-sample rates and its panel composition is unknown (S07, UV02). |
| Regulatory framing implying legal availability or approved status. | No approved product was located, and the FDA position could not be verified at all this session (C23, F01 to F05). |

## 7. Where there is no genuine contradiction

Two areas are sometimes presented as contested and are not.

- **Whether TB-500 is prohibited in sport.** It is named explicitly and prohibited at all
  times (S10), corroborated by the 2026 review's statement that both compounds remain banned
  in sport (S03) and by the existence of validated detection methods (S01, S02). Only the
  section citation is uncertain, not the prohibition.
- **Whether human evidence for TB-500 exists.** It does not. This is established by a complete
  registry query returning exactly one recruiting study with no results (S08, S09), and
  corroborated by an independent peer-reviewed review (S03). An absence established this way
  is a finding, not a gap.
