## United States

### Approval status

**MOTS-c is not approved by the FDA for any human use.**

Per USADA, retrieved 2026-07-19: MOTS-c is an experimental peptide being explored for
therapeutic purposes related to ageing and obesity, and it is not approved by the Food
and Drug Administration for use in humans.

- Jurisdiction: United States
- Source: USADA (secondary)
- Checked: 2026-07-19
- **Verification: the USADA statement itself is VERIFIED. The underlying FDA position is
  NOT independently verified.**

### Compounding

USADA further states that FDA prohibits compounding pharmacies from making medications
containing MOTS-c.

- Jurisdiction: United States
- Source: USADA (secondary), reporting an FDA position
- Checked: 2026-07-19
- **Verification: NOT INDEPENDENTLY VERIFIED.** The FDA 503A bulk drug substances
  category pages were attempted directly and returned HTTP 404, so the FDA category
  classification could not be read. A human must confirm which category, if any, MOTS-c
  sits in, and quote FDA's exact wording.

### What this means in practice

There is no approval pathway and, on the reported position, no lawful compounding
pathway either. Material reaching a person therefore comes from an unregulated channel
with no regulatory assurance of identity, purity, or sterility.

## Clinical trial registration status

**One interventional trial is registered and listed as RECRUITING.**

- **NCT07505745.** "A Phase 2a, Randomized, Double-blind, Placebo-controlled Study to
  Evaluate the Efficacy, Safety, and Pharmacodynamics of MOTS-c (a Mitochondrial-Derived
  Peptide) in Adults With Prediabetes and Overweight/Obesity."
- Sponsor: Hudson Biotech. Estimated enrolment 120. Start date February 2026.
- **No results are posted.**
- Checked: 2026-07-19. **Verification: VERIFIED** against the ClinicalTrials.gov API. An
  adversarial control query using a fabricated NCT ID correctly returned HTTP 404,
  confirming the retrieval was genuine.

**A registration is a statement of intent, not evidence of effect or safety.** This entry
exists so a member who encounters "MOTS-c is in clinical trials" understands exactly what
that does and does not mean.

Separately, **NCT03998514** tested **CB4211, an analog and a different molecule**, not
MOTS-c. It completed with no posted results. It does not establish anything about MOTS-c.

## Anti-doping

**MOTS-c is prohibited in sport.**

Per USADA, MOTS-c falls under the WADA Prohibited List **Section 4.4 Metabolic
Modulators, 4.4.1 Activators of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK)**.

USADA also states that athletes **cannot obtain a therapeutic use exemption**, because
there is no approved therapeutic use for this experimental drug.

- Jurisdiction: international, WADA and USADA
- Source: USADA
- Checked: 2026-07-19
- **Verification: VERIFIED** as USADA's stated position.
- [ ] Confirm against the current official WADA Prohibited List before publication, since
      the List is reissued annually and section numbering can move.

**For any member subject to testing, this is a prohibited substance and using it is a
doping violation.** That is stated plainly and is not softened.

## Safety position of the regulator-adjacent source

USADA states outright that it is **unknown under what conditions, if any, MOTS-c is safe
to use**, because no clinical trials have been completed.

This is recorded here rather than in the safety section because it is a regulatory-body
statement rather than a research finding. It is the opposite of a safety assurance.

## Other jurisdictions

**Not researched.** No statement is made about the status of MOTS-c in the EU, UK,

Approval or permission in one jurisdiction is never evidence of status in another, and
silence here means unexamined rather than permitted.
