# MOTS-c Research Guide: editorial review and sign-off gate

Every box starts unchecked. AI drafts never publish automatically. A box is checked by a
named human who personally verified the item.

## 0. The failure modes specific to this compound

- [ ] **The analog is never presented as the compound.** CB4211 is a different molecule.
      Its Phase 1 trial (NCT03998514) has no posted results. No sentence anywhere lets
      CB4211 activity read as MOTS-c evidence.
- [ ] **A registered trial is never presented as a result.** NCT07505745 is recruiting
      with nothing posted. Every mention says so in the same sentence.
- [ ] **Measuring a molecule is never presented as administering it.** Every human study
      retrieved measured endogenous MOTS-c. None gave it to anyone. The Guide must not let
      an observational correlation read as an effect of taking something.
- [ ] The widely repeated "safe and well tolerated" claim for CB4211 traces to sponsor
      communications rather than posted results, and does not appear as fact.

## 1. Content safety: dosing and administration

- [ ] No amount, mg, mcg, IU, mL, or concentration anywhere.
- [ ] No frequency, timing, cycle length, titration, loading, or stacking.
- [ ] No reconstitution, injection technique, or route-of-administration instruction.
- [ ] No acquisition guidance of any kind.

## 2. Content safety: outcomes and claims language

- [ ] No "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures", "eliminates", "reverses".
- [ ] No metabolic, fat-loss, insulin-sensitivity, or body-composition guarantee.
- [ ] No exercise-mimetic or performance claim.
- [ ] No longevity, ageing, or mitochondrial-rejuvenation claim.

## 3. Content safety: safety claims

- [ ] The Guide never states MOTS-c is safe, well tolerated, or free of side effects.
- [ ] The USADA position that it is unknown under what conditions, if any, it is safe to
      use is stated plainly.
- [ ] Anecdotal effect reports are labelled as self-report aggregations, not trial data.
- [ ] Absence of trial adverse event data is never framed as reassurance.

## 4. Preclinical labelling

- [ ] Every animal or cell-culture finding names its species or model in the same sentence.
- [ ] The AMPK mechanism is labelled as coming from mice and cell culture.
- [ ] The 2023 review's own statement that no effective method of clinical application has
      been developed is retained, not softened.

## 5. Human evidence integrity

- [ ] Every human study named states design, population, and limitation.
- [ ] Each is labelled **observational**, and labelled as measuring rather than administering.
- [ ] **The contradiction between PMID 29691953 and PMID 41004666 is presented, not
      resolved by picking the convenient one.** The Guide states they disagree on direction.
- [ ] The unstandardised-assay caveat accompanies any serum MOTS-c figure.

## 6. Citation verification: every item checked by a named human

- [ ] Every PMID, PMCID, and NCT number opened and confirmed to say what the Guide claims.
- [ ] NCT07505745 re-checked for posted results. **Posted results would materially change
      this Guide and require a full redraft, not a patch.**
- [ ] NCT03998514 confirmed as CB4211 and confirmed to have no posted results.
- [ ] No citation in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` is absent from `SOURCE_REGISTRY.md`.
- [ ] Anything marked `[UNVERIFIED]` either verified or removed.

## 7. Regulatory status: re-verified and re-dated

**This packet carries known verification debt. FDA primary sources returned HTTP 404 on
every attempt, so all FDA statements reach the Guide secondhand via USADA.**

- [ ] FDA approval status confirmed against an FDA primary source.
- [ ] FDA compounding position confirmed, with the exact category and FDA wording.
- [ ] WADA section 4.4.1 confirmed against the current official Prohibited List.
- [ ] The no-therapeutic-use-exemption statement confirmed with USADA.
- [ ] Every regulatory line carries a date, a jurisdiction, and a source.
- [ ] `REGULATORY_STATUS.md` re-dated on publication.

## 8. Claim grades

- [ ] Each claim graded individually, never the compound as a whole.
- [ ] **No claim about administering MOTS-c is graded above D or G**, because no
      interventional human evidence exists.
- [ ] Observational correlations are not graded as if they were effects.
- [ ] Vendor and manufacturer sources support no better than E.

## 9. Prohibited claims

- [ ] The prohibited list in `CLAIM_TABLE.md` is present, and none of its entries appears
      anywhere as an assertion.
- [ ] No claim that MOTS-c is approved, in trials in a way that implies validation, or
      supported by CB4211 data.

## 10. Disclosure and framing

- [ ] The Guide is educational, not a purchase recommendation.
- [ ] It states that Xenios does not sell this and does not advise how to obtain it.
- [ ] The unregulated-supply reality is stated plainly.
- [ ] The prohibited-in-sport status is stated without softening.

## 11. House style

- [ ] Zero em dashes.
- [ ] Every technical term glossed on first use, including mitochondrial-derived peptide,
      AMPK, and HOMA-IR.
- [ ] Calm and plain. No hype.

## Standing rules for this gate

1. A reviewer signs only for what they personally verified.
2. No single person may sign more than one role. Five roles means five people.
3. An unchecked box blocks publication. There is no partial publish.
4. If a regulatory statement cannot be verified against a primary source, it is removed
   rather than published with a caveat.
