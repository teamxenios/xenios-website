# MOTS-c: quality and documentation requirements

## Purpose of this document

What a supplier must be able to prove before a product containing this compound could be
offered, and what Xenios Research can currently prove. Every item is unmet.

## The starting position

Xenios has **NOT CONFIRMED** any item below for any MOTS-c product. No supplier document,
certificate of analysis, stability study, or sterility record is on file.

There is a further problem specific to this compound, and it is worth stating before the
checklist because it undercuts the checklist itself.

## The measurement problem sits upstream of the paperwork

**MOTS-c measurement is unstandardised even in the peer-reviewed literature.** The authors
of PMID 41004666 raise exactly this as a likely explanation for why published studies
conflict: methods differ across laboratories, so serum MOTS-c figures are not directly
comparable between papers.

That matters here in a way it would not for a better-characterised compound. If the
research literature cannot yet agree on how to measure this molecule reliably, then a
supplier's assay result is difficult to interpret **even when one is produced**. A
certificate of analysis is only as meaningful as the method behind it.

So for MOTS-c, an assay result is a starting point for a conversation with a supplier,
not an answer.

## No lawful supply route was identified

Per USADA, MOTS-c is not FDA approved and FDA prohibits compounding pharmacies from making
medications containing it. [UNVERIFIED against FDA primary sources, which returned HTTP
404 on every attempt. Requires human confirmation.]

If that position is correct, there is no regulated channel through which a qualified
product could be obtained, and any material comes from a supplier operating outside the
system that would ordinarily produce the documents below.

## The checklist

### A. Identity

- [ ] Analytical confirmation that the material is MOTS-c and **not CB4211 or another
      analog**. This is a live risk: vendor pages routinely blur the distinction.
- [ ] The sequence, confirmed by an orthogonal method.
- [ ] The assay method named, with its validation, given the standardisation problem above.
- [ ] Content against label claim.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] Purity by a validated, stability-indicating method.
- [ ] Named and quantified impurities, including truncated and deletion sequences.
- [ ] Residual solvents and elemental impurities.
- [ ] Counter-ion and salt form.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] Sterility testing for any injectable presentation.
- [ ] Endotoxin testing with a stated limit.
- [ ] Container closure integrity.
- [ ] The manufacturing facility, its registration, and its inspection history.

### D. Stability

- [ ] Stability data supporting the assigned shelf life, in the actual container.
- [ ] Post-reconstitution stability where applicable.
- [ ] Storage and transport conditions with excursion limits.
- [ ] **Shelf life is never inferred.** An unknown expiry blocks use rather than defaulting.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] The actual manufacturer, not only the distributor.
- [ ] The regulatory basis on which the material was made and supplied.
- [ ] Handling between manufacture and receipt.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] A **lot-specific** COA. A COA for a different lot proves nothing about this one.
- [ ] Independent third-party verification, not supplier self-report.
- [ ] Whether the COA covers identity and content, or purity alone.
- [ ] The method behind each COA figure, given the standardisation problem.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] Labelling consistent with the product's actual classification.
- [ ] No therapeutic claim the classification does not support.
- [ ] No implication that a registered trial constitutes approval or evidence.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] A named responsible person at the supplier.
- [ ] An adverse event reporting route and a recall contact.
- [ ] Insurance and indemnity position.
- [ ] Notification obligations if a lot is recalled.

## A note on unverified market claims

Claims about FDA impurity findings, API characterisation problems, and immunogenicity
concerns for MOTS-c circulate widely on secondary and vendor pages. **None was verified
against an FDA primary source**, because those pages were not retrievable.

They are not repeated here as fact. They are recorded as unverified market claims, at
grade E or G, and a human must check the FDA record directly before any of it is stated.

The distinction matters: a plausible-sounding quality concern repeated from a vendor page
is not evidence, even when the concern points in the cautious direction.
