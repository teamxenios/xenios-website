## 4. The oncologic mechanism concern: the highest-stakes item for this compound

- [ ] 4.1 The theoretical oncologic concern is stated in the one-minute summary, not only in the safety
      section.
- [ ] 4.2 The concern is presented as following from the compound's own proposed mechanism, with the
      c-MET proto-oncogene point and the two oncology reviews (PMID 22530990, PMID 33123991) cited.
- [ ] 4.3 The word "theoretical" is never allowed to function as reassurance. The text states in the
      same place that it is theoretical because nobody has been studied, not because it has been ruled
      out.
- [ ] 4.4 The concern is equally never overstated as a demonstrated harm or an observed adverse event.
- [ ] 4.5 No human carcinogenicity claim of any kind appears, in either direction, because no human data
      exists.
- [ ] 4.6 The absence of any located systematic toxicology or carcinogenicity study in any species is
      stated, and stated honestly as "not located" rather than "does not exist".
- [ ] 4.7 The mechanism section and the safety section are explicitly linked, so a reader cannot take
      the mechanism as an unqualified benefit story.

## Standing rules for this gate

1. **No single person may sign more than one role.** Five roles exist so that five different kinds of
   error get caught by five different people. One actor holding two or more signatures is
   self-approval wearing a second hat, and it defeats a five-gate review entirely: the whole value of
   the gate is that the person who wrote or accepted a claim is not the person who clears it. If a role
   cannot be staffed independently, publication waits. It does not proceed with a doubled-up signature.
2. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human sign-off is
   complete.
3. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a reviewer or
   a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed and logged as a
   gap. Omitting is always better than guessing.
5. **Concluding that human evidence is absent is a successful outcome**, not a failure of the research.
   An honest empty evidence table is the correct result for this compound and must not be padded,
   least of all with another compound's trials.
6. **An unretrievable source is a gap, not a finding.** Two regulator checks failed during drafting. No
   statement may be built on what secondary pages say those documents contain.
7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification.
8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, or regulatory
   statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed.
