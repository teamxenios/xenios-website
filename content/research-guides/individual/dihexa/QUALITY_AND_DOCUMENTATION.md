# Dihexa: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing this
compound could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of dihexa, and it
applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state** for dihexa specifically. Xenios has not confirmed any
of these items for this compound. Every checkbox below is unchecked, and none is expected to be
checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below.

1. **Dihexa is an investigational research compound with no marketing approval in any jurisdiction
   identified during this research** (checked 2026-07-21). There is therefore no approved
   manufacturer, no approved specification, and no regulatory authority inspecting a production line
   against a filed standard.

2. **There is no pharmacopoeial monograph and no approved reference standard for dihexa.** This is
   more consequential than it sounds. Even a supplier acting in complete good faith has no official
   published specification to test material against, and no authenticated sample to compare it to.
   Every purity or identity figure a supplier produces is therefore self-referential.

3. **No analytical study testing material sold as dihexa was located.** Nobody appears to have
   systematically checked what is in these vials. That is an absence of information, not a favourable
   result.

4. **Dihexa is nonetheless sold as a consumer nootropic.** Research-chemical and consumer peptide
   vendors sell it, and vendor pages surfaced prominently in every search performed during this
   research, including nationwidepeptides.com, invivochem.com, glpbio.com, thepreptide.com,
   creative-peptides.com, ordinarypeptides.com, and calcmypeptide.com. None of that is scientific
   evidence. It is a grade E or G market claim, with no regulatory identity, purity, or content
   verification behind it.

A note on numbers that will be encountered elsewhere. A widely repeated potency comparison against
brain-derived neurotrophic factor, a specific HGF binding affinity figure, and a CAS registry number
all circulate freely. None was verified against a primary or authoritative source during this
research, and none is reproduced here. Precise-sounding numbers with no traceable source do more harm
than good in health content.

**Quality documentation is a separate question from whether the compound does anything.** Perfect
documentation for dihexa would establish that a vial contains what it claims. It would not create the
human evidence that, as set out in the Guide, does not exist, and it would not resolve the theoretical
oncologic concern that follows from the compound's own proposed mechanism.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any
further conversation. Status for dihexa as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single item.

### A. Identity

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of the exact substance.** Not the name "dihexa". The
      specific chemical entity, stated as N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6)-aminohexanoic amide, with the salt or
      free form specified. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Documented exclusion of the confusable clinical candidate.** Positive evidence that the
      material is dihexa (PNB-0408) and **not** fosgonimeton (ATH-1017, formerly NDX-1017), which is a
      different compound. Naming confusion between these two is the single most common error made about
      this compound and is an identity risk before it is an evidence problem.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A3. Verified registry identifiers.** A CAS number confirmed against an authoritative chemical
      registry. The figure circulating on vendor pages was not confirmed during this research and its
      digits are not reproduced anywhere in this folder. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
      [UNVERIFIED - vendor-sourced, requires human source check]
- [ ] **A4. Confirmed structure and sequence for the specific batch**, by an orthogonal analytical
      method such as mass spectrometry combined with an independent structural technique, not by
      supplier assertion. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set before
      testing rather than after. Note that no pharmacopoeial monograph exists, so any such
      specification is the supplier's own and must be disclosed in full rather than referenced.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A7. A named reference standard used for identity confirmation**, with its own provenance
      documented. No approved reference standard for dihexa exists, so what stands in for one must be
      stated explicitly. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **B1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, with the method, column, and conditions
      disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of it
      there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Peptide-related impurities characterised**, including truncated, deletion, and modified
      sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Aggregation assessed.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **C1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any use
      requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process, documented.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Stability

- [ ] **D1. Stability data under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not just a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **E1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a
      distributor, not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis through to the finished unit**, including every
      intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Batch and lot traceability**, so that any single unit can be traced back to its
      manufacturing record. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **F1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis (COA)** matching the exact lot in hand, not a
      representative or example COA for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. The COA issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated and
      independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Test methods named on the COA**, with acceptance criteria and measured results, not
      "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no commercial
      interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. COA authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** COAs are forged. A
      document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **G1. Label content matching the tested content**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material** that exceed the
      evidence, which for dihexa means no human efficacy or safety claims at all.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. No implication that fosgonimeton's clinical programme applies to this material.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date.** Note that this research
      could not retrieve an FDA or WADA position for dihexa, so any supplier asserting one should be
      asked for the primary source. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **H1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### I. Preclinical safety documentation

This section exists for dihexa specifically because the compound's central safety concern is
mechanistic, and none of it has been closed.

- [ ] **I1. Systematic toxicology in at least one species, published or provided in full.** None in
      any species was located during this research, and its absence was not affirmatively confirmed.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I2. Carcinogenicity assessment.** Dihexa's proposed mechanism is potentiation of HGF
      signalling at c-Met, and HGF and c-Met signalling is an established contributor to tumour
      progression and metastasis. This is the single highest-value missing document for this compound.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I3. Repeat-exposure and chronic-exposure data in any species.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I4. Any characterisation of off-target growth-factor pathway activity.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for dihexa.** No supplier has been assessed
against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent testing has been
commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed. The
purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly what has
and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide exists.
