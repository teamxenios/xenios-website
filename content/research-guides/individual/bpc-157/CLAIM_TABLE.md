# BPC-157 Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## How to read the grades in this table

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

Two things about how grade A is used here. It is applied to documented regulatory,
bibliographic and registry facts, for example that no approved product exists, or that a
named trial has no posted results. It is not applied to any statement about what BPC-157
does in a human body. **No efficacy claim in this table reaches above grade C, and the
single grade C row is an uncontrolled pilot in 12 people with a self-rated outcome.** That
is the honest state of the evidence.

Some low-grade rows are marked allowed on a member-facing surface. In every such case the
claim is a limiting or negative statement (a study failed to separate from placebo, an
evidence base is too small to interpret). Restraining claims may be published at low grade.
Promotional claims may not.

## 6. PROHIBITED claims

These claims must never appear on any member-facing surface for this compound, in any
phrasing, in any channel, including summaries, titles, meta descriptions, social copy,
email, and customer support responses. This section governs over any other section. If a
draft appears to support one of these claims, the draft is wrong, not this list.

| ID | Prohibited claim or framing | Why it is prohibited |
| --- | --- | --- |
| P-01 | That BPC-157 heals, repairs, or accelerates recovery of tendon, ligament, muscle, joint, or connective tissue in humans. | The healing findings are rodent, explant and cell findings. The only human musculoskeletal item identified is a retrospective chart review of 17 people at one clinic, with a second peptide co-administered in some of them. The one adequately designed human trial is recruiting and has produced nothing. |
| P-02 | That BPC-157 heals the gut, repairs the gut lining, treats leaky gut, or treats inflammatory bowel disease in humans. | The single randomized human trial identified, in ulcerative colitis, did not separate from placebo, exists only as a meeting abstract, and FDA called its data inadequate to support efficacy or safety. |
| P-03 | That BPC-157 is safe, well tolerated, has an excellent safety profile, or has no side effects. | FDA states there is insufficient clinical safety information to characterise the safety profile at all. Total documented human exposure is on the order of dozens of people for days to two weeks, with unclear monitoring. |
| P-04 | Any statement that BPC-157 will, does, restores, cures, eliminates, reverses, or is proven to do anything in humans. | No human efficacy claim in this table exceeds grade C, and that row is an uncontrolled pilot in 12 people with a self-rated outcome. |
| P-05 | Any bare mechanism sentence, for example that BPC-157 promotes angiogenesis, upregulates growth factor receptors, or activates a named signalling pathway, without the animal or cell model in the same sentence. | This is the most common failure mode in this subject area. FDA states the molecular targets are unidentified and the mechanisms poorly understood. A mechanism sentence without its model reads as a human fact. |
| P-06 | Citing the ongoing Phase 2 hamstring trial, or the 2015 Phase 1 registration, as support for any effect or for safety. | Neither has produced results. The 2015 registration has none a decade later. An ongoing trial is a question, not an answer. |
| P-07 | Any claim that a large volume of published research supports BPC-157, or any count of studies presented as human evidence. | The volume is real and it is almost entirely rodent and in vitro. FDA identified five human studies in total. A PubMed search restricted to trials and systematic reviews returns zero records. |
| P-08 | That BPC-157 is natural, body-derived, or endogenous, and therefore safe or low risk. | Derivation from a human protein sequence carries no safety implication. FDA's flagged concern is immunogenicity against the peptide, which this framing directly contradicts. |
| P-09 | Any dose, amount, unit, concentration, volume, frequency, timing, cycle length, titration, loading approach, stacking combination, reconstitution instruction, administration technique, or route. | Absolute content safety rule. | P-12 | Any statement that BPC-157 is legal, legal for personal use, or in a legal grey area, or any characterisation of enforcement risk. | Not a claim this Guide is equipped to make. The Guide states approval and listing status by jurisdiction, with dates and sources, and nothing further. |

## 7. Summary for the reviewer

The evidence position for BPC-157 is unusually clear, and it is negative. There is a large
rodent and cell literature, an essentially empty human literature, no approved product in
any country, no pharmacopoeial standard to test a product against, a regulator-flagged
immunogenicity concern, three confounded adverse event reports, and one adequately designed
human trial that is recruiting and has produced nothing. The correct Guide is a short one
that says so plainly, grades almost every claim D or G, and publishes the PROHIBITED list
above as the operative control.
