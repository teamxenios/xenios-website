# BPC-157 Guide: editorial review and sign-off gate

**AI drafts never publish automatically.** Every file in this folder was drafted by an AI system from a
research record and has received no human review. Nothing here may reach a member-facing surface until
every box below is checked by a named human, and until the founder sign-off at the end is complete.

Every box below starts UNCHECKED. An unchecked box blocks publication. Do not check a box on the basis
that something "looks right"; check it only after doing the verification the box describes.

## Section 1. Content safety, hard gates

Any single failure here blocks publication outright.

- [ ] **No dosing anywhere.** No amounts, mg, mcg, IU, mL, units, concentrations, frequency, timing,
      cycle length, titration, loading, stacking, reconstitution, injection technique, or route of
      administration presented as guidance, in any file, including tables, footnotes and captions.
- [ ] Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact sentence "Dosing and administration information is
      intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides." is present.
- [ ] Any route of administration mentioned appears only as a factual description of how a cited study or
      regulator characterised a product, never as instruction.
- [ ] **No treatment directions, protocols or regimens.** Nothing in any file could be followed as
      instructions.
- [ ] **No sourcing or acquisition guidance.** No supplier names presented as options, no purchasing
      routes, no advice on obtaining the compound.
- [ ] **No guaranteed outcomes.** The words "will", "proven to", "restores", "cures", "eliminates",
      "reverses" do not appear as claims about effect. Searched explicitly.
- [ ] Effect language uses "has been studied for", "reported in", "investigated as", "associated with in
      [population]" throughout.
- [ ] **No unsupported safety claims.** "Safe", "well tolerated", or "no side effects" never appear as a
      general statement of fact. Where a study author used such wording, it is clearly attributed to that
      author and immediately qualified.
- [ ] **No medical advice, diagnosis, or self-treatment framing** in any file.
- [ ] **"Research use only" framing is not used to imply human benefit.** Any mention of such labelling is
      accompanied by an explicit statement that it implies nothing about human benefit or safety.
- [ ] No em dashes appear in any file. Searched explicitly.
- [ ] No exclamation marks, hype, sales language, or flattery.

## Section 2. Preclinical to human separation

This is the highest-risk failure mode for this compound. Check line by line, not by impression.

- [ ] **Every preclinical finding carries its species or model in the same sentence as the finding.**
      Checked individually for each such sentence in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` and `FAQ_DRAFT.md`.
- [ ] No bare mechanism sentence exists anywhere that a reader could take as a statement about humans.
- [ ] The Proposed mechanism section is labelled as proposed, is hedged throughout, and names the species
      or model for each pathway claim.
- [ ] Rodent and cell findings are never summarised in the one-minute summary in a way that reads as human
      effect.
- [ ] Section headings themselves distinguish human evidence from preclinical evidence.
- [ ] FDA's caveats on the rodent literature (unidentified molecular targets, poorly understood mechanisms,
      dose-response not established, several studies lacking histopathological confirmation) appear
      alongside the rodent findings, not buried elsewhere.
- [ ] The absence of a dose-response relationship is stated as the anomaly it is.
- [ ] Registered but unreported trials (NCT02637284, NCT07437547) are presented as producing no evidence,
      and are never cited as support for any claim.

## Section 5. Claim grades

Grades apply to individual claims, never to the compound as a whole.

- [ ] Every substantive claim in `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` and `FAQ_DRAFT.md` carries a grade, or sits under a
      graded block.
- [ ] Grades use only the approved scale: A Established, B Supported human evidence, C Early human
      evidence, D Preclinical, E Manufacturer or supplier reported, F Traditional or historical,
      G Unverified, PROHIBITED.
- [ ] No claim is graded A or B. Confirmed by direct check, given that no adequately powered controlled
      human trial has reported a positive result.
- [ ] Any claim graded C is justified by a specific retrieved human study, and the study's design
      limitations appear in the same place as the grade.
- [ ] Every rodent, cell, or explant finding is graded D.
- [ ] Claims resting on conference abstracts described only via FDA's summary are graded G.
- [ ] The default of D or G has been applied wherever a better grade could not be specifically justified.
- [ ] No grade was upgraded on the basis of volume of preclinical literature.
- [ ] **PROHIBITED claims are absent.** Specifically confirmed absent: that BPC-157 heals injuries in
      humans, that it is safe, that it is well tolerated, that it is natural and therefore benign, that it
      is legal to buy or use, that it treats or cures any named condition, that any product is verified or
      pharmaceutical grade, that laboratory-use labelling implies human benefit, and any comparison
      presenting it as equivalent or superior to an approved therapy.
