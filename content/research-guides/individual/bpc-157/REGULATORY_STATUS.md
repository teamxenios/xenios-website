## Principle that governs this document

Approval or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another. Absence of a formal
statement from a jurisdiction is not permission, and is not evidence of safety. A statement
that a substance is unapproved is a statement about its regulatory record, not a statement
about whether it works or whether it is harmful. This document reports regulatory records
only. It contains no legal advice and no assessment of enforcement risk.

## Part 1. United States

### US-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** BPC-157 is not an approved drug. FDA's briefing document for the Pharmacy
Compounding Advisory Committee states that neither BPC-157 free base nor BPC-157 acetate is
a component of an FDA-approved drug, and further states that there is no approved product
containing BPC-157-related bulk drug substances in any country at this time, and that
neither form is found in the European, Japanese, or International Pharmacopeias.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** S01, https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download
**Note:** Drugs@FDA was not searched directly. This finding rests on FDA's own explicit
statement in its own review document, which is a strong primary source for the point.

### US-02. Pharmacopoeial standards

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Statement:** There is no USP or NF drug substance monograph for BPC-157 free base or
BPC-157 acetate, and no USP dietary supplement monograph. There is therefore no official
identity, purity or quality specification against which a product can be tested or a
supplier held.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** S01, https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download

### US-03. Compounding, current listing

**Jurisdiction:** United States, FDA, bulk drug substances for use in compounding.
**Statement:** On FDA's page covering certain bulk drug substances for use in compounding
that may present significant safety risks (content current as of 04/22/2026), BPC-157
appears in the table of bulk drug substances nominated but withdrawn, described as
substances previously in category 2 of the interim policies that were withdrawn by the
nominators. The concern FDA records is that compounded drugs containing BPC-157 may pose
risk for immunogenicity for certain forms of administration, may have complexities with
regard to peptide-related impurities and active pharmaceutical ingredient characterisation,
that FDA has identified no or only limited safety-related information, and that the agency
therefore lacks sufficient information to know whether the drug would cause harm when
administered to humans.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** S02, https://www.fda.gov/drugs/human-drug-compounding/certain-bulk-drug-substances-use-compounding-may-present-significant-safety-risks
**Important caveat:** BPC-157 does not currently appear in the main Category 2 table on that
page. The widely repeated statement that FDA placed BPC-157 in Category 2 in September 2023
could not be verified from any FDA source in this session and must not be published as
current fact. A human reviewer should check the Federal Register and the FDA docket record.
See SOURCE_REGISTRY.md, UV02, and CONTRADICTIONS.md section 1.

### US-04. Compounding, pending FDA decision

**Jurisdiction:** United States, FDA, 503A bulk drug substances list.
**Statement:** FDA is evaluating BPC-157 free base and BPC-157 acetate on its own initiative
after both nominations were withdrawn. The FDA review memo is dated 5/11/2026 and was placed
before the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting scheduled for July 23-24, 2026
(meeting page content current as of 07/17/2026). FDA's stated recommendation is that, on
balance, the physicochemical characterisation, the information on historical use, the lack
of evidence of effectiveness, and the safety information identified for both forms weigh
against them being added to the 503A bulks list. As of the date checked, this was a proposal
pending advisory committee input, not a final determination.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Sources:** S01, https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download and S03,
https://www.fda.gov/advisory-committees/advisory-committee-calendar/july-23-24-2026-meeting-pharmacy-compounding-advisory-committee-07232026
**Action required before publication:** the meeting date has now passed. Retrieve the
outcome and update this section. Do not publish this Guide with US-04 in its current form
without that check.

### US-05. Enforcement, warning letter

**Jurisdiction:** United States, FDA enforcement.
**Statement:** FDA's warning letter to Tailor Made Compounding LLC, dated 04/01/2020, cites
the firm for compounding drug products using BPC 157 among a list of substances, stating
that such products are not eligible for the exemptions provided by section 503A(a) because
they are not the subject of an applicable USP or NF monograph, are not a component of an
FDA-approved human drug, and do not appear on the 503A bulks list.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** S04, https://www.fda.gov/inspections-compliance-enforcement-and-criminal-investigations/warning-letters/tailor-made-compounding-llc-594743-04012020

### US-06. Enforcement, untitled letter

**Jurisdiction:** United States, FDA enforcement.
**Statement:** An FDA untitled letter dated September 14, 2023 to Barclay, Luke, and Pillai
Specialty Pharmacy, PLLC (doing business as Meta Pharmacy Services), Las Vegas, Nevada,
states that the firm compounded drug products using ibutamoren mesylate, BPC-157 acetate and
theanine, that are not eligible for the exemptions provided by section 503A(a), on the same
three grounds recorded in US-05.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** S05, https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/fda-commissioner/barclay-luke-pillai-specialty-pharmacy-pllc-untitled-letter-issued-9142023
**Note:** These two letters were confirmed by direct retrieval. A complete sweep of FDA
enforcement actions naming BPC-157 was not possible, because the warning letter search
interface did not return results to direct retrieval. Two is a floor, not a total.

### US-07. Dietary supplement status

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Department of Defense, Operation Supplement Safety.
**Statement:** DoD states that BPC-157 is an unapproved drug that cannot be legally
prescribed or sold over the counter, that it is not a lawful dietary ingredient, that it
appears on the DoD Prohibited Dietary Supplement Ingredients List and on the WADA Prohibited
List under non-approved substances, and that there is little to no reliable scientific
evidence to support the safety or effectiveness of BPC-157 in humans. DoD also states that
FDA has cautioned against compounded drugs containing BPC-157 because of safety risks and
potential contamination with other substances, and that such products are often labelled in
ways inconsistent with human use.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19. Article dated April 29, 2025.
**Source:** S11, https://www.opss.org/article/bpc-157-prohibited-peptide-and-unapproved-drug-found-health-and-wellness-products

## Part 2. Sport

### SP-01. Anti-doping status

**Jurisdiction:** Sport, World Anti-Doping Agency Prohibited List, reported via the United
States Anti-Doping Agency.
**Statement:** USADA states that BPC-157 is prohibited under the S0 unapproved substances
category of the List.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** S12, https://www.usada.org/spirit-of-sport/bpc-157-peptide-prohibited/
**Limits of this statement.** The page retrieved did not state the list year, and did not
state whether the prohibition is in-competition only or applies at all times.
[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check] The unapproved substances
class is conventionally described as prohibited at all times. The WADA Prohibited List
document itself was not retrieved in this session, and that framing must not be published
until a human confirms it against the current WADA list. A reviewer should also confirm
whether BPC-157 is named explicitly in the list or captured by the class definition.
Vendor claims that BPC-157 is additionally named under a second WADA class were not used and
remain unverified. See SOURCE_REGISTRY.md, UV01 and UV03.
