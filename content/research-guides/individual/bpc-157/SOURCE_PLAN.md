# BPC-157 Source Plan

This document records how the evidence base for the BPC-157 Research Guide was assembled,
what was deliberately excluded, and what a human reviewer still needs to search before
this Guide is published.

All retrieval described here was performed on 2026-07-19. Every regulatory statement in
this Guide carries its own date and source URL and must be re-verified before publication.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## 1. Objective

Establish, from primary sources only, what is actually known about BPC-157 in humans,
what is known only in animal or cell systems, what is unknown, and what its regulatory
status is by jurisdiction. The Guide is written to be correct when the honest answer is
"the human evidence is absent," which is the answer this search produced.

## 3. Queries run

The following queries were executed and their results are reflected in the source registry.

1. PubMed E-utilities, term `"BPC 157" AND (systematic review[pt] OR randomized controlled trial[pt] OR clinical trial[pt])`.
   Result: zero records. This negative result is itself a finding and is cited as one.
2. PubMed record retrieval for the individual studies listed in the source registry, by
   PMID, for records that were confirmed to exist by retrieval.
3. ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 retrieval for the two BPC-157 registrations located.
4. Direct retrieval of the FDA briefing document, the FDA bulk substances page, the FDA
   advisory committee meeting page, and two FDA enforcement letters.
5. Direct retrieval of the USADA and DoD OPSS guidance pages.

## 4. Inclusion criteria

A source was included if all of the following held.

- It was retrieved directly during the 2026-07-19 session, at a URL recorded in the
  source registry.
- It is a regulatory body, a national government agency, a peer-reviewed indexed
  publication, a clinical trial registry entry, or a database query result.
- Its claims can be attributed precisely, with the population and the model or species
  stated.

A source described only inside another retrieved source (for example, the conference
abstracts summarised in the FDA briefing document) is included only as an FDA-reported
item, never as an independently verified paper. No PMID, DOI or citation was constructed
for any such item.

## 5. Exclusion criteria

The following are disqualified as evidence for this Guide and were not used to support
any claim.

- Vendor, retailer, marketplace and compounding pharmacy pages of any kind, including
  their linked certificates of analysis, their citation lists, and their summaries of
  studies. These are commercial documents, not evidence.
- Peptide industry blogs, affiliate content, and supplement review sites.
- Forum, social media and podcast claims.
- Search engine result summaries and AI-generated overviews. One such summary encountered
  during the session asserted a regulatory reversal that the underlying article did not
  support. That assertion was rejected. See CONTRADICTIONS.md.
- Any source that could not be retrieved. Where a claim depended on such a source, the
  claim was dropped and logged as a gap rather than stated with a hedge.
- Any dosing, administration, protocol or acquisition content from any source.

## 6. Handling rules applied

- Preclinical findings carry the species or model in the same sentence as the finding.
  No mechanism sentence appears in this Guide without its model attached.
- Reported effects are written as "has been studied for," "reported in," or "investigated
  as." No outcome is described as guaranteed.
- Safety is never described as established. Where human safety data is absent, the Guide
  says it is absent.
- Where a source states a dose, the dose is not reproduced anywhere in this Guide.
- Statements resting on general background knowledge rather than a retrieved source are
  labelled inline as unverified and requiring a human source check.

## 7. Remaining for a human reviewer

These items could not be resolved in this session and must be closed before publication.

1. **The 503A category history and date.** The widely repeated "Category 2, September
   2023" framing could not be verified from any FDA source. FDA's live page now lists
   BPC-157 under bulk drug substances nominated but withdrawn, not in the dated Category 2
   table. A reviewer should check the Federal Register and the FDA docket record for the
   actual dates and the current status.
2. **The WADA Prohibited List itself.** The 2026 edition PDF was not retrieved. A reviewer
   should confirm the class heading, the prohibited-at-all-times framing, and whether
   BPC-157 is named explicitly or captured by the class definition.
3. **The July 23-24 2026 advisory committee outcome.** The meeting had not occurred as of
   the research date. FDA's recommendation was a proposal, not a determination. This must
   be re-checked and the Guide updated before publication.
4. **Full texts of the FDA-summarised studies.** Ruenzi 2005, Veljaca 2002 and 2003, Lee
   and Padgett 2021, and Lee and Burgess 2025 were not retrieved. They are cited in this
   Guide only as items FDA describes.
5. **A complete FDA enforcement sweep.** The FDA warning letter search interface did not
   return results to direct retrieval. Two letters were confirmed. The true count is
   higher and unknown.
6. **A reported Department of Justice action** against one named compounder appeared in a
   search summary and could not be confirmed from any government source. It is excluded
   from this Guide entirely and should not be added without a retrieved DOJ source.
7. **Non-US trial registries.** The one active Phase 2 trial located is registered on
   ClinicalTrials.gov but conducted outside the United States. Regional registries were
   not searched and may hold additional registrations.
8. **Medsafe (New Zealand) minutes** directly, rather than through FDA's quotation.
