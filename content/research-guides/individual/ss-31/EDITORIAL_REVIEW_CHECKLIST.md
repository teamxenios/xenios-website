**This Guide is an AI draft. AI drafts never publish automatically.** Nothing in this compound's

**The specific risk for this compound.** Most Guides in this series describe compounds with no
human evidence. This one describes a compound with a large human trial programme that mostly
failed, sitting underneath a real FDA approval that is narrow and conditional. The failure mode
here is not overstating thin evidence. It is letting a genuine approval do work it cannot do:
implying benefit for healthy adults, implying that clinical benefit was demonstrated, or implying
that a grey-market research chemical is the approved product. Sections 4, 5, and 7 exist mainly
to catch that.

## Standing rules for this gate

1. **No single person may sign more than one role.** Five gates signed by one person is one gate
   wearing five names. The entire value of a multi-reviewer process is that each reviewer brings
   an independent perspective and a different professional incentive, and a self-approving actor
   defeats a five-gate review entirely. If a role cannot be staffed with a distinct named person,
   the Guide does not publish. It waits.
2. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human
   sign-off is complete.
3. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a
   reviewer or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed
   and logged as a gap. Omitting is always better than guessing.
5. **Reporting that the controlled trials failed is a successful outcome**, not a failure of the
   research and not an attack on the compound. An honest negative reading is the correct result
   here and must not be softened because an FDA approval also exists.
6. **A convenient claim gets the same scrutiny as an inconvenient one.** Two findings in this
   folder support the Guide's cautious position and are flagged UNVERIFIED for exactly that
   reason. A packet that verifies strictly in one direction only is not honest, it is biased in a
   more comfortable direction.
7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification. This compound is specifically time-sensitive because
   its approval is conditional on confirmatory trials that are still running.
8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, or
   regulatory statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed.
