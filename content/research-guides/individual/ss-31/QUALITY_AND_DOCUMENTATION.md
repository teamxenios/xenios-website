# SS-31: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing this
compound could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of SS-31, and
it applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state** for SS-31 specifically. Xenios has not confirmed
any of these items for any material. Every checkbox below is unchecked, and none is expected to
be checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position, and why it is unusual for this compound

Three facts frame everything below, and the first one is genuinely different from most compounds
in this series.

1. **An FDA-approved product containing this molecule exists.** FORZINITY (elamipretide
   hydrochloride) injection received FDA accelerated approval on 19 September 2025 (verified
   2026-07-21). That product has been through FDA quality review. There is an approved
   manufacturer, an approved specification, and a regulatory authority holding a production line
   to a filed standard.

2. **That approval does not extend to grey-market SS-31.** The FDA-approved product and the
   material sold online as a research chemical labelled "SS-31" are **not established to be the
   same material**. Approval attaches to a specific manufactured product with a defined
   formulation, not to a molecule name. Material sold as a research chemical has had none of that
   review, and its identity, purity, and content are unverified.

3. **No SS-31-specific purity, identity, or counterfeit analysis could be located.** No analytical
   study testing material sold as grey-market SS-31 was found during research. Nobody appears to
   have systematically checked what is in these vials. That is an absence of information, not a
   favourable result.

The combination of 1 and 2 is what makes this compound's quality question distinctive, and
riskier than it looks. For most unapproved peptides, a buyer at least knows they are outside the
regulated system. Here, a real approval exists and can be pointed at, which makes it far easier to
believe that a vial bought elsewhere is covered by it. It is not.

The compound also appears under at least five names: SS-31, elamipretide, MTP-131, Bendavia, and
FORZINITY. A shared molecule name is not a shared quality assurance, and Guide copy must not let
the approved-drug name lend borrowed credibility to material bought under a different name.

**A note on circulating market claims.** Secondary reporting retrieved this session describes FDA
warning letters to online peptide sellers in April 2026 over research-use-only labelling combined
with consumer-health marketing, and cites independent laboratory findings that a substantial
share of grey-market peptide samples fail identity, purity, or quantity checks. **These
descriptions came from law-firm, trade, and press blogs, not from a retrieved FDA primary
document.** They are carried as a grade E market-risk claim only. **No specific failure percentage
is reproduced here**, and nothing here is attributed to FDA, until the underlying FDA
warning-letter records are read directly.
**[UNVERIFIED - requires human verification against FDA warning-letter records.]**

**One item from the approved label belongs in a quality document rather than only a safety one.**
The label's contraindication in neonates is driven by an excipient, benzyl alcohol, not by the
peptide. It is a concrete demonstration that what a product contains besides the active molecule
can carry serious risk. For unverified material, nobody knows what else is present.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before
any further conversation. Status for SS-31 as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single
item.

### A. Identity

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of the exact substance**, not the name "SS-31". The
      specific molecular entity, salt form, and formulation, stated in a way that distinguishes it
      from every other thing sold under any of this compound's five names.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Documented relationship to the FDA-approved product**, stated accurately. Either the
      material is the approved product, obtained through the legitimate channel, or it is not, in
      which case the supplier states plainly that the approval does not apply to it.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A3. Verified registry identifiers** confirmed against a primary chemical registry, not
      reproduced from a vendor page. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A4. Confirmed amino acid sequence for the specific batch**, by an orthogonal analytical
      method, not by supplier assertion. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. Full excipient disclosure**, with each excipient named and quantified. The approved
      label's most serious warning is excipient-driven, which makes this a substantive item rather
      than a formality. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A7. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set
      before testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **B1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, with the method and conditions
      disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of
      it there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Peptide-related impurities characterised**, including truncated, deletion, and
      modified sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit
      standard. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Aggregation assessed.** Peptide clumping is a recognised contributor to immune
      reactions against a peptide, and the approved label describes hypersensitivity reactions
      including serious ones. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **C1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any
      route requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process,
      documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Stability

- [ ] **D1. Stability data under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not just a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **E1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a
      distributor, not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis through to the finished unit**, including
      every intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Batch and lot traceability**, so that any single unit can be traced back to its
      manufacturing record. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **F1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis (COA)** matching the exact lot in hand, not a
      representative or example COA for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. The COA issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated and
      independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Test methods named on the COA**, with acceptance criteria and measured results, not
      "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no
      commercial interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. COA authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** COAs are forged. A
      document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **G1. Label content matching the tested content**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material that exceed the
      evidence.** For SS-31 that means no claim of benefit in healthy adults for energy, fatigue,
      performance, recovery, or longevity, since no completed randomized trial in a healthy
      population exists. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. No implication that the FDA approval of FORZINITY covers this material.** This is the
      specific, foreseeable misrepresentation for this compound and it must be excluded
      explicitly. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface, and secondary reporting indicates it has drawn
      regulatory attention when combined with consumer-health marketing.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date**, including that the
      approval is accelerated, conditional, based on an intermediate endpoint, and limited to
      Barth syndrome. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **H1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for SS-31.** No supplier has been
assessed against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent
testing has been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean
passed. The purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can
see exactly what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact
that a Guide exists, or from the fact that an approved medicine containing the same molecule is
on the market.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the compound does anything.
Perfect documentation for a vial of SS-31 would establish that it contains what it claims. It
would not create the healthy-adult evidence that, as set out in the Guide, does not exist, and it
would not undo the three controlled trials that missed their primary endpoints.
