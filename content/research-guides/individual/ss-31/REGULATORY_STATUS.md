# SS-31 (elamipretide) Regulatory Status

**Status checked on 2026-07-21. This page must be re-verified before publication and re-checked
on any subsequent update.** Regulatory positions change, and one item on this page concerns a
confirmatory trial obligation whose outcome does not yet exist.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.
The FDA-approved label for this compound contains full dosing information. None of it is
reproduced here.

## Standing principles for this page

1. Approval, authorisation, or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another. A
   position recorded here for the United States says nothing about any other country.
2. Every statement below carries a jurisdiction, a date checked, and a source URL. A statement
   without all three does not belong on this page.
3. Where a status could not be confirmed by reading the primary document, that is stated
   explicitly rather than filled in by inference.
4. **An approval is defined by its indication, its endpoint, and its pathway.** Reporting that a
   drug was approved, without those three, is not a regulatory statement. It is a headline.

## Integrity note, read before using this page

The FDA approval letter and the Integrated Review PDFs on accessdata.fda.gov both returned HTTP
404 on direct fetch this session. The label content below was recovered through DailyMed, the
National Institutes of Health mirror of FDA labelling, which is an authoritative source for
label text. But the FDA review documents, which contain the agency's own critique of the
evidence base, were not read.

Separately, the regulatory history in US-05 rests on a sponsor press release. It is attributed
as such and is not verified against any FDA primary source.

## United States

### US-01. Approval status and indication

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** FORZINITY (elamipretide hydrochloride) injection received FDA **accelerated
approval on 19 September 2025**, indicated to improve muscle strength in adult and pediatric
patients with Barth syndrome who meet the label's body weight criterion. Barth syndrome is a
progressive, life-limiting, ultra-rare genetic disease. This is a narrow ultra-rare-disease
indication only.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/lookup.cfm?setid=146bf34c-76f2-48db-ac07-fb29cce2cd75
**Verification:** VERIFIED. Retrieved and read from the FDA-approved US prescribing information
via DailyMed.

### US-02. What the approval was based on, and its conditions

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** The approval was based on improvement in **knee extensor muscle strength**, which
the label describes as an **intermediate clinical endpoint** and **not a demonstrated clinical
benefit**. The label states that continued approval may be contingent upon verification and
description of clinical benefit in confirmatory trial or trials.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/lookup.cfm?setid=146bf34c-76f2-48db-ac07-fb29cce2cd75
**Verification:** VERIFIED from the label.
**Why this is the most important line on the page:** the agency did not conclude that clinical
benefit had been demonstrated. It permitted marketing on an intermediate measure while the
question remains open. Nothing on a member-facing surface may describe this approval as a
demonstration that the compound works, and no member-facing surface may mention the approval
without these conditions attached.

### US-03. What the approval explicitly does NOT cover

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** The approval is for the Barth syndrome indication only. Elamipretide is **not
approved for aging, performance, fatigue, longevity, cognition, or general mitochondrial
support** in the United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/lookup.cfm?setid=146bf34c-76f2-48db-ac07-fb29cce2cd75
**Verification:** VERIFIED from the label's indication statement.
**Handling rule:** this line must appear wherever the approval is mentioned. The gap between the
approved indication and the popular consumer positioning of this compound is the widest of any
item in this folder.

### US-04. The approved product is not the grey-market research chemical

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** FDA approval covers a specific manufactured product with a defined formulation
that has been through FDA quality review. The FDA-approved product and material sold on the grey
market as "SS-31" are **not established to be the same material**, and only the approved product
has been through that review.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/lookup.cfm?setid=146bf34c-76f2-48db-ac07-fb29cce2cd75
**Verification:** VERIFIED as a consequence of what the label is. An approval attaches to a
product, not to a molecule name.
**Handling rule:** the compound appears under several names (SS-31, MTP-131, Bendavia,
elamipretide, FORZINITY). Guide copy must not let the approved-drug name lend borrowed
credibility to an unapproved research chemical bought under a different name.

### US-06. Confirmatory trial obligation

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** The label states that continued approval may be contingent upon verification and
description of clinical benefit in confirmatory trial or trials. A phase 4 Barth trial,
NCT07531251, was recruiting at the time of retrieval. Its outcome does not exist.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/lookup.cfm?setid=146bf34c-76f2-48db-ac07-fb29cce2cd75
**Verification:** VERIFIED from the label for the obligation. The trial status was recorded from
the research record and should be re-checked before publication.
**Handling rule:** report the dependency. Do not predict its outcome in either direction.

**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.

**Explicitly excluded:** search results claiming a 2026 status change, from class S0 (non-approved
