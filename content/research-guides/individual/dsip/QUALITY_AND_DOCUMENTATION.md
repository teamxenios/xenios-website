# DSIP: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing this
compound could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of DSIP and
applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state** for DSIP specifically. Xenios has not confirmed any
of these items. Every checkbox below is unchecked, and none is expected to be checked in the near
term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below, and the third is unusual enough to change what this checklist
can even mean.

1. **DSIP is not an approved medicine in any jurisdiction identified** (checked 2026-07-21). There
   is therefore no approved manufacturer, no approved specification, and no regulatory authority
   inspecting a production line against a filed standard.

2. **No study testing material sold as DSIP for identity, purity, sterility, or endotoxin content
   could be located.** Nobody appears to have systematically checked what is in these vials. That is
   an absence of information, not a favourable result.

3. **There is no pharmacopoeial standard to test against.** Because no DSIP gene or receptor has
   ever been identified and there is no approved reference product anywhere, there is no official
   published specification that unapproved material can be verified against for identity, purity, or
   content. This is a deeper problem than an untested supply chain. For most compounds, the question
   is whether anyone has checked. Here, the benchmark for checking does not exist.

4. **The identity question reaches into the molecule itself.** The 2006 review in the Journal of
   Neurochemistry proposes that a DSIP-like peptide, rather than DSIP, may account for effects
   reported in the literature (PMID 16539679, verified 2026-07-21). Perfect documentation of a vial
   would establish that it contains the nine-amino-acid sequence on the label. It would not
   establish that the sequence is the thing the historical literature was about.

Reporting on the FDA's July 2026 advisory committee review indicates FDA cited that the substances
under consideration are not well characterised, and cited insufficient human safety data including
unassessed immunogenicity risk.
[UNVERIFIED - all four FDA primary URLs returned HTTP 404 during research. This rests on
search-index snippets and secondary trade reporting and requires human confirmation against the FDA
source before publication.]

**A note on claims that will be encountered elsewhere.** Research for this Guide surfaced numerous
retailer, clinic-marketing, and peptide-blog pages asserting an absence of dependency, withdrawal,
or organ toxicity, and an absence of any identified lethal amount in animal models. None of these
pages is scientific evidence, none was traceable to a retrieved primary study, and a human
literature of three small studies could not support claims of that shape. They are recorded as
grade E or G market claims to be rebutted and are excluded from this document.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any
further conversation. Status for DSIP as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single item.

### A. Identity

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of the exact substance.** Not the name "DSIP" and not the
      name "emideltide". The specific form, since regulatory filings reportedly distinguish free base
      and acetate forms as separate bulk substances. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Verified registry identifiers.** A primary chemical registry entry confirming the
      sequence and the molecular identity. The sequence and discovery details in the Guide came from
      tertiary background used for orientation only. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
      [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]
- [ ] **A3. Confirmed amino acid sequence for the specific batch**, by an orthogonal analytical
      method such as mass spectrometry combined with sequencing, not by supplier assertion.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A4. Explicit exclusion of confusable substances.** Documented evidence that the material is
      the nine-amino-acid sequence and not a structural analogue, and not the engineered fusion
      construct used in the 2024 mouse study. Analogue results are not DSIP results.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set
      before testing rather than after. Note that no pharmacopoeial standard exists to derive such a
      specification from, so a supplier would have to justify their own. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **B1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, with the method, column, and
      conditions disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of it
      there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Peptide-related impurities characterised**, including truncated, deletion, and modified
      sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Aggregation assessed.** Peptide clumping is a recognised contributor to immune reactions
      against a peptide, and immunogenicity is specifically unassessed for this compound.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **C1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any route
      requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process, documented.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Stability

- [ ] **D1. Stability data under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method. This matters more than usual here, because reviews note rapid
      degradation of the peptide. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not just a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **E1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a
      distributor, not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis through to the finished unit**, including
      every intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Batch and lot traceability**, so that any single unit can be traced back to its
      manufacturing record. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **F1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis (COA)** matching the exact lot in hand, not a
      representative or example COA for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. The COA issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated and
      independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Test methods named on the COA**, with acceptance criteria and measured results, not
      "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no
      commercial interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. COA authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** COAs are forged. A
      document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **G1. Label content matching the tested content**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material** that exceed the
      evidence, which for DSIP means no human efficacy claims and no human safety claims at all.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. No use of the compound's own name as an implied mechanism.** "Delta sleep-inducing" is a
      name from 1970s animal work, and the controlled human study found slow wave sleep unmodified.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **H1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for DSIP.** No supplier has been assessed
against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent testing has
been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed.
The purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly
what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide
exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the compound does anything. Perfect
documentation for DSIP would establish that a vial contains what it claims. It would not create
modern human evidence, which as set out in the Guide does not exist, and it would not resolve the
question of whether the molecule is a genuine endogenous signal at all.
