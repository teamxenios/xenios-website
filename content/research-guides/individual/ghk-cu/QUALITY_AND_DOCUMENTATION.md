# Quality and documentation standard: GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide-1)

## What this document is

This is the documentation a supplier would have to provide before Xenios could make any quality
statement about a product containing this compound.

It is written as a checklist. Every item below is currently **UNMET**. Xenios has not confirmed
any of these items for GHK-Cu, from any supplier, for any product. Nothing in this document
should be read as a statement that such a product exists, that Xenios intends to offer one, or
that any supplier has been evaluated.

An unmet checklist is the accurate current state, and publishing it that way is the point.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

---

## Why this checklist matters more than usual for this compound

Two findings from the research record of 2026-07-19 drive it.

First, category-level quality risk is documented. The ECRI and ISMP white paper on compounded
peptide products (May 7 2026) describes contamination risk, mislabeling, and lack of
standardization in compounded peptide products relative to FDA-approved products, and references
the 2012 contaminated-compounding meningitis outbreak as precedent for real-world harm. That
paper does not name GHK-Cu, so it establishes a category concern rather than a compound-specific
one.
VERIFIED 2026-07-19.
https://assets.ecri.org/PDF/ECRI%20and%20ISMP%20White%20Paper%20on%20Compounded%20Peptide%20Products.pdf

Second, this compound has an identity problem built into its market. The same name covers a
reviewed cosmetic ingredient applied to the skin and a grey-market injectable product. The
quality expectations for those two are not comparable, and documentation appropriate to one is
not evidence about the other. Any supplier package must state which product it describes and
must not borrow assurance from the other category.

## Section 1. Identity

Purpose: establish that the material is what the label says it is, and only that.

- [ ] **1.1 Stated chemical identity.** Full chemical name, the copper complex explicitly stated,
      and the distinction from uncomplexed GHK made explicit. UNMET.
- [ ] **1.2 CAS number and, for cosmetic products, the INCI name (Copper Tripeptide-1).** UNMET.
- [ ] **1.3 Confirmatory analytical identity testing on the specific lot,** by an orthogonal
      method set (for example mass spectrometry plus chromatographic retention against a
      reference standard). Identity asserted on a label without lot-level testing does not
      satisfy this. UNMET.
- [ ] **1.4 Sequence confirmation.** Documentation that the peptide sequence is correct, not
      merely that a peptide of approximately the right mass is present. UNMET.
- [ ] **1.5 Copper content and speciation.** Documentation of how much copper is present and in
      what form, since the complex and free copper are not the same material. UNMET.
- [ ] **1.6 Explicit product category statement.** Whether the material is intended as a cosmetic
      ingredient or otherwise, stated by the supplier, with no cross-category borrowing of
      evidence or safety review. UNMET.

## Section 2. Purity and impurities

Purpose: establish what else is in the material.

- [ ] **2.1 Assay and purity result on the specific lot,** with the method stated. UNMET.
- [ ] **2.2 Peptide-related impurity profile,** including truncated, deleted, and modified
      sequences, and aggregation state. This item is directly relevant to the immunogenicity
      concern reported for compounded injectable peptides. UNMET.
- [ ] **2.3 Residual solvents.** UNMET.
- [ ] **2.4 Heavy metals testing,** with results and limits stated. Note that this compound
      intentionally contains copper, so a heavy metals panel must distinguish specified copper
      from unintended metal contamination. UNMET.
- [ ] **2.5 Residual reagents and counterions from synthesis,** including trifluoroacetate where
      relevant. UNMET.
- [ ] **2.6 Microbiological limits for non-sterile material.** UNMET.

## Section 3. Sterility

Purpose: applies to any product presented as sterile. If a product is not presented as sterile,
that must be stated plainly rather than left ambiguous.

- [ ] **3.1 Sterility test result on the specific lot,** with method and compendial reference.
      UNMET.
- [ ] **3.2 Description of the sterilization or aseptic process,** and validation evidence for it.
      UNMET.
- [ ] **3.3 Container closure integrity documentation.** UNMET.
- [ ] **3.4 Facility registration status and inspection history** for the site performing the
      sterile operation. UNMET.

## Section 4. Endotoxin

Purpose: bacterial endotoxin can cause reactions even when a product is sterile.

- [ ] **4.1 Bacterial endotoxin test result on the specific lot,** with the method and the limit
      applied. UNMET.
- [ ] **4.2 Justification of the endotoxin limit** used, appropriate to the product presentation.
      UNMET.

## Section 5. Stability

Purpose: establish that the material remains what it is for as long as the label claims.

- [ ] **5.1 Stability data supporting the stated shelf life,** under the stated storage
      conditions. UNMET.
- [ ] **5.2 Stability-indicating method,** meaning an analytical method capable of detecting
      degradation, not only of confirming presence. UNMET.
- [ ] **5.3 In-use and post-opening stability** where relevant to the presentation. UNMET.
- [ ] **5.4 Photostability and temperature excursion data,** relevant to a metal-containing
      peptide. UNMET.
- [ ] **5.5 Copper complex integrity over shelf life,** meaning evidence the complex has not
      dissociated. UNMET.

## Section 6. Chain of custody

Purpose: establish that the tested material and the delivered material are the same material.

- [ ] **6.1 Named manufacturer and named manufacturing site,** not a trading company name alone.
      UNMET.
- [ ] **6.2 Country of origin.** UNMET.
- [ ] **6.3 Unbroken lot traceability** from the tested lot to the delivered unit. UNMET.
- [ ] **6.4 Documented storage and transport conditions,** including temperature control where
      required. UNMET.
- [ ] **6.5 Repackaging and relabeling disclosure,** including any intermediate handler. UNMET.
- [ ] **6.6 Written recall and adverse event handling procedure.** UNMET.

## Section 7. Certificate of analysis (COA) and third-party verification

Purpose: establish that the paperwork is real and independent.

- [ ] **7.1 A lot-specific COA** issued for the exact lot supplied, not a representative or
      example COA. UNMET.
- [ ] **7.2 The COA names the testing laboratory,** the test date, the methods, the specifications,
      and the actual results, not only pass or fail. UNMET.
- [ ] **7.3 The COA is traceable to the laboratory** and can be confirmed directly with that
      laboratory. UNMET.
- [ ] **7.4 Independent third-party retesting of the same lot** by a laboratory with no commercial
      relationship to the supplier, covering at minimum identity, purity, and where applicable
      sterility and endotoxin. UNMET.
- [ ] **7.5 Laboratory accreditation evidence** for both the supplier's laboratory and the
      third-party laboratory. UNMET.
- [ ] **7.6 Discrepancy procedure.** A written statement of what happens if the third-party result
      disagrees with the supplier COA. UNMET.

## Section 8. Regulatory and labelling documentation

Purpose: establish that claims made on and around the product are permissible and accurate.

- [ ] **8.1 Statement of regulatory status in each jurisdiction of sale,** dated and sourced.
      UNMET. Note that Xenios could not verify the current FDA compounding category status for
      this compound on 2026-07-19; every relevant fda.gov page returned HTTP 404 to automated
      retrieval. That verification is a prerequisite, not a formality.
- [ ] **8.2 Full label copy for review,** including every claim made on the product, the packaging,
      and any accompanying material. UNMET.
- [ ] **8.3 Confirmation that no claim on the label or in supplier marketing cites the cosmetic
      literature or a cosmetic safety review in support of a non-cosmetic product.** UNMET.
- [ ] **8.4 Confirmation that no trial statistic in supplier marketing is uncited.** Several
      specific trial statistics circulating in this compound's consumer market could not be
      located in PubMed or ClinicalTrials.gov on 2026-07-19. Any supplier repeating them must
      produce the identifier. UNMET.
- [ ] **8.5 Safety data sheet.** UNMET.
