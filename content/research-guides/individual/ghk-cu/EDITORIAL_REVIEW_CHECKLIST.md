# Editorial review and sign-off gate: GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide-1)

## Standing rule

**AI drafts never publish automatically.** No file in this folder reaches a member-facing surface
until every box below is checked by the named human responsible for that section, and until all
five sign-offs at the end are recorded with a name and a date.

An unchecked box blocks publication. A box may only be checked by the human who verified it, and
the person who checked it is recorded next to it. Checking a box on the basis of the draft's own
assertion is not verification.

Files in scope for this gate:

- GUIDE_DRAFT.md
- FAQ_DRAFT.md
- QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md
- this file

Every box below starts UNCHECKED.

---

## Section A. Content safety

- [ ] **A1.** No dosing anywhere. No amounts, mg, mcg, IU, mL, units, concentrations, frequency,
      timing, cycle length, titration, loading, stacking, reconstitution, or injection technique
      appears in any file.
- [ ] **A2.** Where a reader would expect dosing, the exact standard sentence appears:
      "Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research
      Guides."
- [ ] **A3.** No treatment direction, protocol, or regimen appears. Nothing in any file can be
      followed as instructions.
- [ ] **A4.** No sourcing or acquisition guidance appears, and no vendor, marketplace, or supplier
      is named as a place to obtain the compound.
- [ ] **A5.** No medical advice, no diagnosis, and no self-treatment framing appears.
- [ ] **A6.** The phrase "research use only" is not used anywhere, and no equivalent framing is
      used to imply human benefit.

## Section B. Outcome and efficacy language

- [ ] **B1.** No guaranteed outcome language anywhere. The words "will", "proven to", "restores",
      "cures", "eliminates", and "reverses" have each been searched for and confirmed absent as
      efficacy claims.
- [ ] **B2.** All efficacy statements use permitted framing: "has been studied for", "reported in",
      "investigated as", "associated with in <population>".
- [ ] **B3.** The two negative controlled human trials (PMID 1495150 and PMID 16847171) are
      presented prominently and are not softened, hedged into ambiguity, or buried below positive
      material.
- [ ] **B4.** No positive human claim rests on a study that tested a multi-ingredient formulation
      without that limitation being stated in the same passage.
- [ ] **B5.** Manufacturer sponsorship and author financial relationships are disclosed wherever a
      sponsored study is cited.

## Section C. Safety language

- [ ] **C1.** The words "safe", "well tolerated", and "no side effects" do not appear as general
      statements of fact about the compound.
- [ ] **C2.** The safety section is framed as categories of concern, not as reassurance, and the
      absence of a listed concern is explicitly stated not to imply absence of risk.
- [ ] **C3.** The absence of human safety data for systemic administration is stated plainly and
      is not softened.
- [ ] **C4.** The FDA immunogenicity concern is presented as an unverified secondary attribution
      and is not stated as a confirmed FDA position, unless a human has since verified it from
      fda.gov and updated the text.
- [ ] **C5.** Every line labelled [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]
      has either been sourced by a human or removed. The label is not permitted to survive to
      publication.
      Current instances requiring resolution: the copper tolerance line in the safety section, and
      the first three interaction categories in the interactions section of GUIDE_DRAFT.md.

## Section D. Preclinical labelling (highest-risk item for this compound)

- [ ] **D1.** Every preclinical finding carries its species or model in the same sentence as the
      finding. Confirmed by reading each bullet, not by trusting the draft.
- [ ] **D2.** No bare mechanism sentence anywhere reads as a human fact.
- [ ] **D3.** The in vitro and ex vivo record (PMID 37062921) is labelled as such and is not
      presented as human evidence.
- [ ] **D4.** The preprint record (PMID 42245779) is labelled as a preprint and as not peer
      reviewed.
- [ ] **D5.** The nematode record (PMID 42084774) carries its distance from human outcomes in the
      same passage.
- [ ] **D6.** The proposed mechanism section is labelled as proposed throughout, with species and
      system noted, and states that a plausible mechanism is not evidence of a clinical effect.
- [ ] **D7.** Entries for which only a title or bibliographic record was retrieved say so, and no
      effect direction or size is asserted for them beyond what was actually retrieved.

## Section E. Citation integrity

- [ ] **E1.** Every PMID in every file has been opened by a human and confirmed to match the
      described study.
- [ ] **E2.** Every NCT number has been opened by a human and confirmed to match the described
      study, including sponsor, phase, status, enrollment, and the absence of posted results.
- [ ] **E3.** Every URL in every file has been opened by a human and resolves to the cited source.
- [ ] **E4.** No author name, year, journal name, DOI, PMID, or NCT number appears that was not
      retrieved on 2026-07-19 or verified by a human since.
- [ ] **E5.** The vendor-claimed trials (a 2022 alopecia RCT, a 2023 phase II scar trial, a 31.6
      percent wrinkle volume result) appear only as a warning about the information environment,
      never in an evidence table, and are never presented as evidence.
- [ ] **E6.** Every retrieval failure is disclosed as a failure rather than omitted, and no claim
      rests on a failed retrieval beyond what is explicitly marked unverified.
- [ ] **E7.** The two unread review articles (PMID 41966639 and PMID 39963574) have been read by a
      human, and the Guide has been revised if they contain human evidence not currently
      reflected.
- [ ] **E8.** No citation has been reconstructed from memory by anyone at any stage.

## Section F. Regulatory verification

- [ ] **F1.** Every regulatory statement carries a date, a jurisdiction, and a source URL.
- [ ] **F2.** The FDA compounding bulk substance status (503A and 503B) has been checked directly
      on fda.gov by a human, and the Guide text has been corrected to match what was found. This
      is the single most important open gap in the record.
- [ ] **F3.** The CIR safety assessment primary text has been obtained by a human, and the panel's
      exact conclusion and the explicit inclusion of Copper Tripeptide-1 within scope have been
      confirmed or corrected.
- [ ] **F4.** The current WADA Prohibited List has been checked directly by a human, and either a
      sourced statement has been added or the current no-claim position has been retained
      deliberately.
- [ ] **F5.** The drug approval status statement is confirmed to be framed as a negative finding
      from absence of an approval record, not as a retrieved FDA statement of non-approval.
- [ ] **F6.** All regulatory dates have been re-verified within the publication window, and the
      date shown to members reflects the actual verification date rather than the draft date.

## Section G. Claim grades

- [ ] **G1.** Every claim in GUIDE_DRAFT.md carries a grade, and grades are applied to individual
      claims rather than to the compound as a whole.
- [ ] **G2.** Grades default to D or G unless a specific retrieved human study supports better.
- [ ] **G3.** The claims contradicted by the negative controlled trials are graded PROHIBITED, and
      those claims appear on no member-facing surface.
- [ ] **G4.** No claim is graded A or B anywhere in this Guide, given the state of the evidence, or
      any exception is explicitly justified by a named reviewer.
- [ ] **G5.** Manufacturer-sponsored uncontrolled study findings are graded E and are not upgraded
      by repetition.

## Section H. Prohibited claims

- [ ] **H1.** No anti-aging outcome claim for humans appears.
- [ ] **H2.** No hair growth or hair loss outcome claim appears.
- [ ] **H3.** No scar reduction outcome claim appears.
- [ ] **H4.** No wound healing outcome claim for humans appears.
- [ ] **H5.** No wrinkle or skin quality outcome claim appears.
- [ ] **H6.** No claim of any kind is made for injected or systemic use.
- [ ] **H7.** No disease treatment, prevention, or mitigation claim appears in any file.
- [ ] **H8.** No claim borrows cosmetic safety review or topical trial data in support of a
      non-cosmetic product.

## Section I. Disclosure and framing

- [ ] **I1.** The Guide states plainly that it is not medical advice and not a product listing.
- [ ] **I2.** The topical versus injectable distinction is unmistakable in the summary, not only
      deep in the body.
- [ ] **I3.** The AI-drafted, human-reviewed provenance of the content is disclosed to the member
      in the form required by Xenios policy.
- [ ] **I4.** Any Xenios commercial interest relevant to this compound is disclosed, or the absence
      of one is stated.
- [ ] **I5.** The quality checklist state (currently 40 of 40 items unmet) is accurately reflected
      wherever quality is referenced.
- [ ] **I6.** Correction history is present, seeded, and dated.

## Section J. House style

- [ ] **J1.** Zero em dashes in any file. Confirmed by search, not by reading.
- [ ] **J2.** No hype, no sales language, no flattery, no exclamation marks.
- [ ] **J3.** Every technical term is glossed the first time it appears.
- [ ] **J4.** All wording is original. No competitor, retailer, or manufacturer text is copied or
      lightly paraphrased. Confirmed by spot-checking distinctive phrasing against the sources.
- [ ] **J5.** Plain, direct English throughout, readable by an intelligent adult with no
      scientific training.
