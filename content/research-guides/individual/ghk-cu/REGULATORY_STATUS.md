# Regulatory status: GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide-1)

**Status checked on 2026-07-19. This status must be re-verified before publication and before
any member-facing surface repeats it.** Regulatory positions change, and several of the
statements below could not be verified against a primary source this session.

**Approval, registration, review, or permitted status in one jurisdiction is not approval,
registration, review, or permitted status in another.** A cosmetic ingredient review in one
country says nothing about drug approval anywhere, and says nothing at all about an injectable
product.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## United States

### 1. Drug approval status, FDA

**Statement.** No FDA-approved drug product containing GHK-Cu was identified for any medical
indication, by any route, in the sources retrieved. GHK-Cu appears in the United States market
as a cosmetic ingredient carrying the INCI name Copper Tripeptide-1, not as an approved drug.

**Important qualification.** This is a negative finding drawn from the absence of any approval
record in the sources retrieved. It is not drawn from a retrieved FDA statement of
non-approval. Any member-facing wording must preserve that distinction.

**Date checked.** 2026-07-19.

**Source URL.** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copper_peptide_GHK-Cu

**Reviewer action.** Confirm directly against FDA drug approval databases and record the date.

### 2. Compounding bulk drug substances, 503A and 503B, FDA

**Statement.** COULD NOT VERIFY FROM A PRIMARY SOURCE. Multiple secondary sources retrieved
this session assert that GHK-Cu has been handled differently by route, with injectable GHK-Cu
treated as raising safety concerns (immunogenicity from aggregation, and peptide-related
impurities) while non-injectable use was treated more permissively, and that category status
changed during the 2023 to 2026 period. Direct fetches of the FDA 503A bulks page, the 503B
bulks page, the page on bulk drug substances that may present significant safety risks, and the
FDA nominated-bulks PDF all returned HTTP 404 to automated retrieval.

The route-based distinction is directionally consistent across independent secondary sources,
but the specific category assignment and its current date are not verified. Nothing in this
section may be published in any form until it is confirmed against fda.gov by a human.

**Date checked.** 2026-07-19.

**Source URL (attempted).**
https://www.fda.gov/drugs/human-drug-compounding/certain-bulk-drug-substances-use-compounding-may-present-significant-safety-risks

**Reviewer action.** This is the single most important open item in the whole record. Confirm
GHK-Cu's current status on the compounding bulk substance lists, including any distinction by
route, and record the date checked.

### 3. Cosmetic Ingredient Review, expert panel assessment

**Statement.** A CIR Expert Panel safety assessment titled "Safety Assessment of Tripeptide-1,
Hexapeptide-12, Their Metal Salts and Fatty Acyl Derivatives, and Palmitoyl Tetrapeptide-7 as
Used in Cosmetics" exists, published in the International Journal of Toxicology in 2018, and
its ingredient scope includes the metal salts of tripeptide-1, the category into which Copper
Tripeptide-1 falls. Per secondary retrieval, the panel's stated conclusion was that these
ingredients are safe in cosmetics under the practices of use described in the assessment.

**Primary verification failed.** The CIR PDF returned unparseable compressed streams, the
journal page returned HTTP 403, and a PubMed query for the assessment returned zero results.
The conclusion wording, and the explicit inclusion of Copper Tripeptide-1 by name within scope,
require human confirmation.

**Two constraints on use, even after verification.** First, CIR is an industry expert panel
review of cosmetic ingredients, not a drug approval and not a government authorization.
Second, a cosmetic-use conclusion says nothing about an injectable product, and must never be
cited in support of one.

**Date checked.** 2026-07-19.

**Source URL (attempted).**
https://www.cir-safety.org/sites/default/files/tripep062014final.pdf

## Standing notes for any member-facing surface

1. Every regulatory statement published must carry its jurisdiction, the date it was checked,
   and a working source URL.
2. Cosmetic ingredient status and drug approval status are different things, and neither
   transfers to the other.
3. Topical and injectable products are treated separately throughout this record and must be
   presented separately to a member. Applying a cosmetic-ingredient review or topical trial
   data to an injectable product is the most common way a reader is misled about this
   compound.
4. Nothing in this document is medical, legal, or regulatory advice, and nothing here should
   be read as guidance on obtaining or using any product.
5. Re-verify every item above before publication and record the new date checked.
