## How do I raise a concern about something I received?

Through member support, with the order reference, a description of what is wrong, and photographs
of the item and the packaging where the problem is visible.

Photographs matter more here than they would elsewhere. Most of the eligible circumstances below
turn on physical evidence, and evidence captured before packaging is discarded is the difference
between a resolvable concern and an unresolvable one. Keep the outer packaging until the concern is
closed.

Honest current state: nothing has shipped, because commerce is blocked or pending written approval
in every lane. This policy is being written before it operates rather than improvised after the
first problem arrives.

The specific intake route, response time, and the person or party who handles a concern are NOT
CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions. Fulfillment ownership is agreed in principle for roughly
the first 60 days (Mitch for peptides and Quantum, xenios for supplements), but the written record
covering who holds title, who ships, and who handles a return is incomplete.

## Can I return a product because I changed my mind?

No. There are no ordinary returns.

Change-of-mind returns, buyer's-remorse returns, and unopened-item returns are not accepted. This is
a real restriction and it is stated before purchase rather than discovered afterward.

The reason is chain of custody. Once a unit leaves controlled handling, Xenios Research cannot
verify how it was stored, at what temperature, for how long, or whether it was opened. A returned
unit therefore cannot honestly be resold to another member, and cannot be represented as being
within specification. Accepting such returns would mean either destroying the stock quietly or
passing an unverifiable unit to someone else, and the second option is not acceptable.

Buy accordingly. If you are uncertain about an item, the correct time to resolve that uncertainty is
before ordering.

## What circumstances may be eligible for a replacement or refund?

Five, each requiring verification:

1. **Damage.** The item arrived physically damaged.
2. **Loss.** The shipment did not arrive.
3. **Incorrect item.** What arrived is not what was ordered.
4. **Missing item.** Part of the order is absent from a delivered shipment.
5. **Temperature compromise.** The shipment arrived outside its required storage conditions.

Where one of these is verified, the intended remedy is a full replacement or a full refund. Not a
partial credit, not a store credit, and not a discount on a future order.

This remains subject to final counsel review. Outside counsel has not been engaged for this scope,
so the policy above is the committed intent rather than a settled legal position, and it may change
before it operates.

## What does "verified" mean in practice?

It means the concern is substantiated by evidence rather than accepted or refused on assertion.

In practice that involves the order record, the photographs you provide, carrier tracking and
delivery records, and, where relevant, the packaging itself. The point of naming verification
explicitly is that it cuts both ways: it is a bar you have to clear, and it is a commitment that a
cleared concern is honored rather than argued down.

The specific evidence standard, who applies it, the deadline for raising a concern after delivery,
and the appeal path if a concern is refused are NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions. Those
are policy decisions that require the fulfillment arrangement and counsel review to be complete.

## How would temperature compromise be verified?

Today, it could not be, and that is the most uncomfortable answer on this page.

Temperature-controlled shipping has not been validated. There is no packaging specification, no
transit temperature data, no defined excursion threshold, and no documented response when a shipment
arrives outside range. Storage conditions themselves are unknown for every product, including the
acceptable range in transit and after receipt.

NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions.

Without a defined threshold there is no line to measure an arrival against, which means temperature
compromise is currently a named eligible circumstance with no working verification method behind it.
Validated cold chain handling is a blocking item for commerce, and it is tracked as one rather than
deferred.

## Who actually handles my concern, and how quickly?

Unresolved.

For roughly the first 60 days, fulfillment ownership is agreed in principle as Mitch for peptides
and Quantum, and xenios for supplements. What is not documented is who holds title to inventory, who
physically ships, who processes a replacement or refund, and what happens to these arrangements at
the end of that initial period.

Response times, resolution times, and support routing all appear on member-facing surfaces, and none
of them can be honestly stated against an undocumented arrangement. Rather than publish a service
window that no one has committed to in writing, this answer records the gap.

## What if my concern is about how I feel rather than about the package?

Contact a qualified healthcare professional. In an emergency, contact your local emergency services
immediately rather than support.

Xenios Research does not provide medical, clinical, legal, or financial advice, does not diagnose,
and cannot assess an individual health situation. That boundary holds in every channel, including
support, and it is not a liability formality. Support staff are not clinicians, and a research
education platform is not a place to be triaged.

Guides describe what has been studied and what remains contested. They are a set of questions to
take to a professional, not an answer to substitute for one.
