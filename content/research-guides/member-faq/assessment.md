## What is the assessment, and why is it mandatory?

The assessment is a structured set of questions about your goals, your current routine, your sleep,
your training, your constraints, and the things you have already tried. It is the input that makes
anything else on the member site specific to you rather than generic.

It is mandatory because without it, a Blueprint is not a Blueprint. It would be a template with your
name at the top, which is precisely the kind of thing Xenios Research exists to not produce. A
platform that personalizes nothing while charging a monthly fee for personalization is selling an
impression, and the assessment is what stops that from being the case here.

## How long does it take, and when is it due?

Roughly 10 minutes. It is one sitting for most members.

It is due within 3 days of activation. The window is short on purpose. The assessment is most
accurate when your reasons for joining are still fresh, and a deadline measured in days rather than
weeks keeps the account from sitting in a half-started state where you are paying monthly for
something you have not switched on.

You can save partway through and return, as long as you complete it inside the window.

## What happens if I miss the 3 day window?

Your membership stays active and you keep being billed. What you do not get is a Blueprint, because
there is nothing to build one from, and the tracker stays locked.

You can complete the assessment late and proceed normally from that point. Nothing is permanently
forfeited by missing the window.

The blunt version: the 3 day rule is an expectation, not a penalty gate. The actual cost of missing
it is that you pay for a month in which the membership does very little for you.

## What is the tracker, and when does it unlock?

The tracker is the ongoing record you keep inside the member site: what you actually did, how you
actually slept and trained and felt, and what changed over time. It is the thing that turns a
one-time snapshot into a trend.

It unlocks after you complete the assessment, not at activation. The reason is that a tracker
without a baseline records motion without direction. The assessment is the baseline.

## Is this a medical assessment? Are you diagnosing me?

No, and no.

The assessment is not a clinical intake, not a diagnostic instrument, and not a screening tool. It
is not reviewed by a clinician, and Xenios Research does not employ one in this process. No answer
you give produces a diagnosis, a clinical interpretation, or a treatment direction, and no output
from it should be read as one.

If something in your health is genuinely concerning you, the correct next step is a qualified
clinician, not this form. That remains true no matter how thorough the assessment feels while you
are filling it in.

## What do you do with my answers?

Your answers feed your Blueprint and your monthly and quarterly plans. Samuel Boadu reads them when
reviewing your Blueprint, which is the point of a human review.

Your answers are personal health context and are treated as sensitive. They are not used to generate
public marketing content, and they are not shared with suppliers.

## Can I change my answers later?

Yes. Circumstances change, and an assessment frozen at activation would get less useful every month.

Updating your assessment does not automatically rewrite an existing Blueprint. A material change
(a new injury, a significant change in training, a change in what you are actually trying to
achieve) is a reason to request a Blueprint update, which is a separate action described in the
Blueprint FAQ.

## What if I do not want to answer something?

Skip it. Very few fields are strictly required.

The honest tradeoff is that every skipped field makes your Blueprint more generic in that area. If
you decline to describe your training, the plan cannot say anything specific about your training. It
is your information and your call, and the effect of withholding it is a less specific document, not
a rejected account.

Do not overstate or invent answers to get a more detailed plan. A confident plan built on inaccurate
inputs is worse than a cautious plan built on accurate ones.
