# Founding Membership

Member education. This file is an AI draft at workflow state `draft`. Nothing here is approved,
published, or member-visible until Samuel Boadu reviews and publishes it.

Where something is committed but not yet operating, this page says so directly. A membership FAQ
that describes features as though they already work is the fastest way to lose a member's trust,
and it is not how Xenios Research is built.

---

## What am I actually paying for?

Founding Membership costs $50 to activate once, then $25 each month while the membership stays
active. There is no annual plan.

What the fee buys is access, not products. Specifically, it buys entry to the private member
website, the assessment, your Blueprint, the Guide library, and the monthly and quarterly planning
formats (Xenios 30 and Xenios 90).

What it does not buy is anything shipped to you. Membership and commerce are separate. Peptide
commerce is not authorized. Quantum commerce is not authorized. Supplement reseller activity is not
authorized. Every commerce path is either blocked or pending written approval, so no membership
tier grants purchase rights today.

If you joined expecting the membership to be a storefront subscription, that expectation is wrong,
and it is better to know that now than after the first monthly charge.

## Why is there a $50 activation charge on top of the monthly $25?

The activation covers the one-time work that happens before you have an account at all: reviewing
your application, running identity and age verification, provisioning a secured account, and
producing your first Blueprint after your assessment.

That work happens once per member. Charging it once, rather than folding it into a higher monthly
rate that every long-term member keeps paying, is the more honest structure.

The activation is charged after your application is approved, not when you apply. Applying is free,
and approval is not guaranteed.

## Why do I have to be 21 or older, and why do you verify identity?

The 21 and older requirement is a Xenios Research policy, applied to every member with no
exceptions and no discretion. It is not a suggestion tied to a checkbox.

Identity and age verification exist because the subject matter here is research education about
compounds that are not consumer products. An education platform covering that material has an
obligation to know that the person reading it is an adult, and that the account belongs to the
person it claims to belong to. Verification also protects you, because it makes account takeover
substantially harder.

The uncomfortable part: verification means submitting identity documentation to a verification
process, and if verification fails, you do not get an account. Approval is reviewed by a human, and
Xenios Research can decline an application without providing a detailed reason.

## What am I agreeing to before I pay?

The agreements come before the payment screen, deliberately. You review and accept them first, and
only then is a payment method requested. You should not discover the terms of a thing after you
have paid for it.

The agreement set covers the membership terms, the privacy terms, and the research and education
boundaries (what Xenios Research does, and what it explicitly does not do, including that it does
not provide medical, clinical, legal, or financial advice).

Read them rather than clicking through. The section worth your attention most is the boundary
section, because it is the section that defines what you are not getting.

## Why is multi-factor authentication mandatory?

Your account holds your assessment answers and your Blueprint. That is personal health context, and
a password alone is not adequate protection for it. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is therefore
required rather than offered, and it cannot be switched off from account settings.

Honest current state: MFA enforcement for members is committed policy and is not yet live in
production as of 2026-07-19. An internal security review of the current codebase records that no
member MFA enforcement, no passkey support, no device-session view, and no session revocation
interface exists yet. Those are on the required-changes list, not the completed list. Until they
ship, treat your password as the only thing standing in front of your account, and make it a strong
and unique one.

## How do I cancel, and what happens to time I have already paid for?

Cancellation is self-service from your account. You do not have to email support, wait for a reply,
or explain yourself to anyone. Making a member argue with a person in order to leave is a retention
tactic, not a service, and Xenios Research does not use it.

The part that people dislike, stated plainly: cancelling ends your access immediately, and any
remaining paid time is forfeited. If you cancel on day 3 of a paid month, you lose the other 27
days. There is no proration and no partial refund.

That is a real cost, and you should factor it in before cancelling mid-cycle. If you intend to
leave, cancelling near the end of a billing period rather than the start costs you less.

Honest current state: self-service cancellation is the committed design. The current production
surface uses support-mediated cancellation, and the self-service path is an identified required
change rather than a shipped one. Until it ships, cancellation runs through support.

## Does the membership make me a customer for peptides or supplements?

No.

Membership is education and planning. It is not a purchase authorization, a wholesale account, a
practitioner account, or a supply relationship. Guides are member-only, but completing a Guide is
not a prerequisite for commerce and does not unlock commerce, because commerce is not open.

No Xenios Research surface should ever present a Guide as a step toward obtaining a compound. If
you encounter a page that reads that way, it is a content defect, and reporting it is genuinely
useful.

## What is honestly not built yet?

As of 2026-07-19, the following are committed but not operating: member MFA and passkey support,
device-session visibility and session revocation, self-service cancellation, and a member dashboard
that replaces the current static activation-pending page. Account update and deletion behavior is
described in some existing copy at a level that runs ahead of what is implemented, and that copy is
being corrected rather than defended.

Commerce, in all lanes, is blocked or pending written approval. No date is committed for any of it,
because committing to a date that depends on legal review and supplier documentation would be a
guess presented as a plan.

If a feature matters enough that you would cancel over it not existing, cancel. That is a
reasonable response to an accurate description, and the description is not going to be softened to
prevent it.
