# Waitlists

Member education. This file is an AI draft at workflow state `draft`. Nothing here is approved,
published, or member-visible until Samuel Boadu reviews and publishes it.

A waitlist is the honest version of an out-of-stock page. It tells you where you stand without
pretending to know when something arrives.

---

## What is a waitlist here?

A waitlist is a queue attached to a specific product record that has no available stock. Joining it
records that you want the product and places you in line behind everyone who joined earlier.

That is the whole mechanism. It is not a reservation, not a pre-order, not a deposit, and not a
purchase. Nothing is charged, and no unit is set aside in your name.

## Can I join a waitlist today?

No.

Commerce is blocked or pending written approval in every lane (peptides, Quantum, and supplements),
and a waitlist is part of the commerce surface rather than the education surface. Until commerce is
authorized in writing, there is nothing to queue for.

A product record can exist in the catalog while its commerce state stays disabled. Seeing a record
does not mean the record is orderable, backorderable, or waitlistable.

## What will I actually see if I am on a waitlist?

Two things: your position in the queue, and the current status of the product.

Position is a number, shown to you and only to you. Status is a plain-language state describing
where the product stands rather than when it arrives.

What you will not see is anyone else's identity. The queue is anonymous in both directions. You
cannot see who is ahead of you, they cannot see you, and no member's participation in a waitlist is
disclosed to another member.

## Why won't you show me a restock date?

Because Xenios Research does not know it, and a date presented without knowledge is a guess wearing
the clothes of a commitment.

Restock timing depends on supplier lead times, the written fulfillment arrangement, validated
shipping, and commerce authorization. None of those are settled. The supplier of record is NOT
CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions, and the operational record of who holds inventory and who
ships is incomplete.

An estimated date would be more comfortable to read and more useful to plan around, which is exactly
why it would be more damaging when it slipped. Status without a date is less satisfying and more
accurate, and accuracy is the trade being made deliberately.

If a date ever appears on a waitlist surface, it means someone put a sourced, written commitment
behind it.

## Does my position guarantee I get a unit?

No.

A position tells you how many people joined ahead of you. It does not reserve inventory, and it does
not promise that inventory arrives in a quantity that reaches your number. If a restock is smaller
than the queue, people below the cutoff do not receive a unit in that cycle.

Position also does not survive every possible change. If a product record changes materially, or if
a product is never authorized for sale, the queue attached to it does not entitle anyone to
anything.

Stating this plainly is better than letting a queue position feel like an order confirmation.

## Can I pay to hold my place or move up the queue?

No. There is no paid priority, no expedited placement, and no membership tier that reorders the
queue.

Founding Membership grants access to the member website, the assessment, your Blueprint, the Guide
library, and the planning formats. It does not grant purchase rights, and it does not grant queue
advantage. Selling position in a scarcity queue would turn an availability problem into a revenue
stream, and that is not a business Xenios Research is building.

There is also no price to charge for it, since final pricing is not set for any record.

## What happens when stock arrives?

In the design, members are notified in queue order and given a window in which to act before the
position passes to the next member.

The specifics are NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions: how notification is delivered, how
long the window stays open, whether an unclaimed position returns to the queue or is forfeited, and
whether partial restocks are allocated strictly in order. Those depend on the fulfillment
arrangement and on commerce systems that do not exist yet.

This answer deliberately stops at the design intent rather than describing a claim window that has
not been decided.

## Can I leave a waitlist?

Yes. Leaving is self-service in the design, and it takes effect immediately.

The cost of leaving is your position. Rejoining later places you at the back of the queue, because
a queue that lets people return to their old position is not a queue. If you are unsure, staying
costs nothing, since no charge is attached to a waitlist.

## What is honestly not built yet?

As of 2026-07-19, waitlists do not operate. There is no queue, no position, no status feed, and no
notification path, because no product is available for commerce in the first place.

Unresolved and openly tracked: the supplier of record, unit format and count, storage conditions and
shelf life, validated temperature-controlled shipping, final pricing, brand reseller authorization,
and the written fulfillment arrangement covering who holds inventory and who ships.

No date is committed for waitlists opening, for the same reason no restock date is given.
