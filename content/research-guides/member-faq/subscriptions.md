# Subscriptions

Member education. This file is an AI draft at workflow state `draft`. Nothing here is approved,
published, or member-visible until Samuel Boadu reviews and publishes it.

This page describes how subscriptions are designed to work. It is written before subscriptions
operate, and it says so in each answer rather than at the bottom in small type.

---

## Can I start a subscription today?

No.

Peptide commerce is not authorized. Quantum commerce is not authorized. Supplement reseller
activity is not authorized. Every commerce path is blocked or pending written approval, which means
there is nothing to subscribe to regardless of what any product page appears to offer.

A product record existing in the catalog is not the same as that product being purchasable. Catalog
state and commerce state are separate on purpose, so that the research and education work can be
built and reviewed honestly without a checkout button quietly appearing beside it.

If a surface ever presents a subscription as available, that is a defect, and reporting it helps.

## What subscription frequencies are planned?

Three: every 30 days, every 60 days, or every 90 days.

That is the full set. There is no weekly option, no custom interval in days, and no annual prepay.
Three intervals cover the realistic range without turning a shipping schedule into a configuration
exercise.

Which products would be eligible for which intervals is NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier
questions. Interval eligibility depends on unit format and storage behavior, and neither is
documented yet.

## What am I able to change once a subscription is running?

The design gives you direct control over all of the following, from your account, without contacting
support:

- your next charge date
- your next shipment date
- pausing the subscription
- skipping a single upcoming shipment
- rescheduling a shipment to a different date
- the quantity per shipment
- the frequency (moving between 30, 60, and 90 days)
- the payment method on file
- cancelling the subscription outright

The principle behind that list is simple. A subscription you cannot easily stop is not a
convenience, it is a trap, and cancellation sits in the same settings area as everything else rather
than behind a support conversation.

Honest current state: this is the committed design, not a shipped feature. None of these controls
operate today because no subscription can exist today.

## What discount do I get for subscribing?

Unknown. Discount percentages are not set.

No subscription discount, no member price, no compare-at price, and no subscription price has been
determined for any record. Pricing is an open item held by Samuel Boadu, and it is not being
estimated here.

This matters more than it might seem. If this page printed a plausible-sounding percentage, you
would reasonably plan around it, and a number invented for the sake of a complete-looking FAQ is
still an invented number. There is no percentage to give you, so none is given.

Prices and any subscription discount appear only after they are set, and no record becomes
commerce-enabled without one.

## Why are the next charge date and the next shipment date separate?

Because they answer different questions, and collapsing them hides the one members actually care
about.

The charge date is when money moves. The shipment date is when a package leaves. Showing a single
combined date forces you to guess which event a given date refers to, and the guess is usually
wrong at exactly the moment it matters, such as when you are trying to move a delivery before
travel.

Both dates are member-editable in the design, and both are shown explicitly rather than inferred.

The operational sequencing between them (how far in advance a charge occurs relative to a shipment,
and what happens if you move one and not the other) is NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions.
That sequencing depends on fulfillment arrangements that are not yet documented in writing.

## If I pause a subscription, does my membership pause too?

No. They are separate, and they bill separately.

Founding Membership is $50 to activate once, then $25 each month while the membership stays active.
That charge is for access to the member website, the assessment, your Blueprint, the Guide library,
and the planning formats. It continues whether or not any subscription exists, and pausing a
subscription does not reduce or pause it.

The reverse also holds in the design: cancelling a subscription does not cancel your membership.
Cancelling your membership is a separate action with its own consequences, described in the
Founding Membership FAQ.

## Does changing the quantity mean changing how much I take?

No, and the distinction is deliberate.

Quantity in a subscription is a commerce control. It sets how many units are included in a
shipment, which affects the size of the charge and how often you need shipments. It is not a
personal usage setting, and it should not be read as one.

Dosing information is intentionally excluded from every Xenios Research surface, including this one.
Guides, product records, and account settings do not carry amounts, concentrations, frequency of
use, timing, cycle length, or administration information, and adjusting a quantity slider is not a
substitute for a conversation with a qualified professional.

## What happens to a paused subscription over time?

In the design, a pause holds the subscription in place without charging you and without shipping.
It is not a cancellation, and resuming does not require reapplying or being re-approved.

What is NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions: whether a pause has a maximum duration, what
happens to a paused subscription if a product becomes unavailable, and whether a paused subscription
retains any queue position for a product that is out of stock. Those depend on inventory and
fulfillment arrangements that are not documented yet.

Rather than describe a behavior that has not been decided, this answer stops here.

## What is honestly not built yet?

As of 2026-07-19, none of this operates. There is no live subscription, no charge, no shipment, and
no member-facing control surface for any of it, because commerce in every lane is blocked or pending
written approval.

Also unresolved and openly tracked: final pricing and any subscription discount, unit format and
count for peptide products, storage conditions and shelf life, validated temperature-controlled
shipping, and the written fulfillment arrangement that determines who ships and who handles a
problem.

No date is committed for subscriptions opening. A date here would depend on legal review, supplier
documentation, and written brand authorization, none of which are complete, and presenting a guess
as a plan is the thing this library exists to avoid.
