## What is a Blueprint?

Your Blueprint is a written document that takes your assessment answers and turns them into a
structured view of where you are, what you are working toward, and what the sensible order of
operations looks like.

It covers the whole picture rather than one lever: sleep, training, nutrition, recovery, stress,
and the habits underneath all of them. It also names what Xenios Research is not going to promise
you, which is a section most platforms leave out.

It is a planning document. It is not a prescription, not a protocol, and not a set of instructions
to follow.

## What is the difference between a Preliminary Blueprint and a Samuel-Reviewed Blueprint?

A **Preliminary Blueprint** is generated from your assessment without a human having read it yet.
It is available quickly, and it is genuinely useful for orientation, but it is a first pass. It can
be generic in places, it can miss context that a person would catch, and it can weight something
incorrectly.

A **Samuel-Reviewed Blueprint** has been read line by line by Samuel Boadu, corrected, and
published. The reasoning has been checked against what you actually wrote, not just against the
pattern your answers resemble.

The distinction is displayed on the document itself, always. You should never have to guess which
one you are reading. Any Blueprint you receive tells you its own review state on its face.

## What does the 48 hour review commitment cover?

The commitment is that a Preliminary Blueprint moves to a Samuel-Reviewed state within 48 elapsed
hours of it being generated.

Elapsed hours, not business hours. A Blueprint generated on Friday evening is reviewed by Sunday
evening, not by Tuesday. Measuring in business days is how a two day promise quietly becomes a five
day one, and that is not the commitment being made.

What the 48 hours does not cover: it is a commitment to a review, not to an outcome you like. The
review may conclude that your Preliminary Blueprint was substantially correct and change very
little. That still counts as the commitment being met.

## What do the status labels mean?

Every Blueprint carries exactly one label:

- **Preliminary.** Generated from your assessment. No human has reviewed it yet. Read it as a first
  pass.
- **Samuel Review Pending.** In the queue, inside the 48 hour window. Content is unchanged from
  Preliminary until the review lands.
- **Samuel Reviewed.** Read, corrected where needed, and published by Samuel Boadu. This is the
  version to work from.
- **Updated.** A previously reviewed Blueprint that has been revised, usually because your
  circumstances changed or you requested a change. The revision date is shown.
- **More Information Needed.** The review found a gap that has to be closed before the Blueprint
  can be responsibly finished.

## What does "More Information Needed" actually mean, and what do I do?

It means the review reached a point where continuing would require guessing about you, and guessing
was the wrong call.

Common reasons: a stated goal that conflicts with a stated constraint, an injury or limitation
mentioned without enough context to plan around, or a skipped assessment section that the rest of
the plan depends on.

What you do is answer the specific questions attached to the label. They are shown with the
Blueprint, and they are narrow rather than a request to redo the assessment. Once you answer, the
Blueprint returns to review.

This label is not a criticism of your answers and it is not a delay tactic. A document that quietly
fills a gap with an assumption is less useful than one that says out loud where the gap is.

## Does my Blueprint tell me what to take, and how much?

No.

Dosing information is intentionally excluded from every Xenios Research surface, including your
Blueprint. There are no amounts, no schedules, no timing, no cycle lengths, and no administration
guidance anywhere in the system, and there is no membership tier or review state that unlocks them.

Your Blueprint also does not direct you to obtain any compound. Where a Guide is relevant to
something in your plan, it is linked as education about what a compound has been studied for. A
link is not a recommendation to acquire it, and peptide commerce is not authorized in any case.

If you are here specifically for dosing direction, this platform does not provide it and is not
going to. That is a design decision, not a gap waiting to be filled.

## Is a Blueprint medical advice?

No. It is not medical, clinical, legal, or financial advice, it is not a diagnosis, and it is not a
treatment plan.

Samuel Boadu reviews your Blueprint as the founder of Xenios Research, not as a clinician. The
review is a judgment call about coherence, specificity, and whether the plan actually reflects what
you wrote. It is not a clinical sign-off, and it should not be treated as one.

Nothing in a Blueprint substitutes for a qualified clinician, and nothing in it should be used to
delay seeing one.

## What if I disagree with my Blueprint?

Say so. A Blueprint you do not believe in is a document you are not going to use, which makes it
worthless regardless of how well-reasoned it is.

Disagreement is a legitimate reason to request an update, and it is handled the same way as a
change in circumstances. Explain what you think is wrong and why. The revised version carries the
**Updated** label with its revision date, and the earlier version remains visible so you can see
what changed.

You are not required to justify the disagreement to anyone's satisfaction. It is your plan.
