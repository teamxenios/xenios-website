## What is a Xenios 30?

A Xenios 30 is a monthly plan. It takes the direction set in your Blueprint and turns it into what
the next 30 days actually look like: the focus for the month, the specific things you are working
on, and the small number of measures you are tracking.

The Blueprint answers where you are going. The Xenios 30 answers what you are doing between now and
the end of the month. Most people do not need the whole map every day. They need this month.

## What is in one?

A month's plan holds a stated focus (usually one primary area rather than five competing ones),
the practical actions attached to that focus, what you are tracking in the tracker, and a short
note on what would count as the month having gone well.

That last part matters. A plan without a stated definition of a good outcome is difficult to
evaluate honestly at the end, because it can be reinterpreted after the fact to match whatever
happened.

## Does it tell me what to take?

No. Dosing information is intentionally excluded, and a Xenios 30 does not direct you to obtain any
compound.

A monthly plan works with the things you control directly: training, sleep, food, recovery,
consistency, and the habits underneath them. Where a Guide is relevant to something in your plan, it
appears as education about what a compound has been studied for. That is not a recommendation to
acquire it, and commerce is not authorized in any lane.

## How does it relate to my Blueprint?

The Blueprint is the parent document and changes rarely. The Xenios 30 is a child document and
changes every month.

If your Blueprint is at **Preliminary** or **Samuel Review Pending**, any Xenios 30 built from it
inherits that provisional status, and the plan says so. A monthly plan cannot be more settled than
the Blueprint it came from, and it does not pretend otherwise.

## What happens at the end of the month?

The month closes with a review of what actually happened against what was planned, and the next
Xenios 30 is built from that.

The review is meant to be honest rather than encouraging. A month where you did about half of what
you intended is a useful data point about whether the plan was realistic, and recording it as such
produces a better next month than recording it as a near miss.

## Can I change it mid-month?

Yes, and there are two paths.

Anything you adjust yourself, in your own execution, needs no permission at all. It is your month.

A formal plan change, meaning a revision to the documented plan, is the same early plan-change
request described in the Xenios 90 FAQ. You get one free early plan-change request per month.

The reason a formal change is not unlimited is not commercial. It is that a plan revised every few
days stops being a plan and becomes a running commentary, and nothing in it stays in place long
enough to tell you whether it worked.

## What if the month goes badly?

Then it goes badly, and it gets recorded that way.

A month lost to illness, work, travel, or motivation is a normal outcome, and the correct response
is usually a smaller next month rather than a more aggressive one to compensate. Plans that try to
recover lost ground by escalating tend to fail twice.

Nothing about a poor month affects your membership, your Blueprint status, or your standing. There
is no scoring, no streak to protect, and no adherence metric that anyone is grading you against.
