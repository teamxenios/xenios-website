# Asking Questions on Telegram

Member education. This file is an AI draft at workflow state `draft`. Nothing here is approved,
published, or member-visible until Samuel Boadu reviews and publishes it.

Telegram is the member question channel. This page covers what you can send, how long an answer
takes, what each status on your question means, and the things the channel deliberately will not do.

---

## What can I send, and when?

Text questions and voice questions, at any hour. The channel accepts input 24 hours a day, seven
days a week.

Accepting input around the clock is not the same as answering around the clock. Nobody is sitting at
the other end at three in the morning waiting for your message. What the channel guarantees is that
your question is captured whenever you think of it, rather than lost because you were awake at an
inconvenient time.

Voice questions exist because some questions are easier to say than to type, and because a spoken
question often carries context that a typed one strips out. Voice notes are subject to the same
length and handling rules described in the privacy and media controls FAQ.

## How long will an answer take?

The target is approximately 12 hours.

That is a target, not a guarantee, and the difference matters. Questions that need a source to be
retrieved and verified take longer than questions about how the platform works. Questions that touch
a claim's evidence grade take longer still, because changing or defending a grade goes through the
same review path as any other research content. Questions arriving in a batch queue behind each
other.

There is no service level agreement attached to the 12 hour figure, no credit or refund if it is
missed, and no escalation path that makes it move faster. Stating it as a target you can plan around
is honest. Stating it as a promise would not be.

## What do the question statuses mean?

**Pending.** Received and queued. Nobody has looked at it yet.

**Being Reviewed.** Someone is actively working on it. For a research question this is usually the
longest phase, because it is where sources are retrieved and checked.

**More Information Needed.** The question cannot be answered as written and something has been asked
back to you. This status stops the clock. A question sitting here is waiting on you, not on us, and
it will keep sitting until you reply.

**Answer Ready.** The answer has been written, reviewed, and delivered to you.

**Completed.** The exchange is closed. You can ask a follow-up, and a follow-up opens a new question
rather than reopening the old one.

## Can I mark my question urgent?

No. There is no self-service urgency flag, and there will not be one.

The reason is simple and slightly unflattering to all of us: when everyone can mark their own
question urgent, everyone does, and the flag stops meaning anything. Priority set by the person
waiting is not priority, it is just a louder queue.

Questions are ordered by the reviewer, using what the question actually requires. A question that
needs one factual correction may be answered before a question submitted earlier that needs three
sources retrieved and verified. That ordering will occasionally feel unfair from where you are
sitting, and it is still the ordering that gets the most questions answered well.

## Why can't I see my number in the queue?

Because a public position number would be misleading and would create pressure in the wrong
direction.

Questions are not answered in strict arrival order, so a position number would not predict your wait.
It would also turn the queue into something members compare against each other, which changes the
channel from a place you ask a real question into a place you watch a counter. Neither outcome makes
your answer better or faster.

You can always see the status of your own question. You cannot see a queue depth, an aggregate wait
time, or anyone else's position. That boundary is intentional.

## What happens if my question is an emergency?

The channel is not for emergencies, and it does not attempt to handle them.

If a message contains emergency language, the response directs the person to emergency services. It
does not triage, it does not assess, it does not advise, and it does not attempt to keep the
conversation inside the channel. That behavior is deterministic rather than a judgement call, and it
runs regardless of what else the message contains.

Stated plainly: if you are experiencing a medical emergency, contact your local emergency services
now. Do not send a message here and wait. A channel with an approximately 12 hour target is
structurally incapable of helping you in the next few minutes, and pretending otherwise would be
dangerous.

## Who actually answers, a person or an AI?

Both, in a fixed order, and the order is the part that matters.

Drafting is assisted. Publishing is not. An AI draft never reaches you automatically. Samuel Boadu
is the founder reviewer and publisher, and an answer moves from drafted to delivered only after
human review. There is no path where a model's output reaches a member unreviewed.

This is why the target is measured in hours rather than seconds. The delay is the review, and the
review is the product.

## What kinds of questions will not be answered?

Several categories, and they are refusals rather than backlogs.

Dosing questions of any kind. That includes amounts, units, concentrations, frequency, timing, cycle
length, titration, stacking, reconstitution, and administration technique or route. Dosing
information is intentionally excluded from every Xenios Research surface, including this channel,
and asking privately does not change that.

Anything that amounts to a protocol, a regimen, or instructions you could follow.

Medical, clinical, legal, or financial advice, including any question that asks whether something is
right for your situation, your bloodwork, your medication, or your history. Those are questions for a
licensed professional who knows you, and Xenios Research is not one and does not simulate one.

Requests to confirm product facts that are not documented. Where a fact is unknown, the answer will
say NOT CONFIRMED rather than estimate. Composition, concentration, purity, certificate content,
supplier identity, shelf life, storage, and price are not guessed.

Requests for a source to be produced when none was retrieved. If a claim cannot be traced to a real,
retrievable source, the honest answer is that it cannot, and no reference will be constructed to
fill the space.

## What if I hear nothing, or the answer does not resolve it?

Check the status first. A question sitting at More Information Needed is waiting on your reply and
will not move until you send one, and that is the most common reason a member believes they have
been forgotten.

If your question is well past the 12 hour target with no status movement, send a follow-up in the
same channel. It will not jump the queue, and it does surface the case.

If an answer does not resolve your question, say so directly and say which part is unresolved. A
follow-up that names the specific gap is answered faster and better than one that restates the
original question, because it tells the reviewer where the first attempt failed.
