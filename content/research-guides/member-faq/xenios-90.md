## What is a Xenios 90, and how is it different from a Xenios 30?

A Xenios 90 is a three-month roadmap. A Xenios 30 is one month inside it.

The difference is what each is for. A month is short enough to execute and long enough to be
consistent, but it is too short to show whether an approach is actually working. Three months is
long enough for that to become visible, and short enough to still be a real plan rather than an
aspiration.

Running both is the normal case. The Xenios 90 sets the arc, and each Xenios 30 is the current month
of it.

## What do Foundation, Progression, and Consolidation mean?

Every Xenios 90 is structured in three phases, one per month.

**Foundation.** Establishing the basics of the approach and getting them consistent. This month is
deliberately unambitious. Most people find it slower than they expected, and most people who skip
it end up repeating it later.

**Progression.** Building on what became consistent in Foundation. This is where the plan gets more
demanding, because there is now something stable to build on.

**Consolidation.** Holding what was gained and making it durable, rather than pushing into a fourth
month of escalation. This phase looks like a plateau on paper. It is the phase that decides whether
the previous two months produced a change or a spike.

The order is not adjustable, and Consolidation is not optional. A roadmap that only escalates
produces a good 60 days and an unremarkable year.

## What is Review Week?

Once a month, one week is set aside as Review Week: the phase that just finished is reviewed against
what was planned, and the next phase is set from what actually happened.

It is not a rest week and it is not a pause. Execution continues. What changes is that the plan is
formally examined rather than assumed to still be correct.

Review Week is also the natural moment for a plan change, which is why the free early plan-change
request exists for changes that cannot wait for it.

## What is the free early plan-change request?

You get one free early plan-change request per calendar month. It is the formal way to revise a
documented plan without waiting for Review Week.

Use it when something material changed: an injury, a schedule change that makes the plan impossible,
a change in what you are actually trying to achieve, or a plan that is clearly not working and has
had a fair run.

"Free" here means it does not carry a cost and does not require justification to anyone's
satisfaction. Ask, state what changed, and the plan is revised.

## What if I want more than one change in a month?

Then you wait for Review Week, or for the next month's free request.

The honest reason for the limit: a plan changed repeatedly inside a single month never runs long
enough to generate information. If the approach is revised every ten days, the end of the quarter
arrives with no idea which version was worth keeping. The limit protects the value of the plan
rather than the platform's workload.

## Do I have to finish the 90 days?

No. There is no commitment period, no lock-in, and no penalty for abandoning a roadmap partway.

The tradeoff is worth being clear about. Stopping during Progression, which is the most common exit
point because it is the most demanding phase, means the roadmap does not reach Consolidation, and
Consolidation is the phase that makes the work durable. Leaving at that point is a legitimate choice
and it does forfeit the part of the structure that was doing the retention work.

You can also start a new Xenios 90 at any time. Nothing requires you to finish one before beginning
another.

## Does a Xenios 90 tell me what to take?

No. Dosing information is intentionally excluded from every phase, and a roadmap does not direct you
to obtain any compound.

Guides linked from a phase are education about what a compound has been studied for. They are not
recommendations to acquire anything, guide completion is not a prerequisite for anything, and
peptide, Quantum, and supplement reseller commerce are all blocked or pending written approval.

## What is not built yet?

As of 2026-07-19, the Xenios 90 structure (three phases, monthly Review Week, one free early
plan-change request per month) is a settled design. The following are NOT CONFIRMED: whether phase
transitions receive a named human review, how Review Week is surfaced and reminded in the member
site, and whether roadmap history is retained and comparable across successive Xenios 90 cycles.

Those are open product decisions rather than shipped behavior, and this page is not going to
describe them as though they already work.
