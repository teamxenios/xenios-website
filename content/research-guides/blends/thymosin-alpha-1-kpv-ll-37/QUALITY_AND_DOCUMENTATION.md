# Thymosin Alpha-1 + KPV + LL-37: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing these
compounds could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of this
product and would apply to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state**. Xenios has confirmed none of these items. Every
box below is unchecked, and none is expected to be checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below.

1. **The composition and ingredient ratio of this product is not confirmed** (checked
   2026-07-21). This is the first open question and it precedes every other item in this
   document. A mixture whose proportions are unknown cannot be specified, cannot be tested
   against a specification, and cannot be assessed at all.

2. **A blend carries quality obligations that a single-ingredient product does not.** Identity
   and content must be established for **each component separately**, not for the preparation as
   a whole. And the mixture must be demonstrated to be **uniform**, so that any portion contains
   the same proportion of each component as any other. These are set out in section B below and
   they have no analogue in a single-ingredient packet.

3. **None of the three components is an approved drug in the United States** (checked
   2026-07-21). Thymosin Alpha-1 is reported to be approved as a prescription medicine in some
   other countries, which creates a specific identity hazard rather than an assurance: material
   sold outside a regulated chain can borrow the credibility of a real regulated drug without
   being that drug. There is no approved manufacturer of this preparation, no approved
   specification, and no regulator inspecting a production line against a filed standard.

4. **No analytical study of material sold as Thymosin Alpha-1, as KPV, or as LL-37 could be
   located** for any of the three. Nobody appears to have systematically checked what is in these
   vials. That is an absence of information, not a favourable result.

What is documented is how the unregulated channel performs generally. A peer-reviewed 2024
market-surveillance study in the Journal of Medical Internet Research identified 317 online
pharmacy links, of which 134 were illegal. Test purchases from six illegal pharmacies yielded
three injection vials, all considered probable substandard or falsified products. Visual
inspection showed non-compliance on 59 to 63 percent of criteria. Measured active content
exceeded the label by 28.56 to 38.69 percent, while measured purity was 7.7 to 14.37 percent
against a claimed 99 percent, and endotoxin was detected in every sample (PMID 39509151, verified
2026-07-20). **That study tested a different peptide. It tested none of the three components of
this blend.** It is evidence about the supply channel and is cited only as such.

A note on numbers that will be encountered elsewhere: research for the component Guides surfaced
several striking gray-market failure statistics covering sequence errors, heavy metals, residual
solvents, certificate-of-analysis discrepancy rates, and mass audit failure rates. Every one
traced to vendor blogs, forum posts, or marketing pages, with no locatable primary source. They
are excluded here and must not be added.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before
any further conversation. Status as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single item.

### A. Composition of the blend

This section exists only because this is a blend. It comes first because nothing else can be
assessed until it is answered.

- [ ] **A1. The exact composition of the preparation, stated in writing by the supplier.** Which
      components are present, and nothing else present that is not declared.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. The ratio of the three components, stated in writing.** Not implied by listing order,
      not approximate, not proprietary. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A3. That stated ratio independently verified analytically**, on the lot in hand, rather
      than accepted from the supplier's document. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A4. A rationale for the composition**, and disclosure of whether any study supports the
      combination as formulated. No such study was located during research.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. All excipients, carriers, buffers, and any other constituent declared and
      characterised**, not only the three named actives. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. A written specification for the preparation as a whole**, with acceptance criteria
      set before testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Per-component identity and assay, and blend uniformity

These requirements exist for a blend and have no single-ingredient equivalent. A result for
"the product" is not a result for any component in it.

- [ ] **B1. Identity established separately for each of the three components** in the finished
      preparation, by an orthogonal analytical method rather than by supplier assertion.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED for all three.
- [ ] **B2. Assay, meaning measured content, reported separately for each of the three
      components** in the finished preparation, against a stated acceptance range for each.
      A single combined figure is not acceptable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED for all three.
- [ ] **B3. Blend uniformity demonstrated**, so that any portion of the preparation contains the
      same proportion of each component as any other portion. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Content per component demonstrated to be consistent across units and across the
      batch**, not only within a single sampled unit. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Absence of interaction between components in the preparation demonstrated**,
      including that no component degrades, binds, or otherwise alters another during
      manufacture or storage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Analytical methods shown to be specific for each component in the presence of the
      other two.** A method validated on a single compound may not resolve it in a mixture.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B7. Reference standards for each of the three components named and traceable.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED for all three.

### C. Component identity, individually

- [ ] **C1. Unambiguous chemical identity of each exact substance.** Not the names Thymosin
      Alpha-1, KPV, and LL-37. The specific forms, including salt or free base where regulatory
      filings distinguish them, as they do for KPV. Status: NOT CONFIRMED for all three.
- [ ] **C2. Verified registry identifiers for each component**, confirmed against a primary
      chemical registry. Figures circulating on vendor pages for KPV were not confirmed during
      research and are not reproduced. Status: NOT CONFIRMED for all three.
      [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]
- [ ] **C3. Confirmed amino acid sequence for each component in the specific batch**, by an
      orthogonal method such as mass spectrometry with sequencing. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Explicit exclusion of confusable compounds for each component.** Documented evidence
      that the material is Thymosin Alpha-1 and not Thymosin Beta-4, TB-500, or thymosin fraction
      5; that it is KPV and not KdPT, (CKPV)2, full-length alpha-MSH, or a melanocortin receptor
      agonist; and that it is native LL-37 and not a fragment or an engineered analog. Every one
      of these substitutions is common in commercial material. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Salt form, counter-ion, and water content stated and measured for each component.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C6. For the Thymosin Alpha-1 component specifically, documented evidence of whether the
      material is the pharmaceutical-grade substance or unregulated material.** The trial and
      labelling data referenced in the Guide describe the pharmaceutical product used under
      medical supervision. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **D1. Quantified purity by a validated, stated method**, reported per component, with the
      method and conditions disclosed rather than a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of
      it there is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. Peptide-related impurities characterised for each component**, including truncated,
      deletion, and modified sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit
      standard. Manufacturing impurities including heavy metals were among the reported
      regulatory concerns for peptides of this kind. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D6. Aggregation assessed for each component and for the mixture.** Peptide clumping is a
      recognised contributor to immune reactions against a peptide, and a mixture introduces
      opportunities for it that a single component does not. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **E1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any
      route requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.** The channel
      evidence cited above found endotoxin in every sample of a different peptide tested, which
      makes this a priority item rather than a formality. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process,
      documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Stability

Stability is a heavier obligation for a blend than for a single ingredient, because each
component can destabilise on its own and can also destabilise in the presence of the others.

- [ ] **F1. Stability data under stated storage conditions, over a stated duration, using a
      stability-indicating method**, reported for each component. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. Stability of the mixture as formulated**, demonstrated rather than inferred from the
      components separately. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Where components have different stability profiles, the shortest governs.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. Photostability and temperature-excursion data, including behaviour during shipping.**
      Sensitivity to temperature and vigorous handling in ways that can alter chemical
      composition was among the reported regulatory concerns for peptides of this kind.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Degradation products identified and quantified for each component**, not just a
      decline in assay value. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F7. For the LL-37 component specifically, data addressing its documented instability.**
      The peer-reviewed literature reports native LL-37 is susceptible to enzymatic breakdown,
      unstable at high salt concentrations, and of low stability in biological fluids.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **G1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site**, for the finished
      preparation and for each component's raw material. Not a brand, not a distributor, not a
      marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. Regulatory inspection history of each site**, and the quality standard each operates
      to. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis of each component through blending to the
      finished unit**, including every intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. Batch and lot traceability for the finished preparation and for each component
      input**, so that any single unit traces back to three manufacturing records.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G7. The blending step itself documented**, including equipment, process, controls, and
      validation. This step exists only for a blend and is where uniformity is won or lost.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **H1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis matching the exact lot in hand**, not a
      representative or example certificate for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. The certificate reporting each component separately**, with per-component identity,
      assay, and purity. A certificate reporting only "the blend" is not acceptable.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. The certificate issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation
      stated and independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Test methods named on the certificate**, with acceptance criteria and measured
      results, not "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H5. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no
      commercial interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot, reporting each
      component separately. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H6. Certificate authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.**
      Certificates are forged. A document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H7. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### I. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **I1. Label content matching the tested content for each component**, within a stated
      tolerance. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material that exceed the
      evidence.** For this preparation that means no claim of any kind about the combination,
      because no combination evidence exists. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I3. No presentation of component evidence in a way that reads as support for the
      combination**, on the label, in accompanying material, or in any marketing.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I4. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I5. Accurate regulatory status stated for each component, with jurisdiction and date**,
      including that approval of a single agent elsewhere is neither United States approval nor
      approval of a mixture. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I6. No conflation of orphan drug designation with marketing approval.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### J. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **J1. A documented adverse event reporting route, with a named responsible party.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **J2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **J3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **J4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has confirmed no quality documentation item for this preparation.** No supplier has been
assessed against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent
testing has been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean
passed. The purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can
see exactly what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact
that a Guide exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the preparation does anything.
Perfect documentation would establish that a vial contains what it claims, in the stated
proportion, uniformly, and uncontaminated. **It would not create evidence that the combination
does anything, because as set out in the Guide, no study of the combination exists.** Those are
two independent failures, and fixing one would not touch the other.
