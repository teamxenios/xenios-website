# Blend Source Plan: Thymosin Alpha-1 + KPV + LL-37

This document records how the evidence base for this blend Guide was assembled, what was
deliberately excluded, and what a human reviewer must still do before anything in this folder
reaches a member.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## 1. The question this search was built to answer

A blend Guide has one question at its centre, and it is not the same question an individual
compound Guide asks. The question is:

**Has anyone studied this combination?**

Everything else is secondary. A blend is not the sum of its parts. Putting three biologically
active molecules into one preparation creates a new pharmacology, a new interaction profile,
and a new set of unknowns that no single-agent study can speak to. So the search was designed
to look first for combination evidence, and only afterwards to describe what is known about
each component on its own.

The answer, stated plainly and up front so that nothing later in this folder can be
misread: **no study of this three-component combination was found in any of the three
component research records.** Not a weak study. Not an underpowered study. None.

## 2. Objectives, in order

1. Determine whether any study, of any design, tested Thymosin Alpha-1 together with KPV and
   LL-37. Also determine whether any study tested any two of the three together.
2. Establish, separately and without merging, what each component's own evidence base shows.
3. Establish the regulatory position of each component, with a jurisdiction and a date attached
   to every statement, and record the difference between approval elsewhere and approval in the
   United States.
4. Record the composition question. The exact ingredient ratio of the Xenios product is not
   confirmed, and no ratio is stated or implied anywhere in this folder.

The search was designed so that an empty combination evidence table would be a valid and
reportable result rather than a gap to be filled with component substitutes.

## 3. What was searched, and how

This Guide was drafted from three web-verified component research records, each assembled in
a prior research session against primary databases and registries. Those records are the only
factual basis for this folder. No citation was added from memory.

| Component record | Retrieval date | Databases and registries it covers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Thymosin Alpha-1 (Thymalfasin) | 2026-07-19 | PubMed (via PMID lookup), ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT04487444), PubMed Central, secondary regulatory reporting, a secondary drug reference monograph. |
| KPV (Lys-Pro-Val) | 2026-07-20 | ClinicalTrials.gov API v2, PubMed via NCBI E-utilities (esearch, esummary, efetch), regulatory trade press, an advocacy public comment. |
| LL-37 (human cathelicidin) | 2026-07-19 | ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 (a 50-record sweep on the term LL-37), PubMed, PubMed Central, secondary legal and trade reporting. |

## 4. The combination search, and its result

The three component records were read in full and searched for any of the following:

1. A trial, of any phase or design, administering Thymosin Alpha-1, KPV, and LL-37 together.
2. A trial administering any two of the three together.
3. A preclinical study, in any species or model, of any two-way or three-way combination.
4. Any pharmacokinetic, interaction, stability, or compatibility study of any pairing.
5. Any regulatory record, safety communication, or case report concerning the combination.

**Result: nothing was found in any of these five categories.**

Supporting detail from the component records, which is what makes this a searched finding
rather than an assumption:

- The Thymosin Alpha-1 record contains five human evidence entries. All five study Thymosin
  Alpha-1 as a single agent, in sepsis, in COVID-19, or in chronic hepatitis B. None involves
  KPV or LL-37.
- The KPV record contains an empty human evidence array. A direct ClinicalTrials.gov API query
  on the term KPV returned zero studies, and a PubMed search restricted to the clinical trial
  publication type returned zero records. There is therefore no KPV trial at all, single-agent
  or combination.
- The LL-37 record includes a systematic sweep of 50 ClinicalTrials.gov records returned for
  the term LL-37, which established that only two of the 50 administered LL-37 as an
  intervention (NCT02225366 and NCT04098562). Both are single-agent studies. The remainder are
  observational biomarker studies or vitamin D and probiotic trials measuring a person's own
  LL-37 as a readout.

A combination trial involving KPV cannot exist in the registry record, because the registry
record contains no KPV trial of any kind. That single fact forecloses the possibility of
registered three-component evidence.

## 5. Inclusion criteria

A source was admitted to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md if all of the following held.

1. It appears in one of the three component research records at a recorded URL. Nothing was
   admitted from memory, and no identifier (PMID, NCT, DOI, author, year, journal, or URL) was
   reconstructed.
2. It is used only for what it actually studied. A single-agent source supports a single-agent
   statement and never a combination statement.
3. Its species or model is identifiable, so that no preclinical finding can be written without
   its model in the same sentence.
4. For a regulatory statement, a jurisdiction, a date, and a URL are all present.
5. Its verified flag from the component record is carried through unchanged. A source recorded
   as verified=false in the research is recorded as UNVERIFIED here and cannot carry a claim.

## 6. Exclusion criteria

A source or a claim was excluded if any of the following held.

1. **Combination inference.** Any statement that assembles component findings into something a
   reader could take as evidence about the blend. This is the governing exclusion for this
   folder and it outranks the others.
2. **Vendor, retailer, clinic, and compounding-pharmacy pages** as evidence. They may be cited
   only to document where an unverifiable claim originated, and such entries are marked
   NON-EVIDENCE. The component records specifically flag vendor assertions of a completed
   February 2026 regulatory reclassification, vendor assertions of KPV human tolerability, and
   vendor presentation of a post-hoc LL-37 subgroup number, all of which are corrected rather
   than repeated.
3. **Search-engine answer summaries** substituted for the underlying document. The KPV record
   documents summaries that described a regulatory meeting outcome in the past tense before the
   meeting had occurred, which is exactly the failure this rule exists to catch.
4. **Any statistic without a locatable primary source.** Both the KPV and LL-37 records name
   sets of circulating gray-market failure percentages that trace only to vendor blogs. They are
   excluded by name in CLAIM_TABLE.md so they cannot re-enter later.
5. **Evidence about adjacent compounds presented as evidence about a component.** This applies
   to Thymosin Beta-4 and TB-500 (a different peptide from Thymosin Alpha-1), to thymosin
   fraction 5 (the crude extract, not the purified peptide), to KdPT, to the dimer written as
   (CKPV)2, to full-length alpha-MSH, and to engineered LL-37 analogs.
6. **Any dose, route, schedule, concentration, or preparation detail** appearing in an admitted
   source. None is reproduced anywhere in this folder.
7. **Any statement of the Xenios product's composition or ingredient ratio.** It is not
   confirmed. See section 8.

## 7. Grading approach

Claims are graded individually, never as a product. The blend as a whole receives no grade,
because grading a blend would imply a body of blend evidence exists.

The default grade is D (preclinical) or G (unverified). A grade of C or better requires a
specific retrieved human study of the thing being claimed. Two consequences follow:

- **No claim about the combination is graded above G.** There is no combination study, so
  there is nothing for a higher grade to rest on.
- A component claim may reach a higher grade where a real human trial supports it, but that
  grade attaches to the component studied alone, in the population studied, by the route
  studied. It does not transfer to the blend.

Claims that could mislead a member regardless of hedging are marked PROHIBITED and listed
separately in CLAIM_TABLE.md.

## 8. The composition question, which is open

The exact composition and ingredient ratio of the Xenios product is **NOT CONFIRMED**. No
ratio, proportion, relative amount, or ordering by quantity is stated or implied anywhere in
this folder, and none may be added.

This is the first open question for any supplier, and it is listed first in
QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md for that reason. Until composition is documented and independently
verified, the product cannot be described accurately at all, and nothing downstream of that
description can be assessed.

## 9. What remains for a human reviewer

These are open items, not tidying. Each should be closed before publication.

1. **Re-run the combination search independently.** Query the trial registries and the
   bibliographic databases for each two-way pairing and for the three-way combination, and
   record the result and the date. The finding of this folder is that there is none. That
   finding should be re-established by a human, not inherited.
2. **Obtain the product composition and ratio from the supplier in writing**, then verify it
   analytically rather than accepting the document.
3. **Open the FDA pages directly.** Every fda.gov URL returned HTTP 404 across two separate
   component research sessions, and a mirror returned HTTP 403. Every FDA statement in this
   folder therefore rests on secondary reporting and is marked accordingly.
4. **Establish the current United States compounding position for each of the three components
   separately**, because they are at three different points in the same process, and confirm
   each from a primary FDA record.
5. **Read the Federal Register notice (document 2026-07361, dated 2026-04-16) in full.** It was
   blocked by a redirect and its public-inspection PDF was unparseable in the KPV research
   session.
6. **Confirm the outcome of the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of 23 to 24
   July 2026**, which had not occurred as of the KPV research date and whose agenda covered KPV.
   No outcome is stated anywhere in this folder.
7. **Confirm whether cathelicidin LL-37 is in fact scheduled for advisory committee review
   before the end of February 2027.** That claim rests on secondary legal and trade sources; the
   FDA 2027 meeting-materials page returned HTTP 404.
8. **Confirm the Thymosin Alpha-1 United States position**, which the research describes as
   genuinely unsettled: an April 2026 investigative report describes a reversal as anticipated
   and contested, while vendor sites assert a completed February 2026 reclassification that
   could not be verified in any independent source.
9. **Confirm non-US approval of thymalfasin from a primary regulator record.** The commonly
   repeated country count traces to marketing material and is not reproduced here.
10. **Read the current WADA Prohibited List directly.** No anti-doping status is stated for any
    of the three components, because the list could not be retrieved in any of the three
    research sessions. Athletes must be directed to their own anti-doping authority.
11. **Retrieve the flagged LL-37 counter-finding.** A PubMed Central record (PMC6013660) whose
    title asserts that LL-37 promotes the growth of a pulmonary fungal pathogen was surfaced by
    title only and never read. It is flagged, not used. If accurate it is directionally
    important and belongs in the safety section.
12. **Retrieve the FDA advisory committee briefing documents** for each component, and check
    whether concerns circulating in secondary summaries are actually the agency's words. None
    is attributed to the agency anywhere in this folder.
13. **Retrieve the full text of the sources read only in abstract**, specifically the diabetic
    foot ulcer trial (PMID 37480520), the COVID-19 meta-analysis whose randomised versus
    observational composition could not be confirmed (PMID 37845598), and the hepatitis B
    meta-analysis whose risk-of-bias assessment is unknown (PMID 18078676).
14. **Check whether the Cochrane review on Thymosin Alpha-1 in chronic hepatitis B has since
    been completed.** The retrieved record (PMC8929401) is a protocol only and contains no
    pooled findings. Search engines misrepresented it as a completed review during research.
15. **Reconcile the date stamps.** Two component records carry retrieval dates of 2026-07-19 and
    one carries 2026-07-20, while this folder is dated 2026-07-21. Reconcile before publication
    so every dated statement is exact.
