# Blend Claim Table: Thymosin Alpha-1 + KPV + LL-37

One row per discrete claim. Grades apply to individual claims, never to a product, and never to
a blend. Source IDs refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

---

## THE RULE THAT GOVERNS EVERY ROW BELOW

**What is known about three ingredients separately tells you nothing reliable about the three
of them together.** Mixing compounds produces a preparation with its own behaviour in the body,
its own interactions between the parts, and its own list of things nobody has looked at. The
only thing that can establish what a combination does is a study that gave that combination to
something and watched what happened.

No such study exists for Thymosin Alpha-1 with KPV with LL-37. That does not leave the
combination with weak support, or preliminary support, or theoretical support. It leaves the
combination with **no evidence at all**, which is a different category from weak evidence.

Practically, for this table: no row in the component sections may be read as a combination
claim, and no two rows may be added together to produce one. Any statement about the blend must
find its support in section 1, and section 1 contains no effect claims.

---

## The headline finding

The three component research records were searched for any study of the combination, for any
study of any two-way pairing, and for any interaction, compatibility, or pharmacokinetic work
on any pairing. Nothing was found in any category (CB-01, CB-02, CB-03).

One structural fact makes this especially firm. There is no registered clinical trial of KPV of
any kind, single-agent or otherwise: a direct trial registry query returned zero studies
(KP-01) and a bibliographic search restricted to the clinical trial publication type returned
zero records (KP-02). A registered three-component trial containing KPV therefore cannot exist
in that record.

A second fact matters as much. The component evidence that does exist is substantially
negative. The largest and best-designed trial ever run on Thymosin Alpha-1 found essentially no
effect on its primary endpoint (TA-01). The largest human trial of LL-37 missed its prespecified
primary endpoint (LL-01). KPV has never been studied in a person at all (KP-01, KP-02). A blend
assembled from these three parts is not assembled from three established agents.

## PROHIBITED claims

The following may not appear on any member-facing surface, in any file in this folder, in any
phrasing, hedged or otherwise. A claim listed here is prohibited because it would mislead a
member regardless of the qualifiers attached to it.

### Prohibited because they assert combination evidence that does not exist

1. That this combination has been studied, tested, trialled, or evaluated in any setting.
2. That the components work together, complement one another, act synergistically, or are
   chosen for how they interact. No study examined any pairing.
3. That combining the three is supported by, justified by, or built on the evidence for the
   individual ingredients. Component evidence is not combination evidence.
4. Any presentation that lists findings for each ingredient in sequence in a way that reads as
   a case for the mixture. This is prohibited even where every individual sentence is accurate,
   because the arrangement carries the false claim.
5. That the absence of combination studies means the combination is untested but reasonable, or
   low risk, or a sensible extrapolation. It means nothing is known.
6. Any dose, ratio, proportion, or relative amount of the three components. Composition is not
   confirmed.

### Prohibited because they misstate the component evidence

7. That Thymosin Alpha-1 reduces mortality in sepsis. The largest and best-designed trial found
   no effect, and it was the confirmatory trial the earlier positive study called for.
8. That the earlier sepsis trial establishes benefit, or that it may be cited without the later
   null trial alongside it.
9. That Thymosin Alpha-1 has established benefit in COVID-19. A positive pooled estimate from
   largely non-randomised data beside a null randomised trial is a pattern of confounding.
10. That approval of thymalfasin outside the United States is United States approval, or that
    orphan drug designation is approval.
11. That any human data on KPV exists, that KPV has demonstrated human tolerability, or that
    there are dozens of publications establishing its efficacy. The claim of demonstrated human
    tolerability is not merely unproven, it is contradicted by the regulator's own reported
    finding that human exposure data are absent.
12. That KPV acts through melanocortin receptors, which the retrieved literature contradicts, or
    that its mechanism is established, which a peer-reviewed review explicitly denies.
13. That evidence for alpha-MSH, KdPT, (CKPV)2, Thymosin Beta-4, TB-500, thymosin fraction 5, or
    an engineered LL-37 analog is evidence for a component of this blend.
14. That the largest LL-37 trial was a positive trial, or citation of its favourable subgroup
    figure without stating that the primary endpoint failed and the figure is post-hoc.
15. That the 2014 and 2021 LL-37 trials are two positive trials. The earlier signal failed to
    replicate.
16. That LL-37 has shown potency against cancer. The retrieved record enrolled 4 participants,
    one of whom withdrew for lack of efficacy.
17. That LL-37 is an established antimicrobial in people. In the one retrieved human trial that
    measured bacterial colonisation, it did not significantly outperform placebo.
18. That any preclinical finding, from any species or model, describes what happens in a person.

### Prohibited because they misstate safety or quality

19. That any component, or the combination, is safe, well tolerated, or free of side effects.
20. That absence of reported harm is a safety record. It reflects absence of study.
21. That trial-level tolerability in acutely ill supervised hospital populations, or in wound
    application over weeks, extends to elective long-term use by healthy adults.
22. Any claim of purity, sterility, identity, potency, blend uniformity, or content per
    component for any commercial material. Nothing has been confirmed.
23. The specific circulating gray-market failure statistics named at BL-Q-07.
24. That Xenios supplies, sources, endorses, tests, or can obtain any of these compounds, or any
    statement of how or where a member could get them.

### Prohibited because they misstate regulatory or anti-doping status

25. Any anti-doping status for any component or for the combination. The list could not be
    retrieved.
26. Any statement of an outcome for the July 2026 advisory committee review, which had not
    occurred as of the research date.
27. Any assertion that the United States compounding position for Thymosin Alpha-1 has been
    settled or reversed, which independent reporting describes as contested and pending.
28. That not checked in a jurisdiction means permitted in that jurisdiction.

### Prohibited framing

29. Guaranteed outcome language of any kind: will, proven to, restores, cures, eliminates,
    reverses, guarantees.
30. Research use only framing used as an implicit signal of human benefit.
31. Any comparison to an approved medicine that implies equivalence.
32. Any protocol, regimen, sequence, or direction a reader could follow as instructions.
