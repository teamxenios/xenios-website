# Thymosin Alpha-1 + KPV + LL-37 (blend)

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

---

## THE RULE THIS GUIDE IS BUILT ON

**A mixture is not the sum of its ingredients, and the research on each ingredient does not
carry over to the mixture.**

When two or more active compounds are put into one preparation, what results is a new thing.
It behaves in the body in its own way. The parts can affect each other, and can affect how the
body handles each of them. Questions arise that do not exist for any of the parts alone. None
of that is answered by knowing what each ingredient does on its own, because none of it has
been looked at.

So the standard is simple and it is strict. Unless a study gave **this combination** to
something and recorded what happened, there is no evidence about this combination. Not thin
evidence. Not indirect evidence. Not theoretical evidence. **None.**

We searched all three component research records for any study of this combination. There is
none. What follows is therefore an honest account of three separate compounds, presented so
that the separateness is impossible to miss.

## One-minute summary

This product combines three compounds: Thymosin Alpha-1, KPV, and LL-37. The honest position,
and the most important thing in this Guide, is that **no study of the combination exists**.

- No study testing Thymosin Alpha-1, KPV, and LL-37 together was found, in people or in any
  animal or laboratory model.
- No study testing any two of the three together was found either.
- No study of how these components affect one another, of whether they are chemically
  compatible in one preparation, or of how the body handles them together, was found.
- One fact makes this especially firm. There is no registered clinical trial of KPV of any
  kind, so a registered combination trial containing KPV cannot exist in the trial registry
  record (verified 2026-07-20).

**The exact composition and ingredient ratio of this product is not confirmed.** No proportion
is stated anywhere in this Guide, because none is known. That is the first question any
supplier would have to answer.

On the individual components, the picture is not what a reader might expect from a product
built out of them.

- **Thymosin Alpha-1** is the unusual one. It is a real pharmaceutical (called thymalfasin) and
  is reported to be an approved prescription medicine in some countries outside the United
  States, principally for chronic hepatitis B. It has been tested in properly sized randomised
  trials. The largest and best-designed of those, in 1,089 adults with sepsis, found
  essentially no effect on 28-day death rates against placebo (verified 2026-07-19). The
  strongest evidence on this compound is a null result. It has no United States marketing
  approval, and approval elsewhere is not approval here.
- **KPV** has **zero human studies**. Not thin data, absent data. A direct trial registry query
  returned zero studies and a literature search restricted to clinical trials returned zero
  records (verified 2026-07-20). When the United States regulator restricted it, the reported
  reason included an absence of human exposure data.
- **LL-37** has genuine randomised human trials, and they are narrower and more negative than
  its marketing suggests. The largest, in 148 treated patients with hard-to-heal leg ulcers,
  **missed its primary endpoint** (verified 2026-07-19). Every retrieved human trial applied it
  to a wound or injected it into a skin tumour, and none enrolled healthy people.

On safety, the correct statement for the combination is not that it is safe. It is that nothing
is known about it. The component safety records are short-term, in ill and medically supervised
people, and they do not extend to elective long-term use by healthy adults. Absence of reported
harm reflects absence of study.

Xenios does not sell these compounds, does not supply them, and does not tell members how to
obtain or use them. This Guide exists so that a member encountering this blend elsewhere has an
accurate account of what is and is not known.

**Claim grade for "this combination is effective for any human purpose": G (Unverified).**
**Claim grade for "this combination has been studied": G (Unverified). No study was located.**
Grades apply to individual claims. No grade is assigned to the product as a whole.

## What it is

This is a preparation containing three separate peptides. A peptide is a short chain of amino
acids, the building blocks that make up proteins.

### Thymosin Alpha-1

A 28-amino-acid peptide that is present naturally in humans and was originally isolated from
calf thymus tissue. Its synthetic pharmaceutical form is called thymalfasin. It is generally
described as an immunomodulator, meaning a compound that adjusts immune function rather than
one that attacks a microbe directly.

It is commonly confused with three other things, and their evidence is not its evidence:

- **Thymosin Beta-4, also sold as TB-500.** A structurally and functionally unrelated peptide
  associated with tissue repair rather than immune function, with a different regulatory and
  anti-doping position.
- **Thymosin fraction 5.** The crude thymic extract that Thymosin Alpha-1 was originally
  purified from. It is not the same as the single purified peptide.
- **Thymalfasin the regulated drug product versus research-grade or unregulated powder sold
  under the same chemical name.** These are not the same product, and the second carries no
  assurance of identity or purity.

### KPV

A tripeptide, meaning a chain of exactly three amino acids: lysine, proline, and valine, whose
single-letter codes K, P, and V give it its name. It corresponds to the tail end of a larger
hormone called alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone, usually shortened to alpha-MSH, and in the
scientific literature it is written as alpha-MSH(11-13).

Two peer-reviewed reviews describe KPV as lacking the entire sequence needed to bind any of the
known melanocortin receptors, which are the cell-surface receptors the full hormone acts
through, while reportedly keeping anti-inflammatory activity without the pigment effect of the
full hormone.

It is commonly confused with **KdPT** (a related but different tripeptide), with **(CKPV)2** (a
dimer, meaning two linked copies, studied separately), with **full-length alpha-MSH**, and with
**melanocortin receptor agonists** such as melanotan-type compounds. None of their findings are
KPV findings.

A further identity point: regulatory filings treat KPV free base and KPV acetate as separate
substances, so the name KPV alone does not pin down which material is in a product.

Commonly quoted chemical registry identifiers for KPV appeared during research only on vendor
pages and were not confirmed against a primary chemical registry, so they are deliberately not
reproduced here. [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]

### LL-37

The only cathelicidin antimicrobial peptide found in humans. It is 37 amino acids long,
released from a precursor protein called hCAP-18, encoded by the CAMP gene, and produced
naturally by human surface-lining cells and immune cells.

The single most important interpretive point about LL-37 is this: **most published studies
indexed under the name LL-37 measure a person's own naturally produced LL-37 as a biomarker,
not the effect of giving LL-37 to someone.** In a systematic sweep of 50 trial registry records
returned for the term, only two administered LL-37 as an intervention (verified 2026-07-19).
The rest were observational studies or vitamin D and probiotic trials using LL-37 as a readout.
Raising your own LL-37 through some other means is a different thing from receiving LL-37.

A second point: much of the LL-37 research literature concerns engineered modified versions,
built specifically because the native peptide is unstable. Findings on those analogs are not
findings about the native peptide.

## Why interest exists

The rationale for a blend like this is easy to describe, and worth understanding without
endorsing it.

Each component has an immune or inflammatory story attached to it. Thymosin Alpha-1 is
described as restoring immune competence in states of immune exhaustion. KPV is described in
preclinical work as suppressing inflammatory signalling. LL-37 is described as a host-defence
peptide with antimicrobial and wound-healing roles. Placed side by side, those three
descriptions suggest complementary parts of one system.

That impression is the thing to be careful about, and it is worth naming plainly.

**First, a story about how three molecules might cooperate is not evidence that they do.** No
study examined any pairing of these components. A mechanistic rationale describes why someone
thought a combination was worth trying. It says nothing about what happens when it is tried,
and in this case nobody appears to have tried it in any recorded study.

**Second, the mechanisms themselves are less settled than the rationale implies.** A 2010
peer-reviewed review states plainly that KPV's exact signalling mechanism is unknown. The
receptor attribution commonly given for Thymosin Alpha-1 could not be traced to a primary
mechanistic paper during research. And the properties of LL-37 that are proposed to help are
the same properties proposed to make it toxic to human cells.

**Third, and most importantly, the component evidence runs the other way.** In the two largest
and best-designed human trials of any component of this blend, the compound did not do what was
hoped. Thymosin Alpha-1 showed essentially no effect on death rates in a 1,089-patient sepsis
trial. LL-37 missed its primary endpoint in a 148-patient wound-healing trial. KPV has never
been given to a person in any recorded study. A blend built from these three parts is not built
from three established agents.

**Fourth, interest is not an outcome.** Interest explains why researchers thought something was
worth studying. It does not describe what happened.

## Terminology

Every technical term used in this Guide, in plain English.

- **Peptide.** A short chain of amino acids, the building blocks of proteins. Shorter than a
  protein.
- **Tripeptide.** A peptide made of exactly three amino acids. KPV is one.
- **Blend or combination.** A preparation containing more than one active compound. It is a
  distinct thing from any of its ingredients.
- **Randomised controlled trial.** A study where participants are assigned by chance to receive
  the treatment or a comparison, which is the design best able to separate an effect from a
  coincidence.
- **Placebo-controlled.** The comparison group receives an inactive preparation.
- **Blinded.** Participants, and in a double-blind study the researchers too, do not know who is
  receiving which. Single-blind means only one side is unaware, which leaves more room for
  expectation to influence results.
- **Open-label.** Nobody is blinded. Everyone knows who is receiving what.
- **Primary endpoint.** The single main outcome a trial commits to measuring before it starts.
  Whether a trial succeeded or failed is judged on this.
- **Post-hoc analysis.** An analysis decided on after the results are in. It can generate ideas
  but cannot confirm them, because looking at enough slices of data will eventually turn up an
  apparently favourable one by chance.
- **Subgroup.** A slice of the trial population, for example only patients with larger wounds.
- **Meta-analysis.** A study that pools results from several earlier studies. Its reliability
  depends entirely on the quality of what went into it.
- **Heterogeneity, and I-squared.** A measure of how much the pooled studies disagree with each
  other. A high figure means the pooled number is holding together studies that found different
  things, which weakens it.
- **Hazard ratio, relative risk, odds ratio.** Different ways of expressing how much more or
  less often something happened in one group than another. A value of 1.0 means no difference.
- **Confidence interval.** The range the true value is likely to sit in. If that range crosses
  1.0, the result is compatible with no effect at all.
- **Null result.** A study that found no difference between the groups.
- **Preclinical.** Research done in animals or in cells in a dish, not in people.
- **In vitro, or cell culture.** In a dish. Even when the cells are human-derived, this is not a
  study in a person.
- **Immunomodulator.** A compound described as adjusting immune function.
- **Immunogenicity.** Whether the body mounts an immune response against a substance given to it.
- **Pharmacokinetics.** How the body absorbs, distributes, breaks down, and clears a substance.
- **Autoantigen.** Something the body's own immune system can mistakenly target as foreign.
- **Type I interferon.** A family of immune signalling molecules involved in antiviral defence
  and in several autoimmune conditions.
- **NF-kappaB.** A signalling system inside cells that switches on inflammatory genes.
- **Granulation index.** A measure of new tissue forming in a healing wound. It is an
  intermediate measure, not the same as a wound closing.
- **Contraindication.** A situation in which a substance should not be used.
- **Orphan drug designation.** A development incentive status granted to encourage work on rare
  diseases. It is not marketing approval, and it is frequently presented as though it were.
- **Compounding.** Preparation of a medicine by a pharmacy for an individual, as distinct from
  manufacture of an approved product.
- **Bulk drug substance list.** A regulatory list governing which raw substances a compounding
  pharmacy may use.
- **Advisory committee.** A panel that advises a regulator. Its recommendation is advice, not a
  final determination.
- **Blend uniformity.** Whether a mixture is consistent throughout, so that any portion contains
  the same proportion of each ingredient as any other.
- **Certificate of analysis.** A document stating what testing found for a specific batch.

## Proposed mechanism

Everything in this section is proposed. None of it is established as what actually happens in a
treated person, and **none of it has been studied with the three components present together**.

### There is no proposed mechanism for the combination

No study examined how these three compounds behave in the presence of one another. Any account
of how they might work together would be constructed rather than observed, and this Guide does
not construct one. Two of the three are described in the literature as acting on immune
function, in ways that are not obviously the same direction, which makes the unexamined
interaction question substantive rather than a formality.

### Thymosin Alpha-1, proposed

Described as an immunomodulator rather than a direct antiviral or antimicrobial. The proposal
most commonly advanced involves maturation of dendritic cells, enhanced T-cell function, and
adjusted output of immune signalling molecules. A second strand proposes restoration of immune
competence in states of immune exhaustion, rather than general immune boosting.

The specific receptor attribution commonly given for this mechanism could not be traced to a
primary mechanistic paper during research and is therefore not reproduced here.
[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]

The mechanistic pathway is largely inferred from cell-culture and animal systems, plus immune
markers measured inside human trials. Two such markers were observed:

- In the 361-patient severe sepsis trial, a monocyte surface marker associated with reversal of
  sepsis-related immune paralysis improved more in the treated group at day 3 and day 7.
- In the COVID-19 pilot, among patients on lower levels of oxygen support, the treated group
  had on average 3.84 times more CD4+ T cells on day 5 than on day 1.

**In both cases the marker moved and the clinical outcome did not clearly follow.** That
dissociation is the central caution about this compound: a plausible and partly measurable
immune effect that has not translated into reliable clinical benefit in the largest and
best-controlled trial available.

### KPV, proposed

All of this derives from cell-culture and animal work. No mechanism has been confirmed in a
living human.

The most-cited proposal is suppression of NF-kappaB signalling. In an immortalised human
bronchial epithelial cell line studied in the laboratory, KPV was reported to inhibit NF-kappaB,
to reduce matrix metalloproteinase-9 activity, and to reduce interleukin-8 and eotaxin
secretion, proposed to act by blocking nuclear entry of the p65RelA subunit. In that same study,
a different related peptide acted through a melanocortin receptor while KPV did not.

In cultured human keratinocyte cell lines and hamster ovary cells studied in the laboratory,
alpha-MSH, KPV, and ACTH produced rapid calcium responses inside the cells without the
accompanying second messenger of the classical melanocortin pathway, which the authors read as
signalling by a different route.

A frequently repeated proposal about uptake through an intestinal peptide transporter appeared
constantly in marketing material during research, but no primary paper establishing it was
retrieved. It is therefore not stated here as a mechanism.
[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]

The most honest available summary comes from a 2010 peer-reviewed review, which states that the
exact signalling mechanism used by KPV and related peptides currently is unknown. Marketing
material describing a confident mechanism is ahead of the published science.

### LL-37, proposed

LL-37 is described as a positively charged peptide proposed to act by disrupting the membranes
of microbes, with reported activity in laboratory work against various bacteria, some fungi,
and some viruses. Beyond that, it is described as having wide-ranging effects on immune cell
recruitment, inflammatory signalling, blood vessel formation, and skin regrowth, which is the
proposed basis for the wound-healing trials.

Three qualifications belong immediately alongside that description.

**The same property proposed to kill microbes is proposed as the basis of its toxicity to human
cells.** A 2025 review states that in laboratory culture LL-37 damages the membranes of human
red blood cells, lymphocytes, and fibroblasts at concentrations similar to those required for
antimicrobial activity. On that framing, the separation between the intended effect and damage
to the body's own cells is narrow by mechanism, not incidentally.

**A second proposed mechanism is immunologically adverse.** LL-37 is proposed to form complexes
with a person's own DNA, and those complexes are proposed to activate a type of immune cell to
produce type I interferon. This is advanced as a driver of psoriatic inflammation and as a
contributor to systemic lupus erythematosus. So the same molecule is proposed as host defence
and as an autoimmune driver, depending on context and level.

**Third, the native peptide is documented as unstable.** A 2025 review reports it is susceptible
to enzymatic breakdown, unstable at high salt concentrations, and shows low stability in
biological fluids. That is a mechanistic argument against the native peptide having durable
activity beyond where it is placed, and it is the stated reason the research field has pursued
modified versions.

live controversy.

built on a body of work that appears to lean observational and that disagrees with itself
substantially, sitting beside a null randomised trial, is a recognised pattern of confounding
rather than corroboration. The meta-analysis authors themselves state randomised trials are
still required.

**None of it studied healthy people.** Every retrieved trial enrolled acutely or chronically ill
patients. The evidence base and the way this compound is marketed do not overlap.

placebo on it.

any route other than application to a wound or injection directly into a skin tumour, and no
retrieved human trial enrolled healthy people. Naming those routes here marks where the evidence
stops. It is not guidance of any kind.

## Preclinical evidence

Preclinical means research in animals or in cells in a dish. It is not research in people, and
a preclinical result never establishes what happens in a person. Every finding below carries
its species or model.

**There is no preclinical evidence for the combination.** No study in any species or model gave
these three compounds together, or any two of them together.

### KPV

- In two mouse models of inflammatory bowel disease, KPV was reported to be associated with
  earlier recovery and greater regain of body weight, and in one of the models with
  histologically reduced inflammatory changes, with effects appearing partly independent of
  melanocortin-1 receptor signalling.
- In a mouse model of ulcerative colitis, together with cultured colonic epithelial cells and
  macrophages, KPV carried inside an engineered hyaluronic-acid-functionalised nanoparticle was
  reported to be associated with reduced mucosal damage and lower tumour necrosis factor alpha,
  and to outperform non-targeted formulations. **The tested article was an engineered targeted
  delivery system, not plain KPV**, and the authors attributed the advantage substantially to
  the delivery vehicle. This result does not transfer to unformulated KPV.
- In an immortalised human bronchial epithelial cell line studied in the laboratory, KPV was
  reported to inhibit NF-kappaB signalling, matrix metalloproteinase-9 activity, and
  interleukin-8 and eotaxin secretion.
- In cultured human keratinocyte cell lines and hamster ovary cells studied in the laboratory,
  alpha-MSH, KPV, and ACTH produced calcium responses without the classical second messenger.
  This was a signalling characterisation study, not an efficacy study.
- A 2008 peer-reviewed review reports that anti-inflammatory effects of the parent hormone
  alpha-MSH were observed across a range of animal models, and positions KPV as a candidate for
  possible future treatment. Most of the cited work is on the full hormone, not on KPV, and the
  review's forward-looking phrasing is itself evidence that nothing had been demonstrated in
  people at the time.
- A search for KPV together with alpha-MSH returned 17 records spanning delivery systems,
  structural modification, imaging probes, and reviews. None is indexed as a clinical trial.
  Titles were verified; the findings in 11 of them were never read, and no claim in this Guide
  rests on them.

A caution belongs with the mouse results. Rodent colitis models have a long history of failing
to predict human inflammatory bowel disease outcomes.

### LL-37

- In laboratory culture of human red blood cells, lymphocytes, and fibroblasts, LL-37 was
  reported to damage cell membranes at concentrations similar to those required for its
  antimicrobial effect. This is a safety-relevant finding, not an efficacy finding.
- In laboratory and biochemical characterisation, native LL-37 was reported to be susceptible to
  enzymatic breakdown, unstable at high salt concentrations, and of low stability in biological
  fluids. Low stability, cytotoxicity, and production cost are described as the principal
  barriers to clinical use, and are the reason modified versions are pursued.
- In laboratory culture of human plasmacytoid dendritic cells, together with measurements in
  human serum, LL-37 complexed with a person's own DNA was reported to activate those cells to
  produce type I interferon. Higher serum LL-37 was reported in patients with systemic lupus
  erythematosus than in healthy individuals, and antibodies against LL-37 were reported to
  correlate with antibodies against DNA. The source review itself notes that some studies find
  no association with disease activity.
- Elevated levels of a person's own LL-37 have been reported in ulcerative colitis, psoriasis,
  rosacea, and chronic periodontitis. Direction of causation is not established by those
  observations.

A flagged item that is **not** used as evidence: a record whose title asserts that LL-37
promotes the growth of a pulmonary fungal pathogen was surfaced in a search listing and never
read. If accurate it would be a directionally important counter-finding, which is why it is
noted rather than omitted. It must not be stated as fact on the strength of a title, and a human
reviewer must retrieve it.

### Thymosin Alpha-1

**No preclinical study of Thymosin Alpha-1 was retrieved during research for this Guide.** The
preclinical record here is empty because none was retrieved in that session, **not** because
none exists. That is a limitation of this Guide, and it is stated as one rather than as a
finding about the literature.

## Contradictions and disagreement

The full treatment is in CONTRADICTIONS.md. In brief:

1. **Does ingredient evidence carry over to a mixture?** No. The most important disagreement in
   this Guide, and it is not a close question. Three separate facts about three molecules do not
   combine into one fact about one preparation.
2. **Does KPV have human data?** Vendor pages say yes. The registry, the literature database,
   and the regulator's reported rationale all say no. The vendor claim is not merely unproven,
   it is contradicted.
3. **Does Thymosin Alpha-1 reduce death in sepsis?** Resolved in favour of no. A large
   double-blind trial superseded a smaller single-blind one that had suggested benefit.
4. **Does Thymosin Alpha-1 help in COVID-19?** Unresolved but weighted toward no established
   benefit. A positive pooled estimate from largely non-randomised data sits beside a null
   randomised trial.
5. **Is Thymosin Alpha-1 an approved medicine?** Approved somewhere, not approved in the United
   States, and designation is not approval. All three statements are true at once and the
   distinctions are the point.
6. **Has the United States position on Thymosin Alpha-1 changed?** Genuinely unsettled. Vendors
   assert a completed change; the only independent reporting describes a pending and contested
   one.
7. **Was the largest LL-37 trial positive?** No. It missed its primary endpoint. The favourable
   figure quoted from it is post-hoc and subgroup-restricted.
8. **Is LL-37 protective or harmful?** Both are supported in the peer-reviewed literature. It is
   a dual-natured molecule and any account giving only the host-defence side is incomplete.

## Limitations

What this Guide cannot tell you, and why.

1. **It cannot tell you anything about the combination**, because nothing has been studied. This
   is the governing limitation and everything else is secondary to it.

## Quality and identity

The full requirements are in QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md, where every item is currently unmet.
The points that belong in the Guide itself:

**Composition is the first unanswered question.** The exact composition and ingredient ratio of
this product is not confirmed. A blend cannot be assessed at all until it is known what is in it
and in what proportion.

**A blend adds quality requirements that no single-ingredient product has.** Identity and
content must be established for each component separately, not for the mixture as a whole. And
the mixture must be shown to be uniform, so that any portion contains the same proportion of
each component as any other. Neither has been confirmed here.

**No analytical study of material sold as any of these three compounds was located.** That is a
gap, not a favourable result. Nobody appears to have systematically checked what is in these
vials.

**An identity risk is specific to Thymosin Alpha-1.** Because thymalfasin genuinely is an
approved medicine somewhere, unregulated material can borrow the credibility of a real regulated
drug without being that drug. The trial and labelling data in this Guide describe the
pharmaceutical product used under medical supervision. They do not describe unregulated material.

**An identity risk is specific to KPV.** Regulatory filings treat KPV free base and KPV acetate
as separate substances, so the name alone does not pin down the material.

**Two identity risks are specific to LL-37.** The native peptide is documented as unstable, so it
can degrade during storage or shipping. And because much of the research field works with
engineered analogs, a product labelled LL-37 may contain a fragment or a modified version whose
evidence base is different. This is reasoning from the published stability and modification
literature, not a documented finding about specific marketed products.

**What is documented is how the unregulated channel performs generally.** A peer-reviewed 2024
market-surveillance study found that every vial purchased from illegal online pharmacies was a
probable substandard or falsified product, with measured purity far below the labelled claim and
contamination detected in every sample. **That study tested a different peptide. It tested none
of these three.** It is evidence about the channel and is cited only as such.

**A note on numbers encountered elsewhere.** Research surfaced several striking gray-market
failure statistics covering sequence errors, heavy metals, residual solvents, certificate
discrepancy rates, and audit failure rates. Every one traced to vendor blogs, forum posts, or
marketing pages with no locatable primary source. They are excluded here and must not be added.

## Safety categories

**The safety of this combination has not been studied.** Nothing is known about how these three
components behave together in a person, in either direction. Everything below concerns a single
compound studied alone, and none of it can be assumed to hold when three are combined.

Absence of reported harm reflects absence of study. It is not a safety record.

### Categories that follow from the component records

**Deliberate immunosuppression.** Labelling for the Thymosin Alpha-1 pharmaceutical describes it
as contraindicated in people being deliberately immunosuppressed, including organ transplant
recipients, unless benefits clearly outweigh risks. This is the clearest actionable signal in
this Guide and it follows directly from the proposed immune-enhancing mechanism. The source is a
secondary drug reference reflecting product labelling, not the primary approved label, which
could not be retrieved.

**Prior hypersensitivity.** The same labelling describes a contraindication in people with a
history of hypersensitivity to Thymosin Alpha-1 or to any component of the injection.

**Autoimmune disease and predisposition to it.** LL-37 is described in the peer-reviewed
literature as an autoantigen in psoriasis and is implicated in the mechanisms of systemic lupus
erythematosus. Higher levels of a person's own LL-37 have been reported in patients with lupus,
and antibodies against LL-37 correlate with antibodies against DNA. Whether administering LL-37
could trigger or worsen such a condition has not been studied in any retrieved trial. The source
review notes some studies find no association with disease activity.

**Damage to the body's own cells.** In laboratory culture, LL-37 damages human red blood cells,
lymphocytes, and fibroblasts at concentrations similar to those needed for its antimicrobial
effect. Whether that occurs at any exposure reached in a person has not been established.

**Broad suppression of inflammation.** The KPV preclinical work centres on suppressing
inflammatory signalling. Inflammation is a normal protective response, so any agent investigated
for broadly suppressing it raises unanswered questions about infection risk, about masking of
symptoms, and about interaction with existing immune or inflammatory conditions. None of these
has been studied in humans for KPV.

**Unpredictable off-target effects.** Because KPV's molecular target is not established, its
downstream effects in a person cannot be predicted from the existing literature.

**Product-quality risk, distinct from the molecules.** Material obtained outside a regulated
supply chain has unverified identity, purity, sterility, and contamination status. The reported
regulatory rationale for restricting peptides of this kind included immunogenicity risk from
injectable peptides, manufacturing impurities including bacteria and heavy metals, and
sensitivity to temperature and handling that can alter chemical composition.

### What the reassuring-looking data does and does not cover

Component trial safety records were unremarkable within their settings. In the 1,089-patient
sepsis trial, no safety outcome differed significantly from placebo. In the earlier sepsis
trial, no drug-related severe adverse event was reported. In the COVID-19 pilot, nine serious
adverse events occurred among treated patients, none deemed related to the drug. In the LL-37
wound trials, local reactions were mostly mild to moderate with no consistent group differences,
and serious adverse events were assessed as unrelated to treatment.

Those records cover short-course use in acutely ill, medically supervised hospital populations,
and application to chronic wounds over weeks. **They carry essentially no information about
elective long-term use by healthy adults, which is how these compounds are typically promoted.**
Adjusting immune function in a person whose immune system is working normally is not a
low-stakes intervention.

One further caution about a specific claim. Labelling for Thymosin Alpha-1 asserts that across
clinical experience in over 2,000 individuals no clinically significant adverse reactions
attributable to it were reported. That is manufacturer-derived characterisation, not independent
safety monitoring. Sweeping claims of freedom from toxicity are common in marketing for this
compound and are not reproduced here.

### Populations with no data

For the combination, all populations are unstudied. For the components, no data was located on
pregnancy, breastfeeding, children, older adults outside the specific trial populations,
immunocompromised people, people with autoimmune or inflammatory disease, or people taking
immune-modulating medication.

## Interactions

**No study of how these three components interact with one another was located.** Their combined
effect is unstudied, and it cannot be assumed to equal the sum of the parts. This is a
substantive gap rather than a formal one, because two of the three are described as acting on
immune function in ways that are not obviously the same direction.

**No interaction data with any medication or supplement was located for KPV.** For the other two
components, no interaction study was retrieved either. Unstudied does not mean absent.

Categories a member should raise with a clinician, derived from the proposed mechanisms and the
one labelling-derived contraindication rather than from interaction studies:

- Any medication that deliberately suppresses immune function, including after an organ
  transplant.
- Medication for autoimmune conditions, including psoriasis and lupus.
- Medication for inflammatory conditions.
- Any current infection, or any recent or planned vaccination.
- Any condition or medication where the immune system is already atypical.

That list is what to ask about. It is not a list of known interactions, because none is known.

## Questions for a qualified professional

If you are considering this, these are questions worth taking to a clinician who knows your
history. This Guide does not answer them and does not tell anyone what to do.

**About the combination**

1. Given that no study has tested these three compounds together, what would you want to know
   before forming any view?
2. What could happen when three compounds that act on immune and inflammatory signalling are
   given together, that would not happen with any of them alone?
3. How would either of us know whether something that happened was caused by the combination, by
   one component, or by something else entirely?

**About the product**

4. If the composition and ratio are not confirmed, what am I actually considering?
5. What documentation would you want to see about identity and content for each component
   separately, and about whether the mixture is uniform?
6. What would you want to see about sterility and contamination?

**About my own situation**

7. Do I have any condition, or take any medication, that makes adjusting immune function a
   particularly poor idea for me?
8. I have read that one component is described as an autoantigen in psoriasis and is implicated
   in lupus. Does anything in my history or family history make that relevant?
9. Am I on anything that suppresses immune function, or that treats an autoimmune or inflammatory
   condition?
10. Are there tests you would want before, and monitoring you would want during, if I proceeded
    against your advice?

**About the evidence**

11. The strongest trial of one component found no effect, and the largest trial of another missed
    its primary endpoint. How should that change how I read the marketing for this product?
12. A third component has never been given to a person in any recorded study. Does that change
    anything for you?
13. What would you want to see published before you would consider this reasonable?

**About stopping**

14. What would make you tell me to stop immediately?
15. Who do I contact, and how quickly, if something happens?
