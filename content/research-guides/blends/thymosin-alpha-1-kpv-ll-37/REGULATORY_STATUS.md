# Blend Regulatory Status: Thymosin Alpha-1 + KPV + LL-37

**Status as recorded on 2026-07-21.** Every statement carries a jurisdiction, a date, and a
source. Source IDs refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

---

## Standing principles for this page

1. **Every statement carries a jurisdiction, a date, and a source.** A regulatory statement
   without all three is not a statement, it is a rumour.
2. **Regulatory status expires.** Nothing here should be relied on more than 90 days after the
   date shown. Two of the three components are at live, moving points in the same process.
3. **A regulator's decision applies in that regulator's jurisdiction only.** Approval elsewhere
   is not approval here.
4. **Approval of a single agent is not approval of a mixture containing it.** No regulator has
   evaluated this combination.
5. **A designation is not an approval.** Orphan drug designation is a development incentive
   status and is routinely presented as though it were marketing approval.
6. **An advisory committee recommendation is advice, not a determination.** Formal restriction or
   permission requires the regulator's own action.
7. **Where a status could not be verified, this page says so** rather than repeating a claim from
   a source with a commercial interest in it.
8. **Not checked is not permitted.** An unexamined jurisdiction is recorded as unexamined.

## Integrity note, read before using this page

Across all three component research sessions, every attempt to reach the United States
regulator's own web pages failed. Direct fetches of fda.gov returned HTTP 404 and a mirror
returned HTTP 403, in a pattern consistent with automated-access blocking rather than the
documents being absent. A Federal Register notice was blocked by a redirect and its

not be retrieved or parsed on any attempt.

**Consequently, every United States statement on this page rests on secondary sources:**
investigative journalism, regulatory trade press, a professional society, an advocacy submission,
and a secondary drug reference. Those are reasonable sources for reporting what a regulator did.
They are not the regulator.

A human reviewer must open the primary pages directly and confirm each statement before
publication. Until that happens, nothing on this page should be treated as settled.

## The combination

### BL-01. Approval status of the combination

**Jurisdiction:** All jurisdictions examined.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Statement:** This three-component combination is not an approved medicine in any jurisdiction,
and no regulator has evaluated it as a combination. No marketing authorisation, no approved
specification, and no regulator assessment of the preparation as sold was located.
**Source:** TA-08, KP-07, LL-09, and the absence of any combination record across all three
component research sessions.
**Verification:** Consistent across all three research records. The specific United States
statements it rests on are themselves secondary and require confirmation.

### BL-02. Why component status does not settle blend status

**Jurisdiction:** All.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Statement:** The three components sit at three different points in the same United States
process, and none of those positions describes the mixture. A regulator evaluating a single
substance for use in compounding is not evaluating a preparation that combines it with two
others. Nothing on this page should be read as a regulatory position on the product as sold.
**Source:** TA-08, KP-07, KP-08, LL-09.

## Thymosin Alpha-1

### TA-R-01. United States marketing approval

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Statement:** Thymosin Alpha-1, also called thymalfasin, has not received United States
marketing approval for any indication. Approval in other countries does not confer or imply
United States approval.
**Source:** TA-08 (ProPublica investigative reporting, 2026-04-03, describing the regulatory
posture toward thymosin-alpha as a compounded, non-approved substance), together with TA-09 (a
secondary drug reference monograph which does not assert United States approval).
**Verification:** **UNVERIFIED at source.** Direct retrieval of the regulator's approval database
was attempted and every fda.gov URL returned HTTP 404. A human reviewer must confirm this against
the primary approval record before publication.

### TA-R-02. Approval outside the United States

**Jurisdiction:** Non-United States jurisdictions.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Statement:** Thymalfasin is reported to be an approved prescription medicine under a brand name
in a number of countries, principally for chronic hepatitis B, with first approval commonly
attributed to the mid-1990s.
**Source:** TA-09 (secondary drug reference).
**Verification:** **UNVERIFIED.** No primary regulatory source, meaning a national regulator's
approval record or a company regulatory filing, was retrieved to confirm the approval list. **The
commonly repeated country count appears predominantly in vendor and marketing material and is
deliberately not reproduced anywhere in this folder.** What can be stated carefully is the point
that matters: it is approved somewhere, and it is not approved in the United States.

### TA-R-03. Orphan drug designation

**Jurisdiction:** United States and European Union.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Statement:** Search results referenced orphan drug designation in both jurisdictions for
thymalfasin in a liver cancer indication. **Orphan drug designation is a development incentive
status, not marketing approval.**
**Source:** TA-08 (referenced), designation records themselves not retrieved.
**Verification:** **UNVERIFIED.** Neither designation record could be retrieved, so no designation
number and no date is recorded. The reader-facing point stands regardless of verification:
designation is routinely misrepresented as approval in marketing material, and this page states
the distinction so that confusion is not possible.

### TA-R-04. United States compounding, Category 2 placement in 2023

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Statement:** In 2023, 19 peptides including thymosin-alpha were placed into Category 2 of the
interim 503A bulks list, the category for substances that raise significant safety risks for
compounding. Reported rationale included immunogenicity risk from injectable peptides,
manufacturing impurities such as bacteria or heavy metals, temperature and handling sensitivity
that can alter chemical composition, and a limited human evidence base.
**Source:** TA-08 (ProPublica, 2026-04-03, reporting the agency's stated rationale).
**Verification:** VERIFIED as reporting. The underlying regulator document was not retrieved.
This is journalistic reporting of regulatory reasoning, not the regulator's document.

### TA-R-05. United States advisory committee, December 2024

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Statement:** In late 2024 the regulator presented review documents on six peptides including
thymosin-alpha to its Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee, and the committee sided with the
agency, endorsing the position that these peptides were too risky for general dispensing. Search
metadata further indicated a proposal that thymosin alpha-1 acetate not be included on the 503A
bulks list, and a committee vote against inclusion at a meeting dated 2024-12-04.
**Source:** TA-08.
**Verification:** **UNVERIFIED for the vote itself.** The meeting materials and minutes could not
be opened, so vote counts are not confirmed and are not stated. A committee vote is advisory
only; formal restriction requires the regulator's own rulemaking.

### TA-R-06. United States current position: unsettled

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Statement:** Independent reporting dated 2026-04-03 describes the regulator, under current
department leadership, as poised to reverse the 2023 peptide categorisation, with the position
that the 2023 decision was improper and that there was no safety signal, and with former agency
officials disputing that characterisation. **As of that reporting this was an anticipated
reversal, not a completed one.**
**Source:** TA-08.
**Verification:** VERIFIED as reporting of a pending and contested action.

**Conflicting claim, recorded and rejected.** Multiple peptide-vendor and clinic websites assert
as settled fact that thymosin alpha-1 was reclassified to Category 1, or restored to legal
compounding, in February 2026. **That assertion could not be verified in any primary or
independent source**, and the April 2026 independent reporting is inconsistent with the matter
being settled. A source with a financial interest in a product being available is the weakest
possible source for the claim that it has become available. The chronology is also internally
inconsistent: an April 2026 piece describing a reversal as pending is hard to reconcile with a
completed February 2026 reclassification.

**This status is genuinely in flux and must be re-verified immediately before publication**,
because a Guide that states the wrong legal status could directly influence a purchasing
decision.

## KPV

### KP-R-01. United States approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-20.
**Statement:** KPV is not an approved drug for any indication.
**Source:** KP-07 (BioSpace trade reporting, 2026-04-16).
**Verification:** VERIFIED as reporting. No approval was found in any retrieved source, and the
regulator's own process treats KPV as an unapproved bulk substance under evaluation for
compounding, which is only coherent if it is unapproved.

### KP-R-02. United States compounding, Category 2 placement

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-20.
**Statement:** KPV was placed in Category 2 of the 503A bulk drug substances framework, the
category for substances raising significant safety risks pending further evaluation. A stated
reason, attributed to a September 2023 agency statement, was an absence of human exposure data
for KPV.
**Source:** KP-07.
**Verification:** VERIFIED as reporting. **This is the single most important regulatory fact
about this component: the agency itself characterised the human data as absent, not merely
thin.**

### KP-R-03. United States reconsideration under way

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-20.
**Statement:** The regulator is reconsidering the Category 2 classification as part of a broader
peptide review. KPV-related bulk drug substances, free base and acetate, were scheduled for
review at the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of 23 to 24 July 2026.
**Source:** KP-08 (RAPS, 2026-04-16), corroborated by KP-07.
**Verification:** VERIFIED as reporting.

### KP-R-04. The advisory committee review is PENDING, with no outcome stated

**Jurisdiction:** United States.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-20.
**Statement:** As of the research date of 2026-07-20, the meeting scheduled for 23 to 24 July
2026 had **not yet occurred**. No outcome is stated anywhere in this folder.
**Source:** KP-07 and KP-08, both dated 2026-04-16 and both describing the meeting in the future
tense.
**Verification:** VERIFIED as reporting.

**Timing warning, handle carefully.** During research, multiple search-engine summaries and
vendor status-tracker pages asserted in the past tense that the committee had discussed KPV on
23 July 2026 and that a regulatory position had been proposed. No such outcome could be verified,
and the two contemporaneous trade sources describe the meeting in the future tense. This page
therefore states no outcome.

**Note for whoever updates this page.** The meeting date has now passed relative to this
document's date of 2026-07-21. That does not license inferring an outcome. A human must read the
actual record, not a summary of it. And a committee recommendation, whatever it is, is advisory
and is not a final regulatory determination.

## Anti-doping

### AD-01. World Anti-Doping Agency

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19 and 2026-07-20.
**Statement:** **No anti-doping status is stated for Thymosin Alpha-1, for KPV, for LL-37, or for
the combination.**
**Source:** TA-10, KP-10, LL-10, all UNVERIFIED.
**Verification:** **NOT RETRIEVED IN ANY SESSION.** The prohibited list could not be retrieved or
parsed on repeated attempts across all three component research sessions, returning no usable
content. Every search result asserting a status for any of the three came from peptide-vendor or
peptide-directory websites, which are not acceptable sources for an anti-doping claim, and all
were discarded.

**What this means for an athlete.** Nothing on this page tells you whether any of these
substances is permitted. An athlete subject to testing must consult the current prohibited list
directly and their own anti-doping authority. Do not rely on a seller's statement, and do not
read the absence of a statement here as permission.

**A related fact that causes real confusion.** Thymosin Beta-4, also sold as TB-500, is a
different peptide from Thymosin Alpha-1, with a different anti-doping position. The two are
frequently conflated in commercial material.

**A note on inference.** The prohibited list includes a category covering substances with no
current approval by any governmental regulatory health authority for human therapeutic use.
Whether any of these components falls under it is a logical inference from that definition rather
than a retrieved statement about these substances, and **this folder deliberately does not make
that inference on a member's behalf.**
