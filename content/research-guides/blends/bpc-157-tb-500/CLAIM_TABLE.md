# BPC-157 + TB-500 Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Grades apply to individual claims. Neither component is graded as a
whole and the product is never graded as a whole. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

---

## The rule that governs every row below

**A mixture is its own subject. Studying BPC-157 tells you about BPC-157. Studying TB-500 tells you
about TB-500. Neither tells you about the two of them given together, because the pair has its own
absorption, its own interaction behaviour and its own unanswered questions. Where no study
administered this pair, the correct entry is not a low grade. It is no evidence at all.**

This table enforces that rule structurally. Section 1 is the combination section, and a claim may
appear there only if it cites a source that actually concerns the pair. Component evidence lives in
sections 3 and 4 and may not be moved, summed, averaged or narrated into section 1. Any row that
does so is a defect, not a judgement call.

## The headline findings

1. **No study of BPC-157 and TB-500 administered together was located.** Not a randomised trial, not
   an open label study, not a case series, not a registration. Section 1 contains no efficacy claim
   and no safety claim, and that emptiness is the finding.
2. **The only human record specific to the pair is an adverse event report.** FDA's FAERS search
   through 2025-12-04 returned three reports for BPC-157 in total. One concerned a product
   containing BPC-157 and TB500, in which a 40 year old woman developed diffuse hyperpigmentation and
   gingival darkening, reproducible on rechallenge and resolving on discontinuation (S01).
3. **TB-500 has no human evidence base at all.** One registered human study exists, it is recruiting,
   its primary endpoints are adverse event counts, and no results are posted (S23).
4. **BPC-157's human evidence base is five small studies, four of which exist only as FDA
   summaries**, and the only randomised controlled trial among them did not separate from placebo
   (S01).
5. **The composition and ratio of the Xenios product are not confirmed.** No row in this table may
   be read as applying to a specific formulation, because no formulation has been specified.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical, meaning animal or cultured cells |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

No claim in this table is graded A or B. Grade C appears only for the small, uncontrolled or null
BPC-157 human studies that were genuinely retrieved, and every C row carries its design limits.
**Every claim in section 1 is graded G, without exception.**

These may not appear on any member-facing surface, in any form, hedged or otherwise. This is the

## Reviewer sign-off checklist for this table

1. Every row in section 1 cites a source that concerns the pair, and no row in section 1 cites a
   single ingredient study.
2. No component finding has been moved, merged or narrated into a statement about the combination
   anywhere in this folder.
3. Every preclinical sentence names its species or model in the same sentence as the finding.
4. Every TB-500 claim has been checked against the fragment versus full length protein distinction.
5. No sentence contains a dose, amount, schedule, or preparation detail, and every route mention
   exists solely to state that evidence is absent.
6. Every regulatory sentence carries a jurisdiction, a date and a source.
7. Every claim graded G or marked UNVERIFIED has been closed by a human check or removed.
8. No citation appears anywhere in this folder that is not present in SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.
