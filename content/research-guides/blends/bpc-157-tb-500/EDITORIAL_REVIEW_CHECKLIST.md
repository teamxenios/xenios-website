## 0. The blend integrity gate. Check this section first.

**The principle this section enforces:** ingredient research and mixture research are different
research. What is known about BPC-157 was learned by studying BPC-157. What is known about TB-500 was
learned by studying TB-500. Neither exercise looked at the two together, so neither produces a
finding about the two together. Where no study administered the pair, the honest entry is not a low
confidence claim. It is the absence of a claim. A reviewer who finds component evidence doing the
work of combination evidence anywhere in this folder should stop and fail the gate, regardless of how
carefully the sentence is hedged.

## Standing rules for this gate

1. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human sign-off
   is complete.
2. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
3. **Ingredient evidence is not mixture evidence, and no amount of careful hedging converts one into
   the other.** A blend has its own pharmacology and its own unknowns. If no study gave these two
   together, the folder reports nothing about the two together. This rule governs every other rule in
   this gate, and section 0 exists to enforce it.
4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a reviewer
   or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed and logged as
   a gap. Omitting is always better than guessing. Four BPC-157 human studies in this folder exist
   only as FDA summaries and must never acquire constructed identifiers.
5. **Concluding that evidence is absent is a successful outcome**, not a failure of research. An
   honest empty combination table is the correct result here and must not be padded, softened, or
   filled with component material.
6. **Substituting one molecule for another is a factual error, not a simplification.** Full length
   thymosin beta-4 data is not TB-500 data, in any context, at any grade.
7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification. The pending FDA advisory committee review makes these
   compounds specifically time sensitive.
8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety or regulatory
   statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed by its named
   reviewer.
