# BPC-157 + TB-500 Regulatory Status

**Status checked 2026-07-21. This page must be re-verified before publication and re-checked on any
subsequent update.** Regulatory positions change, and one item on this page concerns a review that
had not taken place at the time of checking.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Standing principles for this page

1. Every statement below carries a jurisdiction, a date checked and a source. A statement without all
   three does not belong on this page.
2. Approval, authorisation or prohibition in one jurisdiction says nothing about another. No
   inference is drawn across borders.
3. Where a status could not be confirmed by reading a primary document, that is stated explicitly
   rather than filled in by inference.
4. An advisory committee recommendation is advisory. It is not a final agency determination. A staff
   recommendation placed before a committee is a proposal.
5. **A regulatory position on one component is not a position on the other, and neither is a position
   on the blend.** No regulator retrieved for this page has evaluated the combination as a product.
   Statements below concern individual substances.

## Integrity note, read before using this page

The two halves of this page are not equally verified, and the reason is a property of the research
sessions rather than of the compounds.

**BPC-157 statements rest on primary FDA documents**, retrieved and read, including a 68 page
briefing document, the current bulk substances safety page, the advisory committee meeting page, and
two enforcement letters.

**TB-500 statements do not.** Every fda.gov URL attempted during the TB-500 research returned HTTP
404 to that session's fetcher, including pages on compounding status and a warning letter to
GenoGenix LLC concerning thymosin beta-4. No FDA claim about TB-500 is verified anywhere in this
folder, and none is made.

**Anti-doping status for both components rests on the United States Anti-Doping Agency**, not on the
World Anti-Doping Agency Prohibited List itself, which returned empty content or was not retrieved on
every attempt across both research sessions.

A human reviewer must open the relevant regulator pages directly and confirm each statement before
publication.

A note on dates. The underlying retrievals were made 2026-07-19. This page is dated 2026-07-21.
Every entry must be re-confirmed as of the publication date, and the header re-dated accordingly. A
stale date on this page is itself a defect.

## The combination

### CB-01. No regulator has evaluated this combination as a product

**Jurisdiction:** all jurisdictions checked.
**Statement:** No regulatory evaluation, authorisation, approval or prohibition specific to a
BPC-157 and TB-500 combination product was located in any jurisdiction. Regulatory positions recorded
on this page concern the individual substances. FDA's briefing document notes that products
frequently combine BPC-157 with other unapproved peptides, TB-500 or thymosin beta-4 among them, and
raises this as a reason that benefit claims and harm signals become unattributable. That is an
observation that such products are sold, not an evaluation of one.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download
**Verification:** Primary FDA document, retrieved and read (S01).
**Handling rule:** never present a regulatory position on either component as a position on the
blend, and never present FDA's observation that these are co-formulated as regulatory familiarity or
acceptance.

## United States, BPC-157

### US-B-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** BPC-157 is not an approved drug. FDA's July 2026 briefing document states that neither
BPC-157 free base nor BPC-157 acetate is a component of an FDA approved drug, and that there is no
approved product containing BPC-157 related bulk drug substances in any country at this time, nor is
either form found in the European, Japanese or International Pharmacopeias.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download
**Verification:** Primary FDA document, retrieved and read (S01).

### US-B-02. No pharmacopoeial standard

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration and the United States Pharmacopeia.
**Statement:** There is no United States Pharmacopeia or National Formulary drug substance monograph
for either BPC-157 form, and no United States Pharmacopeia dietary supplement monograph.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download
**Verification:** Primary FDA document, retrieved and read (S01).
**Why this matters beyond regulation:** without a monograph there is no official identity, purity or
quality specification that any supplier can be held to. See QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md.

### US-B-03. Current listing on the bulk substances safety page

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** On FDA's page for certain bulk drug substances used in compounding that may present
significant safety risks, content current as of 2026-04-22, BPC-157 appears in the table headed bulk
drug substances nominated but withdrawn, described as substances previously in category 2 of the
interim policies that were withdrawn by the nominators. The listed concern states that compounded
drugs containing BPC-157 may pose risk for immunogenicity for certain routes of administration and
may have complexities regarding peptide related impurities and active pharmaceutical ingredient
characterisation, that FDA has identified no or only limited safety related information for the
proposed routes, and that the agency therefore lacks sufficient information to know whether the drug
would cause harm when administered to humans.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/drugs/human-drug-compounding/certain-bulk-drug-substances-use-compounding-may-present-significant-safety-risks
**Verification:** Primary FDA page, retrieved and read (S02).
**Important:** BPC-157 does **not** currently appear in the main Category 2 table on that page.

### US-B-04. The disputed September 2023 Category 2 date

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** A widely repeated claim that BPC-157 was placed in Category 2 in September 2023 and
that this is its current status **could not be verified from any FDA source**. FDA's live page now
presents BPC-157 under nominated but withdrawn rather than in the dated Category 2 table, so that
table's date column does not apply to it.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/drugs/human-drug-compounding/certain-bulk-drug-substances-use-compounding-may-present-significant-safety-risks
**Verification:** The FDA page was retrieved and read; the September 2023 date was not found in it
(S02).
**Handling rule:** do not repeat the Category 2 since September 2023 framing as current fact. The
practical consequence at US-B-05 is unaffected and may be stated. A reviewer should check the Federal
Register and FDA docket record for the actual history.

### US-B-05. Not eligible for 503A compounding, confirmed by two enforcement actions

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** FDA has stated in enforcement correspondence that products compounded with BPC-157 are
not eligible for the exemptions provided by section 503A(a), because the substance is not the subject
of an applicable United States Pharmacopeia or National Formulary monograph, is not a component of an
FDA approved human drug, and does not appear on the 503A bulks list. A warning letter to Tailor Made
Compounding LLC dated 2020-04-01 cites the firm for compounding with BPC 157 among other substances.
An untitled letter dated 2023-09-14 to Barclay, Luke, and Pillai Specialty Pharmacy PLLC, trading as
Meta Pharmacy Services, states the firm compounded products using BPC-157 acetate among others, on
the same three grounds.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Sources:**
https://www.fda.gov/inspections-compliance-enforcement-and-criminal-investigations/warning-letters/tailor-made-compounding-llc-594743-04012020
https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/fda-commissioner/barclay-luke-pillai-specialty-pharmacy-pllc-untitled-letter-issued-9142023
**Verification:** Both primary FDA enforcement documents, retrieved and read (S04, S05).
**Note:** a systematic search of all FDA warning letters naming BPC-157 could not be completed, as
FDA's warning letter search interface did not return results to direct retrieval. Two letters were
verified by direct retrieval. That is a floor, not a total.

### US-B-06. Under evaluation, with NO OUTCOME

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** Both nominations for BPC-157 free base and BPC-157 acetate were withdrawn by the
nominators, and FDA is evaluating both substances on its own initiative. An FDA review memo dated
2026-05-11 was placed before the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of 23 to 24 July
2026, with the meeting page content current as of 2026-07-17. FDA's stated recommendation is that on
balance the physicochemical characterisation, information on historical use, lack of evidence of
effectiveness, and safety information identified for both substances weigh against them being added
to the 503A bulks list.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Sources:** https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download and
https://www.fda.gov/advisory-committees/advisory-committee-calendar/july-23-24-2026-meeting-pharmacy-compounding-advisory-committee-07232026
**Verification:** Both primary FDA documents, retrieved and read (S01, S03).
**Status of this item:** **the meeting had not occurred as of the checks behind this page, and no
outcome exists.** FDA's recommendation is a staff proposal pending advisory committee input, not a
final determination. No outcome is stated anywhere in this folder.
**Handling rule:** describe the review as pending. When the record becomes available, read the
primary record rather than a summary of it, and remember that a committee recommendation is advisory
rather than final. This page returns to the review gate if it is more than 90 days old at
publication, and this item makes the compound specifically time sensitive.

### US-B-07. Political signals are not regulatory changes

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Department of Health and Human Services, reported.
**Statement:** Journalism dated 2026-02-03 reports that HHS leadership has signalled openness to
loosening restrictions on unapproved peptides, and that FDA leadership responses in December 2025
were open yet non-committal, describing a plan to review peptides to determine which are harmless.
The retrieved article provides **no evidence that any classification was formally reversed.**
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://undark.org/2026/02/03/bpc-157-peptide-fda/
**Verification:** Science journalism, retrieved and read, used for regulatory and political context
only and never as scientific evidence (S13).
**Excluded by name:** an assertion encountered in a web search summary that HHS announced plans to
reverse the classification in February 2026. That is not supported by the article actually retrieved
and must not be published.

### US-B-08. Clinical trial registrations

**Jurisdiction:** United States, ClinicalTrials.gov.
**Statement:** Two registrations for BPC-157 were located. NCT02637284, a Phase 1 pilot safety and
pharmacokinetics study in healthy volunteers, has no posted results, an overall status of Unknown,
and a last update posted 2015-12-17. NCT07437547, a randomised double blind placebo controlled Phase
2 trial in acute grade II hamstring strain at Peking University Shenzhen Hospital, was recruiting as
of a last update of 2026-02-22, with estimated study completion 2028-02-17. Neither has results, and
neither studies the combination.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Sources:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT02637284 and
https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07437547
**Verification:** Primary registry records, retrieved and read (S06, S07). Registry entries are
sponsor supplied and are not verified by the registry.

## United States, TB-500

### US-T-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** No FDA approved TB-500 product was located. The only registered human study of the
TB-500 fragment is an ongoing Phase 1/2 safety study with no posted results, which is inconsistent
with any approved status. Full length thymosin beta-4 products, RGN-259 and NL005, also remain
investigational, with trials completed or ongoing and no approval identified.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.term=thymosin+beta+4
**Verification:** Primary registry query, retrieved and read (S22). This is an inference from the
absence of an approval and the presence of an early phase study, not a retrieved FDA statement.

### US-T-02. Compounding status: NOT ESTABLISHED

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** **This Guide states no position on the FDA compounding status of TB-500 or thymosin
beta-4.** Conflicting claims circulate in secondary and commercial sources about whether the
substance sits in Category 2 of the interim 503A bulks list, whether it was removed in 2026, and
whether it appeared on a July 2026 advisory committee agenda. None could be verified. Every fda.gov
URL attempted during the TB-500 research returned HTTP 404, and the Federal Register record retrieved
for the April 2026 advisory committee meeting notice, document 2026-07361, contains only a generic
abstract naming no substances.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.federalregister.gov/api/v1/documents/2026-07361.json
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED as to TB-500's status. The Federal Register record was retrieved and
read, and is uninformative on this question (S25).
**Handling rule:** no compounding status claim for TB-500 may be published until a human checks
fda.gov directly. **Do not infer TB-500's status from BPC-157's.** The two were researched in
different sessions with different access, and BPC-157's status being easier to verify says nothing
about TB-500's.

### US-T-03. An enforcement document that could not be retrieved

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** A warning letter to GenoGenix LLC, reference 718739, dated 2026-01-20, concerning
thymosin beta-4, appeared in search results during research. **The FDA page could not be retrieved
and its content is recorded nowhere in this folder.** Nothing is asserted about what it contains.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** Not retrievable in the research session.
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED. Existence indicated by search metadata only.
**Handling rule:** a reviewer should retrieve this letter directly. It would be directly relevant

### US-T-04. Clinical trial registration

**Jurisdiction:** United States, ClinicalTrials.gov.
**Statement:** A full registry query for thymosin beta 4 returned 18 registered studies, of which
exactly one studies the TB-500 fragment. NCT07487363 is a Phase 1/2 randomised, double blind, placebo
controlled, sequential escalating cohort study in an estimated 80 adults with stable atherosclerotic
cardiovascular disease and endothelial dysfunction, sponsored by Hudson Biotech. It started
2026-02-05, was first posted 2026-03-23, is recruiting, and has no posted results. Its primary
outcomes are incidence of treatment emergent adverse events over 12 weeks and incidence of serious
adverse events over 28 days.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Sources:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.term=thymosin+beta+4 and
https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies/NCT07487363
**Verification:** Primary registry records, retrieved and read (S22, S23). Registry entries are
sponsor supplied.
**Correct use:** this registration is proof that human study of TB-500 has only just begun. It is not
support for anything.

## United States, other federal position

### US-D-01. Department of Defense

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Department of Defense, Operation Supplement Safety.
**Statement:** In an article dated 2025-04-29, the Department states that BPC-157 is an unapproved
drug that cannot be legally prescribed or sold over the counter, is not a lawful dietary ingredient,
appears on the Department's prohibited dietary supplement ingredients list and on the World
Anti-Doping Agency list under non-approved substances, and that there is little to no reliable
scientific evidence to support the safety or effectiveness of BPC-157 in humans. It also states that
FDA has cautioned against compounded drugs containing BPC-157 due to safety risks and potential
contamination with other substances, and that such products are often mislabelled not for human
consumption, research use only, or for research purposes only.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.opss.org/article/bpc-157-prohibited-peptide-and-unapproved-drug-found-health-and-wellness-products
**Verification:** Primary US government page, retrieved and read (S11).
**Note:** this entry concerns BPC-157. No equivalent Department of Defense statement on TB-500 was

## Anti-doping

### AD-01. BPC-157

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency, via the United States Anti-Doping Agency.
**Statement:** USADA states that BPC-157 is prohibited under the S0 unapproved substances category of
the List. The USADA page retrieved did not itself state the list year, nor whether the prohibition is
in competition only or at all times.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.usada.org/spirit-of-sport/bpc-157-peptide-prohibited/
**Verification:** National anti-doping organisation page, retrieved and read (S12). Secondary to the
World Anti-Doping Agency Prohibited List, which was not retrieved.
**Excluded:** vendor claims that BPC-157 is additionally named under section S2. Not used, unverified.

### AD-02. TB-500

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency, via the United States Anti-Doping Agency.
**Statement:** USADA's summary of changes to the 2018 Prohibited List states verbatim that
thymosin-B4 and its derivatives, for example TB-500, were added as examples of prohibited growth
factors under S2.3. TB-500 is therefore explicitly named. This verifies the 2018 list change.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.usada.org/athlete-advisory/2018-prohibited-list-summary-of-major-changes/
**Verification:** National anti-doping organisation page, retrieved and read (S24).

### AD-03. What could not be confirmed

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency.
**Statement:** The World Anti-Doping Agency Prohibited List document itself was **not retrieved in
either research session.** WADA pages returned empty content across multiple attempts. The current
edition's section numbering, the scope of prohibition, the current wording, and whether each
component is named explicitly or captured by a class definition are therefore all unconfirmed. Note
that the two sources above place the two components under different sections, S0 for BPC-157 and
S2.3 for TB-500, which is plausible but unverified against the List itself.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Source:** https://www.wada-ama.org/en/prohibited-list
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED. Not read.
**Handling rule:** the Guide may state that both components appear to be prohibited, attributed to
USADA and dated. It may not state a section number as current fact. Athletes subject to testing are
directed to their own anti-doping authority.

### AD-04. Detection is documented, not theoretical

**Jurisdiction:** Anti-doping science, general and equine.
**Statement:** Validated detection methods exist for TB-500 and its metabolites. Analytical work
developed methods for simultaneous detection of N-acetylated LKKTETQ and its metabolites in equine
urine and plasma, and a separate study found one metabolite, Ac-LKK, persisting as detectable up to
72 hours in rats. A 2026 review states that thymosin beta-4 and TB-500 remain banned substances in
sport.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-21.
**Sources:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23084823/ ,
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38382158/ and https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41476424/
**Verification:** Peer reviewed literature, retrieved and read (S15, S16, S17).
**Why this is on a regulatory page:** it makes the anti-doping consequence a concrete testing risk
rather than a formal prohibition with no means of enforcement.

## Other jurisdictions

No regulatory statement was retrieved for the European Union, the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia,
Japan or any other jurisdiction, for either component or for the blend. No national regulator outside
the United States was consulted directly in either research session, and no trial registry outside
ClinicalTrials.gov was searched.

The absence of an entry here means the question was not investigated. It does not mean the compounds
are unregulated, permitted or approved anywhere. Approval or prohibition in one jurisdiction is not
approval or prohibition in another, and the reverse also holds: nothing about another country may be
inferred from the United States position recorded above.

One partial exception is recorded above at US-B-01. FDA states there is no approved product
containing BPC-157 related substances in any country at this time, and that neither form appears in
the European, Japanese or International Pharmacopeias. That is an FDA statement about the world, not
a retrieved statement from each of those authorities.
