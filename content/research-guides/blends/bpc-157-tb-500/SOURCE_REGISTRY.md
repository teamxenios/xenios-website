# BPC-157 + TB-500 Source Registry

Every source behind this blend Guide, with its retrieval status and, critically, what it is
evidence **about**. Sources marked UNVERIFIED were not successfully read and cannot carry a claim
on their own.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Verification key

- **VERIFIED 2026-07-19**: retrieved and read in a component research session at the URL shown.
- **UNVERIFIED**: the fetch failed, was blocked, or returned unparseable content. Existence may be
  known from metadata, but the content was not read.
- **NON-EVIDENCE**: retrieved, but disqualified as a source of scientific fact by the exclusion
  rules in SOURCE_PLAN.md.

Note for the reviewer: entries are stamped 2026-07-19 because that is the date the underlying
retrievals were made. These files carry a review date of 2026-07-21. Reconcile the two before
publication, and re-confirm every regulatory entry as of the publication date.

## Subject key, which is the part that matters for a blend

Every source carries one of the following subject tags. A source may only support a claim in the
matching section of CLAIM_TABLE.md.

- **[BPC]** studied BPC-157 alone.
- **[TB5]** studied the TB-500 fragment, Ac-LKKTETQ.
- **[TB4]** studied full length thymosin beta-4 or a formulated product made from it. **This is a
  different molecule from TB-500 and may never support a TB-500 claim.**
- **[COMBO]** concerns BPC-157 and TB-500 together.
- **[CHANNEL]** concerns the unregulated supply channel generally, not either component.
- **[CONTEXT]** background, not scientific evidence.

There is exactly one **[COMBO]** entry in this registry, it is an adverse event report rather than
a study, and it sits inside a larger document. That is the shape of the combination evidence base.
