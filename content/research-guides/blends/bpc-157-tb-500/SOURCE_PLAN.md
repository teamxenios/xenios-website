# BPC-157 + TB-500 Source Plan

This document records how the evidence base for this blend Guide was assembled, what was
deliberately excluded, and what a human reviewer must still do before anything in this folder is
published.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## 1. The question this search had to answer

A blend Guide has a harder job than a single-ingredient Guide, because there are three separate
evidence questions and they must never be allowed to blur into one.

1. What is known about BPC-157 on its own?
2. What is known about TB-500 on its own?
3. What is known about the two of them given together?

Question 3 is the one this product actually raises, and it is the question that marketing material
almost never asks. The search was therefore designed so that finding nothing for question 3 would be
a reportable result rather than a hole to be filled with material from questions 1 and 2.

The governing rule for this folder, stated once here and repeated in the Guide, the claim table and
the review checklist:

> A finding about an ingredient is not a finding about a mixture containing that ingredient. Two
> compounds given together form a third thing, with its own absorption, its own interaction profile
> and its own unanswered questions. If no study administered this pair, then there is no evidence
> about this pair at any strength. It is not weak evidence. There is none.

## 2. What the search for combination evidence returned

The two component research records were searched in full for any study, trial, registration, case
series or analysis in which BPC-157 and TB-500 were administered together and an outcome was
measured.

**No such study exists in the retrieved record.** Not a randomised trial, not an open-label study,
not a case series, not a registered trial. The searches behind these records covered PubMed via
NCBI E-utilities, ClinicalTrials.gov via API v2, and a 68 page FDA regulatory review that itself
reports systematic searches of PubMed, Embase, Cochrane and ClinicalTrials.gov.

Four records mention the two substances in proximity. None of them is combination evidence, and the
distinction matters enough to set out individually.

| Record | What it actually is | Why it is not combination evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| FAERS report of diffuse hyperpigmentation and gingival darkening (S01) | A single spontaneous adverse event report in a 40 year old woman taking a product containing both BPC-157 and TB500, with the reaction reproducible on rechallenge and resolving on discontinuation | This is the only human record of the pair anywhere in the retrieved literature, and it is a harm report, not a study. It has no control, no denominator and no measured benefit outcome. FDA states that because the product contained two peptides, no relationship to either one can be assessed. |
| FAERS report of injection site redness and swelling (S01) | A spontaneous adverse event report in a 55 year old woman, confounded by concurrent thymosin | The record says "thymosin" without specifying whether this was TB-500 or full length thymosin beta-4. It cannot be attributed to this pair. |
| Lee and Padgett 2021 knee pain chart review (S01) | A retrospective review of 17 people at one private clinic, in which some subjects received BPC-157 together with thymosin beta-4 | The second agent is recorded as thymosin beta-4, the 43 amino acid full length protein, not the TB-500 fragment. It is a different pairing, and it is uncontrolled and retrospective in any case. |
| FDA's general observation on co-formulation (S01) | FDA notes that products frequently combine BPC-157 with other unapproved peptides, TB-500 or thymosin beta-4 among them | This is a statement that the combination is sold, not a statement that it has been studied. FDA raises it as a reason that both benefit claims and harm signals become unattributable. |

The correct summary, and the one the Guide leads with: the combination is widely sold and has never
been studied. The only human record specific to the pair is an adverse event report.

## 3. The second structural trap: TB-500 is not thymosin beta-4

The TB-500 research record identifies this as the highest priority accuracy issue in the entire
evidence base, and it compounds the blend problem rather than sitting beside it.

Three distinct things are routinely conflated in commercial material.

1. **TB-500**, a synthetic seven amino acid acetylated peptide, Ac-LKKTETQ, corresponding to
   residues 17 to 23 of thymosin beta-4. This is what is sold under the name TB-500.
2. **Thymosin beta-4**, the full length endogenous 43 amino acid protein. A different, much larger
   molecule.
3. **RGN-259 and NL005**, investigational pharmaceutical products formulated from full length
   thymosin beta-4.

Nearly every human trial cited in support of TB-500 studied category 2 or category 3, usually
delivered topically to an eye surface or a wound surface. None studied category 1. Admitting that
data as TB-500 evidence would be a category error, and admitting it as blend evidence would be two
category errors stacked.

The search therefore had to hold two separations at once: fragment versus parent protein, and
single ingredient versus mixture. Every source in the registry is tagged for both.

An asymmetry worth flagging to the reviewer: FDA sources were reachable during the BPC-157 research
and unreachable during the TB-500 research. The result is that BPC-157 regulatory statements in this
folder rest on primary FDA documents, while TB-500 regulatory statements do not. The two halves of
this Guide are not equally verified, and the Guide says so rather than smoothing the difference.

## 5. Inclusion criteria

A source was admitted to the registry if all of the following held.

1. It was retrieved at a recorded URL in one of the two component research sessions. Nothing was
   admitted from memory. No PMID, NCT number, DOI, author, year, journal or URL anywhere in this
   folder was reconstructed rather than retrieved.
2. Its subject is identifiable as BPC-157, as TB-500, as thymosin beta-4, or as the supply channel,
   and it is tagged accordingly so that no source can silently migrate from one column to another.
3. For any preclinical finding, the species or model is identifiable, so that the model can travel
   in the same sentence as the finding.
4. For any regulatory statement, a jurisdiction, a date and a URL are all available.

## 6. Exclusion criteria

1. **Vendor, retailer, clinic and compounding pharmacy pages are disqualified as evidence.** No
   number, mechanism or safety statement enters this Guide on the authority of a page that sells
   either component or the blend.
2. Search engine answer summaries used in place of the underlying document. One such summary
   asserted that HHS had reversed BPC-157's classification in February 2026. The article actually
   retrieved does not support that assertion. It is excluded by name in the claim table.
3. Any statistic without a locatable primary source. A widely repeated figure holding that roughly
   30 percent of grey market peptide samples are mislabelled, misdosed or contaminated could not be
   traced to any primary analysis and is excluded by name.
4. Full length thymosin beta-4 trial results presented as TB-500 results. Excluded from every
   TB-500 claim, and recorded separately as adjacent molecule evidence so a reviewer can see what
   was set aside and why.
5. Component findings assembled into a statement about the pair. Excluded structurally: the claim
   table has a combination section, and that section may cite only sources that studied the pair.
6. Any dose, amount, concentration, frequency, timing, cycle, titration, reconstitution detail or
   route given as instruction, including where an admitted source states one.

## 7. Grading approach

Claims are graded individually. Neither component is graded, and the product as a whole is never
graded, because a single grade on a mixture would imply the mixture had been assessed as a unit.

The default grade is D (preclinical) or G (unverified). Grade C (early human) is used only for the
handful of small, uncontrolled or null BPC-157 human studies that were genuinely retrieved, and
each C carries the study's design limits in the same row. No claim in this folder is graded A or B.

Every claim about the combination is graded G, without exception, because no source studied the
combination. The one combination specific human record, the FAERS hyperpigmentation report, is
graded G as a signal requiring follow up rather than as a finding.

## 8. What remains for a human reviewer

Each item should be closed before publication. They are open items, not tidying.

1. **Confirm the composition and ratio of the Xenios product.** Nothing in this research record
   establishes what is in this blend or in what proportion. This is the first open supplier question
   and it blocks several others.
2. **Check the outcome of the FDA Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of July 23 to 24,
   2026.** As of the checks behind this folder the meeting had not occurred. FDA staff had
   recommended against adding BPC-157 free base and BPC-157 acetate to the 503A bulks list, but a
   staff recommendation is a proposal and an advisory committee recommendation is advisory.
3. **Establish the FDA compounding status of TB-500 and thymosin beta-4 directly from fda.gov.**
   Every attempt failed during research. No position may be stated until this is closed.
4. **Retrieve the FDA warning letter to GenoGenix LLC, reference 718739, dated 2026-01-20**, which
   search results indicate concerns thymosin beta-4. Its content is recorded nowhere in this folder
   because the page could not be opened.
5. **Read the WADA Prohibited List directly** and confirm the current section, subsection and
   wording for both components. Both are currently sourced from USADA rather than from WADA, and
   the BPC-157 and TB-500 records disagree on which section applies.
6. **Verify the widely cited September 2023 Category 2 date for BPC-157.** FDA's live page now
   presents BPC-157 under nominated but withdrawn rather than in the dated Category 2 table. The
   September 2023 date could not be verified from any FDA source.
7. **Retrieve the four BPC-157 human studies that exist only as FDA summaries** (Ruenzi 2005,
   Veljaca 2002 and 2003, Lee and Padgett 2021, Lee and Burgess 2025). They are cited in this folder
   as FDA reported, never as independently verified papers, and no PMID or DOI was constructed for
   any of them.
8. **Re-run the combination search independently.** A second reviewer should attempt to find any
   study of BPC-157 and TB-500 administered together and record the date and the null result. The
   central finding of this Guide rests on an absence, and an absence deserves a second pair of eyes.
9. **Search trial registries beyond ClinicalTrials.gov**, including the EU Clinical Trials Register,
   ISRCTN, ANZCTR, the WHO ICTRP and the Chinese registry. Both component searches were limited to
   ClinicalTrials.gov, and one of the two BPC-157 trials found there is run in China.
10. **Reconcile the date stamps.** The underlying research checks carry a date of 2026-07-19. These
    files are stamped 2026-07-21. Regulatory statements in the Guide are dated 2026-07-21 and must
    be re-confirmed as of that date, or re-dated to the date they were actually checked.
