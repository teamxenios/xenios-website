# BPC-157 + TB-500: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing these
compounds could be taken seriously. That standard is written independently of these compounds and
applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state**. Xenios has not confirmed a single item below for
this product. Every checkbox is unchecked, and none is expected to be checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because no evaluation is under way. It is the standard a supplier
would have to meet before one could begin.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below.

1. **Neither component is an approved drug in any jurisdiction that was checked** (checked
   2026-07-21). There is no approved manufacturer, no approved specification, and no regulatory
   authority inspecting a production line against a filed standard.

2. **There is no pharmacopoeial standard to test against.** FDA states there is no United States
   Pharmacopeia or National Formulary drug substance monograph for either BPC-157 form, no dietary
   supplement monograph, and that the substance is not found in the European, Japanese or
   International Pharmacopeias. No equivalent standard was located for TB-500 either. Without a
   monograph there is no official identity, purity or quality specification anyone can be held to.

3. **The composition and ratio of this product are not confirmed.** This is the first open supplier
   question and it blocks most of the others. Until it is answered, there is no defined article to
   test, no specification to write, and no way to state what a certificate of analysis should cover.

4. **No analytical study of any marketed BPC-157 and TB-500 blend was located.** Nobody appears to
   have tested what is in these products. That is an absence of information, not a favourable result.

### What is documented about the market

FDA examined certificates of analysis for BPC-157 and found that most contain purity testing results
only, with no information about impurity limits or testing results demonstrating control of the
impurity profile. For one form, a nominator certificate listed a related substances test but the
impurity profiles were unclear because the impurities were not identified or specified, and another
carried only a purity test with no impurity control at all. FDA found the same pattern in
certificates published by commercial peptide sellers (S01, verified 2026-07-19).

FDA also notes that likely impurities of concern for a bulk drug substance include nitrosamines and
potential mutagenic and teratogenic substances, and that available data do not address these. Solid
phase peptide synthesis introduces residual solvents, coupling reagents, activators, catalysts,
scavengers, and isomeric and deletion peptide impurities (S01).

For TB-500, an analytical study reported that a non natural synthesis impurity is detectable in
equine plasma after administration of a TB-500 containing product, confirming that marketed material
carries synthesis derived contaminants. That study did not characterise purity across the consumer
market (S15, verified 2026-07-19).

More broadly, counterfeit and illegal peptide preparations are a documented problem. A 2015
analytical study developed screening methods covering 25 peptides already seized in Europe or
identified in underground forums (S21, verified 2026-07-19). **The retrieved record does not state
whether TB-500 or thymosin beta-4 was among those 25, and reports no mislabelling rate.** No
percentage may be attached to either component from this source.

A note on figures encountered elsewhere. Research surfaced a widely repeated claim that roughly 30
percent of grey market peptide samples are mislabelled, misdosed or contaminated. It traces only to
secondary and commercial sources, with no locatable primary analysis. It is excluded here and must
not be added.

### Why a blend is harder, not the same

A single ingredient product has one identity question and one content question. A blend has those for
each ingredient, plus a set that only exists because there are two.

- Does the container hold both intended substances, and only those?
- How much of each, and does that match the label?
- Is the mixture uniform, so that the first unit and the last unit from a batch are the same?
- Is each ingredient chemically stable in the presence of the other, over the stated shelf life?
- Do the two produce degradation products together that neither produces alone?

FDA's observation that peptides can be extremely sensitive to formulation, process and environmental
conditions, leading to aggregation and degradation, applies with more force where two peptides share
a container (S01). None of these questions has been answered for this product.

## The checklist

Every item is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any further
conversation. Status as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single item.

### A. Identity, per component

- [ ] **A1. Unambiguous chemical identity of each exact substance.** Not the names BPC-157 and
      TB-500. The specific forms. FDA treats BPC-157 free base and BPC-157 acetate as different
      active ingredients, and states the common name BPC-157 is used commercially for multiple salts,
      derivatives and even different active moieties. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Explicit confirmation that the TB-500 component is the seven amino acid fragment
      Ac-LKKTETQ and not full length thymosin beta-4.** These are different molecules, and vendors use
      the names interchangeably. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A3. Verified registry identifiers for each component**, confirmed against a primary chemical
      registry. Status: NOT CONFIRMED. [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]
- [ ] **A4. Confirmed amino acid sequence for each component in the specific batch**, by an orthogonal
      analytical method, not by supplier assertion. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Explicit exclusion of confusable substances**, documented. For the TB-500 component this
      means excluding full length thymosin beta-4 and formulated products made from it. For the
      BPC-157 component it means excluding the other salts and derivatives sold under the same name.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A6. Salt form, counter-ion and water content stated and measured, per component.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A7. A written specification each component is tested against**, with acceptance criteria set
      before testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Blend composition and uniformity, requirements that exist only because this is a blend

- [ ] **B1. Declared composition: which substances are present, and the content of each.**
      Currently unknown. This is the first open supplier question and it blocks B2 through B6.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. Measured content per component in the finished article**, by a method able to distinguish
      the two, with acceptance criteria and measured results for each. A single combined figure is not
      acceptable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Blend uniformity demonstrated.** Evidence that the two components are evenly distributed,
      so that content per component is the same at the top, middle and bottom of a batch and from one
      unit to the next. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Compatibility of the two components demonstrated**, physically and chemically, under the
      stated storage conditions and over the stated shelf life. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Degradation products of the mixture identified and quantified**, including any that arise
      only when the two are together and not from either alone. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Justification for combining these two substances in one article**, stated by the supplier
      and assessed against the evidence. There is no study of the combination, so a supplier's
      justification cannot rest on one. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Purity and impurities, per component and for the mixture

- [ ] **C1. Quantified purity of each component by a validated, stated method**, with method, column
      and conditions disclosed, not a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. A named impurity profile for each component**, listing what the other material is, not
      only how much of it there is. FDA found market certificates for BPC-157 to be purity only.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Peptide related impurities characterised for each component**, including truncated,
      deletion and modified sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Nitrosamines, potential mutagenic substances and potential teratogenic substances
      addressed**, which FDA states available data do not do for BPC-157.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Residual solvents, coupling reagents, activators, catalysts and scavengers quantified**
      against a recognised limit standard. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C6. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C7. Aggregation assessed for the mixture, not only for each component alone.** Aggregation is
      a driver of the immunogenicity concern FDA raises, and two peptides in one container is an
      untested aggregation environment. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **D1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the article is intended for any route
      requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.** FDA found no
      endotoxin data available for the proposed BPC-157 product forms. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** FDA found no container closure data.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process, documented**,
      including whether either component is affected by the process used.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D6. Particle size and microbial quality data**, which FDA records as unavailable for the
      proposed forms. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Stability

- [ ] **E1. Stability data for the finished blend under stated storage conditions**, over a stated
      duration, using a stability indicating method able to track each component separately.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. In-use stability after any preparation step**, for the mixture. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Photostability and temperature excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping. FDA
      notes significant aggregate formation can occur during storage or under stress conditions.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Evidence that neither component accelerates the degradation of the other** over the shelf
      life. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **F1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site**, for each component and for the
      blending operation, which may be three different parties. Not a brand, not a distributor, not a
      marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. Regulatory inspection history of each site**, and the quality standard each operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis of each component through blending to the
      finished unit**, including every intermediate handler. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Batch and lot traceability for each component and for the finished blend**, so that any
      single unit can be traced back to both component manufacturing records.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents, per component.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **G1. A batch specific certificate of analysis matching the exact lot in hand**, not a
      representative or example certificate for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. Separate identity and assay results for each component on that certificate.** A single
      combined purity figure for a two ingredient article is not acceptable and does not describe the
      product. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. The certificate issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated
      and independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. Test methods named**, with acceptance criteria and measured results, not the word pass.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Independent third party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no commercial
      interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot, covering both components.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G6. Certificate authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** Certificates
      are forged. A document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G7. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **H1. Label content matching the tested content, per component**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material that exceed the
      evidence.** For this product that means no human efficacy or safety claims for either component,
      and no claim of any kind about the combination, since none has been studied.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. No claim, implicit or explicit, that the two ingredients work together, complement each
      other or are synergistic.** No study supports any such claim. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. No use of research use only or not for human consumption framing as an implicit signal of
      human benefit.** The Department of Defense records this as a common mislabelling pattern for
      BPC-157 products. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H5. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date, for both components.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### I. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **I1. A documented adverse event reporting route, with a named responsible party.** FDA notes
      that 503A compounders generally do not report adverse events, which makes this a substantive
      requirement rather than a formality. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

**Xenios has not confirmed any quality documentation item for this product.** No supplier has been
assessed against this checklist, no certificate of analysis has been reviewed, no independent testing
has been commissioned, and no material has been examined.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed.
The purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly
what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide
exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether the compounds do anything. Perfect
documentation here would establish that a container holds what it claims, in the proportions claimed,
free of specified contaminants. It would not create the combination evidence that, as set out in the
Guide, does not exist. A precisely characterised mixture of two compounds that have never been
studied together is still a mixture of two compounds that have never been studied together.
