# KLOW Regulatory Status

Every statement on this page carries a jurisdiction, a date, and a source. Statements without
all three do not belong here and are not made.

**Status date for this entire page: 2026-07-21.**

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Standing principles for this page

1. **The combination has no regulatory status anywhere.** KLOW as a product has never been
   submitted to or evaluated by any regulator. Every evaluation below concerns an individual
   bulk substance assessed on its own, and one regulator treats even a substance's free base and
   its acetate salt as separate substances. None of these evaluations covers this blend, and
   none may be described as though it did.
2. **Not checked is not permitted.** Where a jurisdiction was not searched, this page says so and
   states no position.
3. **Unverified is not confirmed.** Where a source could not be retrieved, the statement resting
   on it is marked and is not upgraded by repetition.
4. **No outcome is asserted for a proceeding that has not concluded.**
5. **Regulatory status expires.** Any publication carrying a regulatory date more than 90 days
   old returns to the editorial gate. The pending advisory committee review makes this page
   specifically time-sensitive.

## Integrity note, read before using this page

The four supplied research records did not reach regulator sources equally well.

The BPC-157 record retrieved the agency's briefing document, its bulk substances list page, its
advisory committee meeting page, and two enforcement letters **directly**. Statements in
section US-B below are correspondingly strong.

The GHK-Cu and KPV records did **not** reach agency pages. Direct fetches returned HTTP 404 and
HTTP 403 during that research, which the records describe as consistent with automated-access
blocking rather than genuine absence. Every statement in sections US-G and US-K therefore rests
on trade reporting, professional-society reporting, or an advocacy submission rather than on the
regulator. Each is labelled that way in the sentence that makes it.

The anti-doping Prohibited List was not retrieved in any supplied record.

A reviewer must close these gaps before publication rather than accepting a uniform-looking
page built on uneven foundations.

## The product

### KLOW-00. The combination

**Statement.** As at 2026-07-21, KLOW as a combination product has no regulatory status in any
jurisdiction. No regulator has evaluated it, cleared it, approved it, prohibited it by name, or
issued any determination about it as a product.

**Jurisdiction.** All. **Date.** 2026-07-21. **Source.** No source, and that is the finding.
Regulatory evaluations in the retrieved record assess single bulk drug substances one at a
time.

**Note.** The composition of KLOW is also unconfirmed, so even the question of which substance
evaluations might be relevant cannot be settled. See QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md.

## United States, BPC-157, a reported component

### US-B-01. Approval status

**Statement.** Neither BPC-157 free base nor BPC-157 acetate is a component of an FDA-approved
drug. The agency states that no approved product containing these substances exists in any
country at this time, and that neither is found in the European, Japanese, or International
pharmacopoeias.

**Jurisdiction.** United States, with the international statement made within the same US
document. **Date checked.** 2026-07-21. **Source.** FDA Briefing Document, Pharmacy Compounding
Advisory Committee, July 23-24 2026 (S01). https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download
**Status.** VERIFIED, retrieved directly.

### US-B-02. Current listing on the bulk substances page

**Statement.** On the agency's page titled "Certain Bulk Drug Substances for Use in Compounding
that May Present Significant Safety Risks", content current as of 04/22/2026, BPC-157 appears in
the table of substances nominated but withdrawn, described as substances previously in category
2 of the interim policies that were withdrawn by the nominators. It does not currently appear in
the main category 2 table. The listed concern states that compounded drugs containing the
substance may pose a risk for immunogenicity for certain routes of administration, may have
complexities regarding peptide-related impurities and characterisation of the active ingredient,
and that the agency has identified no or only limited safety-related information for the
proposed routes, so it lacks sufficient information to know whether the drug would cause harm.

**Jurisdiction.** United States. **Date checked.** 2026-07-21. **Source.** S02.
**Status.** VERIFIED, retrieved directly.

**Caution.** Numerous secondary sources state that this substance holds category 2 status as of
a September 2023 date. That specific framing could not be verified from an agency source, and
the agency's own page now presents the substance differently. Do not repeat the category and
date as current fact.

### US-B-03. Review pending, with no outcome

**Statement.** The agency is evaluating BPC-157 free base and BPC-157 acetate on its own
initiative following withdrawal of the nominations. Its review memo is dated 5/11/2026 and was
placed before the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of July 23-24, 2026 (meeting
page content current as of 07/17/2026). The staff recommendation stated that on balance the
physicochemical characterisation, information on historical use, lack of evidence of
effectiveness, and safety information identified for both substances weigh against them being
added to the 503A bulks list.

**As at 2026-07-21 that meeting has not taken place. No outcome exists and none is asserted
here.** A committee recommendation, when made, is advisory and is not a final agency
determination.

**Jurisdiction.** United States. **Date checked.** 2026-07-21. **Sources.** S01, S03.
**Status.** VERIFIED, retrieved directly.

### US-B-04. Enforcement

**Statement.** A warning letter dated 04/01/2020 to a named compounding firm cited it for
compounding drug products using BPC 157, stating such products are not eligible for the
exemptions provided by section 503A(a) because they are not the subject of an applicable USP or
NF monograph, are not a component of an FDA-approved human drug, and do not appear on the 503A
bulks list. An untitled letter dated 09/14/2023 to a named specialty pharmacy cited it on the
same three grounds in respect of BPC-157 acetate.

**Jurisdiction.** United States. **Dates.** 04/01/2020 and 09/14/2023. **Date checked.**
2026-07-21. **Sources.** S04, S05. **Status.** VERIFIED, both retrieved directly.

**Note.** The research record states that a systematic search of all enforcement letters naming
this substance could not be completed, because the search interface returned errors to
automated retrieval. Two letters were confirmed. That is a floor, not a total.

### US-B-05. Department of Defense

**Statement.** A Department of Defense Operation Supplement Safety article dated April 29, 2025
states that BPC-157 is an unapproved drug that cannot be legally prescribed or sold over the
counter, is not a lawful dietary ingredient, appears on the Department's prohibited dietary
supplement ingredients list, and that there is little to no reliable scientific evidence to
support its safety or effectiveness in humans.

**Jurisdiction.** United States, Department of Defense. **Date of article.** 2025-04-29.
**Date checked.** 2026-07-21. **Source.** S07. **Status.** VERIFIED.

### US-B-06. New Zealand, quoted within the US document

**Statement.** The agency's briefing document quotes the New Zealand medicines regulator stating
that there are currently no approved products or pending applications for medicines containing
BPC-157 in New Zealand. This is a second-hand citation within a US document; the New Zealand
source was not retrieved directly.

**Jurisdiction.** New Zealand, reported within a United States document. **Date checked.**
2026-07-21. **Source.** S01. **Status.** VERIFIED as a quotation, the underlying source NOT
retrieved.

## United States, GHK-Cu, a reported component

### US-G-01. Approval status

**Statement.** No approved drug product containing GHK-Cu was identified for any medical
indication by any route in the retrieved sources. This is a negative search result from the
absence of any approval record, not a retrieved statement of non-approval from the regulator.

**Jurisdiction.** United States. **Date checked.** 2026-07-21. **Source.** S23, a tertiary
background entry, plus the absence of any approval record in the retrieved material.
**Status.** UNVERIFIED as a regulator statement. Report as a negative finding only.

### US-G-02. Compounding position

**Statement.** The compounding position for this substance could not be confirmed. Multiple
secondary sources report that the injectable route has been treated differently from the
non-injectable route, on grounds of immunogenicity from aggregation and peptide-related
impurities, and that its category status changed during 2023 to 2026. Direct fetches of the
relevant agency pages all returned HTTP 404 to automated retrieval during that research.

**Jurisdiction.** United States. **Date checked.** 2026-07-21. **Source.** S25, retrieval
failed. **Status.** UNVERIFIED. The route-based distinction is directionally consistent across
independent secondary sources, but the specific category assignment and its date are not
confirmed and must be checked by a human before publication.

### US-G-03. Cosmetic ingredient review

**Statement.** A Cosmetic Ingredient Review expert panel safety assessment covering tripeptide-1
and its metal salts is reported to exist, published in 2018, with a conclusion of safety in
cosmetics under the practices of use described in that assessment. Primary verification failed:
the assessment PDF returned unparseable content, the journal page returned HTTP 403, and a
bibliographic query returned zero results.

**Jurisdiction.** United States, industry expert panel, not a government regulator.
**Date checked.** 2026-07-21. **Source.** S24. **Status.** UNVERIFIED.

**Essential caveat.** A cosmetic ingredient safety assessment addresses a cosmetic product used
as described in that assessment. It does not transfer to any other product form, and the
research record identifies this exact substitution as the most common way a reader is misled
about this compound.

## Anti-doping

### AD-01. What can be said

**Statement.** A national anti-doping organisation states that BPC-157 is prohibited under the
S0 unapproved substances category of the Prohibited List. The page retrieved did not itself
state the list year or whether the prohibition applies in competition only or at all times.

**Jurisdiction.** Sport, United States national anti-doping organisation. **Date checked.**
2026-07-21. **Source.** S06. **Status.** VERIFIED as a secondary statement. The Prohibited List
itself was NOT retrieved in any supplied research record.

Separately, a Department of Defense article dated April 29, 2025 states that BPC-157 appears on
the World Anti-Doping Agency Prohibited List under non-approved substances (S07).

### AD-02. What cannot be said

**No anti-doping status is stated here for KLOW, for GHK-Cu, for KPV, or for TB-500, in either
direction.** The Prohibited List was not retrieved in any supplied research record. The KPV
research record notes that whether that substance falls under the non-approved substances
category is a logical inference from the category definition plus its unapproved status, and
not a retrieved statement about that substance. An inference is not a regulatory fact and is not
published as one. Claims found on vendor pages that a given substance is not prohibited were
excluded as unacceptable sources.

**Any athlete subject to testing must consult their own anti-doping authority directly. Nothing
on this page is a substitute for that.**
