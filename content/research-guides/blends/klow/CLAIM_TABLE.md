# KLOW Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Grades apply to individual claims, never to the product as a
whole. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## The rule that governs every row below

**Studying the parts is not studying the mixture.** A blend is its own pharmacological object.
Putting several compounds into one preparation creates new questions about how they interact,
how they affect each other's stability and disposition, and what they do together that none of
them does alone. None of those questions is answered by evidence gathered on the compounds
separately. So unless a study administered this actual combination, there is no combination
evidence. That is not a statement that the combination evidence is weak or preliminary. It is
a statement that it does not exist.

Every source in this folder was searched for a study of the KLOW combination. None was found.
Section 1 below records that result. No row anywhere in this table may be combined with
another to manufacture a statement about the blend, and no grade in this table is a grade on
the product.

## The second rule, specific to this product

**The composition is not confirmed.** A legacy internal catalog entry reports an ingredient
list and stated strengths (S00). No supplier specification, certificate of analysis, or
manufacturing record supports it. This table therefore contains no claim about what KLOW
contains, at what strength, or in what proportion. Component sections below are labelled
"reported component" throughout, and every one of them carries that qualifier.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical (animal or cultured cells) |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

No claim about the KLOW combination is graded above G, because no study of the combination
exists. Where an individual component has genuine human trial evidence, that evidence is
graded on its own merits and is labelled as being about that component alone.
