# CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin Blend: Source Plan

This document records how the evidence base for this blend Guide was assembled, what was
deliberately excluded, and what a human reviewer still has to do before anything in this folder
reaches a member.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## 1. What a blend Guide has to establish that a single-compound Guide does not

A blend is not the sum of its parts. Building this folder required answering four questions in a
fixed order, and the order matters because answering them out of sequence is how component
findings get quietly reassembled into combination support.

1. **Does any study of the combination exist?** This is asked first, before any component evidence
   is read for content, so that the answer cannot be influenced by how impressive the component
   material looks.
2. **What is actually in the Xenios product?** Composition and ratio, confirmed by document rather
   than assumed from the product name.
3. **What does the evidence for each component separately show, and what does it not show?**
4. **What is unknown specifically because the two are combined?** Interaction, additivity,
   sequencing, and shared-pathway effects are their own category of unknown, not a footnote to the
   component sections.

Question 1 was answered first, and the answer was none. Everything downstream of that is written
so that no reader, and no future editor, can mistake component material for combination material.

## 2. The combination search

The two web-verified component research records supplied for this Guide were searched in full for
any study, of any design, in any species, that administered CJC-1295 in either form together with
ipamorelin.

| What was searched | Where | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The full CJC-1295 research record: human evidence, preclinical evidence, safety signals, quality and identity findings, contradictions, unknowns, gaps, and the source list | Component record for CJC-1295, verified 2026-07-19 | No study of the combination. Ipamorelin appears once, in the identity section, and only to distinguish the GHRP and ghrelin-mimetic class from the GHRH-analogue class as a different receptor family. |
| The full ipamorelin research record: same fields | Component record for ipamorelin, verified 2026-07-19 | No study of the combination. The record states directly, under quality and identity, that a product labelled as CJC-1295 with ipamorelin contains two distinct pharmacologically active compounds and that no trial of such a blend was located. |
| The combined human evidence sets of both records, checked for any trial in which both compounds were present | Both records | Zero. Four human records exist for CJC-1295 (three studies plus one terminated registration) and four for ipamorelin (two published studies, one registered trial with no posted results, one analytical study). None involved both compounds. |
| The combined preclinical sets of both records, checked for any animal or in vitro work combining a GHRH analogue with a GHS-R1a agonist | Both records | Zero. Every preclinical entry in both records tested a single compound, with the exception of analytical and doping-detection papers that assayed several secretagogues in parallel rather than administering them together. |
| The unknowns lists of both records | Both records | The ipamorelin record names the combination explicitly as an unknown: interaction with other growth hormone axis compounds, notably the widely sold CJC-1295 blend, has no located trial evidence in combination. |

**Result: no evidence of any grade exists for this combination.** Not weak evidence, not indirect
evidence, not preliminary evidence. None. That finding is the spine of this Guide and it is stated
in GUIDE_DRAFT.md, CLAIM_TABLE.md, and EDITORIAL_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md.

An honest empty combination evidence table is the correct output here, and it was not padded with
component findings, mechanistic plausibility, or class-level reasoning dressed up as support.

## 4. Inclusion criteria

A source was admitted to the registry only if all of the following held.

1. It was retrieved at a recorded URL during the component research sessions of 2026-07-19.
   Nothing was admitted from memory. No PMID, NCT number, DOI, author, year, journal, or URL was
   reconstructed.
2. It addresses CJC-1295 in a named form, or ipamorelin, or is explicitly labelled as addressing
   something adjacent (the peptide supply channel, the regulatory framework, the anti-doping
   position) and is used only for that adjacent purpose.
3. Its species or model is identifiable, so that no preclinical finding can be written without the
   model in the same sentence.
4. For CJC-1295 specifically, the source makes clear which molecule it studied: the albumin-binding
   DAC form, or the short-acting modified GRF(1-29) form. A source that does not resolve this is
   admitted only with that ambiguity recorded.
5. For regulatory statements, a jurisdiction, a date checked, and a URL are all available.
6. Its verified flag from the component research record is carried through unchanged. A source
   marked unverified in the research stays unverified here and cannot carry a claim on its own.

## 5. Exclusion criteria

A source or a claim was excluded if any of the following held.

1. **Vendor, retailer, telehealth clinic, and compounding-pharmacy pages are disqualified as
   evidence.** They may be referenced only to document where an unusable claim originated, and such
   an entry is marked non-evidence. This matters unusually much for this blend, because the blend
   is largely a commercial construct: it is sold as a pair far more than it has been studied as a
   pair, and in this case it has not been studied as a pair at all.
2. Any component finding presented, framed, or positioned in a way that could be read as support
   for the combination. This is the exclusion rule specific to a blend Guide, and it applies to
   layout and adjacency as well as to wording.
3. Human pharmacokinetic and safety findings for CJC-1295 with DAC used to describe modified
   GRF(1-29), or the reverse. The component research identifies this as a misattribution of data
   across two pharmacologically dissimilar molecules, and treats it as a safety issue rather than a
   labelling one. No such transfer appears anywhere in this folder.
4. Any inference of the form "both compounds raise growth hormone, therefore together they raise it
   more" or "therefore the effects add". Additivity is an empirical question that nobody has
   answered for this pair. It is recorded as an unknown, never as a benefit.
5. Search-engine answer summaries used in place of the underlying document, and any regulatory
   outcome asserted in the past tense that could not be traced to a primary record.
6. Any specific amino acid substitution positions, half-life figures, or pharmacokinetic numbers
   for modified GRF(1-29). These appeared only in vendor material during component research and are
   excluded by name.
7. Any dose, amount, concentration, frequency, timing, cycle, ratio-as-instruction, route,
   reconstitution, or preparation detail appearing in an admitted source. None is reproduced.

## 6. Grading approach

Claims are graded individually. Neither component is graded as a whole, and the product is never
graded as a whole.

- The default grade is D (preclinical) or G (unverified).
- A grade of C or better requires a specific retrieved human study of the exact compound described.
  A small number of component claims meet that bar, all of them biomarker or pharmacology findings
  rather than outcome findings, and each carries a note saying so.
- **No claim about the combination is graded above G, and no combination claim is member-facing.**
  There is no study to support a higher grade, and a grade of D would falsely imply that preclinical
  combination work exists.
- Claims that would mislead a member regardless of hedging are marked PROHIBITED and listed
  separately in CLAIM_TABLE.md.

## 7. What remains for a human reviewer

These are open items, not tidying. Each should be closed before publication.

1. **Confirm the composition of the Xenios product.** The exact composition and the ingredient
   ratio are NOT CONFIRMED. This is the first open supplier question and it is listed first in
   QUALITY_AND_DOCUMENTATION.md. No ratio is stated or implied anywhere in this folder, and none
   may be added until a supplier document establishes it.
2. **Establish which CJC-1295 molecule the product contains**, the albumin-binding DAC form or
   modified GRF(1-29). The two have different pharmacology and very different evidence bases, and
   the product name does not distinguish them. Until this is documented, the Guide can describe
   only both possibilities.
3. **Open the FDA pages directly in a browser.** fda.gov was completely unreachable during both
   component research sessions. Every FDA statement about ipamorelin in this folder rests on
   secondary reporting, and FDA's actual position on the five CJC-1295 forms considered in December
   2024 is unverified.
4. **Retrieve the FDA advisory committee briefing document on ipamorelin.** The component research
   identifies it as the single most important primary source on that compound's safety assessment,
   and it is missing.
5. **Confirm the outcome of the December 4, 2024 advisory committee consideration of the five
   CJC-1295 forms.** The consideration is verified from the Federal Register. The outcome is not.
6. **Read the current WADA Prohibited List directly.** Both components are described as prohibited
   in sport on the authority of peer-reviewed anti-doping papers rather than the List document.
   Confirm the current section and wording for both.
7. **Verify the reported Phase II trial subject death account for CJC-1295** against a primary
   record. It appears only in a tertiary encyclopedia source. It must not be published as fact and
   must not be published as debunked.
8. **Run an independent trial-registry and literature search for the combination.** This folder's
   combination finding rests on a thorough search of two component research records, not on a fresh
   registry query naming both compounds together. A human should run that query directly so the
   central finding of this Guide rests on a primary search. The expected result is zero, and a zero
   result should be recorded with its date and query string.
9. **Retrieve the component abstracts recorded at title level only.** Four ipamorelin preclinical
   records and several 2026 review articles for CJC-1295 have verified identifiers but unread
   content.
10. **Search the additional trial registries** listed at the end of section 3.
11. **Resolve the date stamp.** Files in this folder carry a review date of 2026-07-21 while the
    underlying component research carries retrieval timestamps of 2026-07-19. Both dates appear
    honestly throughout. Reconcile them before publication so no dated statement is ambiguous.
