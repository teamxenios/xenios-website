# CJC-1295 and ipamorelin blend: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing these
compounds could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of these two
compounds and applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state**. Xenios has not confirmed a single item on this list.
Every checkbox below is unchecked, and none is expected to be checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

---

## The first open supplier question

**What is in this product, and in what proportion?**

The exact composition and the ingredient ratio of the Xenios product are **NOT CONFIRMED**. No supplier
documentation has been reviewed, no assay has been seen, and no independent testing has been
commissioned. No proportion is stated or implied anywhere in this Guide set, and none may be added
without a document behind it.

This is question one, ahead of purity, ahead of sterility, ahead of everything. A product whose
composition is undocumented cannot be assessed on any other axis, because every other test needs to know
what it is testing for and against what specification.

**The second open question sits directly underneath it: which CJC-1295 molecule is in it?**

The name "CJC-1295" is used commercially for two chemically different compounds. One carries an added
group that binds albumin in the blood and greatly extends how long it persists, and it is the version
essentially all published human research studied. The other lacks that group, is more accurately called
modified GRF(1-29), and a 2026 peer-reviewed review states it is essentially uncharacterised in the
peer-reviewed human literature with no controlled clinical studies evaluating it in humans.

A purchaser cannot determine which molecule they have from the product name alone. Regulatory filings
treat the two, and their different salt forms, as separate bulk substances: five distinct CJC-1295 forms
were named in a single Federal Register notice. The single commercial name distinguishes none of them.
Until a document establishes which molecule is present, the product's active ingredient is not
identified.

## What a blend requires that a single-ingredient product does not

A blend is not one product with two names. It is two active compounds sharing a container, and that adds
requirements that do not exist for a single ingredient.

**Per-component identity.** Each compound must be independently confirmed to be what it is claimed to be.
Confirming that "peptides are present" is not confirmation.

**Per-component assay.** Each compound must be independently quantified. A total peptide content figure
tells you nothing about the split between the two, and the split is the product.

**Blend uniformity.** The mixture must be demonstrably consistent throughout a container and from
container to container. Peptides of different molecular sizes and different physical behaviours can
separate. A blend that is correct on average and uneven in practice is not a controlled product, and the
person receiving it has no way to detect the problem.

**Content per component against a stated specification**, with acceptance criteria set before testing
rather than after, so that "in specification" means something.

**Cross-contamination control.** A facility producing multiple peptides must document that neither
component carries traces of unrelated compounds from other production runs.

None of these has been established for this product.

## The starting position

Three facts frame everything below.

1. **Neither ingredient is an approved drug product in any jurisdiction checked** (checked 2026-07-19).
   There is therefore no approved manufacturer, no approved specification, and no regulatory authority
   inspecting a production line against a filed standard for either compound, let alone for a mixture of
   the two.

2. **No independent analysis of any marketed product containing both compounds was located**, and no
   published purity or identity survey of consumer products labelled as either ingredient was located.
   Nobody appears to have systematically checked what is in these vials. That is an absence of
   information, not a favourable result.

3. **This compound is documented as circulating in unlabelled form.** CJC-1295 was identified by mass
   spectrometry in an unlabelled illicit pharmaceutical preparation of unknown origin submitted by police
   and customs authorities (PMID 21204297, 2010). The contents were unknown until analysed. That study is
   16 years old, so read it as documentation that the problem exists rather than as a measurement of the
   current market.

What is documented about the channel generally. A 2026 peer-reviewed review states that real-world use of
these compounds is complicated by quality-control uncertainty and compositional variability characteristic
of unregulated peptide supply chains, and notes this is especially acute for agents lacking any human
validation, which includes the short-acting CJC-1295 version. In an analytical quality-control study of
commercially obtained synthetic peptides, one of five products examined was a totally different peptide
than labelled, and two thirds of the others had insufficient quality by the study's stated purity and
impurity criteria (PMID 18342612). **That study tested a different peptide and did not test either
ingredient of this blend.** Counterfeit and illegal injectable peptide preparations are a recognised
enforcement problem in Europe, sufficient that controlling agencies developed a dedicated screening method
covering 25 peptides already detected in seized products (PMID 26003685); the retrieved abstract did not
confirm whether either ingredient was among them.

A note on numbers encountered elsewhere. Vendor material for these compounds presents specific
pharmacokinetic figures for the short-acting CJC-1295 version while citing human trials conducted on the
longer-acting one. Those figures were not confirmed by any peer-reviewed or regulatory source and are
excluded here. They must not be added.

## The checklist

Every item is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before any further
conversation. Status as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single item.

- [ ] **A1. Documented composition of the finished product**, naming every active ingredient present.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. Documented ratio or content per component**, stated in the specification and measured on the
      batch. This is the first open supplier question and nothing else can be assessed without it.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A3. Unambiguous identification of which CJC-1295 molecule is present**, the albumin-binding form
      or modified GRF(1-29), including the specific salt form, since regulatory filings treat five
      CJC-1295 forms as separate bulk substances. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A4. Unambiguous chemical identity of the ipamorelin component**, including salt form.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Verified registry identifiers for both components**, confirmed against a primary chemical
      registry. Figures circulating in vendor material were not confirmed during research and are not
      reproduced anywhere in this Guide set. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
      [UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]
- [ ] **A6. Confirmed amino acid sequence for each component in the specific batch**, by an orthogonal
      analytical method rather than by supplier assertion. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A7. Explicit exclusion of confusable compounds.** Documented evidence that the material is not
      sermorelin, tesamorelin, CJC-1293, GHRP-2, GHRP-6, hexarelin, anamorelin, macimorelin, or
      ibutamoren, and that neither component has been substituted for the other or for a cheaper

- [ ] **A8. Counter-ion and water content stated and measured for each component.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A9. A written specification the batch is tested against**, with per-component acceptance criteria
      set before testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Blend-specific quality

- [ ] **B1. Per-component identity confirmation on the finished blend**, not on the individual raw
      materials before mixing. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. Per-component quantitative assay on the finished blend**, so the actual split between the two
      compounds is measured rather than assumed from what went in.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Blend uniformity demonstrated within a container**, showing the composition does not vary
      through the material. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Blend uniformity demonstrated between containers across the batch**, with sampling from
      beginning, middle, and end of the run. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Compatibility and co-stability of the two components demonstrated**, showing that neither
      degrades the other and that the mixture is stable as a mixture rather than only as separate
      substances. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Cross-contamination control documented** for a facility handling multiple peptides, with
      cleaning validation and carryover limits. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B7. A validated analytical method capable of resolving both components in the presence of each
      other**, since a method developed for one compound alone may not separate it from the other.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **C1. Quantified purity for each component by a validated, stated method**, with the method,
      column, and conditions disclosed rather than a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is, not only how much of it there
      is. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Peptide-related impurities characterised for each component**, including truncated, deletion,
      and modified sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit standard.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C6. Aggregation assessed for each component and for the mixture.** Peptide clumping is a
      recognised contributor to immune reactions against a peptide, and one regulator's stated concern
      about this compound class was immunogenicity risk. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **D1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any route
      requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process, documented.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Stability

- [ ] **E1. Stability data for the blend under stated storage conditions**, over a stated duration, using
      a stability-indicating method that tracks both components. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. In-use stability after any preparation step**, for the mixture rather than for either
      component alone. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Degradation products identified and quantified for each component**, not just a decline in
      assay value. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Evidence that the two components degrade independently**, or characterisation of any
      interaction between their degradation pathways. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **F1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site.** Not a brand, not a distributor,
      not a marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. Regulatory inspection history of that site**, and the quality standard it operates to.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis of each component through to the finished unit**,
      including every intermediate handler and the blending step itself.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. Batch and lot traceability for each component separately and for the finished blend**, so any
      single unit can be traced back to both manufacturing records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including temperature
      records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents** for both components.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **G1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis matching the exact lot in hand**, covering the
      finished blend and not only the individual raw materials. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. The certificate issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation stated and
      independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. Test methods named on the certificate**, with acceptance criteria and measured results per
      component, not "pass". Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no commercial
      interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot, resolving both components.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Certificate authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.** Certificates are
      forged. A document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G6. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **H1. Label content matching the tested content for each component**, within a stated tolerance.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. The label identifying which CJC-1295 molecule is present**, rather than the ambiguous

- [ ] **H3. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material** that exceed the

- [ ] **H4. No claim of synergy, additive effect, or complementary action** between the two components,
      none of which has been tested. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H5. No use of "research use only" framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That framing
      is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H6. Accurate regulatory status stated for each component, with jurisdiction and date.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### I. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **I1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party.

- [ ] **I3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean passed. The
purpose of publishing the checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can see exactly what has
and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact that a Guide exists.

Quality documentation is also a separate question from whether anything works. Perfect documentation here
would establish what a vial contains and in what proportion. It would not create the combination evidence
that, as set out in the Guide, does not exist. A precisely characterised mixture of two compounds that
have never been studied together is still a mixture of two compounds that have never been studied
together.
