## 0. The blend rule: the highest-risk failure in this packet

**Findings about an ingredient are not findings about the mixture it sits in.** A combination is its own
pharmacological entity, with its own behaviour in the body, its own interaction profile, and its own
unmeasured questions. Research conducted on one component describes that component and cannot be
extended to the pair. Where no study administered THIS combination, the combination has no evidence at
all. Not slight evidence. Not indirect evidence. None.

This section is checked first, before anything else, and a failure here is not correctable by adding a
qualifier elsewhere.

- [ ] 0.1 The combination evidence section of `GUIDE_DRAFT.md` states plainly that no study of the two
      compounds given together was located, in humans or in any animal model.
- [ ] 0.2 The combination evidence table is empty and is explicitly identified as the finding, not as an
      incomplete section.
- [ ] 0.3 Section 1 of `CLAIM_TABLE.md` contains no effect claims, and no combination claim anywhere is
      graded above G.
- [ ] 0.4 **No component finding appears in any position that implies combination support.** Checked
      structurally, not only by wording: no component citation footnotes a sentence about the blend, no
      component study appears under a blend heading, and no summary bullet names both compounds and then

- [ ] 0.5 Every component finding in every file is labelled as being about one ingredient studied alone.
- [ ] 0.6 The words "synergy", "synergistic", "additive", "complementary", "work together", and
      "potentiate" do not appear as descriptions of what this product does, confirmed by search across

- [ ] 0.7 The additive rationale appears only as a named, untested hypothesis, and is immediately

- [ ] 0.8 The four possible outcomes of combining two agents on one axis (addition, redundancy,

## Standing rules for this gate

1. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from two
   component research records. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human
   sign-off is complete.
2. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
3. **No single person may sign more than one reviewer role.** Restated here because it is the rule most
   likely to be relaxed under time pressure, and relaxing it removes the independence the gate exists to
   provide.
4. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a reviewer or
   a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed and logged as a gap.
   Omitting is always better than guessing.
5. **Concluding that combination evidence is absent is a successful outcome**, not a failure of the
   research. An honest empty table is the correct result here and must not be padded with component
   findings, mechanistic reasoning, or class-level plausibility.
6. **The composition of the product remains NOT CONFIRMED until a document says otherwise.** No ratio may
   be inferred from a product name, a vendor page, or common practice.
7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any Guide published with a regulatory date more than 90 days old
   returns to this gate for re-verification. The unresolved CJC-1295 status makes this folder
   specifically time-sensitive.
8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, composition, or
   regulatory statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed.
9. **If a combination study is ever located, the whole folder returns to this gate.** It would change the
   central finding, and every file is written around that finding.
