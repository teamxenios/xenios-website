# CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin Blend: Regulatory Status

**Status reviewed for this Guide on 2026-07-21. The underlying checks were performed on 2026-07-19
and each entry carries its own date checked. This page must be re-verified before publication and
re-checked on any subsequent update.** Regulatory positions change, and several items on this page
are unresolved.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Standing principles for this page

1. Every statement carries a jurisdiction, a date checked, and a source URL. A statement missing any
   of the three does not belong here.
2. Approval, authorisation, or registration in one jurisdiction is not approval in another. Nothing
   recorded here for the United States says anything about any other country.
3. Where a status could not be confirmed by reading the primary document, that is stated explicitly
   rather than filled in by inference.
4. An advisory committee recommendation is advisory. It is not a final agency determination.
5. Each component is treated separately. The two compounds are on different procedural tracks, and a
   status for one is not a status for the other.
6. **There is no regulatory status for the combination as such.** No product containing both
   compounds is approved anywhere, which follows from neither ingredient being approved anywhere, and
   no regulator has evaluated the pair as a pair in anything retrieved.

## Integrity note, read before using this page

The United States agency's own website was unreachable throughout both component research sessions.
Every attempted URL returned HTTP 404, including the bulk drug substances pages, the category pages,
the advisory committee meeting pages, the briefing documents, and the meeting minutes. The public
comment docket returned HTTP 403. The anti-doping Prohibited List could not be retrieved.

The consequence differs by component and the difference matters.

- **CJC-1295 regulatory statements below rest on primary government notices** retrieved from
  govinfo.gov, which publishes the Federal Register. That is a genuine primary record. What those
  notices do not contain is the agency's recommendation or the committee's outcome, so those remain
  unknown.
- **Every ipamorelin regulatory statement below rests on secondary accounts:** investigative
  journalism, a compounding trade association report, and a legal-practice analysis. These are
  careful sources and they are not the regulator.

A human reviewer must open the agency pages directly and confirm each statement before publication.

## United States: CJC-1295

### US-C-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** No approved drug product containing CJC-1295 in any form was identified. The only
registered clinical trial located, a Phase 2 study, was terminated with no results posted. A 2026
peer-reviewed review states that neither the albumin-binding form nor the form without that group
holds approval for performance or body-composition indications.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Sources:**
https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies/NCT00267527
https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/endocrinology/articles/10.3389/fendo.2026.1822475/full
**Verification:** Primary registry record retrieved and read, plus a peer-reviewed review retrieved
and read.

### US-C-02. Consideration for the compounding bulk substances list, December 2024

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, via the Federal Register.
**Statement:** A Federal Register notice, docket FDA-2024-N-4777, announced a Pharmacy Compounding
Advisory Committee meeting on December 4, 2024, at which five CJC-1295 forms were being considered for
inclusion on the Section 503A Bulk Drug Substances List: CJC-1295 free base, CJC-1295 acetate,
CJC-1295 DAC free base, CJC-1295 DAC acetate, and CJC-1295 DAC trifluoroacetate. The notice states
only that they were being considered. It does not state the agency's recommendation.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2024-10-25/html/2024-24828.htm
**Verification:** Primary government notice, retrieved and read.
**Note for the reader:** the five separately named forms are themselves evidence of the identity
problem described in the Guide. Regulatory filings treat the albumin-binding and non-albumin-binding
forms, and their different salt forms, as distinct substances. The single commercial name
"CJC-1295" does not distinguish among them.

### US-C-03. The outcome of that consideration is UNKNOWN

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** What the agency proposed for each of the five forms, how the committee voted, and the
current list status are all unverified. No outcome is stated on this page.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED. Every agency URL attempted returned HTTP 404, including the briefing
document, the category list page, the meeting page, and the meeting transcript. The comment docket
returned HTTP 403.
**Handling rule:** state no outcome and no current status. When the record becomes available, read it
directly rather than a summary of it, and remember that a committee recommendation is advisory rather
than final.

### US-C-04. Reported category listing and its stated rationale

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, reported secondhand.
**Statement:** A 2026 peer-reviewed narrative review states that the agency listed CJC-1295 as
prohibited in the significant-safety-risk category of the bulk drug substances list, citing reports of
vasodilatory reactions and tachycardia.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/endocrinology/articles/10.3389/fendo.2026.1822475/full
**Verification:** UNVERIFIED at primary source. The review is peer-reviewed and was retrieved and
read, but the underlying agency document could not be reached, so neither the listing nor the stated
safety rationale is independently confirmed here, and its currency is unknown.
**Handling rule:** not member-facing until a human confirms it at the agency. See CLAIM_TABLE.md
BLEND-S-10.

### US-C-05. The category framework itself changed in January 2025

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, via the Federal Register.
**Statement:** A final guidance announced January 7, 2025, docket FDA-2015-D-3517, ends the
categorisation of bulk drug substances into Categories 1, 2, and 3 for substances nominated on or
after that guidance's publication date. The notice does not mention CJC-1295 or peptides by name, so
it does not by itself establish this compound's status.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2025-01-07/html/2024-31546.htm
**Verification:** Primary government notice, retrieved and read.
**Handling rule:** readers encountering category language about CJC-1295 should treat it as possibly
describing a superseded framework. State the limitation with the fact, never the fact alone.

### US-C-06. CJC-1295 is NOT on the July 2026 advisory committee agenda

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, via the Federal Register.
**Statement:** The notice announcing the July 23 to 24, 2026 Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee
meeting, docket FDA-2025-N-6895, published April 16, 2026, lists peptide substances including BPC-157,
KPV, TB-500, MOTS-C, DSIP, Semax and Epitalon. CJC-1295 is not mentioned.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2026-04-16/html/2026-07361.htm
**Verification:** Primary government notice, retrieved and read.
**Why this entry exists:** it directly contradicts vendor pages claiming CJC-1295 is pending July 2026
review. Treat that claim as false or, at minimum, unsupported.

### US-C-07. Clinical trial registration

**Jurisdiction:** United States, ClinicalTrials.gov.
**Statement:** A registry query for CJC-1295 returned exactly one study, NCT00267527, a Phase 2 study
of 120 participants with HIV-associated visceral obesity, sponsored by ConjuChem, terminated with no
results posted and no reason for termination given.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.term=CJC-1295&fields=NCTId,BriefTitle,OverallStatus,StudyType,Phase,Condition,InterventionName,StartDate,CompletionDate,LeadSponsorName&pageSize=50
**Verification:** Primary registry query, retrieved and read.

## United States: ipamorelin

### US-I-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration.
**Statement:** Ipamorelin is not an approved drug. No approval or marketing authorisation was
identified in any jurisdiction during research. It is an investigational compound whose sponsor
development programme was discontinued.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.term=ipamorelin&format=json&pageSize=50
**Verification:** Primary registry query, retrieved and read.

### US-I-02. Placement in the significant-safety-risk category, 2023

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, reported secondhand.
**Statement:** According to investigative reporting of the agency's review, in 2023 the agency placed
ipamorelin in Category 2 of the interim 503A bulk drug substances list, the category for substances
identified as presenting significant safety risks for use in compounding. The reporting states the
agency cited immunogenicity risk for injectable peptides.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.propublica.org/article/peptide-safety-fda-compounding-pharmacies
**Verification:** SECONDARY. Retrieved and read. The agency's own pages were unreachable.

### US-I-03. The advisory committee voted against inclusion, October 29, 2024

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, reported secondhand.
**Statement:** At the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of October 29, 2024, the
committee voted against adding ipamorelin to the 503A bulks list, alongside L-theanine, ibutamoren
mesylate, and kisspeptin-10. The agency had recommended against inclusion. Individual vote counts were
not available in the retrieved source.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://a4pc.org/news/pcac-votes-against-four-nominated-bulk-drug-substances
**Verification:** SECONDARY, from a compounding trade association. Retrieved and read.
**Why this matters for the reader:** ipamorelin has already been through committee review and was not
recommended for compounding eligibility. That places it on a different procedural track from peptides
still awaiting a first review, and it is the context that makes several circulating commercial claims
implausible.

### US-I-04. The stated basis was insufficient data, not demonstrated harm

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, reported secondhand.
**Statement:** Reporting states the agency concluded that insufficient data existed to address known
safety concerns for ipamorelin. The same reporting states that in the studies the agency examined,
subjects experienced adverse events including death, while noting that causation was not definitively
proven.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Sources:**
https://www.propublica.org/article/peptide-safety-fda-compounding-pharmacies
https://a4pc.org/news/pcac-votes-against-four-nominated-bulk-drug-substances
**Verification:** SECONDARY. Retrieved and read. The briefing document that would establish the
agency's detailed assessment could not be retrieved.
**Mandatory context:** insufficient evidence of safety is a distinct finding from evidence of harm and
must be stated as such. The adverse event statement must always carry its context, which is that the
trials in question enrolled post-surgical bowel resection patients, a population with meaningful
baseline mortality and morbidity independent of any study drug. Presenting the statement without that
context is misleading in either direction. See CLAIM_TABLE.md BLEND-S-06.

### US-I-05. Ipamorelin was not among the April 2026 category removals

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, reported secondhand.
**Statement:** On April 15, 2026, the agency removed a group of peptides from Category 2 of the
interim 503A bulks list. According to a legal-practice analysis of that action, ipamorelin was not
among the peptides removed. The same analysis states that the seven peptides set for review at the
July 23 to 24, 2026 committee meeting, under Federal Register docket FDA-2025-N-6895, do not include
ipamorelin.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.boesensnowlaw.com/blog/fda-advances-peptide-compounding-review-category-2-removals-announced-and-pcac-hearing-confirmed/
**Verification:** SECONDARY, a legal-practice analysis citing a specific docket and date. Retrieved
and read. Corroborated on the agenda point by the primary Federal Register notice at US-C-06.
**Contradicted by:** multiple vendor, telehealth clinic, and supplement marketing pages asserting that
ipamorelin was among peptides removed and is now or soon will be available for compounding. Those
sources are commercially interested and several conflate a political announcement of intent with a
completed legal action. See CONTRADICTIONS.md entry X-05.
**Handling rule:** state the position conservatively and date-stamped. It could not be confirmed at
the agency.

### US-I-06. Category removal would not authorise compounding

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Food and Drug Administration, reported secondhand.
**Statement:** The same legal analysis states that substances removed from Category 2 remain unlawful
for compounding until their formal addition to the Bulks List, which requires completion of formal
rulemaking after committee evaluation.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.boesensnowlaw.com/blog/fda-advances-peptide-compounding-review-category-2-removals-announced-and-pcac-hearing-confirmed/
**Verification:** SECONDARY. Retrieved and read.
**Why this is on the page:** the opposite reading circulates widely in commercial material and
materially misleads readers about legality. Any text touching 2026 regulatory news must make this
distinction explicitly.

### US-I-07. Reported political context, which is not a legal change

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Department of Health and Human Services, reported secondhand.
**Statement:** Reporting states that as of April 2026 the agency faced pressure to reverse its 2023
position, with stated interest in making roughly 14 peptides more accessible, the specific peptides
unspecified.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://www.propublica.org/article/peptide-safety-fda-compounding-pharmacies
**Verification:** SECONDARY. Retrieved and read.
**Handling rule:** reported policy pressure does not by itself change the legal status of anything.
Label it as context. Never present it as a status.

### US-I-08. Clinical trial registration

**Jurisdiction:** United States, ClinicalTrials.gov.
**Statement:** Registered studies for ipamorelin include NCT00672074, a Phase 2 study of 117
participants completed December 2009 with no results posted, and NCT01280344, a Phase 2 dose-finding

**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Sources:**

**Verification:** Primary registry records, retrieved and read.

## The combination

### COMB-01. No regulatory evaluation of the pair

**Jurisdiction:** All jurisdictions checked, which is the United States only.
**Statement:** No product containing both CJC-1295 and ipamorelin is approved in any jurisdiction
identified. Nothing retrieved indicates that any regulator has evaluated the two compounds in
combination. Each has been considered separately, on separate procedural tracks, at separate meetings.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19, reviewed 2026-07-21.
**Sources:** the registry queries and Federal Register notices cited above.
**Verification:** This follows from the component records. It is a reasoned statement about the
absence of a retrieved evaluation, not a retrieved statement from a regulator that the combination was
never evaluated. A human reviewer should confirm it if any weight is placed on it.
**Handling rule:** the absence of a regulatory evaluation of the combination is not a permission, an
exemption, or a favourable status. It is an absence.

## Anti-doping

### AD-01. CJC-1295

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency, international.
**Statement:** GHRH analogues including CJC-1295 and CJC-1293 are prohibited in sport at all times,
not in competition only.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4830873/
**Verification:** Confirmed from a peer-reviewed anti-doping analytical paper referencing the
Prohibited List, with the paper's reference being to the 2015 List. The current-year Prohibited List
page is JavaScript-rendered and returned no parseable content, so the exact current section number and
wording are UNVERIFIED.

### AD-02. Ipamorelin

**Jurisdiction:** World Anti-Doping Agency, international.
**Statement:** Ipamorelin is prohibited in sport. A peer-reviewed anti-doping analytical paper that
studied ipamorelin states that growth hormone releasing peptides stimulate secretion of endogenous
growth hormone and are listed on the Prohibited List. That same paper establishes that ipamorelin
metabolites are detectable in human urine by anti-doping laboratories, so the detection capability is
not hypothetical.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25869809/
**Verification:** The prohibited status is corroborated by a peer-reviewed paper. The Prohibited List
document itself could not be retrieved. Multiple attempts returned empty content or HTTP 403. The
exact section number and the verbatim list entry are UNVERIFIED.

### AD-03. Handling rule for both

Publish no section number and no verbatim list wording for either compound, because neither was read
from the List. State that both compounds are described in the peer-reviewed anti-doping literature as
prohibited, and direct any athlete subject to testing to their own anti-doping authority rather than
to an inference made here. A tested athlete faces a real sanction risk with both components, and a
product containing both contains two prohibited substances rather than one.

## Other jurisdictions

No regulatory statement was retrieved for the European Union, the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia,
Japan, or any jurisdiction other than the United States, for either component or for the combination.
No national regulator outside the United States was consulted during component research, and no trial
registry outside ClinicalTrials.gov was searched.

The absence of an entry here means the question was not investigated. It does not mean the compounds
are unregulated, permitted, or approved anywhere. Approval in one jurisdiction is not approval in
another, and the reverse also holds: an unconfirmed status elsewhere may not be inferred from the
United States position recorded above.
