# BPC-157 + TB-500 + GHK-Cu Regulatory Status

**Compiled 2026-07-21 from sources checked 2026-07-19.**

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Standing principles for this page

1. Every statement carries a jurisdiction, a date and a source. A statement missing any of the
   three does not belong here.
2. Regulatory status expires. Nothing on this page may be published without re-verification
   immediately beforehand, and the date must be updated to the actual verification date.
3. An advisory committee recommendation is advice to an agency. It is not a final agency
   determination and must never be reported as one.
4. Not checked never means permitted. Where a jurisdiction was not examined, that is stated as
   not examined.
5. Where a status could not be verified, the gap is published as a gap. No position is adopted
   from commercial sources.
6. Regulatory status is not evidence of effectiveness or safety in either direction. A substance
   being unapproved does not mean it is harmful, and a substance being permitted somewhere does
   not mean it works.

## Integrity note, read before using this page

Two dates are in play. The underlying research was conducted on 2026-07-19 and these files carry
a review date of 2026-07-21. Where this page says checked 2026-07-19, that is the actual
retrieval date and it should not be silently advanced.

Three further cautions specific to this compound set:

## The combination

### CB-01. No regulator anywhere has evaluated this combination

**Jurisdiction:** all. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** not evaluated.

The combination of BPC-157, TB-500 and GHK-Cu has not been evaluated, classified, approved or
rejected by any regulator in any jurisdiction, because no such product has been submitted for
evaluation anywhere. This follows from the finding that none of the three components is an
approved drug and that no combination product exists in any registry or approval record examined.

Nothing is pending for the mixture. Where this page describes a pending review, that review
concerns a single component and must not be presented as concerning the product.

**Source:** derived from B01, B02, B03 in SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

## United States: BPC-157

### US-B-01. Approval status

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** not an approved drug.

FDA's briefing document for the Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of 23 to 24 July
2026 states that neither BPC-157 free base nor BPC-157 acetate is a component of an FDA-approved
drug, and further that there is no approved product containing BPC-157-related bulk drug
substances in any country at this time, nor is either form found in the European, Japanese or
International Pharmacopeias.

**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download (B01)

### US-B-02. Position on the bulk drug substances safety-risk list

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Page content current as of:**
04/22/2026.

On FDA's page listing certain bulk drug substances for use in compounding that may present
significant safety risks, BPC-157 appears in the table headed bulk drug substances nominated but
withdrawn, described as substances previously in category 2 of the interim policies that were
withdrawn by the nominators. It does not currently appear in the main Category 2 table on that
page.

The listed concern states that compounded drugs containing BPC-157 may pose risk for
immunogenicity for certain routes of administration and may have complexities with regard to
peptide-related impurities and characterisation of the active pharmaceutical ingredient, that
FDA has identified no or only limited safety-related information for the proposed routes, and
that the agency therefore lacks sufficient information to know whether the drug would cause harm
when administered to humans.

**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/drugs/human-drug-compounding/certain-bulk-drug-substances-use-compounding-may-present-significant-safety-risks (B02)

### US-B-03. The widely cited September 2023 date is NOT verified

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** unverified, do not
publish.

Numerous secondary sources state that FDA placed BPC-157 in Category 2 in September 2023 and that
this is its current status. That date could not be verified from any FDA source during research,
and FDA's own live page now presents the substance under nominated but withdrawn rather than in
the dated Category 2 table, so that table's date column does not apply to it.

The practical consequence is unchanged and may be stated: the substance is not on the 503A bulks
list, has no applicable monograph, and is not a component of an approved drug, so compounding
with it does not qualify for the section 503A exemptions. That consequence is confirmed
independently by the two enforcement letters below.

A human should check the Federal Register and the FDA docket record before any date is asserted.

**Sources:** B02, and the contradiction recorded at C-08 in CONTRADICTIONS.md.

### US-B-04. Enforcement, warning letter

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Letter dated:** 04/01/2020. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

FDA's warning letter to Tailor Made Compounding LLC cites the firm for compounding drug products
using BPC 157 among a list of substances, stating that such products are not eligible for the
exemptions provided by section 503A(a) because they are not the subject of an applicable USP or
NF monograph, are not a component of an FDA-approved human drug, and do not appear on the 503A
bulks list.

Note for the quality section: FDA records that material used in at least one of the small
published human studies of this compound was compounded by this firm.

**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/inspections-compliance-enforcement-and-criminal-investigations/warning-letters/tailor-made-compounding-llc-594743-04012020 (B04)

### US-B-05. Enforcement, untitled letter

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Letter dated:** 9/14/2023. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

FDA's untitled letter to Barclay, Luke, and Pillai Specialty Pharmacy PLLC states that the firm
compounded drug products using BPC-157 acetate among other substances that are not eligible for
the exemptions provided by section 503A(a), on the same three grounds.

This is a second, independent enforcement instance. A systematic search of FDA warning letters
could not be completed during research because the search interface did not return usable
responses to direct retrieval. There are very likely other letters. **Two is a floor, not a
total.**

**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/fda-commissioner/barclay-luke-pillai-specialty-pharmacy-pllc-untitled-letter-issued-9142023 (B05)

### US-B-06. Review pending, NO OUTCOME

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Memo dated:** 5/11/2026. **Meeting scheduled:** 23 to 24 July
2026. **Meeting page content current as of:** 07/17/2026. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Status:** PENDING. No outcome is stated on this page.

After both nominations were withdrawn, FDA began evaluating BPC-157 free base and BPC-157 acetate
on its own initiative. FDA's review memo, dated 5/11/2026, was placed before the Pharmacy
Compounding Advisory Committee meeting of 23 to 24 July 2026. FDA's stated recommendation is that
on balance the physicochemical characterisation, information on historical use, lack of evidence
of effectiveness, and safety information identified for both forms weigh against them being added
to the 503A bulks list.

**As of the date on this file that meeting has not occurred.** No outcome may be stated, and an
advisory committee recommendation is in any case advice to the agency rather than a final agency
determination.

**Sources:** https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download (B01);
https://www.fda.gov/advisory-committees/advisory-committee-calendar/july-23-24-2026-meeting-pharmacy-compounding-advisory-committee-07232026 (B03)

### US-B-07. Department of Defense

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Department of Defense. **Article dated:** 29 April 2025.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

Operation Supplement Safety states that BPC-157 is an unapproved drug that cannot be legally
prescribed or sold over the counter, is not a lawful dietary ingredient, appears on the DoD
Prohibited Dietary Supplement Ingredients List and on the WADA Prohibited List under non-approved
substances, and that there is little to no reliable scientific evidence to support the safety or
effectiveness of BPC-157 in humans.

**Source:** https://www.opss.org/article/bpc-157-prohibited-peptide-and-unapproved-drug-found-health-and-wellness-products (B11)

### US-B-08. New Zealand, cited second-hand within an FDA document

**Jurisdiction:** New Zealand. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** second-hand citation.

FDA's briefing document quotes the New Zealand medicines regulator as stating that there are
currently no approved products or pending applications for medicines containing BPC-157 in New
Zealand. This is a citation within the FDA document. The New Zealand source was not retrieved
directly and this should be treated as second-hand until it is.

**Source:** https://www.fda.gov/media/193343/download (B01)

## United States: TB-500 and GHK-Cu

### US-T-01. Approval status, TB-500

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** no approved product
located.

No FDA-approved TB-500 product was located. The only registered human study of this molecule is
an ongoing Phase 1/2 safety study with no posted results, which is inconsistent with any approved
status. Investigational products formulated from full-length thymosin beta-4 also remain
investigational, with no approval identified.

This is a negative finding from an absence of any approval record in the retrieved sources, not a
retrieved regulatory statement of non-approval. It is stated as such.

**Source:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies?query.term=thymosin+beta+4 (T08);
https://clinicaltrials.gov/api/v2/studies/NCT07487363 (T09)

### US-G-01. Approval status, GHK-Cu

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** no approved drug
product located.

No FDA-approved drug product containing GHK-Cu was identified for any medical indication, by any
route. Again, this is a negative finding from the absence of any approval record in the retrieved
sources rather than a retrieved statement of non-approval, and it is stated as such.

**Source:** derived from the searches recorded at G05, G08, G09, and the background entry G11.

### US-G-02. Cosmetic ingredient status

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

GHK-Cu appears in the United States market as a cosmetic ingredient under the INCI name Copper
Tripeptide-1.

**This must be read precisely.** Cosmetic ingredient status concerns topical cosmetic use. It is
not a drug approval, it is not a finding of effectiveness, and it says nothing about the safety
of a product administered any other way. Vendors routinely present cosmetic ingredient standing,
and the cosmetic expert panel review discussed below, in support of products that are not
cosmetics and are not topical. That is the most likely misreading of this entire page.

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copper_peptide_GHK-Cu (G11, tertiary background)

### US-G-03. Cosmetic expert panel safety assessment, NOT RETRIEVED

**Jurisdiction:** United States, Cosmetic Ingredient Review. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19.
**Status:** NOT RETRIEVED. Conclusion not asserted.

A Cosmetic Ingredient Review safety assessment covering tripeptide-1, its metal salts and related
derivatives is reported to exist and to have been published in 2018. Direct retrieval failed: the
PDF returned unparseable compressed streams, the journal page returned HTTP 403, and a PubMed
query returned zero results.

Neither the panel's conclusion nor the explicit inclusion of Copper Tripeptide-1 by name within
its scope is asserted anywhere in this packet. A human must retrieve the assessment before
anything is said about it. Even if fully confirmed, it would concern cosmetic topical use and
would not transfer to a product given another way.

**Source:** https://www.cir-safety.org/sites/default/files/tripep062014final.pdf (G12, UNVERIFIED)

### US-TG-01. Compounding status for TB-500 and GHK-Cu, COULD NOT BE VERIFIED

**Jurisdiction:** United States. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** UNVERIFIED. No claim
is made in either direction.

Conflicting claims circulate about whether these substances sit in a particular category of the
FDA interim compounding framework, whether their status changed during 2026 after nominations
were withdrawn, and, for GHK-Cu, whether the injectable route has been treated differently from
non-injectable use.

**Every claim of this kind encountered during research came from a commercial or vendor-adjacent
source, and every attempted fda.gov URL returned HTTP 404 to the fetcher in both sessions.** The
Federal Register record retrieved for the April 2026 advisory committee meeting notice contains
only a generic abstract naming no substances.

No position may be published. Adopting either would mean repeating a vendor claim as a regulatory
fact. A human must check fda.gov directly.

One observation for the reviewer: if the route-based distinction reported for GHK-Cu were
confirmed, it would reinforce rather than soften the central caution in this packet, because it
would mean the regulator treats the injectable route as the concerning one.

**Sources:** T12, G13 (both UNVERIFIED);
https://www.federalregister.gov/api/v1/documents/2026-07361.json (T11, retrieved but
uninformative)

## Anti-doping

**The WADA Prohibited List itself could not be retrieved for any component.** Attempts returned
empty or unusable content. Everything below comes from a national anti-doping organisation and is
attributed accordingly. An athlete must check with their own anti-doping authority directly.

### AD-01. BPC-157

**Jurisdiction:** anti-doping, via USADA. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

USADA states that BPC-157 is prohibited under the S0 unapproved substances category of the List.

The page retrieved did not state the list year, and did not state whether the prohibition applies
in competition only or at all times. Those details are therefore **not asserted here**, and must
not be added from background knowledge. Vendor pages claiming an additional listing under a
different section were not used and that claim is unverified.

**Source:** https://www.usada.org/spirit-of-sport/bpc-157-peptide-prohibited/ (B12)

### AD-02. TB-500

**Jurisdiction:** anti-doping, via USADA. **Date of the change described:** 2018 Prohibited List.
**Date checked:** 2026-07-19.

USADA's summary of changes to the 2018 Prohibited List states that thymosin-B4 and its
derivatives, giving TB-500 as an example, were added as examples of prohibited growth factors
under S2.3. TB-500 is therefore explicitly named rather than captured only by a class definition.

This verifies the 2018 list change. The current 2026 list wording and subsection numbering were
**not** independently confirmed, because the WADA pages returned empty content. Some secondary
sources assert a different section or a different listing year; the USADA statement is the only
position traceable to an anti-doping authority document actually retrieved.

The practical point is unaffected and safe to state: TB-500 is explicitly named and prohibited.
Detection is not theoretical. Validated methods exist in both equine and mammalian samples, and
one metabolite was detectable for up to 72 hours in rats.

**Sources:** https://www.usada.org/athlete-advisory/2018-prohibited-list-summary-of-major-changes/
(T10); https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23084823/ (T01);
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38382158/ (T02)

### AD-03. GHK-Cu, STATUS UNKNOWN

**Jurisdiction:** anti-doping. **Date checked:** 2026-07-19. **Status:** could not be determined.

A direct fetch of the Prohibited List returned no usable page content, and every search result
addressing GHK-Cu's anti-doping status was a peptide retailer or affiliate blog, which is
disqualified as evidence under the sourcing rules.

**No claim about GHK-Cu's prohibited or permitted status is made here, in either direction.** Its
absence from this page must not be read as permission. An athlete must check the current list and
their sport's authority directly.

**Source:** https://www.wada-ama.org/en/prohibited-list (G14, UNVERIFIED)
