# BPC-157 + TB-500 + GHK-Cu: quality and documentation requirements

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## Purpose of this document

This document does two separate things, and the distinction matters.

First, it states what quality documentation **should** exist before any product containing these
compounds could be taken seriously by Xenios. That standard is written independently of this
product and applies to any supplier presenting any material.

Second, it records the **honest current state**. Xenios has not confirmed a single item on this
checklist. Every box below is unchecked, and none is expected to be checked in the near term.

This is not a supplier evaluation, because there is no evaluation under way. It is the standard a
supplier would have to meet before one could begin.

## The first question, before anything else

**The exact composition and ingredient ratio of this product is NOT CONFIRMED.**

This sits before every other item because it is logically prior to all of them. Identity testing
requires knowing what should be present. Assay requires knowing how much should be present.
Uniformity requires a target composition to be uniform against. Stability requires a defined
formulation. Until a supplier states the composition and ratio in writing and it is verified, none
of the questions below can even be asked properly.

No ratio, proportion or relative content figure appears anywhere in this packet, and none may be
added.

## The starting position

Four facts frame everything below.

1. **None of the three components is an approved drug in any jurisdiction that was checked**
   (checked 2026-07-19). There is therefore no approved manufacturer, no approved specification,
   and no regulatory authority inspecting a production line against a filed standard for any of
   them.

2. **There is no official standard to test one of them against.** FDA states there is no USP or NF
   drug substance monograph for BPC-157 free base or BPC-157 acetate, no USP dietary supplement
   monograph, and that the substance is not found in the European, Japanese or International
   Pharmacopeias.

3. **The name does not identify the substance.** FDA states that BPC-157 is a common name rather
   than a standardised one, and that it has encountered multiple salts and derivatives, including
   different active moieties, sold commercially under that same name. FDA's own comment is that
   inconsistent naming represents a safety risk because a person may be dosed with a different
   bulk substance than intended. Separately, a product name does not reveal whether a TB-500 vial
   is claimed to contain the seven-amino-acid fragment or the 43-amino-acid protein.

4. **No testing of material sold under these names could be located.** No peer-reviewed study
   testing material sold as this blend, or as any of its three components, for identity, purity,
   sterility or endotoxin content was found. Nobody appears to have systematically checked what
   is in these vials. That is an absence of information, not a favourable result.

What is documented is how this class of product performs generally. FDA examined certificates of
analysis published by commercial peptide sellers for one component and found that most contain
purity testing only, with no information about impurity limits or testing results demonstrating
control of the impurity profile. A doping-control study found a non-natural synthesis impurity
detectable in horse blood plasma after administration of a TB-500-containing product. A 2026
white paper on compounded peptide products addresses contamination risk, mislabelling and lack of
standardisation as category-level concerns, citing a 2012 contaminated-compounding outbreak as
precedent for real-world harm; it does not name any component of this blend and is cited at the
category level only. A 2015 analytical study developed screening covering 25 peptides seized in
Europe or identified in underground forums, with the authors stating that counterfeit and illegal
peptide substances could imply severe health threats; the abstract does not state whether any
component of this blend was among them and reports no mislabelling rate, so no percentage may be
attached to anything here.

A note on numbers encountered elsewhere: research surfaced a widely repeated claim that roughly
30 percent of grey-market peptide samples are mislabelled, misdosed or contaminated. It traces to
no primary analysis and is excluded. It must not be added.

## The checklist

Every item below is a requirement a supplier would have to satisfy, with documentation, before
any further conversation. Status as of 2026-07-21 is **NOT CONFIRMED** for every single item.

### A. Composition and product definition (blend-specific)

- [ ] **A1. The exact composition stated in writing by the supplier**, naming each component
      unambiguously. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A2. The ratio or proportion of each component stated in writing** and held constant across
      batches. Status: NOT CONFIRMED. No figure is known and none is reproduced in this packet.
- [ ] **A3. A rationale for the composition**, stating on what basis these three compounds and
      these proportions were selected. Status: NOT CONFIRMED. Note that no study of this
      combination exists, so no evidence-based rationale is currently available to any supplier.
- [ ] **A4. A written product specification** covering the finished blend, with acceptance
      criteria set before testing rather than after. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **A5. Change control**, so that any alteration to composition or process is documented,
      justified and notified rather than made silently between batches. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### B. Per-component identity (blend-specific)

A blend must be identified component by component. Confirming that a vial contains "a peptide
blend" establishes nothing.

- [ ] **B1. Unambiguous chemical identity of each of the three components separately**, not the
      trade names. This includes the specific form of BPC-157, since FDA treats the free base and
      the acetate as different active pharmaceutical ingredients. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B2. Confirmed amino acid sequence for each peptide component in the specific batch**, by
      an orthogonal analytical method rather than by supplier assertion. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B3. Explicit confirmation that the TB-500 component is the acetylated seven-amino-acid
      fragment and not full-length thymosin beta-4**, or an explicit statement of which is
      present. These are different molecules with different evidence bases and no product name
      distinguishes them. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B4. Explicit confirmation that the copper peptide component is the intended copper complex
      and not plain uncomplexed peptide or a different copper peptide**, and confirmation of the
      copper content and its bound state. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B5. Explicit exclusion of confusable and commonly co-formulated compounds**, with
      documented evidence that no unlisted peptide is present. FDA records that products in this
      market are frequently co-formulated with other unapproved peptides.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B6. Salt form, counter-ion and water content stated and measured for each component.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **B7. Verified registry identifiers for each component**, confirmed against a primary
      chemical registry rather than taken from vendor material. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### C. Per-component assay and blend uniformity (blend-specific)

This section is the one most likely to be skipped, and it is the one that distinguishes a blend
from a single-ingredient product.

- [ ] **C1. Quantified content of each component separately**, by a validated method, with the
      method disclosed. A single figure for total peptide content is not an assay of a blend.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C2. Content of each component measured against the stated specification**, with the
      measured result reported rather than a pass or fail. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C3. Blend uniformity demonstrated within a batch**, so that the composition is the same
      throughout the material and does not vary by position, layer or fill order.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C4. Unit-to-unit consistency demonstrated across a batch**, so that separate units contain
      the same composition. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C5. Batch-to-batch consistency demonstrated across batches.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C6. A validated analytical method capable of separating and quantifying all three
      components in the presence of one another**, with demonstrated absence of interference
      between them. A method validated for one peptide alone does not carry over to a mixture.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **C7. Confirmed absence of chemical interaction between components in the formulation**,
      including complex formation, precipitation or any reaction involving the copper-containing
      component. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### D. Purity and impurities

- [ ] **D1. Quantified purity for each component by a validated, stated method**, with method,
      column and conditions disclosed rather than a bare percentage. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D2. A named impurity profile**, listing what the other material is and not only how much
      of it there is. FDA found that market certificates for one component are purity-only and do
      not demonstrate control of the impurity profile. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D3. Peptide-related impurities characterised for each component**, including truncated,
      deletion and modified sequences arising from synthesis. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D4. Residual solvents quantified** against a recognised limit standard. Solid-phase
      peptide synthesis introduces solvents, coupling reagents, activators, catalysts and
      scavengers. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D5. Elemental impurities and heavy metals quantified** against a recognised limit
      standard. This requires particular care for a product containing an intentional metal
      component, so that intended copper and contaminating metals are distinguished.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D6. Impurities of toxicological concern addressed**, including nitrosamines and
      potentially mutagenic and teratogenic substances. FDA notes that available data do not
      address these for one component. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **D7. Aggregation assessed for each peptide component and for the mixture.** Peptide
      clumping is a recognised contributor to immune responses against a peptide, and FDA
      identifies it as amplifying the immunogenicity concern. Whether the components promote one
      another's aggregation is an additional unstudied question. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### E. Sterility and microbiological safety

- [ ] **E1. Sterility testing by a recognised method**, where the product is intended for any
      route requiring it. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E2. Bacterial endotoxin testing with a stated limit and a measured result.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E3. Bioburden control documented at the appropriate manufacturing stages.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E4. Container closure integrity demonstrated.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E5. Aseptic process validation, or a validated terminal sterilisation process,
      documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **E6. Particulate matter testing.** FDA reports that no particle size, microbial quality,
      endotoxin, sterility, aggregate or container data were available for the proposed product
      forms of one component. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### F. Stability

- [ ] **F1. Stability data for the finished blend under stated storage conditions**, over a stated
      duration, using a stability-indicating method. Stability data for individual components does
      not establish stability of a mixture. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F2. Stability of each component measured separately within the blend over time**, since
      one component may degrade while another does not. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F3. Evidence that no component accelerates the degradation of another.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F4. A justified expiry or retest date derived from that data**, rather than assigned by
      convention. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F5. In-use stability after any preparation step.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F6. Photostability and temperature-excursion data**, including behaviour during shipping.
      FDA states that peptides can be extremely sensitive to formulation, process and
      environmental conditions, and that significant amounts of aggregates can form during storage
      or under stress. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **F7. Degradation products identified and quantified**, not simply a decline in assay value.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### G. Chain of custody and manufacturing provenance

- [ ] **G1. Named manufacturer and named physical manufacturing site**, for the finished blend and
      for each component if they are sourced separately. Not a brand, not a distributor, not a
      marketing entity. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G2. Regulatory inspection history of each site**, and the quality standard each operates
      to. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G3. Documented chain of custody from synthesis of each component through to the finished
      unit**, including every intermediate handler and the blending step itself.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G4. Batch and lot traceability for each component and for the finished blend**, so that
      any single unit can be traced to the manufacturing record of every component in it.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G5. Documented storage and transport conditions across the whole chain**, including
      temperature records. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **G6. Raw material provenance for starting materials and reagents.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### H. Certificate of analysis and third-party verification

- [ ] **H1. A batch-specific certificate of analysis matching the exact lot in hand**, not a
      representative or example certificate for a different batch. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H2. A certificate that reports each component separately**, rather than characterising the
      blend as a single article. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H3. The certificate issued by a named, accredited laboratory**, with its accreditation
      stated and independently checkable. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H4. Test methods named on the certificate**, with acceptance criteria and measured
      results, not the word pass. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H5. Independent third-party confirmatory testing**, commissioned by a party with no
      commercial interest in the result, on a sample drawn from the same lot.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H6. Certificate authenticity verifiable directly with the issuing laboratory.**
      Certificates are forged. A document that cannot be confirmed at source is not evidence.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **H7. Retention samples kept and available for re-testing.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### I. Labelling and claims

- [ ] **I1. Label content matching the tested content for every component**, within a stated
      tolerance. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I2. No efficacy or safety claims on the label or accompanying material that exceed the
      evidence.** For this blend that means no combination claims of any kind, and no human
      efficacy or safety claims for any component. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I3. No implication that ingredient research supports the combination**, in labelling,
      packaging or accompanying material. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I4. No use of research use only framing as an implicit signal of human benefit.** That
      framing is prohibited on any Xenios surface. Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **I5. Accurate regulatory status stated, with jurisdiction and date.**
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

### J. Governance and adverse events

- [ ] **J1. A documented adverse event reporting route**, with a named responsible party.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **J2. A recall and market withdrawal procedure**, tested rather than merely written.
      Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **J3. Documented complaint handling and deviation records.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.
- [ ] **J4. Insurance and liability position documented.** Status: NOT CONFIRMED.

An unchecked box means unverified. It does not mean failed, and it certainly does not mean
passed. The purpose of publishing this checklist while every item is unmet is that a member can
see exactly what has and has not been established, rather than inferring quality from the fact
that a Guide exists.

## Quality is a separate question from evidence

This deserves stating directly, because the two are easily conflated.

Perfect documentation for this product would establish what is in a vial. It would establish that
each component is present, in the stated form, at the stated content, uniformly, free of
identified impurities, sterile, and stable.

It would not establish that the contents do anything. It would not create the human evidence for
the individual components that, as set out in the Guide, is very thin or absent. And it would not
create combination evidence, because combination evidence can only come from studying the
combination. A flawless certificate of analysis and an empty evidence table can coexist without
contradiction, and here they would.
