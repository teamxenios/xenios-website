## 0. The blend rule: the gate that comes before all the others

This section exists because it is the failure this packet is most likely to suffer, and because
it can occur without a single false sentence being written.

**Reviewers must confirm that no part of this packet builds a case for the mixture by stacking
findings about its parts.** Three ingredient evidence bases do not add up to a combination
evidence base. A mixture is a distinct pharmacological entity with its own behaviour, its own
interactions and its own unknowns, and only a study of the mixture itself can address them.
Nothing of that kind was located, so the correct statement everywhere is that combination
evidence does not exist, not that it is limited, early, emerging or indirect.

- [ ] 0.1 The absence of combination evidence is stated in the first screen of `GUIDE_DRAFT.md`,
      before any ingredient is described.
- [ ] 0.2 The same absence is stated in `FAQ_DRAFT.md`, in `CLAIM_TABLE.md`, and in this
      checklist, in each case in its own words rather than as a repeated block.
- [ ] 0.3 No sentence anywhere presents ingredient evidence as combination evidence.
- [ ] 0.4 No sentence anywhere uses synergy, works together, complementary, supports, enhances,
      or any equivalent, about the components.
- [ ] 0.5 **The composed page has been read as a member would read it**, not only the source text.
      Three accurate ingredient sections under one product name read as a case for the product.
      Layout, headings, order and adjacency have been checked for this effect.
- [ ] 0.6 No heading, subheading, summary line, pull quote, image caption, link text or metadata
      field implies a combined effect.
- [ ] 0.7 The absence of combination evidence is described as absence, never as limited,
      preliminary, early, emerging, indirect or promising.
- [ ] 0.8 The two records of two-component co-administration are presented for what they are: one
      series where co-administration destroyed attribution, and confounded adverse event reports

- [ ] 0.9 The reviewer has personally re-run a combination search across the trial registries and

## Standing rules for this gate

1. **AI drafts never publish automatically.** This document set was drafted by an AI system from a
   research record. It carries no editorial authority and is not member-facing until human
   sign-off is complete.
2. **An unchecked box blocks publication.** There is no override and no expedited path.
3. **No single person may sign more than one reviewer role.** The separation is the control. One
   person checking their own work is not review.
4. **Evidence for the parts is not evidence for the whole.** A combination has its own
   pharmacology and its own unknowns. Unless a study tested this combination, this packet says
   there is no combination evidence, and says it in those terms. Any softening of that language
   returns the packet to this gate.
5. **A fabricated or unverifiable citation is the most serious possible failure**, because a
   reviewer or a member may trust it. If a citation cannot be confirmed at source, it is removed
   and logged as a gap. Omitting is always better than guessing.
6. **Concluding that evidence is absent is a successful outcome**, not a failure of the research.
   An honest empty evidence table is the correct result here and must not be padded.
7. **Regulatory status expires.** Any content published with a regulatory date more than 90 days
   old returns to this gate. The pending advisory committee review makes this packet specifically
   time-sensitive.
8. **Material changes restart the relevant section.** Any edit to an evidence, safety, composition
   or regulatory statement after sign-off requires that section to be re-checked and re-signed.
9. **A located combination study halts publication.** If one is found at any point, the packet is
   rebuilt from that study rather than amended around it, and the rebuild is recorded in the
   correction history.
