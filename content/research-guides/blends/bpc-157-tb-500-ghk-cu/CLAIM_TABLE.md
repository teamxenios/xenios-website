# BPC-157 + TB-500 + GHK-Cu Blend Claim Table

One row per discrete claim. Grades apply to individual claims, never to the blend as a whole
and never to any component as a whole. Source ids refer to SOURCE_REGISTRY.md.

Dosing and administration information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research Guides.

## The rule that governs every row below

**A claim graded for BPC-157 alone, or for TB-500 alone, or for GHK-Cu alone, does not transfer
to the blend.** Putting three substances into one preparation produces a distinct
pharmacological entity. It has its own behaviour in the body, its own interactions between the
components, and its own unknowns that do not exist for any component studied on its own.

Only a study that administered this combination can support a statement about this combination.
No such study was located. That is not weak combination evidence, and it is not preliminary
combination evidence. There is no combination evidence, and no quantity of component rows can
be added together to create some.

Every component row in this table therefore carries the same standing restriction: **not
transferable to the blend.**

## The second headline finding: the product is not defined

The exact composition and ingredient ratio of the Xenios product is **NOT CONFIRMED**. No
ratio, proportion or relative content figure appears anywhere in this packet and none may be
added. Until the composition is fixed and verified in writing by the supplier, there is no
defined article to grade, so no claim about the finished product is possible at any grade.

## Grade key

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| A | Established |
| B | Supported human evidence |
| C | Early human evidence |
| D | Preclinical (animal or cultured cells), or a statement about the state of the literature |
| E | Manufacturer or supplier reported |
| F | Traditional or historical |
| G | Unverified |
| PROHIBITED | May not appear on any member-facing surface |

No claim in this table about the combination is graded above G. No claim about any component is
graded A or B. Two component rows reach C, and both concern a molecule that is not in this
blend, which is precisely why they are flagged rather than used.
