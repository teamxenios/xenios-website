Fat loss and weight claims, which are the primary risk for this SKU:

## 18. Product FAQ

Four to six honest questions, answered without dosing and without promises.

**Is this the FDA-approved fat loss peptide?**
No, and that phrase is not accurate for any product. There are FDA-approved tesamorelin drug products,
EGRIFTA SV and EGRIFTA WR, labeled by Theratechnologies Inc. Their approved indication is reduction of
excess abdominal fat in HIV-infected adult patients with lipodystrophy, and their label explicitly
states they are not indicated for weight loss management and have a weight-neutral effect (VERIFIED
2026-07-19, https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=839334d3-8c1d-4c26-9036-2ab524a6ea75).
This SKU is not those products and is not FDA approved.

**What has tesamorelin actually been studied for?**
The main human evidence is in adults with HIV and abdominal fat accumulation. A double-blind randomized
controlled trial of 404 such patients reported visceral adipose tissue decreased by 10.9 percent in the
tesamorelin group versus 0.6 percent in placebo over six months, with improvements in trunk fat, waist
circumference, and body image distress, and reported that initial improvements were rapidly lost on
switching from active treatment to placebo (Falutz 2010). Studied for is not the same as works for, and
a result in that clinical population is not a prediction for anyone outside it.

**Will it help me lose belly fat?**
We cannot say that, and we will not. The population studied was adults living with HIV who had
lipodystrophy, which is a specific clinical condition. The approved label states the product is not
indicated for weight loss management. Anyone reading a visceral fat finding should read the population
along with it.

**Can you tell me how to use it?**
No. Dosing information is intentionally excluded from this record and from every guide. We do not
publish amounts, frequency, timing, reconstitution, or administration technique. Those are questions for
a licensed clinician who knows your history.

**Why can I see this product but not buy it?**
Catalog state and commerce state are separate on purpose. This record exists so the information is
organized and reviewable. Peptide commerce is not authorized, so commerce for this product is disabled
pending written approval.

**Is it safe?**
We cannot answer that, and we will not assert it. There is no verified quality documentation on file for
this SKU and no confirmed composition. Separately, the approved tesamorelin label carries
contraindications including active malignancy and pregnancy, and warnings including increased risk of
neoplasms, elevated IGF-1, fluid retention, and glucose intolerance or diabetes mellitus, and states
that long-term cardiovascular safety has not been established. That is a reason to involve a clinician,
not a reason to feel reassured about an unverified material.
