### Basis for the mappings

The mappings reflect the research areas the named components have been studied in, not results
observed for this product.

- Recover Faster. BPC-157 has been investigated in preclinical models of tissue injury,
  inflammatory bowel disease, and central nervous system conditions. The retrieved review states
  the compound has not been approved by the FDA or other global regulatory authorities,
  attributing this to the absence of sufficient and comprehensive clinical studies in humans.
  Source: Jozwiak M, Bauer M, Kamysz W, Kleczkowska P. Multifunctionality and Possible Medical
  Application of the BPC 157 Peptide, Literature and Patent Review. Pharmaceuticals (Basel), 2025.
  PMID 40005999. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40005999/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.
- Recover Faster. A European prospective randomized study in patients with venous ulcers
  evaluated thymosin beta-4 against safety, tolerability, and ulcer closure endpoints across sites
  in Italy and Poland. This is human work, and it is about thymosin beta-4, not about this product
  or this combination. Source: Guarnera G, DeRosa A, Camerini R. Thymosin beta-4 and venous
  ulcers, clinical remarks on a European prospective, randomized study on safety, tolerability,
  and enhancement on healing. Ann N Y Acad Sci, 2007. PMID 17495250.
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17495250/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.
- Look Better. GHK is described in the retrieved review as a peptide occurring in human serum
  whose measured serum level is reported to decline with age. In that review, cells pretreated
  with GHK-Cu showed a decrease in reactive oxygen species under hydrogen peroxide stress, an in
  vitro finding. In the same review, aged male C57BL/6 mice treated with GHK showed improved
  learning compared with saline treated control mice, a mouse finding. The review did not include
  human clinical trials. Source: Dou Y, Lee A, Zhu L, Morton J, Ladiges W. The potential of GHK as
  an anti-aging peptide. Aging Pathobiol Ther, 2020. PMID 35083444.
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35083444/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

### What the mappings do not rest on

No retrieved source studied this three-component combination in any population. Under the blend
rule, the combination is its own subject, and the evidence above attaches to single components.
The combination claim grade is therefore G, unverified.

Searches conducted on 2026-07-19 and 2026-07-20 surfaced numerous claims of human dermatological
results for copper peptides. Those claims sat on retailer, manufacturer, and vendor pages rather
than on retrievable primary sources, so none of them is recorded, relied on, or paraphrased here.
That absence is logged as a research gap, not treated as a finding.

### Considered and not mapped

Age Better was considered and deliberately not mapped, despite the anti-aging heading in the
retrieved GHK review. Filing this record under Age Better would put a product with no human
evidence in front of a member who arrived asking about aging, and the mapping itself would carry
an implication the evidence does not support. Build Muscle, Get Leaner, Think Sharper, Sleep
Better, Feel More Energized, Gut and Immune Health, Intimacy and Vitality, and Everyday Health
are likewise not mapped.

**How much should I take, and how often?**

Dosing information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research content. Questions about
amounts, frequency, timing, or route belong with a qualified professional who knows your history.
This is a fixed editorial position and not an oversight in this record.

**Is this safe for me?**

Xenios Research does not make general safety statements and cannot assess an individual
situation. No safety documentation for this specific item is on file, and the copper content is
unconfirmed. When the Guide is published it lists the categories of consideration discussed in
the literature so that you can raise them with a qualified professional.

**What is actually in it, and in what proportions?**

The exact ingredient ratio is not confirmed, and xenios holds no written specification for this
item. Whether the copper component is supplied as a copper complexed form is also unconfirmed.
Recording either would be inventing a product fact, so the composition section is deliberately
blank and stays blank until a written document closes it.

**How is this different from the two-component product?**

By name, one further named component. That is all this record can honestly say. Neither
combination has been studied in humans in anything retrieved here, so xenios cannot tell you that
one is stronger, more complete, or better suited to anything. Any page that ranks them is making
that up.

**I have read that copper peptides do a lot for skin. Is that relevant here?**

Most of those statements come from product marketing rather than from retrievable primary
research, so xenios does not repeat them. The primary review retrieved for this record reported in
vitro and mouse findings and did not include human clinical trials. Cosmetic topical research also
concerns a different preparation and a different route, and this record has no confirmed format at
all, so the comparison cannot be assessed.
