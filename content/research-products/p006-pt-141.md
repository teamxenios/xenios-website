## 18. Product FAQ

Four to six honest questions, answered without dosing and without promises.

**I read that this is FDA approved. Is it?**
No, this product is not. There is an FDA-approved drug product containing bremelanotide, marketed as
VYLEESI, and its label describes a melanocortin receptor agonist indicated for premenopausal women with
acquired, generalized hypoactive sexual desire disorder (VERIFIED 2026-07-19,
https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=f1d0c1b5-2f39-4bad-a6a4-0066e3ad5dcf). That
approval belongs to that product. It does not extend to this SKU, and we will not present it as though
it does.

**What has bremelanotide actually been studied for?**
Two identical randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trials, known collectively as
RECONNECT, enrolled 1,267 premenopausal women with hypoactive sexual desire disorder and reported
statistically significant improvement versus placebo in sexual desire and in distress related to low
desire (Kingsberg 2019). Studied for is not the same as works for, and a trial result in a defined
clinical population is not a prediction about any individual.

**Is it studied for men?**
Not in the approved indication. The approved label carries an explicit Limitation of Use stating it is
not indicated for men, and also not for postmenopausal women (VERIFIED 2026-07-19, DailyMed link above).
We are not going to represent it as a men's product.

**Can you tell me how to use it?**
No. Dosing information is intentionally excluded from this record and from every guide. We do not
publish amounts, frequency, timing, or administration technique. Those are questions for a licensed
clinician who knows your history, including your blood pressure and cardiovascular history.

**Why can I see this product but not buy it?**
Catalog state and commerce state are separate on purpose. This record exists so the information is
organized and reviewable. Peptide commerce is not authorized, so commerce for this product is disabled
pending written approval.

**Is it safe?**
We cannot answer that, and we will not assert it. There is no verified quality documentation on file for
this SKU and no confirmed composition. Separately, the approved bremelanotide product is contraindicated
in people with uncontrolled hypertension or known cardiovascular disease and carries warnings including
a transient blood pressure increase, focal hyperpigmentation, and nausea. That is a reason to involve a
clinician, not a reason to feel reassured about an unverified material.
