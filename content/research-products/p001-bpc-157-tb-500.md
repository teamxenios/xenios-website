### Basis for the mapping

The mapping to Recover Faster reflects the research area the named components have been studied
in, not a result observed for this product.

- BPC-157 has been investigated in preclinical models of tissue injury, inflammatory bowel
  disease, and central nervous system conditions. The retrieved review states the compound has
  not been approved by the FDA or other global regulatory authorities, attributing this to the
  absence of sufficient and comprehensive clinical studies in humans. Source: Jozwiak M, Bauer M,
  Kamysz W, Kleczkowska P. Multifunctionality and Possible Medical Application of the BPC 157
  Peptide, Literature and Patent Review. Pharmaceuticals (Basel), 2025. PMID 40005999.
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40005999/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.
- A European prospective randomized study in patients with venous ulcers evaluated thymosin
  beta-4 against safety, tolerability, and ulcer closure endpoints across sites in Italy and
  Poland. This is human work, and it is about thymosin beta-4, not about this product or this
  combination. Source: Guarnera G, DeRosa A, Camerini R. Thymosin beta-4 and venous ulcers,
  clinical remarks on a European prospective, randomized study on safety, tolerability, and
  enhancement on healing. Ann N Y Acad Sci, 2007. PMID 17495250.
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17495250/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

### What the mapping does not rest on

No retrieved source studied this combination in humans. Under the blend rule, the combination is
its own subject, and the evidence above attaches to single components rather than to this
product. The combination claim grade is therefore G, unverified.

## Product FAQ

**How much should I take, and how often?**

Dosing information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research content. Questions about
amounts, frequency, timing, or route belong with a qualified professional who knows your
history. This is a fixed editorial position and not an oversight in this record.

**Is this safe for me?**

Xenios Research does not make general safety statements and cannot assess an individual
situation. No safety documentation for this specific item is on file. When the Guide is
published it lists the categories of consideration discussed in the literature so that you can
raise them with a qualified professional.

**What is actually in it, and in what proportions?**

The exact ingredient ratio is not confirmed, and xenios holds no written specification for this
item. Recording a ratio that has not been confirmed would be inventing a product fact, so the
composition section is deliberately blank. It is logged as an open supplier question and stays
blank until a written document closes it.

**Is there human evidence for this combination?**

Not in anything retrieved for this record. The retrieved literature studies the named components
individually, and mostly in animal models for BPC-157. Evidence for an ingredient studied alone
does not establish evidence for a combination, so the honest answer is that the combination
itself has not been shown to have been studied in humans.

**Why does the record link to research if you are not claiming a benefit?**

Because a member is entitled to see the same basis xenios used to file the record, including how
thin it is. The links show what the components have been studied for. They are not offered as
evidence that this item does anything.

**Can I order this today?**

No. Peptide commerce is not authorized and commerce is disabled for this item. This record
exists as internal documentation. Being able to read it is not an offer to supply it.
