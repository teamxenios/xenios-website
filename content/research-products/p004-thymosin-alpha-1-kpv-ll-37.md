### Basis for the mapping

The mapping to Gut and Immune Health reflects the research areas the named components have been
studied in, not results observed for this product.

- Thymosin alpha-1 has been evaluated in humans for immunocompromised states and malignancies, for
  vaccine response, for sepsis, and for a range of infections, and the retrieved review discusses
  possible repurposing against severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus-2. Source: Dominari A,
  et al. Thymosin alpha 1, A comprehensive review of the literature. World J Virol, 2020. PMID
  33362999. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33362999/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.
- KPV, a tripeptide derived from alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone, was reported to have
  anti-inflammatory potential in murine models of inflammatory bowel disease. In that work, mice in
  a dextran sodium sulfate colitis model, a CD45RB-high transfer colitis model, and a
  melanocortin-1 receptor mutant model showed reduced inflammatory infiltrate and earlier recovery
  compared with controls. Every one of those findings is in mice. Source: Kannengiesser K, et al.
  Melanocortin-derived tripeptide KPV has anti-inflammatory potential in murine models of
  inflammatory bowel disease. Inflamm Bowel Dis, 2008. PMID 18092346.
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18092346/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.
- LL-37 is described in the retrieved review as the only human member of the cathelicidin family of
  antimicrobial peptides, a 37-residue amphipathic helical peptide found throughout the body, with
  described roles in antimicrobial activity, regulation of the inflammatory response, recruitment
  of adaptive immune cells, lipopolysaccharide binding, and wound closure. That review is a
  synthesis of biophysical and structural evidence about the peptide as it occurs in human biology.
  It is not a clinical trial of any product. Source: Durr UHN, Sudheendra US, Ramamoorthy A. LL-37,
  the only human member of the cathelicidin family of antimicrobial peptides. Biochim Biophys Acta,
  2006. PMID 16716248. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16716248/ VERIFIED 2026-07-19.

### What the mapping does not rest on

settings. It establishes that a named molecule has been studied. It establishes nothing about this
product, and it may not be carried onto a product surface in any form.

## Product FAQ

**How much should I take, and how often?**

Dosing information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research content. Questions about amounts,
frequency, timing, or route belong with a qualified professional who knows your history. This is a
fixed editorial position and not an oversight in this record.

**Is this safe for me?**

Xenios Research does not make general safety statements and cannot assess an individual situation.
No safety documentation for this specific item is on file. When the Guide is published it lists the
categories of consideration discussed in the literature so that you can raise them with a qualified
professional.

**What is actually in it, and in what proportions?**

The exact ingredient ratio is not confirmed, and xenios holds no written specification for this
item. Recording a ratio that has not been confirmed would be inventing a product fact, so the
composition section is deliberately blank. It is logged as an open supplier question and stays
blank until a written document closes it.

**I have seen these components discussed in connection with infections and immune conditions. What
does that mean for me?**

It means those named molecules have been studied by researchers in those contexts, in defined
patient populations, in clinical settings. It does not mean this product does anything in those
contexts, and Xenios Research does not present it that way. The distinction is the whole point of
the Guide, which names the population behind every finding so you can see what was actually
studied and in whom.

**Is there human evidence for this combination?**

Not in anything retrieved for this record. One component has been evaluated in humans as a single
molecule. One has retrieved evidence in mice. One is described in a review of the peptide as it
occurs in human biology rather than as a product. None of that is evidence about the three
combined, and evidence for an ingredient studied alone does not establish evidence for a
combination.

**Can I order this today?**

No. Peptide commerce is not authorized and commerce is disabled for this item. This record exists
as internal documentation. Being able to read it is not an offer to supply it.
