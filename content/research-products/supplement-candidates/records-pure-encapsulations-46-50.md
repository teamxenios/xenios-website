# Supplement Candidate Records 46 to 50, Pure Encapsulations

Candidate registry only. Nothing in this file is approved for sale. Xenios holds no written
reseller authorization from Pure Encapsulations or any other brand. Catalog state is separate
from commerce state, and commerce state is blocked on every record below.

Dosing information is intentionally excluded from every record in this file.

## Verification method and its limits (read before using these records)

All five records in this batch are marked UNVERIFIED at the page level. The reason is specific
and worth stating plainly.

The brand domain `pureencapsulationspro.com` returned HTTP 403 Forbidden to every direct
retrieval attempt made on 2026-07-19, across both HTML product pages and the brand's own PDF
product information sheets. No page on that domain was directly retrieved this session.

What that leaves is a weaker verification tier, described honestly:

- **Tier 1, direct retrieval.** Not achieved for any brand product page. Zero records.
- **Tier 2, search index confirmation.** Product URLs below appeared as indexed results under
  the official brand domain, with matching product titles, in WebSearch calls run this session.
  This confirms the URL exists and belongs to the brand. It does not confirm current page
  content, current formulation, or current certification status.
- **Tier 3, search engine page summary.** Composition and allergen details below were relayed
  by the search tool's summarizer rather than read directly. These are recorded as reported
  content and flagged as requiring human confirmation against the live page or the physical
  label before any member-facing use.

No product URL in this file was constructed, guessed, or reconstructed from memory. Every URL
came from a search result returned this session. Where a fact was not retrievable, the record
says so rather than filling the gap.

**One verified correction worth carrying forward.** The NSF certification directory was
retrieved directly and successfully. It shows Pure Encapsulations holding registration under
**NSF/ANSI 455-2, Good Manufacturing Practices for Dietary Supplements**, which is a
facility and manufacturing-practice registration. It is **not** NSF Certified for Sport, and it
is not a product-level certification. No product in this batch was confirmed as NSF Certified
for Sport or Informed Sport. Do not let a GMP registration be presented, internally or to
members, as an athlete-testing certification. These are different programs with different
meanings.

Retrieved source, VERIFIED 2026-07-19:
`https://info.nsf.org/Certified/Common/Company.asp?CompanyName=pure+encapsulations`

label before any member-facing use.

### Allergens (as reported, requires label confirmation)

Reported to contain **milk and soy**. Reported as gluten free and non-GMO.

The milk and soy declaration is the single most important unconfirmed fact in this record. It
is a direct member-safety item, and it must be confirmed against a physical label before this
candidate is shown to anyone.

### Official quality and certification claims

NOT CONFIRMED. No product-level certification was verified for this item. Pure Encapsulations

Informed Sport status was verified for ProbioMood.

### Duplicate-nutrient flags

Duplicates with any other probiotic candidate in the registry. Cross-check records 1 to 45 for
probiotic entries before this candidate is ever shown alongside them. Do not present two
probiotic products together.

Also overlaps functionally with any sleep or stress formula in the registry, which triggers the
overlapping-sleep-formula duplication rule. Review jointly, not separately.

- Dairy allergy or dairy avoidance, and soy allergy, given the reported allergen declaration.
- Members taking immunosuppressive medication.
- Any member with a central venous catheter or significant GI barrier compromise.
- Members using psychiatric medication, where a mood-framed product warrants clinician

### Athlete-testing relevance

Not confirmed as certified for sport. Treat as **not cleared** for tested athletes. Any athlete
under a testing regime requires human review, and the absence of a product-level certification
should be stated plainly rather than glossed.

## Record 49, Iron candidate (hidden and conditional)

- **Brand:** Pure Encapsulations

- **Display state: HIDDEN by default.** This record must not surface in any browsable catalog,

### Why this record is hidden

Iron is the clearest case in this batch for a hard gate. Iron supplementation without a
documented need is not benign, and iron overload is a real harm with a slow presentation. A
member cannot self-assess iron need from symptoms, because fatigue is nonspecific and overlaps
with a dozen other causes. Iron status requires bloodwork and a clinician reading it.

This record therefore exists as a catalog entry that a practitioner may reach deliberately. It
is not reachable by a member browsing, and it must never be returned by an automated
recommendation, a goal mapping, or a quiz.

### Other iron items observed under the same domain

These additional URLs appeared in search results under the official brand domain on 2026-07-19
and are recorded for completeness. None were directly retrieved, and none are separately
verified.

- Iron-C: https://www.pureencapsulationspro.com/iron-c-pe.html
- Iron Liquid: https://www.pureencapsulationspro.com/iron-liquid-120-ml-120.html

Which iron form, if any, belongs in the registry is a practitioner decision and is
**NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions.**

### Goal mappings

**None.** Deliberately unmapped.

Iron is commonly associated with Feel More Energized, and that association is exactly the
problem. Mapping iron to an energy goal invites a member with normal iron status to self-select
into supplementation they do not need. This record carries no goal mapping by design, and that
absence should be treated as a decision rather than an omission to be corrected later.

### Foundation role

Conditional, and gated behind documented need plus clinician involvement. Never core.

### Ingredients (as reported, requires label confirmation)

Iron as iron bis-glycinate, identified by the brand as Ferrochel brand material, with vitamin C
reported as ascorbyl palmitate, in a capsule.

All quantities are intentionally excluded.

Source tier: search engine page summary, not direct retrieval. The vitamin C form in particular
should be confirmed against the label, since ascorbyl palmitate is an unusual choice and the
summary may have conflated fields.

### Allergens (as reported, requires label confirmation)

**NOT CONFIRMED - see open supplier questions.** No product-specific allergen declaration was
retrieved.

### Official quality and certification claims

NOT CONFIRMED at the product level. Company-level NSF/ANSI 455-2 GMP registration only. No NSF
Certified for Sport or Informed Sport status was verified.

### Duplicate-nutrient flags

- Duplicates with any multivitamin containing iron, including most prenatal formulas.
- Duplicates with any other iron candidate in the registry, including the Iron-C and Iron Liquid
  items noted above. Only one iron product may ever be active.
- Absorption conflict with record 48, Calcium (citrate), in this same batch. Calcium and iron
  compete for absorption.
- Absorption conflict with any zinc candidate in the registry. The no-multiple-zinc rule and the
  iron gate should be reviewed together.

### Interaction-review flags (human review required before any recommendation)

Every item below is a hard gate, not a soft note.

- **Hemochromatosis or any iron-loading anemia.** The brand's own reported guidance states iron
  supplementation is not recommended in these conditions. Absolute contraindication for this
  record without clinician direction.
- Absence of documented iron deficiency. No bloodwork, no recommendation.
- Thalassemia and sideroblastic anemia.
- Pregnancy and breastfeeding, where iron need is real but must be clinician directed.
- Members receiving blood transfusions or iron infusions.
- Thyroid medication, tetracycline and fluoroquinolone antibiotics, levodopa, and
  proton pump inhibitors, all of which have absorption or interaction concerns with iron.
- Inflammatory bowel disease and other GI conditions, where oral iron tolerance is a genuine
  issue.
- Households with young children, given the acute toxicity risk of iron ingestion in children.
- Reported GI effects including nausea, vomiting, constipation, and darkened stool.

### Athlete-testing relevance

Not confirmed as certified for sport. Treat as not cleared for tested athletes without human
review. Endurance athletes are a population with genuinely elevated iron-deficiency prevalence,
which makes this record more likely to be requested and therefore more important to keep gated
behind bloodwork rather than behind sport category.
