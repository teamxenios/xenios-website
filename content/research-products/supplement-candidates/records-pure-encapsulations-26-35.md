### The certification finding that matters most

The retrieved FAQ states that the brand's products and its manufacturing and testing procedures are inspected
twice annually by NSF for compliance with FDA Good Manufacturing Practices, that manufacturing takes place at
the company's own facility in Sudbury, Massachusetts under cGMP procedures at an FDA-inspected plant, and that
testing covers identity, potency, microbial contaminants, heavy metals, solvent residues, and allergens.

That is a GMP compliance inspection. It is NOT NSF Certified for Sport, and it is NOT Informed Sport.

The retrieved FAQ makes no claim of NSF Certified for Sport, no claim of Informed Sport, and no claim of
third-party gluten-free certification. Nobody drafting member-facing copy should read "inspected by NSF" as
"NSF Certified for Sport". Those are different programs with different meanings for athletes, and conflating
them is the single most likely error in this batch.

Consequence for every record: athlete-testing suitability is NOT ESTABLISHED across all ten items.

## Known tension, product names that carry a strength

The brand publishes several of these products under names that embed a numeric strength. The content safety
rule for this registry prohibits amounts, mg, mcg, IU, and concentrations anywhere in the file, and that rule
is absolute and overrides completeness.

Resolution applied: the base product name is recorded, the numeric strength is redacted, and the existence of
multiple strength variants is flagged so a human reviewer selects the variant against the official label.
This is a deliberate redaction, not missing data. Reviewers should not treat the absence of a strength as an
indication that only one variant exists.

Affected records in this batch: Vitamin D3, Zinc, NAC, CoQ10 and Ubiquinol, and Ascorbic Acid.
