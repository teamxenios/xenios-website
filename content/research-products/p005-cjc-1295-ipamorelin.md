  components. No human study of this specific combination is on file, and no combination claim may be

## 18. Product FAQ

Four to six honest questions, answered without dosing and without promises.

**Is this an FDA-approved product?**
No. Neither component is an FDA-approved drug product, and no approved product combines them. Nothing
in this record should be read as FDA approval, clearance, or evaluation.
[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]

**What exactly is in it, and in what ratio?**
We do not have that confirmed. The exact ingredient ratio is not confirmed, and we have not confirmed
whether the CJC-1295 component is the DAC form or the non-DAC form. We are not going to estimate it.
Those questions are open with the supplier and are listed at the end of this record.

**What has this actually been studied for?**
The individual compounds have human study records. CJC-1295 was studied in healthy adults for its
effect on growth hormone and IGF-1 secretion (Teichman 2006). Ipamorelin was studied in adults after
bowel resection for postoperative ileus, and that trial did not meet its primary efficacy endpoint
(Beck 2014). The blend itself has no human study on file. Studied for is not the same as works for, and
neither is a statement about what would happen to any individual.

**Can you tell me how to use it?**
No. Dosing information is intentionally excluded from this record and from every guide. We do not
publish amounts, frequency, timing, cycle length, reconstitution, or administration technique. Those are
questions for a licensed clinician who knows your history.

**Why can I see this product but not buy it?**
Catalog state and commerce state are separate on purpose. This record exists so the information is
organized and reviewable. Peptide commerce is not authorized, so commerce for this product is disabled
pending written approval.

**Is it safe?**
We cannot answer that, and we will not assert it. There is no verified quality documentation on file for
this product, no confirmed composition, and no general safety finding that would apply to an individual.
Anyone considering a compound in this class should talk to a licensed clinician.
