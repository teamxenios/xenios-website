Mapped, with the basis stated:

- Think Sharper. Basis: Semax has been studied in humans in the domain of brain network
  activity. A small placebo-controlled study of 24 healthy adults reported a difference in a

## 18. Product FAQ

Written for a member. No dosing, no promises.

**What is in this product?**
It is described as containing three compounds: Semax, Selank and DSIP. We do not know how much
of each. The ratio has not been confirmed by the supplier and we have not verified it
ourselves, so we cannot tell you what the proportions are. We are recording that gap rather
than filling it with a guess.

**Why does the ratio matter so much?**
On a blend, the ratio is most of what the product is. Two products with the same three
ingredients in different proportions are different products. Until we know the ratio, we cannot
describe this one accurately, and we are not going to describe it inaccurately.

**Have these three been studied together?**
Not in anything we found. Every study we retrieved looks at one compound on its own. That means
research about Semax is research about Semax, and it does not tell you what a three-part
combination does.

**What has each one been studied for?**
Semax has been looked at in a small placebo-controlled study of 24 healthy adults using brain
imaging, which measured brain network activity rather than any cognitive or clinical outcome.
Selank has been studied in the area of anxiety, though we were not able to retrieve the primary
trial reports during this review. DSIP has been studied in adults with chronic insomnia using
sleep recordings, and in the study we retrieved the investigators concluded the sleep
improvement was of little clinical significance.

**What does the FDA listing mean?**
On 29 September 2023, United States regulators placed all three of these substances in a
category of bulk substances that raise significant safety concerns for pharmacy compounding.
That is a regulatory safety flag. It is not an approval. Reporting from April 2026 indicates
that Semax was later removed from that category, and the same reporting is clear that removal
does not authorize compounding.

**Can I buy this from Xenios Research?**
No. Commerce for this lane is not authorized and is blocked pending written approval, and this
particular SKU is separately blocked by the unconfirmed ratio. The catalog record exists so that
the documentation work can happen in the open. It is not a storefront.
