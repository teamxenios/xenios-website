## 18. Product FAQ

Written for a member. No dosing, no promises.

**What is Dihexa?**
It is a synthetic compound derived from angiotensin IV, a small peptide that occurs in the
body's renin-angiotensin system. It was developed in an academic laboratory and has been
studied in animals for effects related to learning and memory.

**Why does this record keep mentioning a retraction?**
Because the paper most often cited as establishing how Dihexa works was retracted by its
journal in April 2025. A retraction means the published record has been formally withdrawn.
We think you should know that before you read anything else about this compound, including
what other sellers say about it.

**Does that mean everything about Dihexa is wrong?**
No, and we are not claiming that. Other published work, including a 2021 study in a mouse model
of Alzheimer's disease, exists independently. What the retraction means is that the mechanism
most commonly repeated in marketing material rests on a source that was withdrawn, so we will
not repeat it as established.

**Has it been tested in people?**
We did not find any human clinical trial of Dihexa. Human effects, human safety, and human dose
response are unknown.

**What does the FDA listing mean?**
On 29 September 2023, United States regulators placed Dihexa Acetate in a category of bulk
substances that raise significant safety concerns for pharmacy compounding. That is a
regulatory safety flag. It is not an approval, and its absence would not have been an approval
either.

**Can I buy this from Xenios Research?**
No. Commerce for this lane is not authorized and is blocked pending written approval. The
catalog record exists so that the documentation work can happen in the open. It is not a
storefront.
