2. Any statement that this item lengthens telomeres, activates telomerase, or protects DNA in a

   in vitro. A cell in a dish is not a person, and this is the single most likely claim to be

3. Any statement that this item reduces mortality or risk of death. Reason: prohibited disease
   claim and unsupported outcome claim. Mortality findings in this literature concern the

   Reason: prohibited disease claim. The retrieved tumour findings are from mice and rats.
5. Any statement that this item restores, normalizes, or regulates melatonin, sleep, or
   circadian rhythm. Reason: "restores" is a prohibited outcome verb, and the retrieved
   melatonin findings come from rats, from rhesus monkeys, and from one reported human study

6. Any statement that this item improves vision, eyesight, or retinal function. Reason: the
   retrieved review reports a human study in an eye condition. That study concerns a specific

7. Any statement that this item improves immunity, immune function, or resistance to illness.

8. Any statement about cognitive enhancement, memory, or mental sharpness. Reason: unsupported

9. Any statement about skin, appearance, or visible signs of aging. Reason: unsupported
   anti-aging and appearance claim.
10. Any statement that this item is safe, well tolerated, or free of side effects, and any use
    of the phrase "no reported side effects" drawn from a study summary. Reason: unsupported

11. Any statement that this item is approved, licensed, or recognized by a medicines regulator

## Product FAQ

**How much should I take, and how often?**

Dosing information is intentionally excluded from Xenios Research content. That includes
amounts, frequency, timing, cycle length, and route. Questions about any of those belong with a
qualified professional who knows your history.

**Is this safe for me?**

Xenios Research does not make general safety statements and cannot assess an individual
situation. For this compound there is a further point worth stating directly. The 2025
peer-reviewed review retrieved for this record says that information about this peptide's
safety is missing, and that additional short-term and long-term toxicity study would be
required before it could be considered as an active pharmaceutical ingredient. Bring that to a
qualified professional rather than treating an absence of reported problems as reassurance.
Source, VERIFIED 2026-07-19: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11943447/

**Does this lengthen telomeres?**

The retrieved review reports telomerase activation and telomere lengthening in cultured human
cells studied in vitro, meaning cells in a dish rather than people. Xenios Research will not
carry that finding across to a statement about a person, because the study design does not
support it. What happens in a cultured cell line is a reason to keep researching, not a result
you can expect.

**What has actually been studied in people?**

Less than the volume of published work suggests. The retrieved review describes a very large
preclinical literature (fruit flies, several mouse strains, several rat strains, and rhesus
monkeys) and a much smaller human literature, including a reported study in an eye condition
and a reported study of circadian measures in women. Those human reports have not been
independently retrieved and verified for this record, they come largely from a single research
lineage, and they have not been widely replicated. They are logged as open question 9 rather
than presented as established.

**Is this the same thing as the pineal extract it was based on?**

No. The synthetic tetrapeptide was modelled on the amino acid composition of a bovine pineal
gland extract, but they are different substances. A good deal of the older human evidence in
this area concerns the extract. Xenios Research does not present that evidence as evidence
about this item.

**What is the current legal or regulatory status?**

Not established to the standard Xenios Research requires, and logged as open supplier question
6 below. No regulatory statement is made in this record, because no dated, jurisdiction
specific, sourced statement has been retrieved and verified.

**Why does the catalog show this if I cannot buy it?**

Because catalog state and commerce state are separate on purpose. Peptide commerce is not
authorized, so nothing here is for sale, and the presence of this record is not an offer.
