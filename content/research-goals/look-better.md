# Look Better

## 1. What this goal actually means

This is the goal people are most likely to choose and least likely to say out loud, so it is worth
handling with some care.

"Look better" usually contains three separate things. The first is body composition, meaning the
ratio of muscle to fat and where it sits. The second is skin, meaning texture, tone, and the
visible signs of sun exposure and time. The third is the part nobody puts in a form field, which
is wanting to feel less self-conscious, or to recognize yourself again, or to walk into a room
differently.

Those respond to different things and they respond on very different timescales. Body composition
moves over months with training and nutrition. Skin changes slowly and is dominated by decades of
cumulative sun exposure and by whether you smoke. The third one does not reliably respond to
either, which is the part the industry never mentions.

That last point is the one we would ask you to sit with. Changing how you look does not
predictably change how you feel about how you look. Plenty of people reach the composition they
aimed at and find the feeling they were chasing did not come with it. We are not saying do not
pursue this. We are saying pursue it with your eyes open, because a goal that is actually about
self-worth cannot be closed out by a training plan, and a system that pretends otherwise is
selling you something it cannot deliver.

## 2. Lifestyle foundation

**Sun exposure is the single largest modifiable input to how your skin ages, and it is not close.**
In a randomized trial of 903 adults younger than 55 in Nambour, Australia, participants assigned
to daily broad-spectrum sunscreen use showed 24 percent less skin aging, measured by skin surface
micro-topography at the back of the hand, than those assigned to discretionary use over four and a
half years (Hughes et al., Annals of Internal Medicine, 2013). Read the design of that. It is a
randomized trial with a hard endpoint, in adults, over years. Nothing sold as a skin supplement
has evidence of that character behind it, and the intervention costs very little.

**Do not smoke.** In a comparison of 79 pairs of identical twins with differing smoking histories,
the smoking twins had worse scores for upper eyelid skin redundancy, lower lid bags, malar bags,
nasolabial folds, upper lip wrinkles, lower lip vermillion wrinkles, and jowls, with effects most
apparent in the lower two-thirds of the face (Okada et al., Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery,
2013). Identical twins control for genetics, which is what makes that comparison useful. If you
smoke and you have chosen this goal, this is the item on the page.

**Build and keep muscle, because it is most of what people are actually describing.** When someone
says they want to look leaner or more defined, the underlying variable is usually muscle
underneath rather than the last few pounds on top. Resistance training is the mechanism, and it is
slow, and it works.

**Do not crash diet.** Aggressive restriction reliably costs lean tissue alongside fat, which
produces a smaller version of the same shape and a harder time maintaining it. The visible result
people want almost always requires patience they were not sold.

**Sleep, because it shows.** The AASM and the Sleep Research Society recommend 7 or more hours per
night for adults, and their consensus panel concluded 6 or fewer is inadequate to sustain health
and safety. We are not going to claim a specific effect on skin appearance, because we did not
retrieve a source for that. We include it because it affects training quality, appetite
regulation, and adherence, all of which sit upstream of everything else on this page.

**Be careful about the comparison set.** Most of the reference images in this category are
lighting, posing, dehydration, a specific camera lens, a competition peak that lasted forty-eight
hours, or editing. Some involve pharmacology that is never disclosed. Comparing your body to a
photograph is comparing an ongoing life to a staged moment, and it is a losing arrangement by
construction.

**Know when this stops being our territory.** If your relationship with food, your body, or the
mirror has become distressing or compulsive, that is a clinical matter and it is more important
than any plan. It belongs with a qualified professional. We would rather lose the sale than have
you route an eating disorder through a body composition program, and we mean that literally.

## 3. Fitness and nutrition considerations

These are the areas your Xenios 30 plan is built to address in this goal. The plan itself is
produced from your assessment, so the specific structure is set there rather than described here.

- Resistance training as the primary driver, since visible change is mostly muscle
- A rate of body composition change slow enough to keep the tissue you are training for
- Adequate protein intake to protect lean mass during any period of reduced intake
- Adequate total energy, with restriction treated as a temporary phase and not a lifestyle
- Training the areas that shape overall appearance rather than the areas you are most self-critical about
- Realistic timelines stated in months, and stated before you start
- Sleep, because it drives adherence, appetite, and session quality
- Alcohol reviewed honestly, given its energy contribution and its effect on sleep
- Skin protection habits treated as part of the plan rather than as a separate topic
- Photographs and measurements used sparingly, on a fixed schedule, to reduce daily noise
- Explicit screening for whether this goal is carrying weight that belongs somewhere else

## 6. What Xenios will not promise

- **We do not promise a body fat percentage, a weight, a dress size, or a timeline to any of
  them.** Rate of change varies enormously between people for reasons that are not under your
  control or ours.
- **We do not promise spot reduction.** You cannot direct fat loss to your stomach, your arms, or
  your face. Where you store and lose fat is largely genetic, and every product sold against that
  fact is sold against a fact.
- **We do not promise fewer wrinkles, firmer skin, or a change in skin texture.** We do not treat
  skin. The one item on this page with randomized human evidence for a skin aging endpoint is
  daily sunscreen use, and it is preventive rather than corrective.
- **We do not promise hair growth, hair retention, or changes to hair, nail, or skin quality.**
  Hair loss in particular has real clinical treatments and a large market of products that are not
  them. That conversation belongs with a dermatologist.
- **We do not promise you will look like a reference photograph.** Frame, height, bone structure,
  fat distribution, and muscle insertion points are structural. They set the shape that training
  reveals, and they are not something a plan changes.
- **We do not promise before-and-after results.** We will not use transformation imagery as
  persuasion, because the honest version of it requires disclosing the timeline, the starting
  point, the adherence, and often the pharmacology, and almost nobody discloses all four.
- **We do not promise that any peptide changes human skin appearance or body composition.**
  Whatever the cell and animal literature suggests, that claim is not one we will make.
- **We do not promise you will feel better about your body.** This is the promise the category is
  actually built on and the one least supported by anything. Appearance change and body image
  change are not the same variable, and we will not conflate them to close a sale.
- **We do not diagnose or treat eating disorders, body dysmorphic disorder, dermatological
  conditions, or hair loss.** The assessment is not a diagnosis. If your history or your answers
  raise a clinical question, the correct next step is a licensed clinician, and we will say so
  plainly.

## 8. Take the assessment

The assessment is what makes any of this personal. Your training history, your starting point,
your sun and skin history, your relationship with food, how much time you actually have, and what
you are really trying to change all shift the right answer, and none of that is visible from a
page.

It takes about 10 minutes. It is required within 3 days of activation. It is not a diagnosis, it
is not a prescription, and it does not replace your clinician.

What it produces is a Xenios 30 plan with a realistic timeline, a rate of change that protects the
tissue you are training for, and an honest conversation about what will and will not change.
