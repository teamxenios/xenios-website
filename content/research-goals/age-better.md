# Age Better

## 1. What this goal actually means

Most people who choose this goal are not asking to live longer. They are asking to stay
capable. They want to carry their own bags at seventy, get off the floor without planning the
movement first, keep their thinking clear, and not have the next thirty years be a slow
negotiation with their own body.

That is worth naming precisely, because "anti-aging" as a category sells something else. It sells
the idea that aging is a condition with a treatment, and that the treatment is a purchase. Aging
is not a condition. It is the accumulated result of time, genetics, environment, and several
decades of daily behavior, and only some of that is inside your control.

The part inside your control is larger than most people assume and smaller than the market
implies. The honest version of this goal is compression, not extension. The aim is to spend more
of your life in good working order and less of it in decline, and the levers that actually move
that are unglamorous, well established, and mostly free.

There is a second thing worth saying plainly. Nobody has demonstrated that any product available
to a consumer slows human aging. Not a supplement, not a peptide, not a protocol. The people
selling that certainty are ahead of the evidence, and often far ahead of it.

## 2. Lifestyle foundation

**Strength is the asset that decays and the one you can rebuild.** Loss of muscle and strength is
one of the more predictable features of aging and one of the more responsive to training. In a
systematic review and meta-analysis of 14 randomized controlled trials covering 561 older adults
with sarcopenia, resistance training produced significant gains in handgrip strength and knee
extension strength, and significant improvements in gait speed and the timed up and go test.
Notably, the same review found no significant improvement in appendicular skeletal muscle mass
index or skeletal muscle mass, and the authors attributed the strength gains largely to neural
mechanisms rather than to muscle growth, particularly in shorter interventions (Chen et al.,
European Review of Aging and Physical Activity, 2021). Read that carefully. The measurable win was
function, not size. Function is the thing that determines whether you can get out of a chair.

**Keep moving, at a volume that is unremarkable.** Adults need at least 150 to 300 minutes of
moderate-intensity aerobic activity each week plus muscle-strengthening activity on at least 2
days each week, and physical activity lowers the risk of falls and injuries from falls in older
adults (Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans, 2nd edition, ODPHP). There is no compound in
this category with evidence approaching that.

**Sleep is not optional infrastructure.** The American Academy of Sleep Medicine and the Sleep
Research Society recommend that adults sleep 7 or more hours per night on a regular basis, and
their consensus panel concluded that 6 or fewer hours per night is inadequate to sustain health
and safety in adults (2015 consensus statement, developed by a 15-member panel using a modified
RAND appropriateness method for adults aged 18 to 60).

**Do not smoke, and take the medical basics seriously.** Blood pressure, blood glucose, lipids,
cancer screening appropriate to your age and history, dental care, hearing, and vision are managed
with your clinician and they are not our territory. We mention them because they outrank every
category on this page and because a page about aging that skipped them would be dishonest.

**Protect your relationships and your reasons to get up.** Isolation is not a soft variable in
aging outcomes. We are not going to overstate the mechanism, but the direction is not seriously
disputed, and no product substitutes for people who expect to see you.

**Accept that some of this is not negotiable.** Genetics, the era and place you were born into,
the work you have done, and luck all sit outside your control. A plan that pretends otherwise will
make you feel responsible for outcomes you did not cause. That is not a useful place to operate
from, and it is exactly the emotional pressure this category is usually sold into.

**Be suspicious of urgency in this space.** The specific structure of an anti-aging pitch is a
countdown: every month you wait costs you something irreversible. It is an effective sales device
and it is not an accurate description of how aging works over the timescales in question.

## 3. Fitness and nutrition considerations

These are the areas your Xenios 30 plan is built to address in this goal. The plan itself is
produced from your assessment, so the specific structure is set there rather than described here.

- Resistance training as the anchor of the week, with progression that respects joint history
- Function-first targets such as getting off the floor, carrying, stairs, and single-leg balance
- Aerobic work sufficient to support cardiovascular and metabolic health, most of it easy
- Balance and fall-risk work where age and history make it relevant
- Adequate total protein intake spread across the day, sourced from food first
- Adequate total energy, since under-eating accelerates the loss of the tissue you are protecting
- Bone loading through resistance and impact work where it is appropriate and safe for you
- Sleep quantity and consistency treated as a training variable, not a lifestyle preference
- Alcohol intake reviewed honestly, because it interacts with sleep, recovery, and health risk
- Mobility maintained through use rather than through stretching sessions bolted on at the end
- Clear criteria for when something becomes a clinical question and leaves this system entirely

Three things are worth knowing before you read about this category anywhere else.

First, longevity is the hardest possible endpoint to study in humans. Demonstrating that something
slows human aging requires following people for decades, and almost nothing in this space has been
studied that way. What exists instead is mechanism, cell and animal work, and short human trials
measuring surrogate markers. A surrogate marker moving is not a life changing. Our Guides
distinguish between the two every time.
[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]

Second, a large share of the widely circulated findings for compounds in this group originate in
non-human models, including worms, flies, and rodents, and are reproduced online without the
species named, which lets a lifespan result in an invertebrate read as a human result. Our Guides
name the species and the model in the same sentence as the finding, every time.
[UNVERIFIED - background knowledge, requires human source check]

Third, biological age tests are widely used to sell products in this category. They are
proprietary, they vary between providers, they vary within a provider across repeat samples, and a
result moving is not evidence that you aged less. Treat a biological age number as a
conversation piece, not as an outcome.

## 6. What Xenios will not promise

- **We do not promise you will live longer.** No product, protocol, or plan offered or discussed
  here has been shown to extend human lifespan, and we will not imply it with softer wording
  either.
- **We do not promise to slow, stop, or reverse aging.** Aging is not a condition we treat. Claims
  that a compound reverses aging in humans are not claims we will make regardless of how the
  underlying study was reported elsewhere.
- **We do not promise a lower biological age.** We do not sell against epigenetic clocks, telomere
  length, or any proprietary age score, and we will not present a change in one as proof that
  anything meaningful happened.
- **We do not promise protection from age-related disease.** Not dementia, not cardiovascular
  disease, not cancer, not osteoporosis, not type 2 diabetes. Risk reduction is a clinical
  conversation with your doctor, informed by your history and your numbers, and it is not
  something a membership delivers.
- **We do not promise you will keep the body you had at thirty.** Some changes with age are
  structural and are not reversible by training or by anything else. A plan that implies otherwise
  is setting you up to feel like you failed at something that was never available.
- **We do not promise that a peptide affects human aging.** Whatever the cell and animal
  literature suggests, and however confidently it is stated in the places you have read it, that
  claim is not one we will make.
- **We do not promise that hormone-adjacent compounds are a youth strategy.** Growth hormone axis
  compounds are frequently sold on an anti-aging story that outruns the human evidence, and the
  risk conversation attached to them belongs with a physician, not with us.
- **We do not promise the assessment will find something wrong that others missed.** It is not a
  diagnostic workup and it is not designed to be one.
- **We do not diagnose or treat any age-related condition.** The assessment is not a diagnosis. If
  your history or symptoms raise a clinical question, the correct next step is a licensed
  clinician, and we will say so plainly rather than route you toward a purchase.

## 8. Take the assessment

The assessment is what makes any of this personal. Your age, training history, joint and injury
history, medications, family history, current strength, sleep, and what your week actually
contains change the right answer completely, and none of that is visible from a page.

It takes about 10 minutes. It is required within 3 days of activation. It is not a diagnosis, it
is not a prescription, and it does not replace your clinician.

What it produces is a Xenios 30 plan built around the capacities you want to still have in twenty
years, starting from the ones you actually have now.
