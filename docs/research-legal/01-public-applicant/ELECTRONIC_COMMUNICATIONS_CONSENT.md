## 1. What you are consenting to

Xenios Research delivers its agreements, notices, and program documents
electronically. By accepting this consent you agree that:

1. Xenios ([ENTITY]) may provide you records electronically instead of on paper.
2. Your electronic acceptance (a checkbox, click, or typed confirmation, recorded with
   a timestamp and document version) has the same effect as your handwritten
   signature, to the extent applicable law allows.
3. Electronic delivery to your account's Document Center, or to the email address on
   file with a secure link, counts as delivery to you.

This consent is designed to satisfy the consumer-consent provisions of the federal
E-SIGN Act and state UETA enactments. It must be accepted before the other membership
agreements are presented, because those agreements are themselves electronic records.

## 2. What records are covered

All records related to your application and membership, including: the membership
agreements and acknowledgments presented at activation, this consent itself, privacy
notices, the assessment, your Blueprint and plan documents (delivered as secure PDFs
through the Document Center with email notification), receipts and billing records for
the $50 activation payment and the $25 recurring monthly membership, subscription and
order records, policy-change notices, security notices, and cancellation
confirmations.

Some records may still be sent on paper where law requires it, and Xenios may choose
paper delivery for any record.

## 3. System requirements

To receive and keep electronic records you need:

- A current, supported web browser with JavaScript and cookies enabled.
- A valid email address you check, kept current in your account.
- A PDF viewer for plan documents.
- Enough storage to download and save records, or a printer.
- For members, the ability to complete multi-factor authentication, which is required
  on every member account.

If our requirements change in a way that creates a material risk you cannot access
your records, we will notify you and, as applicable law requires, give you the chance
to withdraw consent without fee.

## 4. Your duty to keep contact details current

Electronic delivery only works if we can reach you. Keep your email address current in
your account, or by emailing research@xeniostechnology.com. Delivery to the address on
file is effective even if you no longer read that mailbox.

## 5. Paper copies

You may request a paper copy of any record we delivered electronically by emailing
research@xeniostechnology.com. Paper copies of the current agreements are also

## 6. Withdrawing your consent

You may withdraw this consent at any time by emailing research@xeniostechnology.com
from the address on file. Withdrawal takes effect within a reasonable time after we
receive it and applies prospectively; it does not undo records already delivered or
accepted electronically.

Be aware of the practical consequence: Xenios Research is an online program. Plans,
agreements, billing, and account security all run electronically. If you withdraw
consent, we may be unable to deliver parts of the program, and continued membership
may not be possible [COUNSEL: confirm the permissible consequence of withdrawal,
including whether membership may be conditioned on electronic delivery for an
online-only service, and any required withdrawal fee disclosure (none is currently
planned)].

## 7. Demonstrating access

Accepting this consent electronically, on the same kind of system that will deliver
your records, reasonably demonstrates that you can access records in the formats we
use. If you cannot read this consent or open a sample PDF, stop and contact
research@xeniostechnology.com before accepting.

## 8. Legal effect

This consent does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it
does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law. Records that the law says cannot be
delivered electronically remain outside this consent.
