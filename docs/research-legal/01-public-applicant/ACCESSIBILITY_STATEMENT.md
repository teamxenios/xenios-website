## 1. Our commitment

Xenios Research intends the program to be usable by members with disabilities. This
statement describes the standard we target, what we do to meet it, what we know does
not meet it yet, and how to tell us when something blocks you. It is written to be
honest rather than reassuring: the program is in its founding phase and accessibility
work is ongoing.

## 2. Conformance target

The target standard is the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)
[CONFIG: conformance version and level; working target WCAG 2.1 Level AA], published
by the W3C. Conformance status today: partially conformant. Some parts of the site and
some documents do not yet fully meet the target, as described in section 4. A formal
audit against the target has not yet been performed [COUNSEL: confirm whether an
external audit should precede publication of this statement].

## 3. What we do

Accessibility work in the program covers:

- Keyboard access: interactive surfaces (entrance, application, member site, checkout,
  tracker) are intended to work without a mouse.
- Screen reader support: pages use semantic structure, labeled form fields, and
  descriptive error messages so assistive technology can present them.
- Contrast and readability: text and interface contrast are checked against the
  target standard.
- Forms: the application, assessment, and checkout identify required fields, explain
  errors in plain language next to the field, and do not rely on color alone.
- Media: captions or transcripts for instructional audio and video content as it is
  produced.
- Documents: plan PDFs (Blueprint, fitness, nutrition) are generated from templates
  that are being made accessible (tagged structure, reading order, alt text).
- Review: accessibility is part of the release checklist for new surfaces, and this
  statement is updated as the state changes.

## 4. Known limitations

Being direct about current gaps:

1. Plan PDFs: the Blueprint, fitness, and nutrition PDFs may not yet be fully tagged
   for screen readers. The content of a plan can be provided in an alternative format
   on request while this is fixed.
2. Progress media features: progress photos and side-by-side visual comparisons are
   inherently visual. We are working on text summaries of tracked progress, but the
   comparison view itself has no non-visual equivalent yet.
3. Voice notes: member voice notes up to 60 seconds are not automatically transcribed
   at launch, so voice-based support content may be inaccessible to members who cannot
   hear it. Text is always available as an equal alternative channel.
4. Third-party surfaces: identity verification, payment, and Telegram support run on
   third-party platforms whose accessibility Xenios does not control. We consider
   accessibility in vendor selection but cannot certify those surfaces.
5. Charts and tracker visualizations: some tracker visuals may lack full text
   equivalents at launch.

## 5. Feedback and alternative access

If any part of the program is difficult or impossible for you to use:

- Email research@xeniostechnology.com with the page or document, what happened, and
  the assistive technology you use.
- We aim to respond within [CONFIG: response target; working target 5 business days]
  and to provide the blocked content or function in an alternative format where
  reasonably possible (for example, plan content by accessible document or email, or
  completing a flow with human help over a supported channel).
- Accessibility feedback goes to the founder, Samuel Boadu, who is the named
  accountable person for the program.

Using an alternative format never costs extra and never reduces what you receive as a
member.
