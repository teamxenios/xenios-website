## 1. Who we are and what this notice covers

Xenios Research is a private, application-based membership program operated by Xenios
([ENTITY]). Membership requires an application, founder review, and approval, and is
limited to people 21 years of age or older.

This notice explains what information Xenios collects when you apply, why it is
collected, how long it is kept, who can see it, and the choices you have. It covers the
application stage only: from your first visit to the application form until your
application is approved, denied, or withdrawn. If you are approved and become a member,
the General Privacy Notice (XR-PUB-004) describes the full program, and this notice
remains accurate for the application-stage data it covers.

## 2. What we collect when you apply

The application is short, targeted at 3 to 5 minutes. We ask only for what we need to
evaluate your application and communicate with you.

### Required information

- Legal name and preferred name.
- Email address (verified).
- Mobile phone number (verified).
- Date of birth, or a declaration that you are 21 or older.
- City, state, and country.
- Occupation and company or organization.
- Applicant type (the category that best describes you).
- Primary goal and secondary goals.
- Reason for joining and why now.
- Referral source and referral code, if you have one.
- Your expectations of the program.
- Required acknowledgments (for example, that the program is private, 21+, and
  application-based).

### Optional information

You may choose to provide, and the application works without:

- Instagram, LinkedIn, X, or website links.
- A professional credential.
- Preferred contact channel and best contact time.

### Information collected automatically

When you use the entrance and application pages we record limited technical and
operational data: the submission IP address, the source page, timestamps, and
application status events (for example submitted, in review, approved, denied). This
data supports security, fraud prevention, and an accurate record of how your
application was handled.

## 3. What we do not collect at the application stage

At application, Xenios does not collect:

- Raw government ID images or numbers. Identity and age verification happen later,
  only after approval, through a separate verification step with its own notice.
- Payment card or bank information. Payment happens only after approval,
  verification, and acceptance of the member agreements.
- Full medical records or a detailed health history. The application asks about goals,
  not medical conditions. The separate member assessment happens only after activation.

If a question on the form ever appears to ask for any of these, do not answer it and
tell us at research@xeniostechnology.com.

## 4. Why we use applicant information

We use application data for these purposes:

1. To evaluate your application. Samuel Boadu, the founder, personally reviews every
   application during the founding phase.
2. To communicate application status: the pending confirmation, requests for more
   information, and the decision.
3. To attribute referrals. If you enter a referral code, we use it to credit the
   referring member under the referral terms. We do not show the referring member your
   application information.
4. To protect the program: fraud prevention, rate limiting, abuse detection, and
   security logging.
5. To meet legal obligations and to keep an auditable record of the application
   decision.

We do not sell applicant information. We do not use applicant information for
third-party advertising. Marketing email is a separate, optional, unchecked-by-default
consent governed by the Marketing Email Consent (XR-PUB-011); declining it never
affects the outcome of your application.

## 6. Who can see applicant information

- Xenios: Samuel Boadu reviews applications. Access inside Xenios is limited to people
  and systems that need it for the purposes above, with access logging.
- Service providers: providers that host our systems, deliver email, or deliver SMS
  process data on our instructions and only as needed to provide their service.
- Referrers: never. A referring member can see that their code was used and any
  resulting store credit, but not who applied or any application content.
- Legal: we may disclose information where the law requires it or to protect the
  rights, safety, and security of Xenios, applicants, and members.

We do not share application data with advertising platforms.

## 7. Your choices and rights

- You can decline to apply, and you can stop an application at any time before
  submitting it.
- You can contact us to access, correct, or request deletion of your application data.
  Deletion is honored subject to the retention needs described above and to applicable
  law. Depending on your state of residence, you may have specific statutory rights of
  access, correction, deletion, and appeal; the state-by-state review is pending
  (see the General Privacy Notice, XR-PUB-004).
- You can withdraw any optional consent (for example marketing email) at any time
  without affecting your application.
- We verify privacy requests against the email on file before acting on them.

To exercise any of these, email research@xeniostechnology.com.

## 8. Security

Application data is stored in access-controlled systems with encryption in transit and
at rest, row-level authorization, and audit logging. The shared entrance password on
/research unlocks only the entrance and application layer; it never exposes member
data, and your application is not visible to other applicants or members. These
measures are designed to protect your data; no system can guarantee absolute security.

## 9. If you are approved

Approval starts a defined sequence: identity and age verification, then the member
agreements, then payment, then account security setup. Each step has its own notice or
consent presented before it happens. The General Privacy Notice (XR-PUB-004) describes
data handling across the whole program, including verification, assessment, tracker,
orders, and support data.
