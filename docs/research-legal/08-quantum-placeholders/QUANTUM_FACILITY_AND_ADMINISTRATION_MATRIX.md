## 1. Purpose

This matrix records, per state, how Quantum could be administered and delivered, and
whether that is permissible. It exists to force a state-by-state answer before any Quantum
commerce. Every permissibility conclusion is reserved for counsel. Empty cells mean the
question is not yet answered and are marked pending counsel. No cell in this document
authorizes any sale, administration, or activation. Quantum stays Coming Soon until the
Quantum Commerce Activation Checklist (XR-QTM-008) is complete.

## 2. Column definitions

- Administration responsibility: who physically administers or applies Quantum (for
  example self-administration by the member, a licensed clinician, or not applicable if
  Quantum is not administered).
- Facility type: where administration or dispensing occurs (for example a licensed
  clinical facility, a pharmacy, direct ship to member, or not applicable).
- Licensed professional involvement: whether a licensed professional must be involved and
  in what role (for example prescriber, supervising clinician, dispensing pharmacist, or
  none).
- State: the US state or territory the row analyzes.
- Per-state permissibility: [COUNSEL] conclusion on whether the row's arrangement is
  lawful in that state, with the condition or restriction that applies.

## 5. Availability rule

A state may be enabled for Quantum only if its permissibility cell is completed by counsel
with an affirmative, condition-bearing conclusion, and the conditions are implemented as
server-side controls. Any state left pending is disabled by default. This matches the
product spec rule that catalog state is separate from commerce state and server-side lane
controls are required.
