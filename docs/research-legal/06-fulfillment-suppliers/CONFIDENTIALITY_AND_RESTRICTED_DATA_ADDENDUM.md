## 1. Purpose

This Addendum is a schedule to the Master Fulfillment Agreement (XR-FUL-001) between [ENTITY] ("Xenios") and [ENTITY] ("Mitch"). The fulfillment relationship requires each party to show the other sensitive information: Xenios discloses member shipping data, order patterns, pricing, and program design; Mitch discloses supplier identities, product master data, costs, quality documents, and operating methods. This Addendum protects both directions.

Confidentiality here is mutual and operational. It is not a substitute for the data protection duties in XR-FUL-009, which control for member personal data.

## 2. Definitions

2.1 "Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by one party (the "Discloser") to the other (the "Recipient") in connection with the fulfillment relationship, whether written, oral, electronic, or observed at a facility, that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential, including the Restricted Data classes in Section 3.

2.2 Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes public through no fault of the Recipient; (b) the Recipient lawfully possessed without a confidentiality duty before disclosure; (c) the Recipient lawfully receives from a third party without a confidentiality duty; or (d) the Recipient independently develops without use of the Discloser's Confidential Information, with written evidence.

## 3. Restricted Data classes

The following classes receive the highest care under this Addendum:

### 3.1 Xenios Restricted Data

1. Member Fulfillment Data (name, shipping address, phone where required, order items, order reference, shipping service). Handling is governed by XR-FUL-009; this Addendum adds confidentiality but never loosens XR-FUL-009.
2. Member identity as such: the fact that a specific person is a Xenios Research member is itself confidential. The program is private and application-based.
3. Order volumes, SKU mix, demand patterns, and any aggregate that could reveal the member base or its behavior.
4. Program design: pricing, membership structure, the Blueprint and plan system, roadmap, and the contents of unreleased documents.
5. Settlement and commercial terms under XR-FUL-012.

### 3.2 Mitch Restricted Data

1. Supplier and manufacturer identities and terms from the product master (XR-FUL-002).
2. Unit costs, wholesale pricing, and margin information.
3. Quality documents: COAs, sterility, endotoxin, identity, and purity records, validation summaries, and shelf-life data.
4. Facility, storage, packout, and security methods.

### 3.3 Shared Restricted Data

Incident details, recall information, complaint and adverse-event communications, and audit findings are confidential to both parties and disclosed outside the parties only as required by law, a regulator, an insurer, or with both parties' written consent. Nothing in this section delays or restricts any legally required report to a regulator.

## 4. Duties of care

4.1 The Recipient must: use Confidential Information only for the fulfillment relationship; restrict access to personnel with a need to know who are bound by written confidentiality duties at least as protective as this Addendum; protect it with at least the care it uses for its own confidential information, and never less than reasonable care; and not reverse engineer, copy, or extract it beyond the permitted purpose.

4.2 Neither party may issue a press release, marketing statement, customer list entry, case study, or social media post naming the other party or the relationship without the other party's prior written consent. Mitch must not state or imply publicly that it fulfills for Xenios Research. 4.3 Product claims discipline: Confidential Information never authorizes public statements about products. Products are research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review. Neither party makes human-use, therapeutic, or outcome claims.

## 5. Compelled disclosure

If the Recipient is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information (subpoena, court order, regulator demand), it must, where lawful: promptly notify the Discloser before disclosing, cooperate with the Discloser's effort to seek protection at the Discloser's cost, and disclose only the portion legally required. Disclosure compelled by law is not a breach when this process is followed.

## 6. No license, no obligation

6.1 Confidential Information remains the Discloser's property. No license, ownership, or other right transfers except the limited use right in Section 4.1.

6.2 Neither party is obligated to disclose any particular information, and either party may stop disclosing at any time. Disclosure creates no commitment beyond the Master Fulfillment Agreement.

## 8. Return or destruction on termination

8.1 On termination or expiry of the Master Fulfillment Agreement, or on the Discloser's earlier written request, the Recipient must promptly return or securely destroy all Confidential Information of the Discloser, including copies, extracts, and derived documents, and certify completion in writing within [CONFIG: certification window, proposed 30 days].

8.2 Exceptions: the Recipient may retain (a) copies required by law or bona fide document retention policy, and (b) automatic system backups not readily accessible, in each case remaining subject to this Addendum until destroyed in the ordinary course.

8.3 For Member Fulfillment Data, the return and deletion mechanics of XR-FUL-009 Section 11 and the Transition and Termination Assistance Schedule (XR-FUL-014) control the timing and certification.
