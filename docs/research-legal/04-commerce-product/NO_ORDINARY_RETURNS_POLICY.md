## 1. The policy in one paragraph

Xenios Research does not accept ordinary returns. Opened products are not returnable. Routine
returns of unopened products (for example, "I changed my mind" or "I ordered too much") are
also not accepted. This is not a convenience decision. It exists to protect product integrity
and chain of custody for every member. Verified problems with an order are handled through a
separate path (Section 4), and that path can end in a full replacement or a full refund.

## 2. Why there are no ordinary returns

The products Xenios ships include research products and supplements whose integrity depends on
storage, handling, and temperature history. Once a product leaves the documented fulfillment
chain, Xenios can no longer verify how it was stored, whether it was exposed to heat, cold,
light, or moisture, or whether it was tampered with. A package that looks unopened proves
nothing about what happened to it.

For that reason:

- no returned product is ever restocked or resold, regardless of its appearance, and
- accepting routine returns would either create waste with no member benefit or, worse, create
  pressure to restock unverifiable product. Xenios refuses both.

Returned, compromised, expired, or recalled products receive a documented disposition and are
destroyed where required. Chain of custody is preserved from lot to member order, which is also
what makes honest recall handling possible.

## 3. What to check when your order arrives

Because ordinary returns are not available, check every delivery promptly. On arrival:

1. Compare the delivered items against the order in your order history. Remember that a split
   order arrives as multiple shipments; an item in a later shipment is not missing (see the
   Split Shipment Disclosure, XR-COM-005).
2. Check each item's label against what you ordered (product and quantity).
3. Check the outer packaging for crush, puncture, or water damage.
4. Check seals and tamper evidence on each product.
5. Where the shipment includes a temperature indicator or coolant, check its state.
6. Photograph anything that looks wrong, including the packaging, before discarding any
   packaging materials.

If anything is wrong, report it promptly through the process in Section 4. Keep the product and
all packaging until Xenios tells you how the report will be resolved.

## 4. The exception path

The no-returns rule never traps a member with a genuinely bad delivery. If your order arrives
damaged, never arrives, contains an incorrect or missing item, or shows a possible temperature
problem, use the Damage, Loss, Incorrect Item, and Temperature Concern Policy (XR-COM-008).
Verified issues are resolved under the Refund and Replacement Policy (XR-COM-009) with a full
replacement or a full refund, or a partial remedy where only part of an order is affected.

Do not ship anything back unless Xenios explicitly instructs and arranges it. Unsolicited
returns cannot be restocked, do not create a refund by themselves, and may be destroyed on
receipt under the disposition rules.

## 5. What this policy does not cover

- Membership fees and membership cancellation are governed by the membership documents,
  including the Immediate Cancellation and Access Termination Acknowledgment, not by this
  policy.
- Subscription product orders follow this same policy once shipped; subscription controls
  (pause, skip, cancel) manage future shipments, not delivered ones.

## 6. Legal posture

This policy is subject to applicable law. Some jurisdictions grant consumers rights that a
store policy cannot remove. Nothing here waives rights that cannot be waived under applicable
law, and nothing here relieves Xenios of duties imposed by law. The final form of this policy
requires counsel, payment processor, insurer, and fulfillment review.
