## 1. Emergency first

If you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 now. Do not report it to Xenios first.
Telegram, email, and the dashboard are not emergency services and are not monitored as one;
the normal support response target is approximately 12 hours. Report to Xenios after you are
safe.

## 2. Purpose

This page tells you how to report a problem with a product bought through Xenios, what to
include so the report can be acted on, and what happens next. Reporting helps protect you and
other members, and it feeds Xenios's quality and recall processes.

## 3. Pick the right category

When you report, Xenios will classify your report into one of these categories. You do not
need to get the category perfect; describing what happened is enough.

1. Support question. A how-to or account question about a product. No defect, no health event.
2. Quality complaint. Something is wrong with the product itself: damaged packaging, wrong
   item, missing item, broken seal, unusual appearance or smell, a lot or expiry problem, or a
   COA mismatch.
3. Product concern. You suspect a problem but are not certain: possible temperature
   compromise in shipping, storage doubt, a label that does not match the product page, or a
   document that looks wrong.
4. Possible adverse event. A health event you think may be connected to a product: an
   unexpected reaction, a new symptom, an interaction you noticed.
5. Serious adverse event. A health event involving hospitalization, disability, a
   life-threatening experience, a congenital anomaly, a medical intervention to prevent one of
   those, or death. Get medical care first, then report.

## 4. How to report

- Telegram: message support 24/7, text or a voice note up to 60 seconds.
- Dashboard: use the support surface in your member account.
- Email: research@xeniostechnology.com.

Do not send passwords, identity documents, or payment card numbers over Telegram. Product and
label photos are fine on any channel.

## 5. What to include

Include as much of this as you can:

1. Product name and SKU (from the product page or your order).
2. Lot number and expiry date (from the physical label).
3. Order number and purchase date (from your order history).
4. Photos: the product, the label showing lot and expiry, and any damage or defect.
5. What happened, when it started, and what you did (for example stopped use).
6. For a possible adverse event: what you experienced, when it began relative to use, whether
   you sought medical care, and whether you were using anything else at the time. Share what
   you are comfortable sharing; Xenios does not need your full medical history, and it will
   not ask you to send medical records over Telegram.

## 6. Preserve the product

Keep the product, its packaging, and any remaining contents unless and until Xenios tells you
otherwise. Do not continue using a product you have reported. A preserved product can be
verified; a discarded one often cannot. Reported or compromised product is never restocked.

## 7. What happens next

1. Your report gets a tracked status: Pending, Being Reviewed, More Information Needed, Answer
   Ready, Completed. Normal response target is approximately 12 hours.
2. Samuel Boadu is the named accountable human for escalations. Serious reports are
   prioritized ahead of routine support.
3. Xenios responds without giving medical advice. Xenios will not diagnose your symptoms,
   tell you whether a reaction was caused by a product, or direct your medication or dosing.
   For health decisions, see your own physician or qualified professional.
4. Verified quality issues (damage, loss, incorrect item, missing item, temperature
   compromise) are handled under the returns policy: full replacement or full refund, subject
   to applicable law.
5. Where a report suggests a lot-level problem, it feeds the quality process (complaint
   review, corrective action, and recall assessment). If a recall follows, notice arrives
   under the Recall Notification Terms (XR-COM-018).
6. For dietary supplements, serious adverse events carry legal reporting responsibilities.
   The written allocation of those responsibilities between Xenios and each brand is being
   confirmed. [COUNSEL: confirm serious adverse-event reporting allocation and the required
   reporting workflow, including MedWatch submission responsibility and timelines.]

## 8. What Xenios will not do

Xenios will not provide diagnosis, treatment, dosing, or medication direction in response to
any report. Xenios will not discourage you from seeking care, from reporting directly to FDA
(MedWatch), or from contacting any regulator. Reporting to Xenios does not limit any right
you have.

## 9. Legal posture

These instructions do not limit rights you have under applicable law and do not relieve
Xenios of duties imposed by law, including complaint-handling, recordkeeping, and
adverse-event obligations. Submitting a report is not an admission by you, and receiving one
is not an admission by Xenios.
