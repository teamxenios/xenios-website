## 1. Purpose

This acknowledgment records that you understand what a dietary supplement is, what Xenios does
and does not claim about supplements, and where questions about your own health belong. It is
presented once, before your first supplement purchase, and again if it changes materially.

## 2. What a dietary supplement is

A dietary supplement is a product taken by mouth that contains a dietary ingredient, such as a
vitamin, mineral, amino acid, or herb, intended to supplement the diet. A supplement is not a
drug. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease, and Xenios does not
present any supplement as doing so.

## 3. The FDA evaluation disclaimer

Where Xenios or a supplement label makes a structure/function claim (a statement about how a
nutrient or ingredient affects the normal structure or function of the body), federal law
requires a disclaimer stating that the claim has not been evaluated by the Food and Drug
Administration and that the product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any
disease. [COUNSEL: confirm the exact required wording and placement of this disclaimer on
product pages, Guides, and checkout, and whether Xenios as a reseller carries an independent
disclosure obligation beyond the manufacturer's label.]

Xenios does not state or imply that any supplement is FDA approved. FDA does not approve
dietary supplements before sale.

## 4. Claims boundaries

1. Xenios maintains a claims registry. Product pages, Guides, plans, Telegram answers, and
   affiliate content are intended to stay within the allowed claims recorded for each product.
2. Structure/function statements are the outer boundary. No Xenios material may make a disease
   claim (a claim to diagnose, treat, cure, mitigate, or prevent disease) about a supplement.
3. Nothing guarantees outcomes. Individual results vary, and no result is promised.

## 5. Labels, allergens, and duplication

1. Read the label. The manufacturer's label and Supplement Facts panel are the authoritative
   statement of ingredients, amounts, and allergens for each product. Xenios shows allergen
   information where the brand provides it, but the physical label controls.
2. Allergies are your disclosure. The mandatory assessment asks about allergies and
   restrictions. Keep those answers current so plans and Foundation recommendations can account
   for them. Xenios cannot account for what it does not know.
3. Duplication is screened, not guaranteed. Xenios's catalog rules are designed to avoid
   recommending unnecessary duplicates (for example two multivitamins, two omega-3 products,
   two magnesium products, or overlapping sleep formulas) without an explicit reason. You
   remain responsible for anything you take from outside Xenios.
4. Some products are conditional. Iron, high-dose vitamin D, melatonin, and hormone-interest
   herbs may be hidden or require review, and pregnancy, breastfeeding, medication
   interactions, athlete drug-testing exposure, and allergen concerns can each make a product
   inappropriate for you even when it is generally available.

## 6. Interactions belong with your own professional

Supplements can interact with medications and medical conditions. Xenios does not diagnose,
prescribe, or provide medication direction, and no Xenios plan, Guide, or answer is medical
advice. Before starting any supplement, and before combining supplements with any medication
or condition, consult your own physician, pharmacist, or qualified health professional. If you
are pregnant, breastfeeding, or subject to athlete drug testing, do so before any purchase.

## 7. Sourcing and reseller authorization

Xenios sells only supplements it is authorized in writing to resell. The initial preferred
brands are Momentous and Pure Encapsulations. No supplement is published for sale until
written reseller authorization, content and image rights, pricing rules, fulfillment, returns,
and allowed claims are confirmed for that product. This is intended to ensure that what you
buy through Xenios is authentic brand product.

## 8. Reporting problems

If a supplement arrives damaged, looks wrong, or you experience a possible adverse event,
follow the Product Concern and Adverse Event Instructions (XR-COM-019). In an emergency, call
911 first. Xenios will respond to reports without giving medical advice.

## 9. What this acknowledgment does not do

This acknowledgment does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it
does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law, including duties about product authenticity,
truthful claims, and required adverse-event handling. It does not make any Xenios material
medical advice, and it does not shift Xenios's own legal obligations to you.

## 10. Your acknowledgment

By accepting, you confirm each of the following:

1. I understand that dietary supplements are not drugs and are not intended to diagnose,
   treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
2. I understand that structure/function claims have not been evaluated by the FDA and that no
   supplement is presented as FDA approved.
3. I will read product labels, including Supplement Facts and allergen information, before use.
4. I will consult my own physician, pharmacist, or qualified professional about interactions
   with my medications and conditions, and before use during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
5. I have provided accurate allergy and restriction information in my assessment and will keep
   it current.
6. I understand no outcome is guaranteed.
7. I understand how to report a product concern or possible adverse event, and that
   emergencies go to emergency services (911 in the US).
