## 1. Scope

This policy states what a member receives when a delivery issue is verified under the Damage,
Loss, Incorrect Item, and Temperature Concern Policy (XR-COM-008). It applies to product orders
only. Membership fees, membership cancellation, and any membership-related refund questions are
governed by the membership documents, not this policy. Ordinary returns do not exist in this
program (see the No Ordinary Returns Policy, XR-COM-007), so this policy is the only route to a
refund or replacement for a delivered-product issue. Refunds for cancelled, declined, reduced,
or legally delayed orders are governed by the Product Order Terms (XR-COM-001), the large-order
review and payment documents (XR-COM-010, XR-COM-011), and applicable law.

## 2. What a verified issue receives

A verified issue receives one of the following, matched to what actually went wrong:

- Full replacement: the affected item or items are shipped again from verified inventory, at no
  additional product or shipping charge.
- Full refund: the price paid for the affected item or items is refunded. Where an entire order
  is affected (for example, a lost single-shipment order), the refund includes the order's
  shipping charge.
- Partial replacement or partial refund: where only part of an order or shipment is affected,
  the remedy covers the affected part. The unaffected part of the order stands.

Where more than one remedy fits, Xenios offers the member the choice. If a replacement is not
possible (for example, the lot is exhausted or the product is unavailable), the remedy is a
refund. Xenios may also offer store credit as an additional option, but store credit is never
imposed in place of a refund the member is entitled to. [COUNSEL: confirm the store credit
option and its wording against processor rules and applicable law.]

## 3. Nothing is restocked

No returned, damaged, temperature-affected, or otherwise compromised product re-enters sellable
inventory, ever, regardless of appearance. Replacements come from verified inventory under lot
control. Compromised product receives a documented disposition and is destroyed where required.
Members never bear the cost of that destruction, and no fee is deducted from a refund or
replacement under this policy.

## 4. How refunds are paid

- Refunds are issued to the original payment method used for the order.
- If the original payment method no longer exists (for example, a closed card account), Xenios
  works with the payment processor's supported path for that situation, and with the member if
  information is needed. [COUNSEL: confirm the fallback method when the original payment method
  is unavailable.]
- Refunds are never paid in cash, and no one is ever asked to pay anything to receive a refund.
  Research Reps and affiliates cannot collect or pay refunds personally; refunds move only
  through the payment processor.

## 5. Timing

- Xenios initiates an approved refund within [CONFIG: refund initiation target, working default
  5 business days] of the resolution decision.
- After initiation, the time for funds to appear depends on the payment processor and the
  member's bank or card issuer, which are outside Xenios's control. Typical processor-to-bank
  settlement windows apply.
- Replacements enter fulfillment under the normal shipping flow of the Shipping Policy
  (XR-COM-004) and follow its timing rules, including split-shipment behavior where applicable.
- Where a refund is required by law within a specific period (for example, under federal rules
  on delayed or unfilled orders), that legal deadline controls over the targets above.

## 6. Effect on referrals, credits, and commissions

A refund reduces the "net collected revenue" that referral store credits and any affiliate
compensation are calculated from. Refunded amounts are excluded or clawed back from those
calculations under the referral and distribution documents. Store credit used on a refunded
order is restored as store credit, not converted to cash.

## 7. Chargebacks

Members are asked to use the issue process (XR-COM-008) before disputing a charge, because it
is usually faster and preserves the evidence needed to fix the problem. Nothing in this policy
waives or conditions a member's chargeback rights under card network rules or applicable law.

## 8. Final policy pending review

This policy is a launch draft. Its final form requires review by counsel, the payment
processor, the insurer, and the fulfillment partner, and it remains subject to applicable law
in every jurisdiction where members reside. This policy does not waive rights that cannot be
waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law.
