## 1. Purpose and scope

This SOP defines how a Research Rep becomes certified, how certification is recorded, when it must be renewed, and what happens when it lapses. A Research Rep is a trained independent sales representative for private communities and direct relationships. Certification is the gate between the Approved status and the Certified status in the program status flow (Applicant, Approved, Certified, Active, Quality Review, Suspended, Terminated). No rep represents Xenios, earns commission-bearing attribution, or runs events before certification is current.

## 2. Prerequisites

Before training begins, the rep must have completed program approval under the distribution program master: application (legal name, business or entity, contact, channels, audience, expected activity, prior regulated-product experience, conflicts, tax form, payment method) and verification (identity, age, business, tax, sanctions where appropriate, social and channel review, prior claims review), and must have signed the Research Rep Agreement (XR-AFF-003) and the Code of Conduct (XR-AFF-004).

## 3. The 14 training modules

The curriculum is the 14 canon modules. Each module states what the rep must be able to do, not just read:

1. Xenios membership: the private, application-based, 21+ program; the activation order (approval, identity and age verification, agreements, payment, MFA); what a rep may and may not say about admission.
2. Privacy and sensitive data: reps never store health information; no member or applicant data leaves approved systems; what a rep may see (aggregate leads, status categories, eligible conversions, commission, compliance tasks) and never sees (applicant health data, rejection reasons).
3. Approved products and lanes: catalog and commerce states; peptide and Quantum commission lanes are disabled until approved; supplements gated on reseller authorization; no promotion of a disabled lane.
4. FTC endorsement disclosures: clear and conspicuous disclosure of the paid relationship, per platform (XR-AFF-008).
5. Health-claims restrictions: the prohibited claims list, including guaranteed outcomes, cure or treat, "safe for everyone", "FDA approved" or "clinically proven" when not true, and why "research use only" is never a loophole for human-outcome promises (XR-AFF-009).
6. No diagnosis or dosing: reps never diagnose, prescribe, recommend dosing, or guarantee results; how to decline these questions and relay them to Xenios.
7. Telegram and lead handling: how questions and leads flow; Telegram is never a system of record; no passwords, ID documents, health media, or payment data over Telegram.
8. Application boundaries: reps help applicants navigate non-sensitive steps only; reps never handle passwords or IDs, never create or complete accounts, never collect payments personally.
9. Product concerns and adverse events: recognize a product concern or adverse event report, capture nothing sensitive, and route it to Xenios immediately through the approved channel.
10. Referral fraud: the fraud signal catalog (self-referrals, duplicate identities, circular orders, refunded conversion farming, code stuffing, fake leads, rep-created accounts) and consequences (XR-POL-028).
11. Brand and content: approved assets, links, QR codes, templates, and the ban on unapproved ads (XR-AFF-009, XR-AFF-010).
12. Security: account hygiene, MFA on rep accounts, phishing, and reporting suspected compromise.
13. Returns and shipping: the no-ordinary-returns rule, verified claim types, split shipments, and what reps may accurately say about shipping; reps never promise delivery dates.
14. Organization sales: organization partner structure, the disclosed direct-attribution override, event rules (XR-AFF-013), and the anti-MLM hard rules (no pay for recruiting, no unlimited downlines, no forced inventory purchases, rank never primarily recruitment-based).

Module content is versioned. The version each rep completed is recorded.

## 4. Assessment

- Certification requires passing an assessment covering all 14 modules, weighted toward the safety-critical ones (modules 2, 5, 6, 8, 9).

- Format includes scenario questions (for example, a prospect asks about dosing; a prospect describes a symptom) where the only passing answers decline and route correctly.
- Retakes: up to [CONFIG: permitted retakes] after a waiting period of [CONFIG: retake waiting period]; repeated failure returns the rep to Approved status pending Samuel's decision.
- Integrity: assessments are completed by the named rep personally; a rep who has someone else complete it is terminated under XR-AFF-014 (identity misuse).

## 5. Certification record

For each certification, record: rep identity reference, modules completed with timestamps and content versions, assessment score and scenario results, certifying decision and date, certificate ID, and expiry date. Samuel certifies each rep personally during the founding phase. The record is the source of truth for the Certified status; status changes are logged and auditable.

## 6. Recertification

- Standard interval: certification expires [CONFIG: recertification interval, recommended every 12 months].
- Triggered recertification, regardless of interval, when: the prohibited claims list materially changes, a product lane opens or closes (for example a peptide commission lane ever being enabled), FTC disclosure requirements change, or the rep returns from suspension.
- Targeted retraining: a compliance finding under the Affiliate Compliance Monitoring SOP (XR-POL-029) may require retaking specific modules and the related assessment sections before holds release.
- Recertification uses the current module versions and the full assessment unless Samuel approves a delta assessment for a minor content change.

## 7. Lapse and suspension

- A rep whose certification passes its expiry date is automatically moved from Active to Suspended for lapsed certification. This is enforced by status, not by reminder: an uncertified rep cannot hold Active status.
- While suspended for lapse: attribution links are disabled, no new commissions accrue, event participation stops, and existing unpaid commissions sit in held state until recertification or a Samuel decision.
- Grace: reminders are sent at [CONFIG: reminder schedule before expiry, recommended 30 and 7 days]; there is no post-expiry grace period unless Samuel grants one in writing.
- A rep who remains lapsed for more than [CONFIG: lapse termination window] may be terminated under XR-AFF-014.

## 8. Records

Training completions, assessment artifacts, certification records, recertification history, lapse events, and related decisions are retained under XR-POL-005. Quarterly, assessment miss patterns feed back into module content and the approved FAQ so the training fixes what reps actually get wrong.
