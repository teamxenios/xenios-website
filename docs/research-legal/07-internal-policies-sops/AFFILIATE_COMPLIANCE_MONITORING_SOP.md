## 1. Purpose and scope

This SOP defines how Xenios monitors what affiliates, Research Reps, and organization partners actually publish and say, and what happens when their content breaks the rules. The distribution program only works if untrained or careless partners cannot make health claims in Xenios's name. The substantive rules live in the Claims and Content Policy (XR-AFF-009), the FTC Disclosure Policy (XR-AFF-008), the Social Media Policy (XR-AFF-010), and the Event and Community Outreach Rules (XR-AFF-013). This SOP is the internal procedure that checks compliance with them.

## 2. What is monitored

- Published content: social posts, videos, landing pages, email sends, and event materials that use partner links, codes, or Xenios brand assets.
- Paid placement: search ads (misleading paid search is prohibited) and coupon or deal sites where prohibited.
- Testimonials: a testimonial cannot make a claim Xenios could not make directly.
- Disclosures: every affiliate relationship must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously under FTC endorsement rules, in a form suited to the platform.
- Outreach conduct: email, call, text, and Telegram outreach against the consent, opt-out, quiet-hours, and Do Not Call requirements, and the ban on purchased health-condition lists.
- Boundary conduct: any sign a rep is diagnosing, prescribing, recommending dosing, guaranteeing results, handling passwords or IDs, collecting payments personally, storing health information, or running unapproved ads.

## 3. Monitoring cadence

- New partners: review all published program content within the first [CONFIG: new-partner review window, recommended 30 days] after first publication, before the partner leaves enhanced review.
- Ongoing: sample each active partner's content at least [CONFIG: routine monitoring cadence, recommended quarterly], weighted toward partners with prior findings, high conversion volume, or health-adjacent audiences.
- Disclosure spot checks: each cycle, check a random sample of live posts and pages per partner for a clear, conspicuous, correctly placed disclosure. A missing or buried disclosure is a finding even if the content is otherwise clean.
- Event-triggered: a member complaint, a fraud case (XR-POL-028), a processor or platform notice, or any staff sighting opens an immediate review regardless of cadence.
- Record every review performed, including clean ones.

## 4. Claim violation handling

Findings are handled by severity:

- Severity 1 (immediate suspension per the program canon): safety claims, dosing or administration guidance, diagnosis or prescribing conduct, disease claims (cure, treat), "FDA approved" or "clinically proven" statements when not true, "research use only" language paired with human-outcome promises, privacy breach, identity misuse, unauthorized resale, or unapproved product representation. Action: suspend the partner immediately under XR-AFF-014, demand takedown, hold all unpaid commissions, and open a case. Peptide and Quantum promotion is a Severity 1 finding while those commission lanes remain disabled.
- Severity 2 (correct and warn): missing or inadequate FTC disclosure, unapproved but non-health marketing copy, brand asset misuse, outreach outside quiet hours. Action: written correction notice with a takedown or fix deadline of [CONFIG: correction deadline, recommended 72 hours], commission hold on the affected campaign until cured, recorded warning. A repeat Severity 2 finding within [CONFIG: repeat window] escalates to Severity 1 handling.
- Severity 3 (coach): style or template drift with no claim, disclosure, or privacy impact. Action: documented guidance, no hold.

Sales volume never offsets a violation. Takedown compliance is verified, not assumed: the reviewer re-checks the content after the deadline and records the result.

## 5. Commission holds

A confirmed Severity 1 or uncured Severity 2 finding moves the affected commissions to held in the ledger (states: pending, held, approved, paid, reversed, disputed). Commissions earned through violating content may be reversed under the partner agreement; disputes follow the Commission Dispute Policy. Holds release when the finding is cured and Samuel approves release.

## 6. Escalation and decisions

Reviewers document findings and proposed severity. Samuel decides every suspension, termination, commission reversal, and every Severity 1 case personally during the founding phase. Certification consequences (retraining or recertification before reinstatement) route through the Research Rep Training and Certification SOP (XR-POL-030). Money-movement fraud discovered during content review hands off to XR-POL-028.

## 7. Records of review

Each review record retains: partner, date, reviewer, content sampled (with captures or archived copies, since content gets deleted), checklist results, findings and severity, partner communications, cure verification, holds and releases, and final decision, under XR-POL-005. Quarterly, Samuel reviews aggregate findings to tune training modules, approved content, and the prohibited claims list.
