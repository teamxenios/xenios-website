## 1. Purpose and scope

This SOP governs every claim Xenios Research makes, in every channel. A claim is any statement, image, name, or implication that a reasonable person could read as saying what a product or service is, does, or achieves. This includes what Xenios says directly and what it allows others (affiliates, Research Reps, organization partners, testimonial givers) to say on its behalf.

The controlling rule: no claim appears anywhere until it has a claims registry entry marked allowed for that channel, with a named reviewer and a current review date.

## 2. The claims registry

The claims registry is the single system of record for claims. Every entry carries exactly these fields:

```text
claim
product
channel
evidence
allowed
prohibited
reviewer
review date
```

Field meanings:

- claim: the exact wording, image description, or implication being approved. Approval attaches to this wording; changing the wording creates a new entry.
- product: the SKU or program element the claim attaches to, or "program" for membership-level statements.
- channel: where the claim may appear (website, product page, Guide, plan document, email, Telegram reply, social, affiliate content, event material). A claim allowed in one channel is not allowed in another without its own entry.
- evidence: the evidence grade (Section 5) and the traceable sources behind it.
- allowed: the channels and contexts where the claim may run.
- prohibited: known misreadings and adjacent statements that must not run, recorded so drafters do not drift into them.
- reviewer: the named human who approved the entry.
- review date: when the entry was last reviewed. An entry past its review date is treated as not approved until re-reviewed.

## 3. Who may approve

1. During the founding phase, Samuel Boadu is the only person who may approve a claims registry entry. Approval is recorded with his name and date. Model output, drafting tools, and staff suggestions never approve a claim.
2. Scientific, quality, legal, or regulatory reviewers are added where the subject requires them; their review is recorded, but final approval remains with the founder. 3. No self-approval loophole exists for "small" edits: a reworded claim is a new claim.

## 4. What counts as a claim (express and implied)

All of the following are claims and require registry entries before use:

1. Express statements: any sentence about what a product or program does.
2. Implied claims: product names, blend names, system names, and category labels that suggest an effect. A name that implies an outcome is reviewed as a claim, not as branding.
3. Imagery: photos, before-and-after presentations, charts, and visual juxtapositions that imply results. Before-and-after imagery for any product is treated as a results claim.
4. Testimonials and member quotes: reviewed both for what they say and what they imply. A testimonial may not carry a claim Xenios itself could not make. Atypical experiences may not be presented as typical. FTC endorsement rules apply, including disclosure of any material connection.
5. Influencer, affiliate, and Research Rep content: same standard, plus the affiliate rules in Section 7.
6. Guides and educational content: factual statements in Guides are graded under XR-POL-015; any Guide statement used in a selling context becomes a claim under this SOP.
7. Recommendations and plan language: what Xenios 30, Xenios 90, and Q&A responses say about products or expected results.

## 5. Evidence grading

Claims are graded, not products. A product has no global grade; each claim about it carries its own evidence grade:

```text
A Established
B Supported Human
C Early Human
D Preclinical
E Supplier Reported
F Traditional
G Unverified
PROHIBITED
```

Rules:

1. The grade describes the evidence for the specific claim wording, per XR-POL-015.
2. A claim graded PROHIBITED never appears in any channel, under any disclaimer, in any voice. Disclaimers do not launder claims.
3. Which grades may support member-facing statements depends on the product's lane (Section 6) and is a counsel decision. [COUNSEL: confirm the minimum evidence grade permitted per lane and channel, and whether grades D through G may ever appear in a commercial context rather than an educational one.]
4. Evidence for individual ingredients never proves a blend. A blend claim requires its own entry and its own evidence.

## 6. Lane consistency

Every SKU has (or awaits) a written classification memorandum assigning its lane (see XR-POL-016). Every claim must match the lane of the product it attaches to. The website, recommendations, testimonials, Guides, affiliate content, and Telegram messages must all match the lane; a single channel out of lane puts the whole presentation out of lane.

Hard limits in every lane:

1. Peptide products are described as research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review. A research-use label is not a loophole: research-use language is never paired with human-outcome promises.
2. Never "FDA approved", never "clinically proven", never "safe for everyone".
3. No diagnosis, no prescribing, no dosing instructions, no medication direction, in any channel including Telegram replies and Q&A.
4. No outcome guarantees and no "results" promises, consistent with XR-MEM-007.
5. Supplement structure/function claims run only after counsel confirms the wording and any required disclaimer for that lane. [COUNSEL: approve the standard structure/function disclaimer text and its placement rules.]

## 7. Affiliates never exceed Xenios claims

1. Affiliates, Research Reps, and partners may use only claims whose registry entry marks their channel as allowed. Xenios publishes the approved list; everything not on it is prohibited for them.
2. An affiliate may never say what Xenios cannot say. The registry is the ceiling for everyone who speaks about the program.
3. Every affiliate relationship is clearly disclosed under the FTC Disclosure Policy (XR-AFF-008). Claims and content duties, monitoring, and takedown follow XR-AFF-009.
4. A registry withdrawal (Section 8) obligates affiliates to remove the claim on notice; the timing and process live in XR-AFF-009.

## 8. Change, correction, and withdrawal

1. Re-review triggers: the review date arriving, new evidence, a supplier or formulation change, a lane decision, a regulator or counsel communication, or a complaint.
2. When a claim is withdrawn: it is marked prohibited in the registry with the reason and date; it is removed from every Xenios channel; affiliates are notified; cached and printed material is inventoried for correction.
3. If a withdrawn claim was material to a purchase decision, the correction is escalated to the founder to decide member communication. [COUNSEL: advise when a claim correction requires proactive member notice or a remedy.]

## 9. Records

Every registry entry, approval, re-review, and withdrawal is recorded with actor and time and retained per XR-POL-005. The registry must be able to answer, for any past date: what was Xenios allowed to say, where, and on what evidence. Corrections supersede prior entries without erasing them.
