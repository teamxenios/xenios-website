## 1. What this document covers

These terms explain the monthly check-in and Review Week: how your plan refreshes each month, how the review timeline works, and what is asked of you. "Xenios" means [ENTITY], operating the Xenios Research Founding Membership. The check-in produces each month's Xenios 30 (XR-MEM-015) and keeps your Xenios 90 roadmap (XR-MEM-016) on course.

## 2. Review Week

1. Your first Review Week occurs after you complete your first 30-day plan.
2. All continuing members review during the same week each month. One shared week keeps review quality high and turnaround predictable during the founding phase.
3. Within Review Week, the platform may assign you a recommended weekday cohort (for example, a suggested day to submit your check-in) to spread reviews across the week. Cohort assignment is a scheduling recommendation, not a restriction on your access. [CONFIG: cohort assignment rules and whether a member can pick a different day within the week.]

## 3. The monthly flow

Each month follows the same sequence:

```text
Check-in
-> comparison
-> preliminary next plan
-> Samuel review
-> publish within 48 elapsed hours
-> email and Telegram notification
-> member acknowledgment
```

Step by step:

1. Check-in. You answer a short structured check-in about the month: adherence, how the plan felt, changes in your context, and anything that needs attention.
2. Comparison. Xenios compares the month against your prior period using your tracker data and check-in answers. Data completeness is shown separately, so a thin month of logging is visible as thin data, not silently treated as poor progress.
3. Preliminary next plan. A preliminary next-month package is generated: Xenios 30, fitness PDF, and nutrition PDF, aligned to your Xenios 90 phase.
4. Samuel review. During the founding phase, Samuel personally reviews every monthly package before publication.
5. Publication within 48 elapsed hours. The review commitment is 48 elapsed hours, including weekends, from when the preliminary package enters review. This is a commitment target: a monitored service standard Xenios is built to meet, not a contractual guarantee, and missing it in an individual case does not by itself create a right to damages, credit, or refund except where applicable law provides otherwise.
6. Notification. When your documents publish, you receive an email with a secure link, and optionally a Telegram notification. Telegram is never the system of record and plan PDFs are not sent through it.
7. Acknowledgment. You acknowledge each new document in the Document Center. Acknowledgment confirms receipt, not agreement with every recommendation, and it waives nothing. Acknowledgment records are kept with version and timestamp.

## 4. What the comparison is, and is not

The comparison is a planning tool. It looks at adherence, tracker metrics, and your own reports to decide what next month should look like. It is not a diagnosis, not a clinical assessment, and not a judgment of you. Where the best decision is to keep the plan as it is, the next month's package may remain materially unchanged (see XR-MEM-015, section 5).

## 5. If you miss a check-in

Reminders continue until your check-in is complete. If Review Week passes without your check-in, the intended design is that your next plan is prepared from the information available, or held unchanged, until you check in. ## 6. Early changes between check-ins

If something needs to change before your next Review Week, you may use your early plan-change request under the Early Plan-Change Terms (XR-MEM-020): one each month at no extra charge.

## 7. Your part

- Complete the check-in during your Review Week.
- Log tracker metrics through the month to the extent you can; more complete data makes a better next plan.
- Keep your allergies, restrictions, health context, and goals current. Xenios relies on your disclosures and does not independently verify them.

## 8. Limits and safety

1. The check-in, the comparison, and the resulting plans are not medical care, diagnosis, treatment, or a prescription, and they contain no medication direction or dosing instructions.
2. The fitness and nutrition components are governed by XR-MEM-017 and XR-MEM-018, including their stop-if-symptoms guidance.
3. No outcome is guaranteed. Comparisons describe your data; they do not promise results.
4. Emergencies go to emergency services (911 in the US). The check-in and Telegram support are not emergency channels, and emergency language in support routes you to emergency services.

These terms do not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and they do not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law.

## 9. Cancellation

If you cancel your membership, access ends immediately, including the check-in flow and the Document Center, and remaining paid time is forfeited, with no prorated refund unless applicable law requires one. This policy is subject to applicable law and counsel review. Download the documents you want to keep before you confirm cancellation. Cancellation does not erase records Xenios must keep, and your privacy rights survive cancellation.
