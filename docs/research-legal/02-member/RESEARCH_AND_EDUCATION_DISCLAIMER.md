## 1. Purpose

This disclaimer explains what Xenios Research materials are and what they are not. It applies
to every Guide, Blueprint, Xenios 30 plan, Xenios 90 roadmap, fitness PDF, nutrition PDF,
tracker feature, product page, and Telegram answer.

## 2. Materials are educational

1. Guides, plans, and materials are educational. They organize information, explain concepts
   in plain language, and suggest structured starting points. They are written for a general
   member audience, then adjusted using what you disclose in your assessment.
2. Personalization is not medical care. A plan built from your assessment answers is still an
   educational document. The mandatory assessment is not an automated diagnosis or
   prescription, and the tracker provides no diagnosis, dosing, or medication direction.
3. A named human reviews plans. During the founding phase Samuel Boadu reviews every initial
   and monthly plan before publication (see XR-MEM-013). That review is editorial and
   organizational. It is not a medical review, and Samuel is not acting as your clinician.

## 3. Not medical advice

1. Nothing in Xenios Research is medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
2. Xenios does not diagnose conditions, prescribe anything, give dosing instructions, or
   direct the use of medication, anywhere, including in Guides, plans, product pages, and
   Telegram answers.
3. Your own physician or qualified health professional is the right source for medical
   decisions. Consult them before acting on any material, especially where a condition or a
   medication is involved.
4. The membership, the tracker, and Telegram are not an emergency service. Emergencies go to
   emergency services (911 in the United States).

## 4. Research products and classification

1. Peptide products in the member catalog are research products whose classification and
   permitted marketing lane are under formal review. Every SKU receives a written
   classification memorandum before its commerce lane is enabled.
2. This disclaimer does not control classification. Product classification, permitted claims,
   quality requirements, and professional oversight are governed by law and by the actual
   presentation of the product, not by disclaimers. A research-use label is not a loophole,
   and Xenios does not pair research-use language with human-outcome promises.
3. No product is represented as "FDA approved", "clinically proven", or "safe for everyone".
4. Catalog visibility is not a legal conclusion. A product's appearance in the catalog, its
   state label, and its Guide status describe the program's own workflow, not a regulatory
   determination (see the Product Access Policy, XR-MEM-010).

## 5. Guides specifically

1. The Guide library is member-only. Every individual compound and offered blend receives a
   Guide.
2. Guides summarize published information and program context for education. They do not
   instruct use, do not provide dosing, and do not promise outcomes.
3. Guide status labels (Published, Updated, In Review, In Development, Coming Soon) describe
   editorial state only.

## 6. What this disclaimer does not do

This disclaimer does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does
not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law, including duties about lawful product
classification, truthful claims, product quality, privacy, and security.
