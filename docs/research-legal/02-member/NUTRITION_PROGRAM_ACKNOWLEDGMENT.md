## 1. What you are acknowledging

This acknowledgment covers the nutrition programs, nutrition PDFs, and food-related guidance delivered through your Xenios Research membership, including the nutrition components of your Whole-Life Blueprint (XR-MEM-014), Xenios 30 (XR-MEM-015), and Xenios 90 (XR-MEM-016). "Xenios" means [ENTITY].

By accepting, you confirm you have read and understood the statements below. This acknowledgment does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law.

## 2. This is education, not medical nutrition therapy

1. Your nutrition PDFs and guidance are general nutrition education, organized around the goals and preferences you disclosed. They are not medical nutrition therapy, not dietetics services, not diagnosis, and not treatment of any disease or condition.
2. Xenios does not hold itself out as a licensed dietitian or nutritionist practice. Some states restrict who may provide individualized nutrition counseling and what it may be called. [COUNSEL: confirm state licensure boundaries for individualized nutrition guidance, the states where this framing is insufficient, and any required wording or scope changes.]
3. Nothing in a nutrition plan diagnoses a condition, prescribes anything, or directs medication or dosing.
4. No nutrition outcome is guaranteed.

## 3. Your allergies and restrictions are member-disclosed

Your plans are built around the allergies, intolerances, and dietary restrictions you disclose in your assessment and afterward. Understand what that means:

- Xenios relies on your disclosures and does not independently verify them.
- If you do not disclose a restriction, your plan cannot account for it.
- If your allergies or restrictions change, update them promptly. A plan built on stale information can be wrong for you.
- You remain responsible for checking the label and ingredients of any food or product before you consume it. A plan referencing a food is not a certification that it is safe for you.

## 4. When to get your own professional's advice

Consult your own physician, registered dietitian, or other qualified professional before following a nutrition plan if any of these apply: diabetes or blood sugar disorders, kidney or liver disease, gastrointestinal disease, a history of disordered eating, pregnancy or nursing, food allergies with a history of severe reactions, or any condition managed with medication that interacts with diet. Xenios cannot evaluate these situations for you.

## 5. Stop if something goes wrong

If you have an adverse reaction to a food or dietary change, stop and seek care from a qualified professional. If you suspect a severe allergic reaction (trouble breathing, swelling of the face or throat, severe hives, faintness), treat it as an emergency and contact emergency services (911 in the US). Xenios channels, including Telegram support, are not emergency services.

## 6. Record of acceptance

Your acceptance is recorded server-side with a checkbox, timestamp, document version, and member reference. Because this is an acknowledgment that a disclosure was made to you, it is not withdrawable after the fact, but you may stop following any nutrition plan at any time, and you may cancel membership at any time.
