## 1. What the referral program is

Active members can refer people they know to apply for Xenios Research Founding Membership. Referrals are personal recommendations, not a sales role. If your referral becomes an active member, both of you receive store credit:

```text
$15 store credit to you (the existing member)
$10 store credit to the new member
after a 14-day hold
```

Referral rewards are store credit only. They are never cash, and they cannot be converted to cash.

## 2. How a referral works

1. You get a personal referral code or link from the referrals area.
2. Your referral uses it when applying at the private entrance. Attribution is recorded by the system at application time.
3. Every application goes through the same process: Samuel's review, identity and age verification (21+), agreements, activation payment, and MFA. A referral changes nothing about the standards for approval.
4. When the referred person completes activation, the reward enters a 14-day hold.
5. After the hold, if the conditions in section 4 are met, the credits are issued: $15 to your account, $10 to the new member's account.

## 3. What you can and cannot see

Applicant privacy is absolute. As a referrer you will never see:

- whether a specific person applied,
- the content of anyone's application,
- the reason any application was approved or not approved, or
- any applicant's or member's health, identity, or payment information.

At most, the referrals area shows you your own aggregate numbers: credits earned and credits pending. If the person you referred tells you about their application, that is their choice; Xenios will not.

## 4. The hold, and what voids a credit

The 14-day hold exists so credits are only issued for genuine, durable memberships. A pending credit is voided, and an issued credit may be reversed, if within the hold or a subsequent review Xenios finds:

- self-referral (referring yourself, your own alternate identity, or an account you control),
- duplicate or fabricated identities,
- circular orders or refund farming (activations or purchases made to harvest credits and then unwound),
- code stuffing or posting your code in ways the program prohibits, [CONFIG: the prohibited-placement list, for example coupon sites and paid search on Xenios terms]
- fake leads or applications submitted by anyone other than the genuine applicant, or
- membership cancellation or payment reversal by the referred member during the hold.

Fraud voids credits. Deliberate referral fraud is also grounds for suspension or termination of membership and may result in reversal of all related credits. [COUNSEL: confirm the reversal and termination cross-reference to the membership agreement]

## 6. Referrals are not the affiliate program

The referral program is for personal recommendations by members, rewarded in store credit. It pays nothing for recruiting, has no downline, and has no rank. If you want to promote Xenios publicly, at scale, or for monetary compensation, that is the affiliate and Research Rep program, which has its own application, training, approved-content rules, and FTC disclosure requirements. See XR-AFF-001 (Member Referral Terms, program level) and the affiliate program documents. If you are compensated for promoting Xenios in any public content, you must disclose the relationship clearly.

When you refer someone, speak plainly and honestly. Do not make health claims, promise results, or describe any product as approved, proven, or safe for everyone. You are recommending a private membership, not marketing products.

## 7. Program changes

Reward amounts ($15 and $10) and the 14-day hold are launch settings, admin-configurable, and changes apply prospectively with notice. A change never reduces a credit already issued.

## 8. What these terms are not

These terms do not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and they do not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law. Nothing here guarantees approval of any applicant or issuance of any credit outside the conditions above.
