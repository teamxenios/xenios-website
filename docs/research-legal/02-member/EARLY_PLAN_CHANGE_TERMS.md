## 1. What this document covers

These terms explain the early plan-change request: your ability to have your current plan revised between monthly Review Weeks. "Xenios" means [ENTITY], operating the Xenios Research Founding Membership. Plans normally refresh through the monthly check-in (XR-MEM-019); this document covers changes requested before that.

## 2. The core entitlement

You may request one early plan change each month at no extra charge.

- "Each month" means each plan month: the 30-day cycle of your current Xenios 30. [CONFIG: confirm plan-month versus calendar-month counting and how the counter resets at Review Week.]
- The request is included in your membership. Nothing additional is billed for it.
- An unused request does not accumulate. [CONFIG: confirm whether unused requests expire at Review Week or roll over; the intended design is that they expire.]

## 3. What counts as an early plan-change request

A plan-change request is a request to revise your current plan documents before your next Review Week, for example:

- adjusting your fitness plan (schedule, exercise selection, volume, or equipment constraints),
- adjusting your nutrition plan (foods, structure, or newly disclosed restrictions),
- adjusting Blueprint actions or the supplement Foundation, or
- rebalancing the month because your circumstances changed (travel, schedule, injury recovery guidance from your own professional, a goal change).

One request may bundle related adjustments made at the same time. The revised documents are published as new versions through the Document Center, replacing the current month's documents, with prior versions archived.

## 4. What does not count against your request

The following are not counted as your monthly plan-change request:

1. Questions and clarifications. Asking what something in your plan means, through Telegram support, is support, not a plan change.
2. Xenios errors. If a published document contains Xenios's own error (wrong member data, a restriction you disclosed that was missed, a broken document), the correction is free and does not consume your request.
3. Safety concerns. If something in your plan causes symptoms or a suspected reaction, that is a safety matter, not a plan-change request. Stop first, follow the stop-if-symptoms guidance in XR-MEM-017 and XR-MEM-018, and seek care where needed; emergencies go to emergency services (911 in the US). Xenios handles safety-driven plan corrections without counting them against your request.
4. The Review Week refresh itself (XR-MEM-019).

## 5. How a request works

1. Submit the request through the member site plan-change flow [CONFIG: request channel; whether Telegram requests are accepted and logged into the same flow].
2. Describe what needs to change and why. The more specific the request, the better the revision.
3. During the founding phase, Samuel personally reviews the revision, under the same 48 elapsed hour commitment target that governs plan publication (XR-MEM-014, XR-MEM-019). The target is a monitored service standard, not a contractual guarantee.
4. If more information is needed, the revision is held and the specific questions are sent to you.
5. The revised documents publish to the Document Center with email notification and optional Telegram notification, and you acknowledge them like any other plan documents.

You may withdraw a pending request before it is fulfilled; a withdrawn request is not counted. [CONFIG: confirm withdrawal handling in the request flow.]

## 7. Limits

1. A plan change revises education and planning documents. It is not medical care, diagnosis, treatment, or a prescription, and no revision will contain medication direction or dosing instructions.
2. A plan-change request is not a product order, a refund request, or a subscription change. Those follow their own terms and flows.
3. A revision may conclude that the best decision is no material change (XR-MEM-015, section 5). A reviewed "stay the course" response fulfills the request.
4. No outcome is guaranteed from any revision.

These terms do not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and they do not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law.

## 8. Changes to these terms

Xenios may revise these terms prospectively. Material changes are presented for fresh acceptance, with the version, presented text, and timestamp recorded. Changes to how additional requests are handled apply prospectively with notice.
