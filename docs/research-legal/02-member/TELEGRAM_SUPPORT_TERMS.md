## 1. What Telegram support is

Telegram is an optional support channel for active members. Once you link your Telegram account, you can send Xenios questions 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, as:

- text messages, or
- voice notes up to 60 seconds.

Intake is always open; answering is not instant. The normal response target is approximately 12 hours. This is a service target, not a guarantee, and it may be longer during high-volume periods. During the founding phase, answers are reviewed by Samuel Boadu personally.

Questions submitted over Telegram enter the same question system as questions submitted on the member site, with the same statuses and the same rules (see XR-MEM-024, Question and Answer Terms).

## 2. Linking and revoking your Telegram account

1. Linking starts from your member account area, never from an unsolicited message. You request a link, Xenios shows you a one-time code, and you send that code to the Xenios bot from your own Telegram account. The one-time code proves that the Telegram account and the member account belong to the same person.
2. One member account links to one Telegram account. [CONFIG: whether re-linking to a new Telegram account requires step-up authentication]
3. You can revoke the link at any time from the account area. Revocation stops Xenios from sending anything further to that Telegram account and stops messages from that account being treated as yours.
4. Xenios can revoke a link server-side, for example on suspected account compromise or membership termination.
5. If you lose control of your Telegram account, revoke the link immediately from the member site and report it (see XR-MEM-022, section 6).

## 3. The hard list: what never goes over Telegram

Telegram is a convenience channel, not a secure vault. The following never travel over Telegram, in either direction, even if you ask:

- passwords,
- password-reset links or reset tokens,
- identity documents or images of them,
- plan PDFs (Blueprint, fitness, nutrition, or any Document Center file),
- raw health media (progress photos, exercise videos, or their originals),
- payment card numbers or any payment data, and
- your detailed assessment answers.

If you send any of these anyway, Xenios will not act on them over Telegram, will direct you to the correct secure channel, and may delete the message from its side where the platform allows. Xenios staff will never ask for your password, recovery codes, or payment details over Telegram. A message that asks is not from Xenios.

## 4. Telegram is not the system of record

The member website and the secure Document Center are the system of record. Telegram notifications may tell you that a document, plan, or answer is ready, but the document itself lives in the Document Center and answers of record live in the question system. If a Telegram message and the member site ever disagree, the member site controls. Keep decisions and records inside the member site, not in a chat scrollback.

## 5. Queue conduct

- There are no public queue numbers. You will see the status of your own questions only.
- There is no self-mark-urgent option. Every question is triaged by Xenios. Writing "urgent" does not change triage, and section 6 governs anything that is a true emergency.
- Closed questions are not reopened. A follow-up on a completed question creates a new linked question, so the thread of record stays clean.
- You can rate answers. Ratings inform quality review; they do not change queue order.

## 6. Emergencies

Telegram support is not an emergency service, and it is not monitored in real time. If you have a medical or safety emergency, call 911 (or your local emergency number) immediately. Do not wait for a Telegram reply. If a message contains emergency language, the system's response directs you to emergency services; it does not attempt remote care. Support, plans, Guides, and the tracker are not medical care and do not diagnose, prescribe, or give dosing directions.

## 7. What Telegram itself is

Telegram is a third-party messaging platform that Xenios does not own or control. Your use of Telegram is governed by Telegram's own terms of service and privacy policy. Message metadata and content on Telegram's systems are handled under Telegram's practices, which is one reason for the hard list in section 3. Xenios keeps its own record of support conversations for quality, safety, and audit purposes, retained per the Retention and Deletion Schedule (XR-POL-005). Privacy rights over that record are exercised under XR-MEM-027.

## 8. Changes and termination of the channel

Xenios may change, suspend, or discontinue the Telegram channel with notice. Support by email at research@xeniostechnology.com remains available regardless. Membership cancellation revokes the Telegram link; the limited non-member workflow after cancellation runs by email, not Telegram.

## 9. What these terms are not

These terms do not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and they do not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law. Nothing in this document promises outcomes or guarantees a response time.
