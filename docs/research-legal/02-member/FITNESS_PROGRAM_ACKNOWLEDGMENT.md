## 1. What you are acknowledging

This acknowledgment covers the fitness programs, fitness PDFs, and exercise-related guidance delivered through your Xenios Research membership, including the fitness components of your Whole-Life Blueprint (XR-MEM-014), Xenios 30 (XR-MEM-015), and Xenios 90 (XR-MEM-016). "Xenios" means [ENTITY].

By accepting, you confirm you have read and understood the statements below. This acknowledgment does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law.

## 2. Exercise carries risk

Physical exercise carries real risk, including muscle and joint injury, strains, falls, and, rarely, serious cardiovascular events. That risk exists in any exercise program, including this one. Following a written plan does not remove it. You are responsible for exercising within your own limits, using safe form, appropriate loads, and appropriate equipment.

## 3. Stop if symptoms appear

Stop exercising immediately and seek medical attention if you experience any of the following during or after activity:

- chest pain or chest pressure,
- unusual or severe shortness of breath,
- dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting,
- an irregular or racing heartbeat,
- sharp, sudden, or worsening pain, or
- any symptom that feels wrong for your body.

If it may be an emergency, contact emergency services (911 in the US). Do not use Telegram support or any Xenios channel for emergencies; they are not emergency services and are not monitored for that purpose.

## 4. Get clearance from your own professional

Xenios recommends that you obtain clearance from your own physician or qualified health professional before starting or significantly changing an exercise program. This matters especially if you have a known heart, lung, metabolic, or joint condition, are new to exercise or returning after a long break, are pregnant or recently postpartum, or take medication that affects exercise tolerance. Xenios does not provide that clearance and cannot assess your fitness to exercise.

## 5. What the fitness plans are, and are not

1. Your fitness plans are general education, built from the information you disclosed in your assessment and afterward. They are not medical care, diagnosis, treatment, or a prescription.
2. The plans are not personal training supervision. No one from Xenios watches your sessions, checks your form, spots your lifts, or adjusts loads in real time. Execution decisions during a session are yours.
3. The plans rely on member-disclosed information. Xenios does not independently verify your health status. If your information is incomplete or out of date, the plan may not fit you. Keep it current.
4. No fitness outcome is guaranteed. Adherence targets are targets, not promised results.
5. Skipping, scaling, or substituting an exercise that does not feel right for you is always acceptable. Tell Xenios through support or a plan-change request (XR-MEM-020) so your plan can be adjusted.

## 6. Record of acceptance

Your acceptance is recorded server-side with a checkbox, timestamp, document version, and member reference. Because this is an acknowledgment that a disclosure was made to you, it is not withdrawable after the fact, but you may stop exercising under any plan at any time, and you may cancel membership at any time.
