## 1. What the Guide Library is

The Guide Library is the member-only collection of evidence Guides. A Guide is a written, referenced explainer for a single compound or an offered blend: what it is, what the research literature examines, what the evidence looks like, and what remains unknown. Every individual compound and every offered blend in the catalog receives a Guide. The full library is available only to active members; no Guide content is public.

Reading a Guide is never a prerequisite to purchasing an enabled product. Guides inform; they do not gate commerce. Product pages show each Guide's state so you always know what exists:

```text
Guide Published
Guide Updated
Guide In Review
Guide In Development
Guide Coming Soon
```

## 3. Evidence grading: claims are graded, not products

Each substantive claim inside a Guide carries an evidence grade. Understand exactly what a grade is:

1. A grade describes the strength and quality of the published evidence for one specific claim, for example the number, size, design, and consistency of studies examining it.
2. A grade is not a product rating. A high grade on one claim about a compound is not an endorsement of any product, not a safety assurance, and not a promise that you will experience that effect.
3. A grade is not permission. Product classification and the permitted marketing lane for each product are under formal review and are governed by law, not by evidence grades or disclaimers. Peptide products are research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review.
4. Grades change. New research can raise or lower a grade, and Guides are updated accordingly.

Guides never contain dosing instructions, diagnosis, or medication direction. Guides are education, not medical advice, and nothing in a Guide guarantees an outcome.

## 4. Corrections process

Guides are maintained documents, not static PDFs. The process:

1. Any member can report a suspected error from the Guide page or through a question (see XR-MEM-024).
2. Reports are reviewed against the underlying sources. During the founding phase, Samuel Boadu is the accountable reviewer.
3. A confirmed error produces a corrected version. Every Guide carries a version number, a reviewer, a published time, and a change note, consistent with the document controls in XR-MEM-021. Prior versions are archived, not erased.
4. Material corrections (ones that could change a reasonable member's understanding) are announced to members who follow the topic, and the Guide's product-page state shows Guide Updated.

Claims in Guides are also tracked in the internal claims registry, with reviewer and review date, so that what a Guide says stays consistent with what Xenios is permitted to say.

## 5. Topic requests and demand counts

- You can request a Guide topic and follow topics you care about.
- The library may display anonymous demand counts, meaning the number of members interested in a topic, never who they are.
- A request creates no commitment. Xenios decides what to publish, and a topic may be declined where the subject cannot be covered inside the permitted lane.
- Followers of a topic are notified when its Guide publishes or materially updates.

Your requests and follows are membership data, retained per the Retention and Deletion Schedule (XR-POL-005) and covered by your privacy rights under XR-MEM-027. They are never shown to other members in identifiable form and never sent to advertising platforms.
