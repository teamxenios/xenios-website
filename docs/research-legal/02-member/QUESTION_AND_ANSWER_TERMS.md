## 1. What the question service is

Active members can submit questions to Xenios at any time, from the member site or over a linked Telegram account (see XR-MEM-023). Questions can be about the membership, plans, products, Guides, orders, or the platform. Intake is open 24/7. The normal response target is approximately 12 hours; this is a target, not a guarantee. During the founding phase, Samuel Boadu personally reviews answers.

## 2. Question statuses

Every question moves through a defined set of statuses, visible to you for your own questions:

```text
Pending
Being Reviewed
More Information Needed
Answer Ready
Completed
```

- Pending: received and waiting for review.
- Being Reviewed: actively being worked on.
- More Information Needed: Xenios has asked you something and is waiting on your reply. The clock on the response target pauses while a question waits on you.
- Answer Ready: an answer has been published to you and is awaiting your reading or acknowledgment.
- Completed: the question is closed.

## 3. Queue rules

1. No public queue numbers. You see the status of your own questions only. You never see your position relative to other members, and other members never see yours.
2. No self-mark-urgent. There is no button or keyword that promotes your question. Xenios triages every question. Marking a message "urgent" does not change its handling; genuine emergencies are governed by section 5, not by the queue.
3. Closed questions create linked follow-ups. A completed question is not reopened. If you reply to a completed question, the system creates a new question linked to the old one, so context is preserved and the record of what was asked and answered stays intact.
4. Ratings. When a question completes, you can rate the answer. Ratings feed quality review. They do not affect queue position, and a low rating never reduces your access to the service.

## 4. Hard limits on answer content

Answers are educational and operational information about the program and its materials. They are not medical care. In every answer, on every channel:

- no diagnosis: Xenios will not tell you what condition you have or do not have,
- no prescribing: Xenios will not direct you to take, start, stop, or change any medication or product as treatment, and
- no dosing instructions: Xenios will not give amounts, schedules, or administration directions for any research product or medication.

Questions that ask for any of the above receive an answer that explains the boundary and, where appropriate, suggests speaking with a licensed clinician. Product classification, permitted claims, quality, and professional oversight are governed by law, not by disclaimers, and answers must stay inside the lane approved for each product. If your question suggests a possible product concern or adverse event, it is separated into the product-concern process and handled there, with its own record.

## 5. Emergencies

The question service is not an emergency service and is not monitored in real time. If you have a medical or safety emergency, call 911 (or your local emergency number). Do not submit an emergency as a question and wait. If a submitted question contains emergency language, the immediate response directs you to emergency services.

## 6. Records and privacy

Questions, answers, statuses, and ratings are recorded and retained per the Retention and Deletion Schedule (XR-POL-005). These records support quality, safety, and audit needs, and they help Xenios improve the Guides: recurring questions may inform new Guide topics in anonymized, aggregate form. Your questions are never published publicly with your identity, and question content is never sent to advertising platforms. Privacy rights over question records are exercised under XR-MEM-027.

## 7. What these terms are not

- These terms do not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and they do not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law.
- No answer creates a guarantee of results. Answers reflect the information available at the time and can be updated by later answers or corrected Guides.
- An answer is not a substitute for the judgment of a licensed professional who has examined you.
