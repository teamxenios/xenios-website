## 1. Purpose

Every affiliate relationship in the Xenios Research partner program must be disclosed clearly. This policy tells partners (affiliates, Research Reps, and organization partners) exactly how to disclose their connection to Xenios whenever they promote the program or its products. It is designed to satisfy the Federal Trade Commission's Endorsement Guides, which require that a "material connection" (a payment, commission, store credit, free product, event compensation, or any other benefit) be disclosed to the audience whenever it might affect how the audience weighs the endorsement.

This policy is a condition of the partner agreement. Promoting without the required disclosure is a compliance violation with the consequences in Section 8.

## 2. Who must disclose

Anyone with a material connection to Xenios must disclose it in every promotional communication:

- Affiliates earning a commission on links or codes.
- Research Reps earning commission or event bonuses.
- Organization partners and organization owners receiving a revenue share or override.
- Members sharing a referral code for store credit. The member-facing wording rules live in the Member Referral and Store Credit Terms (XR-MEM-026); the same clear-and-conspicuous standard applies.
- Anyone who received free product, payment, or any other benefit in exchange for talking about Xenios.

There is no exemption for small audiences, private groups, or "personal" accounts. If the message promotes Xenios and a benefit exists, disclose.

## 3. The core standard: clear and conspicuous

A disclosure counts only if a typical viewer actually notices and understands it. Xenios applies these tests:

1. Unavoidable. The disclosure appears where the audience cannot miss it. Not behind a "more" link, not in a profile bio, not on a separate page.
2. Same medium as the claim. A spoken endorsement gets a spoken disclosure. A visual post gets a visible disclosure. A video needs both audio and on-screen text.
3. Before or with the promotional message. The audience learns about the connection before they act on the endorsement, not after.
4. Plain language. "I earn a commission from Xenios" works. Vague labels such as "#sp", "#collab", "partner", or "ambassador" alone do not.
5. Every time. Disclosure is per post, per video, per message, per event. A disclosure last month does not cover today's post.

## 4. Placement rules by channel

| Channel | Required placement |
| --- | --- |
| Social feed post | First line of the caption, before any fold or "more" truncation |
| Story or short-form video | On-screen text on each frame or clip that promotes Xenios, in a size and contrast a viewer can read |
| Long-form video | Spoken disclosure at the start and at the first product mention, plus on-screen text at the same points |
| Live stream | Spoken disclosure at the start and repeated at reasonable intervals, so late joiners hear it |
| Podcast or audio | Spoken disclosure at the start of the Xenios segment |
| Blog, article, email | Disclosure at the top, before the first promotional content, not only at the bottom |
| Telegram, SMS, or direct message outreach | Disclosure inside the message itself, in the first message of the conversation |
| In-person or virtual event | Spoken disclosure at the start of the presentation, plus printed or on-slide disclosure on materials (see XR-AFF-013) |

Link-only disclosure (a bare "disclosure" hyperlink) is never sufficient on any channel.

## 5. Approved disclosure wording

Partners must use one of the approved wordings below, or wording pre-approved in writing by Xenios. Editing the meaning of an approved disclosure is prohibited.

- Affiliate: "I earn a commission if you join or buy through my link. I am an independent affiliate of Xenios Research."
- Research Rep: "I am a paid independent Research Rep for Xenios Research. I earn a commission on sales through my link."
- Organization partner: "[Organization name] is a paid partner of Xenios Research and receives a share of sales made through this program."
- Member referral: "I am a Xenios Research member. I receive store credit if you join through my referral."
- Free product or other benefit: "Xenios Research provided me this product for free." (Combine with the commission wording when both apply.)

Short-form addition: "#ad" may be added for platform convention, but it supplements the approved sentence; it does not replace it. Ambiguous tags alone ("#sp", "#partner", "#XeniosFam") never satisfy this policy.

## 6. Disclosure does not license claims

A disclosure makes the relationship honest. It does not make a claim lawful. Every statement in a disclosed post must still comply with the Claims and Content Policy (XR-AFF-009): no guaranteed outcomes, no cure or treat language, no dosing, no "FDA approved", no "clinically proven", and no research-use framing paired with human-outcome promises. Testimonials must reflect honest experience and cannot make claims Xenios could not make directly.

## 7. Monitoring

- Partners must list every channel they will use at application, and register new channels before using them.
- Xenios reviews partner content on a periodic and risk-triggered basis, and may use automated monitoring of registered channels.
- Xenios may require a partner to correct or remove a non-compliant post. The partner must comply within [CONFIG: correction window, hours] of notice.
- Monitoring records (what was reviewed, findings, corrections) are retained per XR-POL-005.

## 8. Consequences

Disclosure failures are handled on an escalating path, except that deliberate concealment is treated as a serious violation immediately:

1. First lapse: correction notice and required fix within the correction window.
2. Repeat lapse: mandatory retraining and a commission hold on affected sales while reviewed.
3. Pattern or deliberate concealment: suspension and investigation under the Suspension and Termination Policy (XR-AFF-014). Commissions attributable to non-compliant promotion may be reversed per XR-AFF-007.

No disclosure violation can be offset by sales volume. A partner who disagrees with a commission consequence may use the Commission Dispute Policy (XR-AFF-015).

## 9. Relationship to law

This policy is designed to meet FTC endorsement requirements, but compliance with this policy does not guarantee legal compliance in every situation, and this policy does not relieve any partner of duties imposed by law. Partners are independent and responsible for their own conduct. This document does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law and does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law.
