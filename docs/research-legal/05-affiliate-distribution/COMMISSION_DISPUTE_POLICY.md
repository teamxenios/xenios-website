## 1. Purpose

Commissions in this program move through defined states (pending, held, approved, paid, reversed, disputed) under the Commission Schedule Templates (XR-AFF-007). When a partner believes a commission decision is wrong, this policy gives them one clear, recorded path to challenge it. The goal is a fair answer on the record, quickly, from a named human, without the dispute itself putting the partner at risk.

## 2. What can be disputed

A partner may dispute any commission decision that affects them, including:

- a reversal of a pending, approved, or paid commission,
- a hold the partner believes is unjustified or has run too long,
- a missing or misattributed conversion (an order the partner believes should have been attributed to their link, code, or approved contractual group),
- the calculation of the eligible commission base on an order (which deductions were applied),
- the rate or schedule version applied to a commission,
- a payout amount that does not match the ledger,
- a commission consequence imposed after an investigation under the Suspension and Termination Policy (XR-AFF-014).

Program status decisions themselves (certification, suspension, termination) are governed by XR-AFF-014 and the partner agreement, not this policy. Only their commission effects are disputable here.

## 3. How to file a dispute

1. Where: through the partner portal dispute function, or by email to research@xeniostechnology.com with the subject line "Commission dispute" while the portal function is not yet available.
2. Deadline: within [CONFIG: filing window, days] of the date the disputed decision appears in the partner's ledger or notice. Late disputes may be declined as untimely, except where applicable law requires otherwise.
3. Contents: the ledger entry or order reference, the decision being disputed, what the partner believes the correct outcome is, and the partner's evidence (Section 4).
4. One dispute per decision. A partner may not refile the same dispute after a final decision, unless they present material new evidence that was not reasonably available before.

Filing a dispute moves the affected commission to the disputed state under XR-AFF-007 Section 7: it is not paid and not finally reversed while the dispute is open.

## 4. Evidence

1. The partner provides what they have: screenshots, link and code records, correspondence, event approvals, or anything else that supports their position. Partners never submit health information, applicant information, passwords, identity documents, or payment credentials as evidence; a dispute never requires them.
2. Xenios reviews its own records: the commission ledger, the attribution system's records, order and refund records, the admin audit log, and any investigation record under XR-AFF-014.
3. The system records are the starting point, not an automatic winner. Where the partner's evidence shows an attribution or calculation error, the record is corrected.
4. All dispute submissions and findings are retained per XR-POL-005 alongside the related ledger entries.

## 5. Review process and timeline

1. Acknowledgment: Xenios acknowledges a filed dispute in writing within [CONFIG: acknowledgment window, days].
2. Review: the dispute is reviewed against the evidence in Section 4. Xenios may ask the partner follow-up questions; the partner has [CONFIG: response window, days] to answer, and the timeline pauses while an answer is awaited.
3. Decision target: Xenios aims to decide a dispute within [CONFIG: decision target, days] of filing, and gives a written status update if it needs longer.
4. Effect on other commissions: an open dispute affects only the disputed entries. The partner's other commissions and payouts proceed normally, unless the partner is under investigation per XR-AFF-014 Section 6.

## 6. Final decision authority

1. Samuel Boadu, Founder, decides every dispute. He is the named accountable human for the program; there is no anonymous "the system" outcome.
2. The decision is written, states its reasons, and states the resulting ledger action: release, pay, correct, uphold the reversal, or another stated outcome.
3. The decision is final within the program. It is entered in the ledger and audit log, and the disputed state ends.
4. A final program decision does not take away any legal rights the partner has. Whether and how further recourse works (for example negotiation, mediation, or the dispute-resolution clause in the partner agreement) is governed by the signed agreement and applicable law. ## 7. No retaliation

1. Filing a dispute in good faith never harms a partner. Xenios will not suspend, terminate, demote, de-prioritize, hold unrelated commissions, or otherwise disadvantage a partner because they filed a dispute.
2. Enforcement under XR-AFF-014 continues to apply on its own merits. A partner who files a dispute remains subject to the same rules as everyone else, no better and no worse.
3. Abuse of this process (fabricated evidence or knowingly false dispute claims) is itself a violation, handled under XR-AFF-014. Honest disputes that simply lose are not abuse.

## 8. Relationship to law

This policy is an internal program process. It does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law. Payment of commissions, deadlines, and remedies remain subject to the signed partner agreement and applicable law, including any state statutes governing payments to independent sales representatives.
