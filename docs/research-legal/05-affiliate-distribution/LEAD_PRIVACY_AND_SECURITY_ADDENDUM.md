## 1. Purpose

This Addendum sets the privacy and security rules for every lead a Xenios Research partner touches. It attaches to and forms part of the partner's governing agreement with Xenios (the affiliate agreement, Research Rep agreement, or organization partner agreement, as applicable). In this Addendum that governing document is called "the Agreement" and the partner is called "the Partner".

The design principle is minimal data. The Xenios Research lead flow is built so that the sensitive part of an application never passes through a partner: the Partner shares an approved link, the prospect applies directly to Xenios at the private entrance, the system attributes the source, and Samuel Boadu reviews the application. The Partner's job is introduction and explanation, not data collection.

## 2. Definitions

- "Lead" means a person the Partner introduces or intends to introduce to Xenios Research.
- "Lead Data" means any personal information about a Lead that the Partner collects, receives, or holds in connection with the Agreement. Examples: name, contact details, channel, expressed interest, and notes of conversations.
- "Health Information" means any information about a person's physical or mental health, conditions, symptoms, medications, lab results, body measurements, or treatment history, and any information a reasonable person would treat as revealing health status.

## 3. Minimal data rule

1. The Partner may collect only the minimum Lead Data needed to make an introduction: name, contact information the Lead volunteers, the channel the Lead came from, and general interest in the program.
2. The Partner must never collect, request, record, or store:
   - Health Information about a Lead or member, in any form, including informal notes;
   - government identification documents or numbers;
   - passwords, one-time codes, recovery codes, or any account credential;
   - payment card numbers, bank details, or any payment credential;
   - application answers, application status details, or rejection reasons.
3. If a Lead volunteers Health Information (for example, describes a condition in a message), the Partner must not record it, must not repeat it, and must direct the Lead to raise health topics directly with Xenios through the membership process. The Partner relays product questions to Xenios without the personal details attached.
4. Payments are never collected by the Partner. All payments run through Xenios checkout. This is a fixed rule of the program, not a configurable option.

## 4. Prohibited sources and targeting

1. No purchased lists. The Partner must not buy, rent, license, or borrow contact lists for outreach on behalf of Xenios Research.
2. No health-condition lists of any kind, purchased or otherwise. The Partner must not build, acquire, or use any list that selects people by medical condition, symptom, medication, or health status.
3. No hidden health targeting. The Partner must not configure advertising or outreach tools to target people by health conditions or inferred health status.
4. No scraping or harvesting of contact details from websites, forums, or member communities.
5. Outreach is limited to people with whom the Partner has a real, existing relationship or who have asked to hear from the Partner, using approved materials.

## 5. Outreach rules (consent, opt-out, quiet hours, Do Not Call)

1. Consent. The Partner may send marketing outreach (email, call, text, or Telegram message) only to people who have consented to hear from the Partner or with whom the Partner has an existing relationship that reasonably covers the contact. Consent records must be kept.
2. Approved templates. Outreach about Xenios Research must use approved templates and approved claims only. The Partner must not improvise product or program claims.
3. Disclosure. Every message must make the Partner's paid relationship with Xenios clear, consistent with FTC endorsement disclosure rules.
4. Opt-out. Every outreach channel must offer a clear way to opt out. The Partner must honor an opt-out promptly, stop all Xenios-related contact to that person, and record the opt-out. 5. Quiet hours. No calls or texts before 8:00 a.m. or after 9:00 p.m. in the recipient's local time zone. This matches the federal telemarketing quiet-hours window. Some states are stricter. [COUNSEL: confirm quiet-hours windows and any stricter state rules that must be listed for partners]
6. Do Not Call. Until counsel completes that analysis, the Partner must not place cold calls or send cold texts promoting Xenios Research to anyone. 7. Recordkeeping. The Partner must keep records of consent, contact, and opt-outs for outreach performed under the Agreement and produce them to Xenios on request.

## 6. Secure handling

1. Lead Data lives in Xenios systems. The Partner submits leads through the approved link, code, or portal, and keeps only the minimal working notes needed to manage their own introductions.
2. Any Xenios-provided account (portal or dashboard) must be protected with a strong unique password and multi-factor authentication where offered, must not be shared, and must be used only by the named Partner.
3. Devices used to handle Lead Data must be protected with a lock screen and kept up to date. Lead Data must not be stored on shared or public devices.
4. Lead Data must not be posted publicly, shared with third parties, or sent over channels Xenios has not approved. Telegram is not a system of record: no identification documents, health details, or payment details ever move over Telegram.
5. The Partner must not sell, rent, or share Lead Data with anyone, and must not use Lead Data for any purpose other than performing under the Agreement.

## 7. What the Partner can and cannot see

The Partner's reporting view is limited by design to:

- aggregate lead counts,
- application status categories,
- eligible conversions,
- commission information, and
- compliance tasks.

The Partner never sees applicant health data, application answers, or the reasons an application was rejected. The Partner must not attempt to obtain this information from the Lead, from Xenios staff, or from any system.

## 8. Security incidents and breach notice to Xenios

1. The Partner must notify Xenios of any actual or suspected loss, theft, unauthorized access, or unauthorized disclosure of Lead Data, or any compromise of a Xenios-provided account, without undue delay and no later than [COUNSEL: confirm notice window; proposed 72 hours] after discovery.
2. Notice goes to research@xeniostechnology.com with "SECURITY" in the subject line. Samuel Boadu (samuel@xeniostechnology.com) is the named accountable human for escalations.
3. The notice must describe what happened, what data is affected, when it was discovered, and what the Partner has done so far.
4. The Partner must cooperate with Xenios's investigation and remediation, must preserve relevant records, and must not make public statements about the incident without Xenios's written approval, except as the Partner is required by law.
5. Xenios's own incident handling follows its incident response plan. Nothing here shifts Xenios's legal duties to the Partner or the Partner's legal duties to Xenios.

## 9. Deletion on termination

1. When the Agreement ends, for any reason, the Partner must stop all outreach on behalf of Xenios Research immediately.
2. Within [CONFIG: proposed 30 days] of termination the Partner must delete all Lead Data in the Partner's possession and certify the deletion to Xenios in writing.
3. The Partner may retain a record only where a law requires retention, and only for as long as that law requires, and must tell Xenios what is retained and why.
4. Opt-out and consent records related to the Partner's own communications may be retained where needed to honor the opt-out.

## 10. Monitoring, audit, and enforcement

1. Xenios may monitor partner content and outreach for compliance and may request evidence of the practices required by this Addendum.
2. A privacy violation, including collecting Health Information, using a purchased list, or ignoring an opt-out, is grounds for immediate suspension under the Agreement, and commissions may be held during investigation.
3. No sales volume offsets a privacy violation. This is a fixed rule of the program.

## 12. Legal posture

This Addendum is designed to satisfy consumer protection, telemarketing, and privacy expectations, subject to counsel confirmation. It does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios or the Partner of duties imposed by law. Nothing in this Addendum makes the Partner an employee, agent for service of process, or health provider of Xenios.
