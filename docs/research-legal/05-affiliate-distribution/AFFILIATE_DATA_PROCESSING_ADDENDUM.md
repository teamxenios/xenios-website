## 1. Purpose and roles

This Addendum governs any personal data a Xenios Research partner processes on Xenios's behalf. It attaches to and forms part of the partner's governing agreement (the affiliate agreement, Research Rep agreement, or organization partner agreement, as applicable). That governing document is "the Agreement" and the partner is "the Partner".

Intended role allocation, subject to counsel confirmation:

- Xenios determines the purposes and means of processing for program data (the business or controller role).
- The Partner processes personal data only on Xenios's documented instructions when acting under the Agreement (the processor or service provider role).
- Where the Partner communicates with the Partner's own audience under the Partner's own brand (for example, the Partner's own newsletter list), the Partner acts for itself and is independently responsible for that processing. This Addendum does not make Xenios responsible for the Partner's own audience data.

## 2. Definitions

- "Program Data" means personal data the Partner processes on Xenios's behalf under the Agreement. Expected categories: lead names and contact details, channel and attribution data, event registration lists for approved events, and compliance and communication records. Program Data deliberately excludes health information, identification documents, credentials, and payment data, which the Partner is forbidden to collect under the Lead Privacy and Security Addendum (XR-AFF-011).
- "Processing" means any operation performed on Program Data, such as collecting, recording, storing, using, disclosing, or deleting.
- "Security Incident" means any actual or suspected unauthorized access to, or unauthorized acquisition, use, disclosure, loss, or alteration of, Program Data.

## 4. Instructions and purpose limitation

1. The Partner processes Program Data only on Xenios's documented instructions. The Agreement, this Addendum, XR-AFF-011, and written instructions from Xenios are the complete instruction set.
2. The Partner must use Program Data only to perform under the Agreement. No other use is permitted.
3. The Partner must not sell Program Data, must not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, and must not receive it in exchange for anything of value from a third party. [COUNSEL: conform this clause to the sale/share definitions in applicable state privacy laws]
4. The Partner must not combine Program Data with data from other sources, except as needed to perform under the Agreement or as a state privacy law expressly permits for a processor or service provider. [COUNSEL: confirm the permitted-combination carve-outs]
5. If the Partner believes an instruction violates law, the Partner must tell Xenios promptly and may pause the affected processing until the question is resolved.

## 5. Confidentiality

1. Program Data is Confidential Information under the Agreement.
2. The Partner must limit access to people who need it to perform under the Agreement, and each such person must be bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Addendum.
3. Confidentiality duties survive termination of the Agreement.

## 6. Security

The Partner must implement and maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the data, designed to protect Program Data against unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. At minimum:

1. Strong unique passwords and multi-factor authentication where offered, on every account used to process Program Data.
2. No shared accounts. Xenios-provided accounts are used only by the named Partner.
3. Device protection: lock screens, current operating system and application updates, and no processing on public or shared devices.
4. Encryption in transit for Program Data wherever the tool used supports it.
5. Access on a need-to-know basis, with access removed when a person no longer needs it.
6. No storage of Program Data in unapproved tools. Telegram, personal social media inboxes, and public documents are not approved storage.
7. Prompt deletion of working copies that are no longer needed.

## 7. Subprocessing restrictions

1. The Partner must not engage any subcontractor or other third party to process Program Data (a "Subprocessor") without Xenios's prior written authorization, specific to each Subprocessor.
2. General-purpose tools the Partner already uses (for example an email client) require disclosure to Xenios but may be approved by category. [COUNSEL: confirm whether category-level approval of ordinary tools is acceptable under applicable processor contract rules]
3. Every authorized Subprocessor must be bound by a written contract imposing obligations at least as protective as this Addendum.
4. The Partner remains fully responsible to Xenios for every Subprocessor's acts and omissions.

## 8. Individual rights requests

1. If anyone contacts the Partner to exercise a privacy right relating to Program Data (access, deletion, correction, opt-out, or similar), the Partner must forward the request to research@xeniostechnology.com within [CONFIG: proposed 5 business days] and must not respond on Xenios's behalf beyond acknowledging receipt.
2. The Partner must give Xenios reasonable assistance in honoring verified requests, including deleting the Partner's copies when Xenios directs it.
3. Opt-outs of the Partner's own communications are handled under XR-AFF-011 Section 5 and must be honored by the Partner directly.

## 9. Security Incidents

1. The Partner must notify Xenios of any Security Incident without undue delay and no later than [COUNSEL: confirm notice window; proposed 72 hours] after discovery, to research@xeniostechnology.com with "SECURITY" in the subject line.
2. The notice must describe the nature of the incident, the data and individuals affected so far as known, the discovery time, and the measures taken.
3. The Partner must cooperate with Xenios's investigation, preserve evidence, and take the remediation steps Xenios reasonably directs.
4. The Partner must not notify any individual, regulator, or the press about a Security Incident involving Program Data unless the law requires the Partner to do so directly; legally required notices must be shared with Xenios in advance where the law allows.
5. Xenios is responsible for its own legal notification duties and will make its own determinations under its incident response plan.

## 10. Audit and verification

1. The Partner must keep records sufficient to demonstrate compliance with this Addendum and produce them to Xenios on reasonable notice.
2. Xenios may assess compliance through written questionnaires, evidence requests, and, where a material concern exists, a reasonable audit on notice, no more than once per year absent an incident. [COUNSEL: confirm the audit clause satisfies the assessment requirements of applicable state privacy laws]
3. The Partner must answer an annual compliance attestation when Xenios requests one.
4. Xenios may take reasonable and appropriate steps to stop and remediate unauthorized use of Program Data, including suspending the Partner's access.

## 11. Return and deletion

1. On termination of the Agreement, or earlier on Xenios's written direction, the Partner must stop processing Program Data, and within [CONFIG: proposed 30 days] must delete or return all Program Data, at Xenios's choice, and certify the deletion in writing.
2. The Partner may retain Program Data only where a law requires retention, only for as long as the law requires, and must tell Xenios what is retained and under what legal duty. Retained data stays under this Addendum.

## 12. State privacy law contract clauses

The comprehensive privacy laws of several states require specific contract terms between a business and its processors or service providers, and several state consumer health data laws impose their own contract and consent rules. This Addendum is intended to supply those terms. Pending counsel review, the parties agree to the following framework:

1. The Partner certifies that it understands the restrictions in Sections 4 and 7 and will comply with them.
2. Xenios may direct deletion of Program Data as described in Sections 8 and 11.
3. The Partner will notify Xenios if it determines it can no longer meet its obligations under applicable privacy law.
4. The Partner will make available to Xenios the information necessary to demonstrate compliance, per Section 10.
5. Program Data is deliberately designed to exclude consumer health data. If any processing of consumer health data on Xenios's behalf is ever proposed, it requires a separate written addendum first. ## 13. Term, survival, precedence

1. This Addendum runs with the Agreement and ends when the wind-down duties in Section 11 are complete.
2. Sections 5, 9, 11, and 12 survive termination to the extent needed to give them effect.
3. If this Addendum conflicts with the Agreement on data protection, this Addendum controls. If it conflicts with XR-AFF-011, the stricter privacy protection controls. [COUNSEL: confirm the order of precedence]

## 14. Legal posture

This Addendum is designed to meet processor-style contracting expectations under United States state privacy laws, subject to counsel confirmation. It does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios or the Partner of duties imposed by law. No provision here makes the Partner an employee or general agent of Xenios. Xenios does not represent itself as a HIPAA covered entity for direct-to-consumer services; the HIPAA applicability analysis is pending (see XR-POL-009).
