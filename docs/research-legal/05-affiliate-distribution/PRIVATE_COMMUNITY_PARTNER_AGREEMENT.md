## 1. Parties and purpose

This agreement is between [ENTITY] ("Xenios") and the private community identified in the signature block (the "Community"), acting through its named leader (the "Community Leader"). A private community here means a membership group, forum, professional circle, or invite-only network whose members interact primarily inside a private space rather than through public channels.

Private communities differ from gyms and companies in one important way: promotion happens inside a trusted private space, among people who know each other. That raises the standard of care. This agreement adapts the Organization Partner framework to that setting, adds community-specific protections, and enables two options the base agreement only references: a sponsored Founding Membership cohort and education events.

## 2. Relationship to the Organization Partner Agreement

2.1 The Organization Partner Agreement (XR-AFF-005) is incorporated into this agreement in full. The Community is an "Organization", the Community Leader is the "Organization Owner", and the defined terms of XR-AFF-005 apply here.

2.2 Where this agreement and XR-AFF-005 differ, this agreement controls for private communities. Where neither speaks, XR-AFF-005 controls.

2.3 Everything absolute in XR-AFF-005 remains absolute here, including: the anti-MLM rules (no recruiting pay, no downlines, no forced inventory purchases, rank never primarily recruitment-based), the excluded lanes (membership fees without separate approval, peptide and Quantum until enabled), the claims rules, the fraud and reversal rules, and the standard member path (application, Samuel's review, identity and age verification, 21+ only, agreements before payment).

## 3. Community standards

3.1 Respect the community's own rules. The Community Leader confirms that promotion of Xenios inside the community is permitted by the community's own rules and platform terms. Xenios never asks a partner to violate the community's rules to promote Xenios.

3.2 Disclosure inside a trusted space. The compensated relationship must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously inside the community itself, not only in an external profile or a pinned post no one reads. Every promotional post, thread, event, and direct conversation carries the disclosure. In a private space, an undisclosed paid recommendation reads as a friend's advice; that is exactly what the FTC Endorsement Guides prohibit.

3.3 No pressure dynamics. The Community Leader must not use moderation power, membership status, or social standing inside the community to pressure anyone toward applying or purchasing. Access to the community and its ordinary benefits never depends on anyone's decision about Xenios.

3.4 Direct messages. Unsolicited promotional direct messages inside the community are prohibited unless the recipient asked or the community's rules expressly permit them, and every direct message carries the disclosure.

3.5 Members of the community remain free to discuss Xenios honestly, including critically. The Community Leader must not delete or suppress good-faith critical discussion to protect the partnership, and must not manufacture testimonials. Testimonials from the community cannot make claims Xenios could not make directly.

## 4. Sponsored Founding Membership cohort (optional)

4.1 What it is. If elected in the economics schedule, the Community may fund Founding Memberships for a fixed number of its members (the "Sponsored Cohort"): some or all of the $50 activation payment and up to [CONFIG: maximum sponsored months] months of the $25 recurring monthly membership, at launch pricing.

4.2 How it works, by design of the standard path:

1. Xenios issues the Community a fixed number of single-use sponsorship codes [CONFIG: cohort size and code mechanics].
2. The Community distributes codes by its own fair, disclosed method. A code is an offer to fund, nothing more.
3. Each individual applies personally at the private entrance, is reviewed by Samuel personally, completes identity and age verification (21+ only), and accepts all agreements personally, before any payment step. Sponsorship changes nothing about the standard of approval and creates no entitlement to approval.
4. At the payment step, an approved applicant may redeem the code, which applies the sponsored amounts. The recurring authorization beyond the sponsored period remains the member's own.

4.3 What the Community learns. The Community sees only aggregate redemption counts. It never learns who applied, who was approved, who was rejected, or who redeemed a code, unless the individual personally chooses to say so. Distribution of a code is not confirmation that the recipient applied.

4.4 Sponsored members are ordinary members. They hold their own accounts, passwords, and mandatory MFA; complete the mandatory assessment within 3 days of activation; and receive Xenios 30 and Xenios 90 like every active member. The Community acquires no rights in or visibility into a sponsored member's account, data, or plans.

4.5 Cancellation and refunds. A sponsored member may cancel like any member: cancellation is self-service, confirmed cancellation ends access immediately, and remaining paid time is forfeited with no prorated refund unless applicable law requires one, as disclosed in the member-facing terms and subject to counsel review. Sponsored amounts already applied are not refunded to the Community unless the economics schedule states otherwise. ## 5. Education events

5.1 The Community may host education events (in person or virtual) about the membership, using only the approved event deck and materials, run by the Community Leader or an Approved Rep.

5.2 Event rules, in addition to XR-AFF-005 section 9: disclose the partnership at the start; present the membership as private, application-based, and never guaranteed; no product claims beyond the approved list; no diagnosis, prescribing, or dosing talk, including informal questions afterward; no collecting payments or paper applications; point people to the private entrance link. Nothing at an event is medical care, and emergencies go to emergency services (911 in the US).

5.3 Approved event bonuses, if any, are set in the economics schedule and paid only for events Xenios approves in advance and verifies [CONFIG: event bonus structure], consistent with XR-AFF-003 section 8.1 where an Approved Rep runs the event.

5.4 Recording an event captures attendees' images and voices; the Community must announce any recording before it starts and follow the platform's and venue's rules. Recordings that include attendees are not marketing assets unless Xenios approves them and the necessary consents exist. ## 6. Aggregate analytics only

6.1 The Community and the Community Leader receive only aggregate analytics, exactly as in XR-AFF-005 section 8: aggregate leads, application status categories in aggregate, eligible conversions in aggregate, payout amounts and states, compliance tasks, and aggregate sponsorship redemption counts.

6.2 In a private community the re-identification risk of small numbers is higher: members know each other, and "2 of 40 approved this month" can point at a person. Xenios may suppress, delay, or bucket any figure below the aggregation threshold [COUNSEL: confirm the threshold; the small-cohort suppression rule of XR-AFF-005 section 8.3 applies with a stricter default here].

6.3 No member-level or applicant-level data, health information, identity documents, or payment details are ever exposed to the Community without the individual's own explicit consent recorded by Xenios. The Community must not attempt to reconstruct individual outcomes from aggregates, polls, or social pressure, and must not keep its own records of who it believes applied.

## 7. Reps inside the community

Any individual promoting Xenios inside the community beyond the Community Leader's own disclosed activity must be an Approved Rep under XR-AFF-003, assigned to the Community as its Contractual Group, and bound by the Code of Conduct (XR-AFF-004). The single-level structure of XR-AFF-005 section 4 applies unchanged: no sub-groups, no chains, and the Community Leader's disclosed override extends only to the approved Contractual Group's direct attributable eligible sales.

## 8. Economics

The Community's economics are those negotiated in the XR-AFF-005 economics schedule: the negotiated direct-attribution revenue share on Eligible Direct Net Collected Revenue, any disclosed Community Leader override under XR-AFF-005 section 5.2, any sponsored cohort under section 4, and any approved event bonuses under section 5.3. Holds, reversals, fraud rules, commission states (pending, held, approved, paid, reversed, disputed), and payment mechanics follow XR-AFF-005 section 7.

## 9. Privacy and data boundaries

9.1 The Community's own member data (its rosters, chats, and records) stays the Community's, outside Xenios systems. Xenios does not ask for it and the Community must not send it. Prospect introductions happen by the approved link, so the individual brings themselves to Xenios.

9.2 No purchased lists, no health-condition targeting, no scraping the community's private spaces for outreach lists. Section 10 of XR-AFF-005 (communications) applies to all outreach connected to this partnership.

9.3 Telegram and other chats are never a place for passwords, identity documents, health information, or payment data, for anyone, in any direction.

## 10. Term, suspension, and termination

Term, suspension, and termination follow XR-AFF-005 section 12, with one addition: conduct that abuses the trust of the private space (undisclosed promotion, pressure through moderation power, attempts to identify applicants, or misuse of sponsorship codes) is cause for immediate suspension. On termination, unredeemed sponsorship codes are cancelled and unspent sponsored funds are settled per the economics schedule [COUNSEL: confirm the settlement of unspent sponsored funds on termination].
