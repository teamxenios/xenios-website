## 1. Parties and purpose

This agreement is between [ENTITY] ("Xenios") and the approved affiliate identified in the signature block (the "Affiliate"). It governs the Affiliate's promotion of the Xenios Research Founding Membership program using approved content and tracked links, and the payment of commissions on eligible sales the Affiliate directly generates. The affiliate program is for creators and partners who publish approved content and links. It is not a membership, not an employment relationship, not a franchise, and not a distributorship.

## 2. Definitions

- "Approved Assets" means the links, QR codes, landing pages, email templates, social posts, event materials, FAQ, approved claims list, disclosure templates, and brand assets Xenios supplies or approves in writing for the Affiliate's use.
- "Attributed" means recorded by Xenios's server-side attribution system against the Affiliate's tracked link or code at the time the referred person applies or purchases. The attribution ledger is the system of record.
- "Eligible Direct Net Collected Revenue" means product revenue actually collected by Xenios from sales directly Attributed to the Affiliate, minus refunds, chargebacks, tax, shipping, store credit, discounts, and excluded products, as computed in section 8.
- "Hold Period" means the post-conversion period in section 9 during which a commission is pending and not payable.
- "Prohibited Claims" means the statements listed in section 6.3 and any claim not on the approved claims list.

## 3. Eligibility, application, and approval

3.1 The Affiliate applied with, at minimum: legal name; business or entity details; contact information; channels and audience; associated organizations; expected activity; prior regulated-product experience; conflicts; sanctions or discipline disclosures where relevant; a completed tax form; and a payment method for commission payout.

3.2 Xenios verifies identity, age, business status, tax status, sanctions where appropriate, and reviews the Affiliate's channels and prior claims history. Approval is at Xenios's discretion and may be withdrawn under section 12.

3.3 The Affiliate's program status is one of: Applicant, Approved, Certified, Active, Quality Review, Suspended, or Terminated. Promotion is permitted only while Active (or Approved where Xenios has not required certification for the Affiliate's lane [CONFIG: whether affiliate certification is required at launch]).

3.4 The Affiliate must keep application information current and must notify Xenios promptly of any material change (new channels, sanctions, or conflicts).

## 4. Limited license to Approved Assets

4.1 Xenios grants the Affiliate a limited, revocable, non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable license to use Approved Assets, unmodified, solely to promote Xenios Research during the term.

4.2 The Affiliate may not: modify Approved Assets except for placement formatting that does not change words or meaning; create unapproved ads, landing pages, or claims; register domains, handles, or accounts confusingly similar to Xenios names; use Xenios marks outside the Approved Assets; or represent an affiliation broader than "approved affiliate."

4.3 All goodwill from use of Xenios marks inures to Xenios. The license ends automatically on suspension or termination, and the Affiliate must then promptly remove or disable Xenios-branded content and links within [CONFIG: takedown window, 5 business days suggested].

## 5. FTC disclosure duty

5.1 The Affiliate must clearly and conspicuously disclose the material connection to Xenios (that the Affiliate is compensated) in every piece of content, on every channel, in a way the audience cannot miss, consistent with the FTC Endorsement Guides. Platform-buried or hashtag-only disclosure at the end of a long caption is not sufficient.

5.2 Xenios supplies disclosure templates in the Approved Assets. Using them is the default; any variation needs written approval.

5.3 Endorsements must reflect the Affiliate's honest opinions and actual experience. The Affiliate may not present a testimonial (their own or anyone else's) making a claim Xenios could not make directly.

## 6. Conduct and prohibited claims

6.1 The Affiliate promotes a private, application-based, 21+ membership. Approval is never guaranteed, and the Affiliate must not state or imply otherwise.

6.2 The Affiliate must not target audiences under 21, must not use purchased health-condition lists, and must not run outreach that violates section 10.

6.3 Prohibited Claims. The Affiliate must never state or imply:

- guaranteed fat loss or guaranteed muscle gain,
- that any product cures, treats, or prevents any disease or condition,
- that any product is safe for everyone,
- "FDA approved" when it is not,
- "clinically proven" when it is not,
- any diagnosis, prescription, dosing instruction, or medication direction, or
- "research use only" as a loophole while promoting human outcomes.

6.4 Peptide products are research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review. The Affiliate may not promote peptide or Quantum products at all until Xenios enables those lanes in writing (see section 8.4). Quantum is Coming Soon: no commerce, no checkout, no sales promotion.

6.5 Xenios may monitor the Affiliate's public content for compliance and may require correction or removal of non-compliant content. The Affiliate must comply within [CONFIG: correction window, 48 hours suggested].

## 7. Attribution and what the Affiliate sees

7.1 Lead flow: the Affiliate shares an approved link; the person reaches the private entrance and applies; the system Attributes the source; Samuel reviews the application; on activation, an eligible conversion enters the Hold Period; after a clean hold, commission becomes payable.

7.2 The Affiliate dashboard shows only: aggregate leads, application status categories, eligible conversions, commission amounts and states, and compliance tasks. The Affiliate never sees applicant health data, identity documents, payment details, or reasons for rejection.

## 8. Commission

8.1 Rate. Xenios pays the Affiliate [CONFIG: commission percentage] of Eligible Direct Net Collected Revenue. The rate is configurable, set per affiliate or per tier by Xenios, and changes apply prospectively with notice.

8.2 Eligible base. Commission is computed as:

```text
collected product revenue directly Attributed to the Affiliate
minus refunds
minus chargebacks
minus tax
minus shipping
minus store credit
minus discounts
minus excluded products
```

8.3 Direct only. Commission is paid only on the Affiliate's own directly Attributed sales. There is no payment for recruiting other affiliates or reps, no downline, no override on another person's sales, and no inventory purchase requirement of any kind.

8.4 Excluded lanes. Membership fee commissions require separate written approval from Xenios [COUNSEL: whether commissioning the $50 activation or $25 recurring membership is permissible and how it must be disclosed]. Peptide and Quantum commissions are disabled until their commercial and claims lanes are approved; no commission accrues on those SKUs in the meantime, even if a sale occurs.

8.5 Nothing accrues on revenue Xenios never collects.

## 9. Holds, reversals, and payment

9.1 Every commission passes through states: pending, held, approved, paid, reversed, disputed.

9.2 An eligible conversion enters a Hold Period of [CONFIG: hold length; not shorter than the refund and chargeback window it protects] before approval. Xenios may extend a hold for a specific commission during a fraud or compliance investigation and may hold all of the Affiliate's commissions during an investigation under section 12.

9.3 A commission is reversed, whether pending or paid, to the extent the underlying sale is refunded, charged back, cancelled, found fraudulent (including self-referral, duplicate identities, circular orders, refunded conversion farming, code stuffing, misleading paid search, prohibited coupon-site placement, or fake leads), or found to violate this agreement. Reversed amounts may be offset against future payouts.

9.4 Approved commissions are paid [CONFIG: payout schedule and minimum payout threshold] to the payment method on file, subject to a valid tax form. Xenios issues tax reporting forms as required by law.

## 11. Independent contractor

The Affiliate is an independent contractor. Nothing here creates employment, agency, partnership, joint venture, or franchise. The Affiliate has no authority to bind Xenios, accept payments for Xenios, or make commitments on Xenios's behalf. The Affiliate is responsible for its own taxes, insurance, and business expenses. ## 12. Term, suspension, and termination

12.1 The term begins on countersignature and continues until terminated. Either party may terminate for convenience with [CONFIG: notice period, 14 days suggested] written notice. Xenios may suspend or terminate immediately for cause.

12.2 Immediate suspension applies for: a safety claim; dosing or prescribing conduct; a privacy breach; account sharing; fraudulent orders; identity misuse; unauthorized resale; or unapproved product representation. Xenios may hold all commissions during the investigation.

12.3 Consumer affiliates may not buy inventory for resale under this agreement. Resale requires the separate wholesale or sub-distributor program with its own verification, insurance, and quality requirements.

12.4 On termination: the license ends, links are deactivated, the Affiliate removes Xenios-branded content per section 4.3, and commissions in a clean approved state are paid in the ordinary cycle. Commissions pending, held, or connected to the conduct causing a for-cause termination may be voided. [COUNSEL: confirm the forfeiture scope on for-cause termination]

12.5 Sections 5 (for published content), 6, 9.3, 11, 13, 14, and 15 survive termination.
