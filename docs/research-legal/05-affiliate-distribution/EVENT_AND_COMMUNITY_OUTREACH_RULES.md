## 1. Purpose

Research Reps and organization partners reach people where they already gather: gyms, teams, clubs, companies, private communities, and professional networks. Events are part of the Research Rep role, and an approved event bonus exists in the compensation architecture (XR-AFF-007, Template C). This document sets the rules for every event and every community outreach activity, so that in-person reach never becomes an unapproved claims channel, a payment shortcut, or a health-data collection point.

An "event" means any organized presentation, table, booth, workshop, seminar, webinar, or group session, in person or virtual, where a partner represents Xenios Research. "Community outreach" means direct contact with a community or its members on behalf of the program, including email, calls, texts, and Telegram outreach.

## 2. Event approval

1. Every event requires advance written approval from Xenios before it is announced or held. There are no pre-authorized event types.
2. The approval request must state: date, location or platform, host organization, expected audience and size, the partner presenting, the materials to be used, and whether any compensation, sponsorship, or venue payment is involved.
3. Samuel Boadu approves, conditions, or declines each event. Approval covers only the event as described. A changed date, audience, or format needs a new approval.
4. The approved event bonus in XR-AFF-007 is payable only for events approved in advance under this section. An unapproved event earns no bonus, and holding one is a violation (Section 9).
5. Organization events (a sponsored Founding Membership cohort session or an education event under an organization partner arrangement) follow the same approval path, tied to the organization's approved contractual group.

## 3. Approved deck and materials only

1. Partners present from the approved event deck and the approved asset library as issued: deck, FAQ, disclosure templates, links, QR codes, and brand assets.
2. Editing the deck's claims, disclosures, or product statements makes it unapproved content. Layout-only localization (venue name, date, presenter name) is permitted.
3. Handouts, slides, signs, and follow-up materials must all come from the approved library or receive written pre-approval under XR-AFF-009 Section 7.
4. Recording an event for later promotional use makes the recording partner content: it must carry the required disclosures and only registry-allowed claims, and it follows the Social Media Policy (XR-AFF-010) when published.

## 4. What may be said at an event

1. The Claims and Content Policy (XR-AFF-009) applies in full to spoken words, slides, demonstrations, and answers to audience questions. No product claims beyond the approved set. No guaranteed outcomes, no cure or treat language, no "safe for everyone", no "FDA approved" or "clinically proven" statements, and no research-use framing paired with human-outcome promises.
2. No diagnosis, no prescribing, no dosing, no lab interpretation, anywhere at an event, including private sidebar conversations. A question that asks for any of these is relayed to Xenios.
3. Partners may explain the membership process factually: private, application-based, approval required, 21+, identity and age verification after approval, agreements before payment. They help applicants navigate non-sensitive steps only.
4. The event disclosure rules in XR-AFF-008 apply: a spoken disclosure at the start of the presentation, plus printed or on-slide disclosure on materials.
5. Peptide SKUs and Quantum are closed lanes. Partners may not promote peptide products at events while the classification lane is under formal review, and may not present Quantum beyond its Coming Soon status. No Quantum commerce, commitments, or administration descriptions.

## 5. No on-site payment collection

1. Reps and partners never collect payments personally. No cash, cards, payment apps, invoices, or "I'll ring you up" arrangements at any event, for membership, products, or anything else.
2. All payment occurs only in the official Xenios checkout, by the applicant or member themselves, after approval, verification, and agreement acceptance in the required order.
3. Partners also never handle passwords or identity documents, at events or anywhere. Someone who needs account or identity help is directed to the official flow and to research@xeniostechnology.com.
4. Collecting a payment personally is an immediate-suspension trigger under XR-AFF-014 (fraudulent orders and unauthorized representation both reach it).

## 6. No health data collection at events

1. Partners collect no health information at events. No symptom forms, health questionnaires, condition conversations recorded in notes, body measurements, photos of attendees' bodies, or "tell me what you're dealing with" intake sheets.
2. Lead capture is limited to: name, contact information, the source event, and the person's consent to be contacted (Section 7). Nothing else.
3. If an attendee volunteers health information, the partner does not record it, does not store it, and does not pass it along informally. The partner directs the person to apply through the private entrance, where information is collected under the program's own consents.
4. Partners never store health information in any form. This is a program-wide rule, and a breach of it is an immediate-suspension trigger under XR-AFF-014.
5. A possible product concern or adverse event mentioned at an event is relayed to Xenios immediately through the partner's trained escalation path, with the person's contact details and their consent to be contacted about it. The partner gives no advice.

## 7. Lead handling

1. Leads are captured only on approved forms (paper or digital) that state who is collecting the information, that the partner is a paid independent partner of Xenios Research, and what the person is consenting to.
2. Captured leads are entered into the Xenios system promptly, within [CONFIG: lead entry window, hours] of the event, and the source event is recorded for attribution. No private spreadsheets, personal phones, or local lead files retained after entry. Paper forms are destroyed after confirmed entry.
3. The interested person's own path is always the same: the partner's approved link or QR code, to the private entrance, to the application. The system attributes the source. Samuel reviews every application.
4. The partner sees aggregate leads, application status categories, eligible conversions, commission, and compliance tasks. The partner never sees applicant health data or reasons for rejection.
5. Follow-up outreach requires the consent captured on the form, honors opt-outs immediately, uses approved templates, respects quiet hours, and follows Do Not Call rules where applicable. Records of consent and opt-out are kept per XR-POL-005.
6. No purchased lists of any kind, and never purchased health-condition lists. No hidden health targeting in outreach (XR-AFF-010 Section 7).

## 8. Organization and community rules

1. An organization arrangement covers a defined, approved contractual group. The organization owner and its approved reps operate inside this document like any partner.
2. Aggregate analytics only: no member-level sensitive data is exposed to the organization without the member's consent.
3. Sponsored cohort and education-event economics are set in the organization's completed schedule (XR-AFF-007, Template D), never negotiated on site.
4. The membership stays private. Partners do not announce who applied, who was approved, or who is a member, at any event or in any community channel.

## 9. Monitoring, records, and consequences

1. Xenios may attend, review recordings of, or audit any approved event, and may require post-event confirmation of the materials used and leads captured.
2. Event approvals, materials versions, lead consent records, and outreach records are retained per XR-POL-005.
3. Violations follow the Suspension and Termination Policy (XR-AFF-014). Safety claims, dosing statements, privacy breaches (including health data collection), personal payment collection, and unapproved product representation at an event trigger immediate suspension. Commission and event-bonus consequences follow XR-AFF-007, and a partner who disagrees may use the Commission Dispute Policy (XR-AFF-015).
4. No violation can be offset by sales volume or event turnout.

## 10. Relationship to law

These rules are designed to keep partner events and outreach within FTC endorsement and advertising rules, telemarketing and Do Not Call rules where applicable, and the program's privacy commitments. They do not guarantee legal compliance in every situation, do not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and do not relieve Xenios or any partner of duties imposed by law.
