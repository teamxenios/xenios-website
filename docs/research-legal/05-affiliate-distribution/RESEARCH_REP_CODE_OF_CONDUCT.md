## 1. Why this exists

You represent Xenios Research to people who trust you. Most of them will never read a regulation. What protects them is how you behave. This code is written to you, in plain language. It is part of your Research Rep Agreement (XR-AFF-003); breaking it has the consequences in section 9.

## 2. Disclose, always

Every time you promote Xenios (a conversation, a post, an event, a group chat), say clearly that you are a compensated Xenios Research Rep. Do not bury it, abbreviate it into a hashtag, or save it for the end. If someone would be surprised to learn afterward that you were paid, you did not disclose properly. Use the disclosure templates in your approved materials.

## 3. Claims discipline

You explain a private membership. You do not sell outcomes.

Never say, suggest, or let stand in your presence:

- guaranteed fat loss or guaranteed muscle,
- cure, treat, or prevent anything,
- safe for everyone,
- FDA approved (it is not),
- clinically proven (unless it is on the approved claims list, it is not),
- any dose, cycle, stack, timing, or way to take any product, or
- "it's research use only" with a wink while describing what it will do for a person's body.

Your own story counts too. A testimonial cannot make a claim Xenios could not make directly. When someone asks a product question you cannot answer from the approved FAQ, the honest answer is: "Good question. I will pass it to Xenios and they will answer you directly."

## 4. Respect the lead

The people you introduce are people, not inventory.

- No pressure. Membership is application-based and approval is never guaranteed. Say so.
- 21+ only. Do not pitch anyone under 21, and do not help anyone misstate their age.
- Their application is theirs. You may explain the steps. You step away entirely from the sensitive ones: identity verification, payment, and the health assessment are between the applicant and Xenios.
- Their privacy is theirs. You will not be told whether they applied, why a decision went the way it did, or anything about their health, identity, or payments. Do not ask them, do not ask Xenios, and do not speculate to others.
- If they tell you personal or health details unprompted, do not write them down anywhere. Redirect them to Xenios channels.

## 5. Data and security

You never touch: passwords, MFA or recovery codes, identity documents, payment details, or anyone's health information. You never collect a payment personally. You never keep notes, spreadsheets, or chat logs containing someone's health details. Telegram and other chats are never a place for any of those things. If someone tries to hand you any of this, decline and point them to the official flow.

## 6. Communications conduct

Contact people only where you have a genuine relationship or proper consent. Honor every opt-out immediately and permanently. Use approved templates. Respect quiet hours. Never buy or use lists, and never target people by health condition. If a channel's rules (or Do Not Call rules) prohibit the outreach, do not send it.

## 7. Escalate, do not improvise

Report to Xenios promptly (research@xeniostechnology.com or the rep channel) when you encounter:

- a product concern or a possible adverse event, however minor it sounds (you report it, you never triage it),
- a suspected privacy or security incident, including your own mistakes,
- a claim someone else made in your presence that violates section 3, or
- any request you are prohibited from fulfilling.

If anyone describes an emergency, tell them to contact emergency services (911 in the US) first. You are not a medical resource and neither is Xenios membership support.

## 8. Events

Run only events Xenios approved in advance, with the approved deck. Disclose your role at the start. No product claims beyond the approved list, no dosing talk (including "off the record" questions afterward), and no collecting payments or applications on paper. Point people to the private entrance link.

## 9. Consequences ladder

Compliance failures are handled in escalating steps, but Xenios may enter the ladder at any step that fits the severity:

1. Coaching note: a documented correction and, where useful, retraining on the relevant module.
2. Quality Review status: your activity continues under closer monitoring; compliance tasks appear on your dashboard and must be completed.
3. Commission hold: commissions move to held while an issue is investigated.
4. Suspension: all activity stops. Automatic and immediate for any of: a safety claim, dosing conduct, a privacy breach, account sharing, fraudulent orders, identity misuse, unauthorized resale, or unapproved product representation.
5. Termination: the agreement ends under XR-AFF-003 section 13, with the commission consequences stated there.

Two rules govern the ladder. First, no claims or privacy violation can be offset by sales volume; being a top performer buys no leniency. Second, self-reporting an honest mistake promptly and completely counts strongly in your favor.

## 10. Reporting concerns about the program

If you believe anything in the program itself (a material, an instruction, an incentive) pushes you toward breaking this code, report it to samuel@xeniostechnology.com. Samuel Boadu is the named accountable human for escalations. No retaliation for a good-faith report.

## 11. What this code is not

This code does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law. It sets conduct expectations; the legal terms of your role are in the Research Rep Agreement (XR-AFF-003).
