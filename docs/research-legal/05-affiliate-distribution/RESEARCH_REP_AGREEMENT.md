## 1. Parties and purpose

This agreement is between [ENTITY] ("Xenios") and the approved Research Rep identified in the signature block (the "Rep"). A Research Rep is a trained independent sales representative who introduces the Xenios Research Founding Membership to private communities and direct relationships, using only approved materials and approved claims. The role exists to expand distribution without letting untrained people make health claims, prescribe products, mishandle data, or build an MLM. This agreement incorporates the Research Rep Code of Conduct (XR-AFF-004).

## 2. Training and certification come first

2.1 The Rep may perform no program activity of any kind (no outreach, no events, no link sharing, no prospect submission) until the Rep has completed all required training modules and passed the certification assessment.

2.2 Required modules: (1) Xenios membership; (2) privacy and sensitive data; (3) approved products and lanes; (4) FTC endorsement disclosures; (5) health-claims restrictions; (6) no diagnosis or dosing; (7) Telegram and lead handling; (8) application boundaries; (9) product concerns and adverse events; (10) referral fraud; (11) brand and content; (12) security; (13) returns and shipping; (14) organization sales.

2.3 Certification includes an assessment. Xenios records the result. Certification must be renewed [CONFIG: recertification interval, annual suggested] and whenever Xenios materially updates a module. An expired certification suspends program activity automatically until renewed.

2.4 The Rep's program status is one of: Applicant, Approved, Certified, Active, Quality Review, Suspended, Terminated. Activity is permitted only while Active.

## 3. Permitted activities

While Active, the Rep may:

1. explain the membership and its process accurately: private, application-based, approval required, 21+ only, identity and age verification, agreements before payment, $50 activation and $25 monthly membership at launch pricing;
2. use approved claims and Approved Assets (as defined in XR-AFF-002 section 2), unmodified;
3. submit qualified prospects through the approved link or intake path so the system attributes the source;
4. organize and run approved events using the approved event deck;
5. help applicants navigate non-sensitive steps of the process (where to apply, what the stages are, what to expect); and
6. relay product questions to Xenios rather than answering beyond the approved FAQ.

## 4. Prohibited activities (the hard list)

The Rep may never, in any setting, on any channel, for any reason:

1. diagnose any person or condition;
2. prescribe anything;
3. recommend dosing, timing, cycles, stacking, or administration of any product;
4. guarantee results of any kind;
5. handle any person's password, account credentials, MFA or recovery codes, or identity documents;
6. collect payments personally, in any form, for membership or products (all payment goes through Xenios's checkout);
7. store any person's health information, in any system, including notes, chats, and spreadsheets; or
8. create or run unapproved ads, landing pages, or content.

These are bright lines. Crossing any of them triggers immediate suspension under section 13 regardless of the Rep's sales record. Sensitive steps (identity verification, payment, health assessment) are completed by the applicant alone, directly with Xenios; the Rep must step away from those steps entirely.

## 5. Claims discipline

5.1 The Rep must never state or imply: guaranteed fat loss; guaranteed muscle; cure; treat; safe for everyone; "FDA approved" when not; "clinically proven" when not; or "research use only" as a loophole while promoting human outcomes.

5.2 Peptide products are research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review; the Rep may not promote them until Xenios enables that lane in writing. Quantum is Coming Soon: no commerce and no sales promotion.

5.3 Testimonials, including the Rep's own story, cannot make claims Xenios could not make directly.

5.4 Nothing the Rep offers is medical care, a medical device, or an emergency service. Emergencies go to emergency services (911 in the US).

## 6. Disclosure

The Rep must clearly and conspicuously disclose the compensated relationship with Xenios in every conversation, post, and event where the Rep promotes the program, consistent with the FTC Endorsement Guides. Xenios provides disclosure templates; using them is the default.

## 7. Lead flow and what the Rep sees

7.1 Flow: the Rep shares an approved link; the prospect reaches the private entrance and applies; the system attributes the source; Samuel reviews; on activation, an eligible conversion enters the hold; after a clean hold, commission becomes payable.

7.2 The Rep's dashboard shows only: aggregate leads, application status categories, eligible conversions, commission amounts and states, and compliance tasks. The Rep never sees applicant health data, identity documents, payment details, or reasons for rejection. If a prospect shares personal or health information unprompted, the Rep does not record it and redirects the person to Xenios channels.

## 8. Compensation

8.1 The Rep earns [CONFIG: rep commission percentage] of Eligible Direct Net Collected Revenue on the Rep's own directly attributed sales, computed exactly as in XR-AFF-002 section 8.2 (collected product revenue minus refunds, chargebacks, tax, shipping, store credit, discounts, and excluded products), plus approved event bonuses of [CONFIG: event bonus structure] for events Xenios approves in advance and verifies.

8.2 No recruitment pay, no downline. The Rep is paid nothing for recruiting another rep, affiliate, or partner. There are no downline levels and no override on another person's sales under this agreement. Rank and tier never depend primarily on recruitment.

8.3 No inventory. The Rep is never required to purchase anything to qualify for compensation, and consumer resale of Xenios products by a Rep is prohibited (resale requires the separate wholesale program).

8.4 Excluded lanes. Membership fee commissions require separate written approval [COUNSEL: same question as XR-AFF-002 section 8.4]. Peptide and Quantum commissions are disabled until their lanes are approved; no commission accrues on those SKUs in the meantime.

8.5 Commission states are pending, held, approved, paid, reversed, disputed. Holds, reversals, fraud rules, and payment mechanics follow XR-AFF-002 section 9 and apply to the Rep identically. [CONFIG: rep hold length and payout schedule]

## 9. Performance tiers

Xenios may operate performance tiers (for example Founding Rep, Research Rep, Senior Research Rep, Organization Partner). Tier placement may reflect eligible sales, training, compliance, answer quality, member retention, and partner development. No claims or privacy violation can ever be offset by sales volume: compliance failures move a Rep down or out regardless of revenue.

## 10. Communications

Outreach by email, call, text, or messaging must follow: prior consent where required, prompt opt-out handling, approved templates, quiet hours, Do Not Call rules where applicable, and recordkeeping. No purchased health-condition lists, ever. Telegram is never a system of record; the Rep must never move passwords, identity documents, health media, or payment data over Telegram or any chat.

## 11. Escalation duties

The Rep must promptly escalate to Xenios (research@xeniostechnology.com, or the rep channel when built): any product concern or possible adverse event a member or prospect mentions; any suspected privacy or security incident; any claim made in the Rep's presence that violates section 5; and any request the Rep is prohibited from fulfilling under section 4. The Rep never triages a possible adverse event; the Rep reports it. Emergencies go to 911 first.

## 12. Independent contractor

The Rep is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, partner, or franchisee of Xenios. The Rep has no authority to bind Xenios, accept payment for Xenios, or settle disputes for Xenios. The Rep is responsible for its own taxes, expenses, and insurance, and must provide a valid tax form before any payout. ## 13. Term, suspension, and termination

13.1 The term begins on countersignature and continues until terminated. Either party may terminate for convenience with [CONFIG: notice period] written notice. Xenios may suspend or terminate immediately for cause.

13.2 Immediate suspension for: a safety claim; dosing conduct; a privacy breach; account sharing; fraudulent orders; identity misuse; unauthorized resale; or unapproved product representation. Xenios may hold all commissions during the investigation.

13.3 The consequences ladder short of suspension (coaching note, retraining, Quality Review status, commission hold) is described in the Code of Conduct (XR-AFF-004) and applies under this agreement.

13.4 On termination: program access ends, links deactivate, the Rep stops representing any Xenios relationship, removes Xenios-branded content within [CONFIG: takedown window], and clean approved commissions are paid in the ordinary cycle. Commissions connected to the conduct causing a for-cause termination may be voided. [COUNSEL: confirm forfeiture scope against applicable sales-representative commission statutes]
