## 1. Status: this lane is not active

Xenios Research has no professional accounts and no institutional accounts. Today, every account in the program is an individual Founding Membership: private, application-based, approval required, 21 and over, with identity and age verification before agreements and payment. There is no practice account, clinic account, lab account, team account, university account, or any other organizational purchasing account, and no one is authorized to present one as available or coming by any particular date.

This document reserves the lane and records the controls that must exist before it can open. Nothing here is an offer, and nothing here is implied to be active.

## 2. Why this placeholder exists

The distribution program master defines the Professional or Institutional Research Account as a future, separate lane for qualified organizations, requiring its own credential, intended-use, purchasing, and documentation controls. It sits in the third build phase of the distribution system, alongside the wholesale lane (XR-AFF-017), and it is deliberately separate from the consumer membership, the affiliate program, and the Research Rep program. Writing the gate down now prevents the lane from being improvised through an individual membership or a rep relationship.

## 3. Rules in force today

1. Professionals may join as individuals. A trainer, coach, physician, or researcher may apply for an individual Founding Membership under the standard process, or participate as a Research Rep or organization partner under those programs. None of these creates a professional or institutional account, and none of them changes what the person may buy or say.
2. No clinical framing. No partner or member may imply that Xenios Research products are supplied for patient administration, clinical use, or professional dispensing. Peptide products are research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review. Quantum is Coming Soon with no commerce, no checkout, and no sales agreement, and any future physician-led product lane requires its own classification, administration, facility, state, claims, and transaction approvals first.
3. Claims discipline applies to professional audiences. A message aimed at a physician or a team is still program content. Reps and affiliates may not make claims to professionals that Xenios could not make directly, and the approved and prohibited claims lists apply unchanged.
4. No organizational purchasing through individual accounts. Buying on behalf of an organization through an individual membership is not permitted. The large and unusual order review (orders over $1,000, quantity inconsistent with ordinary individual use, or triggered risk rules) screens for exactly this pattern.
5. Organization partners are not institutional buyers. An organization partner relationship covers introductions, events, and negotiated direct-attribution economics. It carries no purchasing rights and no member-level sensitive data without consent.

## 4. Controls required before the lane can open

Each control below must be designed, built, and approved before the first professional or institutional account is opened. The list restates the distribution program master's requirement that this lane have its own credential, intended-use, purchasing, and documentation controls; counsel may add to it.

1. Credentialing controls: verification of professional licenses and standing, institutional affiliation, sanctions and discipline history where relevant, and re-verification on a schedule. The identity proofing used for individual members is the floor, not the ceiling, for this lane.
2. Intended-use controls: a written intended-use statement per account and, where appropriate, per order; eligibility decided per SKU against that SKU's written classification memorandum; no account framing that conveys human clinical use where the classification lane does not allow it. A research-use label is not a loophole, and the overall presentation controls.
3. Purchasing controls: named authorized purchasers, quantity and frequency limits, manual review of orders, documented approvals, and payment through Xenios systems only.
4. Documentation controls: recordkeeping sufficient to support audit, chain of custody, product concern and adverse event routing to Xenios, and access for Xenios to verify compliance.
5. Contract suite: a counsel-approved account agreement and terms, tax and payment terms at least as protective as XR-AFF-016, and a data processing addendum where personal data flows between the parties.
6. Regulatory groundwork: per-category classification sign-off, the state matrix for professional and institutional sales, processor approval for the transaction types involved, and insurance review. [COUNSEL: analyze whether selling to professionals or institutions changes the classification or intended-use analysis for any SKU, and which state professional or facility rules attach]
7. Production gate: every required readiness row for this lane approved, with an owner assigned, evidence attached, and a current review date, before any account opens.

## 5. How the lane opens

The sequence is fixed: counsel sign-off on the lane design and per-SKU eligibility first, then the Phase 3 build (institutional accounts, credential storage, purchasing workflow, documentation tooling), then Samuel Boadu's individual approval of each account. Samuel Boadu is the named accountable human for this lane. No account opens on model output, automation, or anyone's assurance that the lane exists.

## 6. Legal posture

This placeholder is an internal planning document. It grants nothing, offers nothing, and binds no third party. It does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and it does not relieve Xenios of duties imposed by law. Classification, claims, quality, and professional oversight are governed by law, not by disclaimers or by this document. Xenios does not represent itself as a HIPAA covered entity for direct-to-consumer services; the HIPAA applicability analysis is pending (see XR-POL-009), and whether an institutional lane would change that analysis is an open counsel question.
