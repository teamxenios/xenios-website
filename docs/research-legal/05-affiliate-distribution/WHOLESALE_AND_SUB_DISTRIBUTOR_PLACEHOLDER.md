## 1. Status: this lane is not active

Xenios Research has no wholesale program and no sub-distributor program. No wholesale, resale, or sub-distribution relationship exists, is offered, or is implied. No partner, Research Rep, organization partner, or member is authorized to buy Xenios Research products for resale, to represent that a wholesale lane exists, or to promise anyone future wholesale access.

This document reserves the lane and records what must be true before it can open. It creates no rights, no offer, and no expectation.

## 2. Why this placeholder exists

The distribution program master defines wholesale and sub-distribution as a future, separate program, planned for the third build phase of the distribution system (after member referrals and basic affiliates, and after Research Reps, certification, and organizations). Writing the gate down now prevents the two failure modes the program is designed against: untrained resellers making health claims, and an affiliate lane quietly turning into an inventory-purchase scheme.

## 3. Rules in force today

While the lane is closed, the following rules from the active program documents govern:

1. Consumer affiliates and Research Reps may not buy inventory for resale under an affiliate or rep agreement. This is a hard rule of the distribution program, not a discretionary one.
2. Unauthorized resale is an immediate suspension ground under the partner agreements, and commissions may be held during the investigation.
3. Compensation is never conditioned on inventory purchases. No one qualifies for any commission, tier, or rank by buying product. This is part of the program's anti-MLM structure.
4. Orders are screened for resale patterns. An order enters manual review when the total exceeds $1,000, when quantity is inconsistent with ordinary individual use, or when risk rules trigger. Quantity inconsistent with individual use is exactly the pattern a disguised reseller produces.
5. Peptide and Quantum commission lanes are disabled, and Quantum has no commerce at all while it is Coming Soon. A closed retail lane cannot be reopened sideways through a reseller.
6. Split fulfillment is not wholesale. During the launch period a fulfillment partner (Mitch) holds and fulfills peptide and Quantum inventory for Xenios's own member orders. That partner fulfills Xenios orders under Xenios's instructions; it does not buy for resale and is not a sub-distributor. The two relationships must never be conflated in any document or conversation.

## 4. Requirements before the lane can open

Every requirement below must be satisfied, evidenced, and approved before any wholesale or sub-distributor account is opened. This list restates the distribution program master and the compliance readiness master; counsel may add to it but nothing may be removed without counsel's written sign-off.

1. Business verification: legal entity, ownership, contact, licenses and registrations relevant to distributing the product categories involved.
2. Resale certificate and sales tax analysis: a valid resale certificate for each relevant state, and a written analysis of Xenios's own collection and registration duties for wholesale transactions. [COUNSEL: perform the sales tax and resale certificate analysis before any wholesale sale]
3. Insurance: product liability, general liability, cargo, and recall coverage appropriate to a distribution relationship, with limits and certificates confirmed. [COUNSEL: confirm required lines and limits with the insurance broker]
4. Quality system: approved supplier controls, written specifications, receiving inspection, quarantine, release, lot tracking, certificates of analysis, stability and expiry handling, first-expiry-first-out stock rotation, temperature excursion handling, complaint intake, corrective action, recall execution, and destruction records.
5. Storage: qualified storage conditions, including temperature-controlled storage where the product requires it, with monitoring records.
6. Chain of custody: documented custody from Xenios to the distributor to the distributor's channel, sufficient to support a recall and to keep returned or compromised product out of stock. Returned or compromised product is never restocked.
7. Claims training: completion of the claims and content training and certification before any product representation, with the same prohibited-claims rules that bind Research Reps. A distributor may never say what Xenios could not say.
8. Territories and channels: approved territories and sales channels in writing. No open-ended resale into unapproved channels, marketplaces, or states.
9. Separate product eligibility: eligibility decided per SKU against that SKU's written classification memorandum. Peptide products remain research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review, and they are not wholesale-eligible unless counsel approves that lane. Supplements are not wholesale-eligible unless the brand's written reseller authorization permits resale and its pricing and channel terms are honored. Quantum is not eligible at all while its lane is closed.
10. Contract suite: a counsel-approved wholesale agreement, a quality agreement, tax and payment terms at least as protective as XR-AFF-016, and a data processing addendum where any personal data flows.
11. Production gate: every required readiness row for the wholesale lane approved, with an owner assigned, evidence attached, and a current review date, matching the production gate rule that governs the rest of the program.

## 5. How the lane opens

The sequence is fixed: written per-SKU classification and counsel sign-off first, then the Phase 3 build (accounts, territories, inventory, and business account tooling), then Samuel Boadu's individual approval of each wholesale account. Samuel Boadu is the named accountable human for this lane; no account opens on model output, automation, or a partner's assurance.
