## 1. Purpose

This policy governs how partners (affiliates, Research Reps, and organization partners) represent Xenios Research on social platforms: feeds, stories, short and long video, live streams, groups, forums, and messaging features of those platforms. It applies to every registered channel and to any unregistered channel a partner uses to promote Xenios, which is itself a violation (Section 3).

## 2. Platform conduct

- Be honest. No fabricated results, fake accounts, purchased engagement, or manufactured scarcity ("only 3 spots left" when untrue).
- Follow each platform's own terms of service and advertising rules. A platform violation that involves Xenios promotion is also a violation of this policy.
- Be respectful. No harassment, no disparagement of identifiable individuals, no arguing health claims in comment threads. Escalate hostile threads to Xenios rather than engaging.
- Stay in lane. Product questions, health questions, and complaint threads are relayed to Xenios. Partners do not answer medical questions in comments or DMs, per XR-AFF-009 Section 4.
- Respect the private nature of the program. The membership is private and application-based. Partners do not publish member lists, member results, applicant names, or screenshots of member areas.

## 3. Registered channels only

- Partners list their channels (platform and handle) at application. Xenios reviews channels as part of verification.
- A new channel must be registered before it is used for Xenios promotion.
- Promotion from unregistered channels, or from accounts the partner does not control, is a violation.
- Account sharing (letting others post from the partner's registered account for Xenios promotion, or sharing partner portal credentials) is an immediate-suspension trigger under XR-AFF-014.

## 4. Approved assets only

- Use the approved asset library as issued: links, QR codes, landing pages, email templates, social posts, event deck, FAQ, disclosure templates, and brand assets.
- Do not edit approved assets' claims, disclosures, or product statements. An edited asset becomes unapproved content.
- Partner-created content beyond the approved library requires written pre-approval before publication (XR-AFF-009 Section 7). Unapproved ads are an immediate-suspension trigger.
- Use only the partner's own attributed links and codes. Code stuffing, posting codes on coupon sites where prohibited, and link cloaking that hides the destination are fraud indicators and are prohibited.

## 5. Disclosure in every promotional post

Every promotional post, story, video, stream, or message must carry the clear and conspicuous disclosure required by the FTC Disclosure Policy (XR-AFF-008), placed per that policy's channel table and using approved wording. No exceptions for reposts, shares, or "casual" mentions. A disclosure in a previous post does not carry over.

## 6. Paid advertising rules

- No paid-search brand bidding without prior written approval. Partners may not bid on "Xenios", "Xenios Research", misspellings, or confusingly similar terms in any search or ad platform without written approval from Xenios. Misleading paid search is a fraud indicator in the program's fraud controls.
- All paid ads are unapproved content unless pre-approved in writing, including boosted posts.
- Ad accounts must belong to the partner. Running Xenios ads from third-party or rented ad accounts is prohibited.
- Approved ads must carry the required disclosure and only registry-allowed claims.

## 7. No health-condition targeting

- Partners may not target advertising or outreach by health condition, disease state, medication use, or similar health signals, on any platform.
- No purchased health-condition lists, ever, for any channel.
- No custom or lookalike audiences built from health data.
- No hidden health targeting: choosing proxies (for example, interest groups that exist to discuss a condition) to reach people by condition is the same violation.
- The program is 21+. Partner promotion must not target minors, and age-gating must be used where the platform provides it. [CONFIG: minimum audience age setting per platform, default 21 where supported.]

## 8. Privacy on social channels

- Never collect health information through social channels: no symptom polls, no condition questionnaires, no "DM me your health goals" funnels that store health details.
- Never request or accept passwords, identity documents, or payment details over social platforms or DMs. Payments only occur on the platform checkout; identity steps only occur in the official flow.
- Lead information collected through approved forms is limited to name, contact, and consent, and is entered into the Xenios system promptly, per XR-AFF-013 Section 6. No private spreadsheets or local lead files.
- Applicant and member information is never exposed: no reading out application statuses, no posting referral results with names.

## 9. Identity and impersonation

- Partner accounts must be clearly the partner's own. No handles, names, or profiles that imply the account is an official Xenios account.
- No use of the Xenios name or brand assets in an account name or profile without written approval; approved format: [CONFIG: approved handle format, for example "Name, Independent Xenios Research Rep"].
- Verified-looking mimicry of Xenios pages, or claiming to speak for Xenios, is identity misuse and an immediate-suspension trigger under XR-AFF-014.

## 10. Monitoring and consequences

Xenios monitors registered channels periodically and on risk triggers, per XR-AFF-008 Section 7 and XR-AFF-009 Section 8. Violations follow the enforcement path in XR-AFF-014, with commission holds and reversals per XR-AFF-007. Safety claims, dosing content, privacy breaches, account sharing, identity misuse, and unapproved product representation trigger immediate suspension. Correction requests must be completed within [CONFIG: correction window, hours].

## 11. Relationship to law

This policy is designed to keep partner social activity within FTC endorsement and advertising rules and platform requirements. It does not guarantee legal compliance in every situation, does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and does not relieve Xenios or any partner of duties imposed by law.
