## 1. Purpose

Partners extend the reach of Xenios Research. They do not extend what Xenios is allowed to say. This policy defines what partners may and may not claim about the membership, the products, and the results, and it binds every partner channel: posts, videos, live streams, emails, messages, events, and conversations. The controlling rule is simple: a partner may never say what Xenios itself could not lawfully say, and testimonials cannot make claims Xenios could not make directly.

## 2. The claims registry

Xenios maintains a claims registry. It is the single source of truth for what may be said, per product and per channel. Each registry row records:

```text
claim
product
channel
evidence
allowed
prohibited
reviewer
review date
```

Rules of use:

1. Approved claims only. A partner may make a claim only if the registry lists it as allowed for that product and that channel.
2. Registry linkage. Every claim in partner content must map to an allowed registry row. Content containing a claim with no registry row is unapproved content.
3. Registry changes flow down. When a registry row changes to prohibited, partners must stop using the claim and correct or remove existing content within [CONFIG: correction window, hours] of notice.
4. When in doubt, ask. Partners relay product questions to Xenios instead of improvising answers.

## 3. Prohibited claims (the hard list)

No partner may state or imply any of the following, in any wording, on any channel:

- Guaranteed fat loss.
- Guaranteed muscle gain.
- Any guaranteed result of any kind.
- That any product or program can cure any condition.
- That any product or program can treat any condition.
- That any product is safe for everyone.
- That any product is FDA approved, when it is not.
- That any product or program is clinically proven, when it is not.
- "Research use only" used as a loophole: research-use language paired with human-outcome promises. A research-use disclaimer does not control when the overall presentation conveys human use.

Implied claims count the same as express claims. Imagery, before-and-after photos, product names, hashtags, emoji, and "what I take and why" storytelling can all imply a prohibited claim without stating it. The test is the overall net impression on a reasonable viewer, not the literal words.

## 4. No diagnosis, no prescribing, no dosing

Partners, including Research Reps, may never:

- diagnose any condition,
- prescribe or recommend any product for a person's condition,
- recommend dosing, timing, cycles, or administration methods,
- interpret labs or health data,
- or guarantee results.

These prohibitions apply everywhere: content, comments, DMs, Telegram, events, and private conversations. A question that asks for any of these is relayed to Xenios. A possible product concern or adverse event is relayed immediately through the partner's trained escalation path, never answered with advice.

## 5. Lane matching

Every product has a classification lane, recorded in a written classification memorandum. Partner content must match the lane:

- Peptides: peptide products are research products whose classification and permitted marketing lane are under formal review. Partners may not promote peptide SKUs at all until Xenios opens that lane in writing. Peptide commissions are disabled (XR-AFF-007).
- Quantum: member-facing state is Coming Soon. Partners may not promote Quantum commerce, take interest commitments, or describe administration. There is no sales lane and no commission.
- Supplements: only SKUs cleared for sale may be promoted, using only registry-allowed structure/function claims. No disease claims.
- Membership and services: partners may describe the membership process, pricing, and included services factually, using approved materials. The tracker, plans, Guides, and support are not medical care, not a medical device, and not an emergency service.

## 6. Testimonials and personal experience

- A testimonial must be the honest, current experience of the person giving it.
- A testimonial cannot make a claim Xenios could not make directly. "It cured my condition" is prohibited even if the speaker believes it.
- Results-flavored testimonials must not imply that the result is typical or guaranteed.
- Partners with a material connection must disclose it with every testimonial (XR-AFF-008).
- Partners may not solicit, script, or pay third parties for testimonials without prior written approval from Xenios.

## 7. Approved content and pre-approval

- Xenios provides approved assets: links, QR codes, landing pages, email templates, social posts, event deck, FAQ, approved claims, prohibited claims, disclosure templates, and brand assets.
- Partners use approved assets as issued. Editing an approved asset's claims, disclosures, or product statements makes it unapproved content.
- New partner-created promotional content that goes beyond approved assets requires written pre-approval before publication. Unapproved ads are prohibited and are an immediate-suspension trigger under XR-AFF-014.

## 8. Monitoring and enforcement

- Xenios reviews express claims, implied claims, imagery, testimonials, influencer content, and recommendations on partner channels, periodically and on risk triggers.
- A safety claim (a claim about curing, treating, or safety for everyone) or any dosing content triggers immediate suspension and investigation under XR-AFF-014.
- Other violations follow the escalating path in XR-AFF-014, with commission holds and reversals per XR-AFF-007.
- No claims violation can be offset by sales volume.

## 9. Relationship to law

Claims, classification, quality, and professional oversight are governed by law, not by disclaimers. This policy is designed to keep partner content within what law permits, but it does not guarantee legal compliance in every situation, does not waive rights that cannot be waived under applicable law, and does not relieve Xenios or any partner of duties imposed by law.
