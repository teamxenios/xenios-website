## Plain-Language Summary

This policy explains how to cancel, when cancellation becomes effective, and how duplicate, reversed, or approved refunds are handled. The business rules below apply unless Xenios publishes a lawful prospective amendment.

## Cancellation Method

Submit cancellation through the cancellation control in the member portal or written notice to samuel@xeniostechnology.com or contact samuel@xeniostechnology.com. Xenios may authenticate the request. Cancellation should be as easy as the method used to enroll when applicable law requires.

## Effective Date

Unless law or a written exception requires otherwise, cancellation is effective at the end of the current paid membership period. Access may continue through that date unless suspended for breach or security reasons.

## Activation Payment

The $50 activation payment includes the first 30 days. Refund treatment is the Cancellation and Refund Policy published in the member portal. Counsel must determine whether any cooling-off, cancellation, or state-specific right applies.

## Duplicate and Administrative Error

Confirmed duplicate payments and Xenios-caused billing errors will be corrected or refunded after reconciliation.

## Reversed or Fraudulent Payments

A reversed, recalled, disputed, unauthorized, or fraudulent payment may result in suspension, collection, setoff where lawful, cancellation, and recovery of direct costs permitted by law.

## Product Transactions

Product refunds, shipping claims, replacements, and damaged-order remedies are governed separately and do not automatically require a membership refund.

## Refund Method and Timing

Approved refunds will be sent through an appropriate lawful method to a verified recipient, ordinarily within 10 business days, subject to provider delays, identity checks, fraud review, and legal holds.

## Abuse

Xenios may deny a refund request based on material misrepresentation, fabricated evidence, unauthorized resale, abusive conduct, or violation of research-use terms, except where law requires a remedy.

## Preserved Rights

Nothing limits a nonwaivable statutory right or remedy.
