## Plain-Language Summary

This standalone agreement directs most covered disputes to confidential individual arbitration after notice and an informal resolution period. Legally required exceptions remain.

## IMPORTANT NOTICE

PLEASE READ CAREFULLY. THIS AGREEMENT AFFECTS HOW DISPUTES ARE RESOLVED. EXCEPT FOR STATED EXCEPTIONS, YOU AND XENIOS AGREE TO RESOLVE COVERED DISPUTES THROUGH INDIVIDUAL BINDING ARBITRATION, NOT A JUDGE OR JURY, AND NOT AS A CLASS, COLLECTIVE, CONSOLIDATED, OR REPRESENTATIVE ACTION.

## Covered Disputes

Subject to the exceptions below, “Covered Dispute” means any past, present, or future claim between you and Xenios or its owners, officers, employees, affiliates, suppliers, contractors, payment recipients, fulfillment providers, or agents arising out of or relating to your application, identity review, agreements, membership, privacy, payments, communications, platform use, orders, products, shipping, cancellation, or the relationship between the parties, whether based in contract, tort, statute, regulation, fraud, misrepresentation, privacy, or another legal theory.

## Informal Notice and Conference

Before filing arbitration, the claimant must send an individualized written Notice of Dispute to samuel@xeniostechnology.com and the business address displayed in the authenticated member portal and on official transaction records describing the claimant, facts, legal basis, requested relief, and a good-faith settlement demand. The parties will attempt individual resolution for 30 calendar days. If requested, each party will personally participate in a telephone or video conference. Limitation periods are tolled only to the extent applicable law allows.

## Agreement to Arbitrate

Unresolved Covered Disputes will be decided by one neutral arbitrator administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) under its applicable consumer rules, as modified by this Agreement. The Federal Arbitration Act governs interpretation and enforcement of this arbitration agreement. Substantive Texas law applies to the merits to the extent not preempted.

## Individual Relief Only

The arbitrator may award only relief necessary to resolve the individual claimant’s dispute. No party may pursue or obtain class, collective, coordinated, consolidated, private-attorney-general, or representative relief on behalf of others, except to the extent a nonwaivable law requires otherwise.

## Jury-Trial Waiver

For any Covered Dispute that is permitted to proceed in court, each party knowingly and voluntarily waives trial by jury to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## Hearing and Fees

Hearings may occur remotely or in Austin, Texas, or by remote proceeding when permitted by the applicable rules, subject to applicable consumer rules and reasonable access. Xenios will pay fees it is required to pay under applicable law and administrator rules. Each party bears its own attorneys’ fees unless a statute, rule, or award permits otherwise.

## Exceptions

Either party may bring an eligible individual action in small-claims court. Either party may seek narrowly tailored temporary or provisional relief in court to protect account security, confidential information, intellectual property, or the arbitral process. Claims excluded from predispute arbitration by federal or state law are not required to be arbitrated.

## Who Decides Enforceability

A court, not the arbitrator, will decide disputes concerning formation, existence, enforceability, scope of the class-action waiver, and application of a statutory exclusion where applicable. Other procedural and merits questions may be decided by the arbitrator as permitted by the applicable rules and law.

## Severability

If a provision other than the individual-action requirement is unenforceable, it will be severed or narrowed. If the individual-action requirement is finally held unenforceable for a particular claim and cannot lawfully be severed, that claim will proceed in court and any arbitrable claims will be stayed as permitted by law.

## Opt-Out Decision

No opt-out is offered except where applicable law affirmatively requires one. Any legally required opt-out procedure will be stated in the published notice.

## Confidentiality of Arbitration

The parties will request that filings, evidence, and hearings remain confidential, except as reasonably necessary to enforce an award, obtain legal advice, comply with law, report to authorities, or exercise protected rights. The arbitrator may enter appropriate protective orders.
