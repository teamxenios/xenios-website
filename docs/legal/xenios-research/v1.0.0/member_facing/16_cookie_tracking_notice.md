## Plain-Language Summary

This notice distinguishes essential authentication and security technologies from optional analytics or advertising technologies.

## Essential Technologies

Xenios may use cookies, local storage, tokens, and similar technologies necessary for authentication, session continuity, security, fraud prevention, load balancing, preferences, and agreement workflows.

## Analytics

If enabled, Xenios may use privacy-reviewed analytics providers disclosed in the current cookie table; analytics are disabled on identity, agreement-signing, and payment-evidence pages to understand aggregate usage and performance. Configure analytics to avoid government-ID images, payment evidence, sensitive form values, and private payment instructions.

## Advertising

Advertising or cross-context behavioral tracking is not used unless specifically disclosed in the current Privacy Policy. It must not be enabled without updating this notice, the Privacy Policy, consent controls, and applicable opt-out mechanisms.

## Choices

You can manage nonessential technologies through the website cookie settings panel. Blocking essential technologies may prevent sign-in or secure operation.
