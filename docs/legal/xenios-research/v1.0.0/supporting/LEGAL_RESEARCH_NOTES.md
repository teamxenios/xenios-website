# Legal Research Notes

These notes identify primary authorities that counsel should verify and apply to the final facts.

1. **Federal Arbitration Act, 9 U.S.C. § 2.** Written arbitration provisions in contracts involving commerce are generally enforceable, subject to grounds that apply to contracts generally and statutory exclusions.
2. **Ending Forced Arbitration of Sexual Assault and Sexual Harassment Act, 9 U.S.C. § 402.** A person alleging covered sexual assault or harassment may elect not to enforce a predispute arbitration agreement or joint-action waiver for that case.
3. **E-SIGN Act, 15 U.S.C. § 7001.** Electronic signatures and records generally cannot be denied effect solely because they are electronic. Consumer electronic disclosures require affirmative consent, paper-copy and withdrawal information, technology requirements, and accessible retained records when the statute applies.
4. **Restore Online Shoppers’ Confidence Act, 15 U.S.C. § 8403.** Internet negative-option charges require clear material terms, express informed consent, and a simple way to stop recurring charges.
5. **Consumer Review Fairness Act, 15 U.S.C. § 45b.** Standard consumer contracts cannot prohibit or penalize honest reviews or take ownership of review content, while lawful confidentiality protections for trade secrets and private information remain possible.
6. **Texas Business & Commerce Code § 521.052.** Businesses must maintain reasonable procedures to protect sensitive personal information and dispose of records appropriately.
7. **Texas Business & Commerce Code § 521.053.** Breach notification obligations can apply when sensitive personal information is acquired by an unauthorized person.
8. **Texas Business & Commerce Code Chapter 541.** Depending on applicability and exemptions, controllers have duties concerning data minimization, consent for sensitive data, privacy notices, security, rights, processors, and assessments.
9. **Texas Business & Commerce Code Chapter 322.** Texas recognizes electronic transactions and signatures under its Uniform Electronic Transactions Act.
10. **Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code Chapter 171.** Texas law recognizes written arbitration agreements, subject to contract defenses and statutory limits.

This list is not exhaustive and does not replace state-by-state review.
